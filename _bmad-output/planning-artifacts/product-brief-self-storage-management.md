# Product Brief: VaultDesk — Self-Storage Facility Management

---

## Executive Summary

Independent self-storage operators in the US are trapped. The dominant software vendor, Storable, rolled up all three major platforms (SiteLink, storEDGE, SpareFoot) under PE ownership and has since raised prices relentlessly, degraded support to near-zero, locked operators into a proprietary payment processor, and stopped building new features. With 40,000+ independent US facilities — the vast majority of the 51,000+ total — there is no compelling, affordable, US-focused alternative.

**VaultDesk** is a flat-rate ($49–79/month), modern, US-built self-storage management platform for independent operators who are fed up with Storable. It does everything operators actually need — unit tracking, tenant management, Stripe-native autopay, automated 50-state lien processing, and an embeddable website widget for online rentals — with none of the payment lock-in, hidden fees, or support black holes that define the incumbent.

The opportunity is textbook: a dominant PE-backed roll-up harvesting margin from a captive customer base, a clear pricing gap at $49–79/month (no legitimate US alternative exists), and a community of operators who actively discuss software alternatives in forums and at industry events. VaultDesk enters as the transparent, operator-aligned choice — the product Storable used to be, before the acquisition.

**Core positioning:** *"Modern self-storage software that works for you, not for Storable's payment processor."*

**Target market:** 40,000 independent US facilities (1–5 locations, up to 300 units/site)  
**Revenue target:** $12K MRR in 12 months (200 paying customers); $1M ARR in 24 months  
**LTD strategy:** $99 (up to 150 units) / $149 (up to 300 units), sold direct via community channels

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Independent self-storage operators — family-owned businesses running 1–5 locations — are systematically underserved by the software that runs their entire business. After Storable acquired SiteLink, storEDGE, and SpareFoot in 2018–2019 (backed by PE firms Cove Hill Partners and later EQT), the consolidated vendor shifted focus from product quality to margin extraction:

- **Payment processor lock-in:** Storable requires operators to use Storable Payments. Payment processing is the #2 controllable expense at any storage facility. Operators report "sky rocketing rates" with no ability to negotiate or switch.
- **Support collapse:** Post-acquisition, Storable's help desk reportedly staffed ~7 people serving 4,000+ locations. Chat-only with no phone support, 1–2 week email response times for billing issues.
- **Feature freeze:** Prior to acquisition, SiteLink and storEDGE shipped new features monthly. Post-acquisition: "lucky to see one or two big fixes a quarter." Chip/tap card readers unsupported for 7+ years. Smartphone payments removed.
- **Predictable price hikes:** Every 1.5–2 years, operators receive rate increase notices with no competitive alternative to switch to.

The pain is not hypothetical — it is actively discussed on Self-Storage Talk (the primary industry forum), in the "Self Storage Owners" Facebook group, and at SSA industry events. Operators know they're trapped. They need an exit.

### Problem Impact

For a typical 150-unit independent facility generating $150K/year in revenue:
- Payment processing at 2.5–3.5% (Storable's bundled rate) costs $3,750–5,250/year — vs. ~$2,100/year at market rate via Stripe
- Software costs are rising ~20–30% per renewal cycle with no improvement in product quality
- Online rental capability (lost revenue from COVID-exposed gap) remains unavailable or requires expensive add-ons
- Lien processing errors (state-specific legal compliance) create liability exposure with manual, error-prone workflows

The emotional impact compounds the financial: operators feel disrespected by a vendor that holds them hostage, treats support requests as burdens, and raises prices simply because switching is hard. This emotional frustration is a powerful acquisition driver — operators are not just looking for a cheaper tool; they want to feel like a customer again.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Competitor | Why It Falls Short |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Storable (SiteLink/storEDGE)** | The problem itself. Payment lock-in, poor support, stalled features, rising costs |
| **Cubby** | AI-native, premium-priced ($$$), targeting enterprise/tech-forward operators — not the $49/mo indie segment |
| **Storeganise** | EU-focused, opaque pricing, limited US lien-law compliance, no US-specific support |
| **Stora** | UK-focused (~$125/mo), not US-compliant |
| **6Storage** | Raised LTD holders' prices 300%, destroying community trust — now "the cautionary tale" |
| **QuikStor** | Desktop-era UX, declining relevance despite recent lien module additions |
| **Tenant Inc.** | Modern but expensive (~$550/month), targeting mid-market not indie |

No US-focused platform exists at $49–79/month with flat transparent pricing, payment processor freedom, integrated lien processing, and online rental capability. This is the whitespace.

### Proposed Solution

VaultDesk is a cloud-based self-storage management platform built specifically for US independent operators. The core product delivers everything needed to run a modern facility:

1. **Unit & facility management** — Visual floor plan grid, unit status (available/occupied/reserved/overlock), unit type catalog, multi-unit batch operations
2. **Tenant management** — Contact records, lease management, move-in/move-out workflows, document storage, notes
3. **Stripe-native autopay** — Operators connect their own Stripe account; VaultDesk never touches payment processing margins. Supports ACH, card, and manual payment recording
4. **Automated lien processing** — State-selected lien workflows: certified mail notices, military status checks (SCRA), auction scheduling, statute-specific timelines for all 50 states
5. **Online rental + website widget** — Public-facing unit availability embed (one line of code), online move-in flow, digital lease signing
6. **Reporting & AR aging** — Occupancy, revenue, delinquency, move-in/move-out trends

**Product promise:** Simple enough to set up in 30 minutes. Complete enough to never need another tool.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Payment processor freedom** — Operators use their own Stripe (or any other processor they choose). VaultDesk does not have a bundled payment product and never will. This is the headline differentiator in every channel.

2. **Flat transparent pricing** — $49/month for up to 150 units, $79/month for 151–300 units. Published on the pricing page. No custom quotes. No surprise renewals. If we raise prices, we give 90 days notice and grandfather existing customers for 12 months.

3. **Integrated 50-state lien automation** — Not a paid add-on, not an external integration. Built into the base product. This eliminates a compliance liability that keeps operators up at night.

4. **Free data migration from SiteLink/storEDGE** — We remove the highest-friction switching barrier. White-glove migration support for the first 200 customers.

5. **Real US-based support** — Named account rep or <4-hour business-hour response SLA. Phone + email. Not offshore chat.

6. **Built for independents, not chains** — No enterprise complexity. No 100-page onboarding guides. No features that only make sense if you manage 500 locations.

## Target Users

**Background:** Linda, 54, owns a 180-unit self-storage facility in suburban Tennessee. She bought it 12 years ago as a retirement investment. Her daughter helps manage it part-time. **Current frustrations:**

- Logs in daily (5–10 min) to check occupancy, review overnight payments, handle any move-ins

### Secondary Users

**Facility Managers / Staff:** Employees or part-time managers who use the system day-to-day. They need a clean, intuitive interface — they're not tech-savvy and don't want a learning curve. Role: read/write tenant and payment records; cannot change billing settings or export data.

**Tenants (indirect users):** Interact with the public-facing online rental flow and tenant portal (pay rent, view lease, request move-out). Not the buyer, but their experience directly impacts operator satisfaction with VaultDesk.

**Accountants / Bookkeepers:** Need clean export of revenue data to QuickBooks or CSV. Occasional users who will request this from operators — making simple financial export a key feature request.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (Must Ship at Launch)

**1. Facility & Unit Management**
- Visual floor plan grid (row/column layout, not a CAD tool — simple table grid)
- Unit status management: Available, Occupied, Reserved, Overlocked, Maintenance
- Unit type catalog (10×10, 10×20, climate-controlled, etc.) with monthly rates
- Bulk status updates (overlock 5 units at once)
- Basic occupancy dashboard (% occupied, revenue by unit type)

**2. Tenant Management**
- Tenant contact records (name, email, phone, address, ID upload)
- Lease assignment (tenant → unit, start date, rate, auto-renew)
- Move-in and move-out workflow with digital checklist
- Tenant notes / activity log
- Delinquency flagging (days past due, current balance)

**3. Stripe-Native Autopay**
- Stripe Connect integration (operators connect their own Stripe account — VaultDesk uses platform model)
- Recurring billing: monthly charge on billing anniversary
- ACH and card support
- Failed payment retry logic (3 attempts over 7 days)
- Manual payment recording (cash, check, money order)
- Basic receipt emailing to tenant

**4. AR Aging & Basic Reporting**
- AR aging report (current, 30, 60, 90+ days)
- Occupancy report (daily snapshot, monthly trend)
- Revenue report (collected vs. expected, by unit type)
- Move-in/move-out log
- CSV export of all data

**5. Automated Lien Processing (US, 50 states)**
- State selection at account setup (pulls correct statute timelines)
- Lien workflow trigger (operator clicks "Start Lien Process" on delinquent tenant)
- Automated notice schedule (Day 1: overlock, Day 14: certified mail notice, Day 30: second notice, Day 45: auction listing, etc. — varies by state)
- Military status check integration (SCRA lookup via DoD SCRA portal)
- Lien log with timestamped audit trail (critical for legal defense)
- Pre-built notice templates (operator customizes facility name/address, system fills tenant/unit/balance)

**6. Online Rental & Website Widget**
- Public-facing unit availability page (hosted by VaultDesk, embeddable via iframe or JS snippet)
- Online move-in flow: unit selection → tenant info → ID upload → lease signing (DocuSign-free e-sign) → payment → confirmation
- Gate code delivery via email/SMS on move-in completion
- Website widget: one-line embed code showing available units + "Reserve Now" button

**7. Multi-User Access**
- Owner role (full access including billing settings and data export)
- Manager role (tenant management, payments, reports — no billing/export)
- Read-only role (view occupancy and reports, no edits)

**8. SiteLink/storEDGE Data Migration Tool**
- Accept SiteLink CSV export format (standard export that SiteLink provides)
- Map and import: tenant records, unit assignments, lease dates, payment history (last 12 months)
- Validation report before final import (shows conflicts, missing fields)
- Migration concierge: dedicated human review for first 200 customers

### Out of Scope for MVP

These are explicitly deferred — they appear in forums but are not blockers for the initial target customer:

- **Access control / gate integration** (PTI, DoorKing, Nokē) — high value but complex; defer to V1.1
- **Multi-site dashboard** (2–5 locations under one login) — defer to V1.2 (needed for secondary segment)
- **Revenue management / dynamic pricing** — advanced feature; Cubby's territory for now
- **Insurance integration** (tenant protection plans) — revenue opportunity but adds compliance complexity
- **QuickBooks direct sync** — CSV export covers this for MVP; API integration deferred
- **StoragePug / website builder replacement** — the widget handles the core need; full website builder is out of scope
- **Mobile native app** (iOS/Android) — mobile-responsive web covers MVP needs
- **Canada / international lien compliance** — US-only for MVP
- **AI features / chatbots / revenue optimization** — not the target positioning; this is Cubby's lane

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is validated when:
1. **50 paying customers** within 6 months of launch
2. **>90% migration completion rate** — operators who start a SiteLink migration successfully go live
3. **Zero lien compliance incidents** — no operator reports a legally deficient notice sequence in the first 12 months
4. **NPS > 50** at 90-day mark from first cohort
5. **<2% monthly churn** — if operators are churning at this rate, the core product has a retention problem that must be diagnosed before scaling marketing
