# Product Brief: TruckStack — Food Truck Operations Platform

---

## Executive Summary

TruckStack is the missing operations layer for America's 36,000–40,000 food truck operators. Food truck owners today run their business across 4–6 disconnected tools — Google Calendar for routes, Instagram DMs for event bookings, spreadsheets for permit deadlines, and email for client follow-up. TruckStack consolidates all of this into a single, mobile-first platform priced at $49/month, replacing their entire fragmented ops stack without touching their POS.

The core insight: no competitor — not Square, not Toast, not Roaming Hunger, not any of the 15 food truck tools reviewed — combines (a) a public-facing "where are we today" location schedule page, (b) professional event/catering booking with contracts and deposits, (c) permit expiration tracking with renewal reminders, and (d) an event organizer CRM. This four-part combination defines a new product category: the food truck ops platform.

The opportunity fits the "boring business SaaS" profile precisely: a small, non-glamorous vertical with real daily pain, no VC-backed consolidator, operators already paying $300/month across fragmented tools, and tight community channels (Facebook groups with 80K+ combined members, r/foodtrucks) that enable community-led growth without a large sales team.

Target go-to-market: AppSumo LTD at $149 (target 500–1,000 sales = $75K–$150K validation + funding), followed by $49/month SaaS tier launch. Revenue target: $1M ARR within 3 years at 10% market penetration.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Food truck operators run legally complex, logistics-intensive small businesses with virtually no purpose-built software support beyond point-of-sale payments. The average food truck operator:

- Manages 30–50 permit and license renewals per year across multiple jurisdictions (health department, city vending license, parking, event-specific permits, commissary agreements) — tracked entirely in spreadsheets or notes apps, with no reminder system
- Receives catering and event booking inquiries via Instagram DMs or email, collects deposits via Venmo, sends contracts as Word attachments — resulting in an unprofessional client experience and no systematic CRM for repeat organizers
- Announces their daily location via Instagram or Facebook posts — with no bookmarkable, customer-subscribable "where are we today" page that they own
- Cannot answer "which locations or events are most profitable?" because their revenue data lives in their POS system with no ops-level aggregation

The consequence: operators face real financial risk from missed permit renewals (fines of $500–$2,000+ or operational shutdown), lost event revenue from disorganized booking workflows, and customer churn from customers unable to track locations. This is not a hypothetical pain — it is documented across Reddit (r/foodtrucks), Facebook groups, and three independent permit tracking startups that have emerged to address the compliance gap alone.

### Problem Impact

- **Compliance risk is existential:** A missed health permit or city vending license renewal can shut a food truck down immediately. With 30–50 renewals per year and no reminder system, operators rely on city notices (often arriving after deadlines). PermitWise and TruckComply exist specifically because this pain is acute enough to pay for a standalone solution.
- **Event revenue is the highest-margin income stream:** Private catering events ($2,000–$8,000 each) represent the best margin for food truck operators, yet the booking workflow is genuinely embarrassing — Instagram DMs, Venmo deposits, emailed Word contracts. Operators lose repeat business because they have no client history system.
- **Location awareness is a daily customer retention problem:** 71% of consumers use Google Maps to find places to eat. Operators who post daily on Instagram are dependent on the algorithm; they have no owned, bookmarkable channel for customers to follow their location.
- **Tool fragmentation costs time operators don't have:** Solo operators managing their own truck, food prep, and customer service cannot afford the cognitive overhead of 4–6 different tools with different logins, mobile apps, and data silos.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Category | Tool Examples | Critical Gaps |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| POS Systems | Square, Toast, Clover | Zero permit tracking; no public location page; no event CRM; no ops-layer features at all |
| Booking Tools | SchedulingKit, Check Cherry | No permit tracking; no public map page; not food-truck-native; limited or no CRM |
| Permit Trackers | PermitWise, TruckComply, PitStop | Standalone only; no integration with scheduling, booking, or CRM |
| Marketplaces | Roaming Hunger | Takes client ownership; operators funnel through Roaming Hunger's platform; no ops management |
| Location Discovery | StreetFoodFinder | Consumer directory, not an operator ops tool |

The root cause of the gap is structural: POS companies focus on payments, booking tools focus on scheduling, compliance tools focus on compliance. No vendor has built the integrated ops layer that sits above the POS and connects all four functions. TruckStack is that layer.

### Proposed Solution

TruckStack is the operations platform for food trucks — not a POS, not a marketplace. It is the layer on top of Square and Toast that handles everything operators currently juggle across 4–6 disconnected tools.

**Four core modules, one platform:**

1. **Location Schedule Page** — A branded, public-facing "where are we today" page with an embedded map. Operators set their weekly/daily locations; customers bookmark or subscribe to location alerts. Operators own their customer relationship (vs. Roaming Hunger's marketplace dependency).

2. **Event & Catering Booking Portal** — A professional client-facing booking page with per-head pricing, menu packages, travel fee configuration, deposit collection (via Stripe), e-signature contracts, and final balance tracking. Replaces the Instagram DM + Venmo + Word contract workflow with a professional system that builds client trust and enables repeat business.

3. **Permit Expiration Tracker** — Dashboard tracking all active permits and licenses with configurable 30/60/90-day email and SMS renewal reminders. Supports the top 20 US cities' permit types with user-contributed expansion. Connects permit status to location scheduling ("this parking permit expires before your scheduled event — renew first").

4. **Event Organizer CRM** — Contact profiles for repeat event organizers (corporate clients, wedding planners, festival coordinators) with event history, past menu selections, notes, and revenue per client. Enables professional relationship management that drives repeat high-margin bookings.

**Critical positioning:** TruckStack does NOT replace Square or Toast. It complements them. This is a feature, not a limitation — it eliminates the switching cost objection entirely and makes TruckStack additive rather than disruptive to operators' existing setup.

### Key Differentiators

1. **The only integrated food truck ops platform:** First mover in combining permit tracking + location scheduling + event booking + event CRM in a single, food-truck-native product.

2. **Permit tracker as the acquisition hook:** The compliance gap is the sharpest pain (existential risk, no POS competitor touches it, PermitWise validates willingness to pay). TruckStack leads with permit tracking to acquire users who stay for the full platform.

3. **Operator-owned location page:** Unlike Roaming Hunger (which requires operators to route clients through the Roaming Hunger marketplace), TruckStack's location page is the operator's own branded URL. Customers follow the operator, not a third-party platform. This creates direct customer relationship ownership that no marketplace can replicate.

4. **POS-agnostic design:** Deliberately avoids the POS switching cost objection. Operators keep Square or Toast; TruckStack wraps around it. This also means TruckStack can eventually import transaction data from Square/Toast for revenue analytics — a future feature that becomes more valuable the longer operators use TruckStack.

5. **Community-ready brand:** "TruckStack" is a name that resonates with the food truck community's self-image as scrappy, independent operators. The product's messaging ("replace 4 tools at $49/month") is crafted for the deal-hunters in food truck Facebook groups.

6. **Boring business defensibility:** The 35K–40K US food truck market is too small for VC-backed competitors or POS giants to prioritize. TruckStack can own this vertical without fighting large, well-funded incumbents.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: Maria — The Solo Truck Owner (core target, ~70% of TAM)**

Maria, 38, left her marketing job five years ago to open "Birria Boss," a Mexican street food truck in Austin, TX. She runs the truck herself with one part-time employee on weekends. Annual revenue: ~$180,000. She uses Square (free plan) for payments, posts daily locations on Instagram, manages event inquiries via DM, tracks permit dates in a Google Sheet she built herself, and keeps a notes app list of repeat corporate clients.

*Her daily reality:* She spent 45 minutes last week on the phone with a corporate client who wanted to book her for their quarterly event — taking notes in her phone, then manually sending a Venmo request for the deposit and emailing a PDF contract she found online and edited in Word. She knows her health permit expires "sometime in the summer" but isn't sure of the exact date. She posts her location on Instagram every morning and answers "where are you today?" DMs several times a day from regular customers.

*What would make her say "this is exactly what I needed":*
- Never worrying about permit renewals again — just a reminder 30 days out
- A booking link she can share with corporate clients that looks professional
- A public page where her regular customers can see her weekly schedule without her having to post every day

*Her WTP:* $29–$49/month or $79–$149 LTD. She's the AppSumo target — will buy a lifetime deal if the ROI story is clear.

**Persona 2: Carlos — The Small Fleet Operator (growth segment, ~20% of TAM)**

Carlos, 44, started with one truck ("Smoke & Roll BBQ") and now runs three trucks across the Dallas–Fort Worth metro. He has five full-time employees. Annual combined revenue: ~$900,000. He uses Toast for payments ($150/month), Homebase for employee scheduling, and a mix of Google Calendar and email for truck-specific scheduling and event bookings. He manages 9 permit renewals per truck per year across three jurisdictions — a compliance overhead that cost him a $1,200 fine last year when a parking permit lapsed.

*His daily reality:* His biggest operational headache is knowing which truck has what permit status, where each truck is scheduled each day, and tracking the event inquiries that come in for the three trucks through different Instagram accounts. His revenue analytics are nonexistent below the POS level — he can't tell which truck, location, or event type generates the best margin.

*What would make him say "this is exactly what I needed":*
- One dashboard showing all three trucks' permit status, upcoming locations, and pending event inquiries
- Per-truck revenue analytics that aggregates across multiple locations and event types
- Fleet scheduling that lets him assign staff to specific truck+location combinations

*His WTP:* $79–$149/month for a fleet tier covering 2–5 trucks. He represents the higher-ARPU upsell path.

---

**Persona 3: Priya — The Event/Catering Specialist (high-LTV segment, ~10% of TAM)**

Priya, 31, runs "Spice Route Catering" — a fusion food truck that does 60% private events (corporate lunches, weddings, festivals) and 40% street service. Annual revenue: ~$250,000. She uses HoneyBook for contracts ($16/month), Square for payments, and a spreadsheet for her event pipeline. She manages 15–20 active event leads at any time and books 3–5 events per month at $3,000–$7,000 each.

*Her daily reality:* HoneyBook is a great general freelancer tool but knows nothing about food trucks — she can't configure per-head pricing, travel fees, minimum headcounts, or menu package options. She maintains a separate spreadsheet for her regular corporate clients with their past event details, preferences, and contact history. Her biggest frustration: HoneyBook + Square + spreadsheet = three systems that don't talk to each other.

*What would make her say "this is exactly what I needed":*
- A booking portal with food truck-specific fields: per-head pricing, travel radius, menu packages, minimum guest count
- A CRM that tracks her corporate clients' event history, preferred menus, and budget ranges automatically
- Integration between booking and permits so she can instantly verify she has the right event permit for each venue

*Her WTP:* $49–$79/month. She'll pay for the event booking + CRM combination alone; the permit tracker is a bonus.

### Secondary Users

**Event Organizers (influencers in the purchase decision)**

Corporate event planners, wedding coordinators, and festival organizers are not paying TruckStack users, but they interact with TruckStack's customer-facing booking portal. Their experience directly determines whether operators who use TruckStack win repeat event business. A professional, frictionless booking portal builds trust with organizers who manage 50+ vendor relationships per year and remember which vendors are easy to work with.

**Food Truck Association Staff**

NFTA and state-level association staff (Texas Food Truck Association, California Mobile Food Vendor Association, etc.) are potential partnership and referral channels, not direct users. They care about tools that help operators stay compliant and professional — TruckStack's permit tracking module aligns directly with their compliance education mission.

### User Journey

**Maria's Journey (Primary User — Solo Operator)**

*Discovery:* Maria sees a post in "Food Truck Nation" Facebook group from another Austin truck owner: "TruckStack just reminded me my health permit was expiring — saved me from a $1,500 fine. Worth every penny." She clicks the link and lands on TruckStack.com.

*Consideration:* She reads the permit tracking feature first ("30/60/90-day reminders for all your permits") and immediately thinks of her Google Sheet. She checks the pricing: $49/month or $149 lifetime. She realizes she's currently spending $0 on permit tracking (using a spreadsheet) but just spent 2 hours last month manually checking all her permit dates. She signs up for the 30-day free trial.

*Onboarding:* The onboarding flow asks her to enter her truck name and state, then walks her through adding her permits. The system recognizes "Austin, TX" and pre-populates the standard permit types (Austin Public Health Food Handler Permit, Austin Vendor's License, Texas Mobile Food Unit Permit) with typical renewal cycles. She adds her actual expiration dates in under 5 minutes. First "aha!" moment: the permit dashboard shows a red warning — her health permit expires in 23 days.

*Core Usage:* Over the next 30 days, she sets up her weekly location schedule, enabling the public "Birria Boss" schedule page. She shares the link on Instagram ("bookmark this instead of checking my stories!"). She uses the event booking portal for the first time when a corporate client asks for her booking link. The client books in 10 minutes, pays the deposit via Stripe, and receives an automatic contract.

*Retention:* Maria renews her health permit on time (for the first time without a close call). She receives her $49/month charge and upgrades to annual billing for 2 months free. She posts in the Facebook group about the permit reminder feature.

*Expansion:* 8 months later, Maria considers a second truck. She upgrades to the fleet tier at $79/month.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver on the permit tracking hook (acquisition) and the location schedule page (daily retention). Event booking and CRM can be lighter-weight in V1 but must be present to tell the "replace 4 tools" story.

**Module 1: Permit Expiration Tracker (MVP priority: critical)**
- Add permits with name, jurisdiction, expiration date, and document upload
- Pre-populated permit types for top 20 US cities (health, vending, parking, commissary)
- 30/60/90-day email reminders (configurable per permit)
- Permit status dashboard with color-coded urgency (green/yellow/red)
- Mobile-first design (operators use this from the truck)

**Module 2: Public Location Schedule Page (MVP priority: critical)**
- Branded URL (e.g., truckstack.app/birriaboss)
- Weekly schedule view with location names + Google Maps embed
- "Today" view prominently displayed
- Customer email subscription for location updates
- Social share links (Instagram, Facebook) for daily location posts

**Module 3: Event/Catering Booking Portal (MVP priority: high)**
- Operator-customizable booking inquiry form (event type, date, headcount, location, notes)
- Quote builder with per-head pricing, travel fee, minimum guest count
- Stripe integration for deposit collection (50% standard, configurable)
- Auto-generated booking confirmation email to client
- Booking calendar (operator view of all pending/confirmed events)
- Basic e-signature for booking agreement (PDF contract template)

**Module 4: Event Organizer CRM (MVP priority: medium — lightweight V1)**
- Contact list for past/current event clients
- Per-client event history (date, location, headcount, revenue, notes)
- Quick note field per contact
- "Book again" link that pre-fills the booking form with client details

**Onboarding:**
- Guided setup wizard: truck name → state → add permits (auto-suggests based on state) → set first week's locations → configure booking portal
- Target: operator has meaningful value within 15 minutes of signup

**Billing:**
- $49/month (single truck) — Stripe billing
- $79/month (fleet: 2–5 trucks) — unlocks per-truck permit dashboards, multi-truck location scheduling
- Annual billing option: 2 months free ($490/year single, $790/year fleet)
- 30-day free trial, no credit card required

**LTD tier:** Single-truck feature set only; excludes fleet management; priced at $149 on AppSumo.

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following are explicitly deferred to post-MVP phases to maintain build focus and a 6–8 week delivery timeline:

- **Mobile native app (iOS/Android):** The MVP will be a mobile-responsive web app. Native apps add significant scope; mobile web covers the truck-usage scenarios adequately for V1.
- **Square/Toast transaction import for revenue analytics:** Technically complex; valuable but not the primary hook. Phase 2.
- **Commissary agreement tracking:** Niche enough that it doesn't belong in the MVP feature set; add in Phase 2 after user feedback.
- **Health inspection log:** Related to compliance but separate from permit tracking; Phase 2.
- **GPS live location broadcast:** Real-time location sharing is a different product scope (location services, battery usage, background processes). Phase 3.
- **Multi-jurisdiction permit filing assistance (auto-fill applications):** Requires deep regulatory data and legal review. Phase 3 or never.
- **Insurance integrations:** Out of scope indefinitely for MVP; explore as a partnership in Year 2.
- **Customer loyalty program:** Adjacent feature; belongs after core ops platform is proven.
- **White-label for food truck franchises:** Target market for Year 2+ expansion; requires different sales motion.

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is validated and ready to scale when:

1. **AppSumo performance:** 300+ LTD sales within first 60 days of launch (signals market demand at scale)
2. **Retention signal:** 30-day retention >70% of trial users who completed onboarding
3. **Core feature adoption:** 80%+ of active users have added at least 3 permits AND set up their location schedule page
4. **Zero churn from permit feature:** No user cancels citing "the permit tracker doesn't work" — this is the anchor feature
5. **Organic referrals:** At least 20% of new signups attributable to peer referrals in Facebook groups or Reddit by Month 3
6. **Net Promoter Score:** NPS >40 in first user survey (Month 2 post-launch)

**Go/no-go for SaaS tier launch:** If AppSumo LTD sales reach 200 within 30 days, proceed immediately with $49/month SaaS launch. If below 200 at Day 30, conduct user interviews with LTD buyers before proceeding.

- Commissary agreement tracking with expiration reminders

**Year 2–3 Vision: The Operating System for Mobile Food Service**
- Expand beyond food trucks to food carts, pop-up restaurants, mobile bars, and festival vendors (92K+ total US mobile food businesses)
- International markets: UK, Canada, Australia, Germany — all have active food truck markets with similar fragmentation
- White-label platform for food truck franchise brands (Cousins Maine Lobster, Kona Ice, etc.) needing fleet management
- Marketplace integration: TruckStack becomes the direct booking layer, enabling operators to receive event leads without Roaming Hunger's commission
- Data network effect: aggregate (anonymized) location performance data enables TruckStack to recommend optimal locations to operators

**Long-term strategic moat:** Once TruckStack has 1,500+ operators with 2+ years of permit history, event records, and client CRM data, the switching cost becomes significant. No competitor can replicate that data layer without years of relationship-building in the food truck community.

## Competitive Positioning Summary

**Tagline:** "Your permit tracker. Your booking portal. Your location page. One platform for food truck ops."

**One-line positioning:** TruckStack is the ops layer for food trucks — not a POS, not a marketplace — the tool that sits on top of Square and Toast to handle everything else.

**Core message to operators:** "You're already paying $300/month for 4 tools that don't talk to each other. TruckStack replaces them all for $49/month — and finally gives you the permit tracker no one else has."

**AppSumo pitch:** "Replace 4 tools. Track all your permits. Get a professional booking portal. All for a one-time $149."

**Competitive moat:** First mover in integrated food truck ops. The integration itself is the moat — PermitWise can track permits, SchedulingKit can handle bookings, but neither integrates with the other, and neither has the full four-module stack. Building all four modules creates a switching cost that a single-feature competitor cannot match.
