# Product Brief: Auto Repair Shop Management

---

## Executive Summary

The US auto repair industry has a massive, validated software gap. There are 174,000+ auto repair establishments — 71% independently owned — and fewer than 3% use a modern cloud-based shop management platform. Every serious, full-featured competitor (Tekmetric, Shopmonkey, AutoLeap, Shop-Ware) starts at $179–$279/month. The only affordable options are ultra-basic tools like ARI ($33–$60/month) that cannot handle DVI, customer texting, or reliable QuickBooks sync.

**The opportunity:** A full-featured, cloud-native auto repair shop management platform priced at $69–$99/month — filling the confirmed dead zone between basic invoicing tools and enterprise-priced platforms. Targeting 1–5 bay independent shops, the product enters a market actively in crisis: Shopmonkey's forced v2.0 upgrade in 2025 generated 67% 1-star Trustpilot reviews and 1,500–3,000 shops actively seeking alternatives right now. Mitchell 1 remains Windows-only with no cloud sync in 2026. Tekmetric confirmed pricing at $199/month with texting locked behind $409/month.

**The product — tentatively called ShopFlow** — will include work orders, Digital Vehicle Inspection (DVI), two-way customer texting, native QuickBooks Online sync, and basic parts catalog lookup at a $79/month base price with no annual lock-in. An AppSumo lifetime deal ($79–$99 LTD) will serve as the primary launch vehicle, targeting the 80%+ of shops still on manual/paper systems who would never consider $179/month but will jump at a one-time deal.

This is a "boring business" play with extraordinary greenfield. Industry penetration is only 2.6% despite a $2.5B+ addressable market. First-mover advantage at the right price point — with the right 4 features — creates a durable competitive moat before well-funded competitors can respond.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Independent auto repair shop owners — mechanics who built their own businesses — are being systematically overcharged, under-served, and held hostage by their software vendors.

The market bifurcation is stark and validated:
- **Too cheap to be useful:** ARI ($33–$60/month) handles basic invoicing but has no DVI, unreliable texting, and no serious QB sync. It's a step above paper.
- **Too expensive for the average shop:** Tekmetric ($199/month), Shopmonkey ($179/month), AutoLeap ($179+/month), and Shop-Ware ($279/month) all start above what a 1–3 bay shop earning $300K–$500K/year can justify.

The result: 80%+ of small independent shops are stuck on manual systems, outdated on-premise software (Mitchell 1 — still Windows-only in 2026), or paying $179+/month for tools with frustrating UX, broken integrations, and unresponsive support.

Three specific pain points are creating a live switching moment as of 2026:
1. **Shopmonkey v2.0 catastrophe**: Forced upgrade in 2025 removed features, doubled clicks, broke QB sync. 67% of Trustpilot reviews are 1-star. Shops are leaving now.
2. **Mitchell 1 cloud migration failure**: Still no native cloud sync. Windows-only. Forcing legacy users to modernize.
3. **QuickBooks sync fragility across all platforms**: Every major tool either lacks native QB integration (Tekmetric requires a third-party connector) or has broken it (Shopmonkey post-v2.0).

### Problem Impact

For a 2-bay shop owner who is also the lead mechanic:
- **Missed revenue**: Shops without DVI lose 20–40% potential average repair order (ARO) because customers can't see/approve additional recommended work digitally.
- **Time tax**: Without integrated customer texting and approvals, every estimate requires a phone call — 5–10 minutes per ticket × 15 tickets/day = 1–2 hours of phone tag daily.
- **Accounting chaos**: Without reliable QB sync, shop owners or their accountants manually re-enter every invoice — a 2–5 hour/week bookkeeping burden.
- **Data hostage risk**: Multiple platforms prevent data export on cancellation. One Mitchell 1 cloud outage caused shops to be unable to work for a full day.
- **Price penalty**: Shops paying $179+/month when $79/month would be adequate are effectively subsidizing enterprise features they never use.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Competitor | Core Failure | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Tekmetric ($199/mo) | Too expensive for 1–3 bay shops; texting locked at $409/mo tier; "busy and unintuitive" UI; no native QB | Eliminates most solo/small shops on price alone |
| Shopmonkey ($179/mo) | v2.0 regression: features removed, more clicks, QB sync broken, 67% 1-star reviews | Live crisis = live opportunity |
| AutoLeap ($179+/mo) | Opaque pricing (sales-only), 60-day exit notice, "not possible" on feature requests | Lock-in risk discourages trial |
| Mitchell 1 ($299/mo) | Windows-only, no cloud sync, cloud migration "horrible and clunky" | Forcing legacy users to find alternatives |
| Shop-Ware ($279/mo) | Premium/enterprise-only, irrelevant for 1–3 bay shops | Wrong market |
| ARI ($33–$60/mo) | No DVI, no customer texting, basic invoicing only | Confirms the dead zone from below |
| Garage360 ($79/mo) | Closest competitor in the dead zone but early-stage, limited features, no brand awareness | First mover wins |

The fundamental failure is architectural: every full-featured platform was built for 5–15 bay multi-location shops and priced accordingly. No one has built for the 100,000+ independent shops with 1–4 bays as the primary target.

### Proposed Solution

ShopFlow: Full-featured auto repair shop management for independent shops at $79/month.

**Core value proposition:** Everything the 2-bay shop owner needs — work orders, DVI, customer texting, QB sync, and parts lookup — in a mobile-first interface that takes 1 day to learn, not 1 week. No annual contracts. Free data export. $79/month flat.

**Go-to-market wedge:** AppSumo lifetime deal at $79–$99 one-time. No auto repair software exists on AppSumo. This gives price-sensitive shop owners a no-risk entry point and creates viral word-of-mouth through the ASOG Facebook group (10,000+ members) and AutoShopOwner forum (6,182 members).

**Moat-building strategy:** Enter the Shopmonkey refugee window NOW (6–12 month window before v2 trust damage fades), establish brand reputation in forums, and use satisfied LTD buyers as social proof to convert monthly subscribers.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Price**: $79/month all-inclusive vs. $179–$409/month at competitors. 56% cheaper than Tekmetric's lowest tier for equivalent features.
2. **DVI at base tier**: Not gated behind a $309/month upgrade tier like Tekmetric. Every customer gets DVI from day one.
3. **Two-way texting at base tier**: Not a premium add-on. Included at $79/month.
4. **Native QuickBooks Online sync**: Built-in, not a third-party connector. The one feature competitors keep breaking.
5. **No annual contract**: Month-to-month. Cancel anytime. Free data export. Directly counters the #1 fear of switching.
6. **Mobile-first simplicity**: Designed for the owner-mechanic who has 5 minutes between jobs, not a dedicated office admin.
7. **AppSumo LTD**: No competitor offers this. Creates a zero-competition acquisition channel with deal-seeking shop owners.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: Marcus — The Owner-Mechanic (1–2 bay startup)**

- **Who:** 38-year-old ASE-certified mechanic who opened his own shop 3 years ago. Runs everything himself — fixes cars, writes estimates, handles billing. Married with two kids.
- **Shop size:** 1–2 bays, revenue $180K–$312K/year, net margin ~18%
- **Current setup:** Paper invoices + QuickBooks manually entered. Sometimes uses free invoice apps. No texting system.
- **Core problem:** Losing time on phone calls for estimate approvals. Customers don't trust verbal estimates — DVI would close more tickets. QB entry takes 3 hours every Sunday.
- **What he needs:** Get off paper, look professional, send DVI links by text, and have QB sync automatically. Won't pay more than $100/month.
- **Decision triggers:** Peer recommendation from another mechanic. AppSumo deal he sees in his Facebook feed. Finds the tool in Google ("cheap auto repair software with QuickBooks").
- **What success looks like:** "I closed 3 extra jobs this month because customers could see the DVI photos and approved the work themselves."

**Persona 2: Sandra — The Growing Shop Owner (2–5 bay, outgrowing ARI)**

- **Who:** 45-year-old who started as a service advisor, bought the shop from a retiring owner 7 years ago. Has 2 technicians. Revenue $500K–$800K/year.
- **Shop size:** 3 bays, 2 techs + her as service advisor, 1 part-time admin

- **Core problem:** ARI can't handle DVI. Her texting workflow is chaos — customers text her personal number and she loses track. QuickBooks sync keeps failing.
- **What she needs:** Upgrade to a full platform without paying $179/month. Specifically needs: DVI, organized customer texting, reliable QB sync, and tech job tracking.
- **Decision triggers:** A friend in her local car council group switched and showed her the DVI workflow. She's been price-blocked by Tekmetric for 18 months.
- **What success looks like:** "I finally have a system. Customer approvals come through text, my accountant gets clean QuickBooks data, and my techs know what job they're on."

**Persona 3: Derek — The Shopmonkey Refugee (active switcher 2025–2026)**

- Success: Can complete a DVI photo inspection from the bay without returning to the front desk.

**Shop Accountants / Bookkeepers**

**Parts Suppliers (Indirect)**

**Marcus's Journey (Primary: Owner-Mechanic)**

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must solve the 5 non-negotiable needs of 1–5 bay independent shops. Every feature below is validated by customer pain points from market research. Nothing is included that doesn't map directly to a confirmed pain point.

**1. Work Orders / Repair Orders**
- Create, edit, and manage repair orders (ROs) with unlimited volume
- Customer and vehicle records linked to ROs (VIN manual entry; VIN decode via API in v1.1)
- Labor line items with description, hours, and rate
- Parts line items with part number, description, quantity, and price
- RO status tracking: Estimate → Approved → In Progress → Complete → Invoiced
- PDF invoice generation and email delivery

**2. Digital Vehicle Inspection (DVI)**
- Photo-based DVI attached to any RO
- Technician captures photos from mobile (iOS and Android)
- Color-coded inspection items: Green (OK), Yellow (Monitor), Red (Urgent)
- Shareable customer link (no app required — opens in browser)
- Customer can view DVI and approve/decline recommended items via the link
- Approval status reflected in real-time on RO

**3. Two-Way Customer Texting**
- Send SMS directly from RO (estimate ready, car ready, approval request)
- Receive customer replies tied to the specific RO/customer record
- Conversation history per customer
- Customizable message templates (estimate ready, DVI link, pickup notification)
- SMS credits model: base plan includes 250 SMS/month; credits for additional usage
- Included at $79/month base tier — not gated behind upgrade

**4. Native QuickBooks Online Sync**
- OAuth-based direct connection to QuickBooks Online (not a third-party connector)
- Sync completed invoices automatically to QB
- Map ShopFlow customers to QB customers
- Map labor and parts to QB income accounts
- Sync settings configurable per shop
- Manual "sync now" trigger plus automatic nightly sync
- Sync error log visible to shop owner

**5. Parts Catalog Lookup**
- Integrated parts search from within an RO (start with NAPA and AutoZone APIs)
- Search by part name or part number
- Add parts directly to the RO from search results with price auto-populated
- Price markup rules (e.g., "always mark up 35%")
- No commitment to stock — lookup only for v1.0

**6. Customer & Vehicle Management**
- Customer records with full contact info
- Vehicle records with year/make/model, mileage, notes, and service history
- Search customers and vehicles
- Import from CSV (supports migration from ARI and Shopmonkey export)

**7. Reporting (Basic)**
- Revenue summary (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Open ROs and aging report
- Top services by revenue
- Invoice history and search

**8. User Roles (Basic)**
- Owner/Admin: full access
- Technician: RO view + DVI photo upload + status updates only
- (No multi-location in MVP)

### Out of Scope for MVP

These features are explicitly deferred to v1.1+ to maintain MVP focus. Each one could become a scope creep vector:

| Feature | Why Deferred | When |
|---|---|---|
| VIN decode API | Nice-to-have; manual entry works for MVP | v1.1 |
| Labor guide (MOTOR, Mitchell) | Expensive API + complex negotiation | v1.1 |
| Inventory / stock management | Complex, not MVP-critical | v1.2 |
| Technician time tracking / job clock | Useful but not blocking adoption | v1.1 |
| Multi-location / franchise | Not the MVP target segment | v2.0 |
| Appointment scheduling / calendar | Competitive differentiator but not blocking | v1.2 |
| Marketing / email campaigns | Out of scope entirely for v1 | v2.0 |
| Credit card processing (integrated) | Use third-party link (Square/Stripe) for MVP | v1.1 |
| Fleet management | Enterprise feature | v2.0 |
| Tire inventory | Specialized vertical | v2.0 |
| AutoLeap-style video DVI | Nice-to-have; photo DVI solves the core problem | v1.2 |
| OBD2 / scan tool integration | Hardware dependency, complex | v2.0 |
| Parts ordering (not just lookup) | Supplier relationship complexity | v1.2 |

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when all of the following conditions are met within 90 days of AppSumo launch:

1. **Adoption signal:** ≥500 LTD purchases on AppSumo + ≥100 monthly subscribers
2. **Usage signal:** ≥60% of active accounts create at least 1 DVI per week
3. **Integration signal:** ≥80% of QB-connected accounts maintain successful sync for 30+ consecutive days
4. **Retention signal:** ≥75% of accounts active at 30 days are still active at 90 days
5. **NPS signal:** NPS ≥ 40 at first survey (60 days post-onboarding)
6. **Qualitative signal:** Forum posts (AutoShopOwner, ASOG) are net positive; no major UX complaints
7. **Support signal:** P1 support tickets < 2% of active users/month; average resolution < 4 hours

**Go/No-Go Decision at 90 days:**
- If all 7 criteria met → Proceed with v1.1 (VIN decode, labor guide, time tracking)
- If criteria 1–4 met, 5–7 partial → Optimize onboarding and support before v1.1
- If fewer than 4 criteria met → Qualitative research to identify root cause before any v1.1 work

### Future Vision

**12–18 months (v1.1–v1.2): Complete the feature set for 2–5 bay shops**

- VIN decode API (automatic year/make/model from VIN input)
- Labor guide integration (MOTOR or All Data — standard flat-rate labor time lookup)
- Technician time tracking and job clock (real vs. book hours)
- Appointment scheduling and service reminder calendar
- Integrated payment processing (Stripe-powered — no processor lock-in)
- Parts ordering (not just lookup): direct order from NAPA/AutoZone from within the RO
- Inspection templates customization (per-shop DVI templates)

**18–36 months (v2.0): Expand the platform**

- Multi-location support (independent shops opening second locations)
- Franchise management tools (small regional franchise groups — 5–15 locations)
- Advanced analytics and profitability dashboard (profit per job, per tech, per service type)
- Fleet management module (commercial accounts with 10+ vehicles)
- AI-powered features:
  - Voice-to-work-order (dictate a repair description, auto-populate the RO)
  - Automated follow-up texting ("Your car is due for an oil change — book now")
  - Smart estimate recommendations based on historical repair data
- EV service module: battery diagnostic tracking, EV certification management, EV-specific inspection items
- Open API for third-party integrations (ProDemand, ALLDATA, Mitchell repair info)

**3–5 year vision: Vertical software leader for independent auto repair**

The long-term vision is to become the dominant platform for independent auto repair shops nationwide — not by chasing enterprise multi-location chains, but by owning the 100,000+ independent 1–5 bay shops that all competitors have ignored. At 5% market penetration (8,700 shops) at $89/month average → $9.3M ARR. At 10% penetration → $18.6M ARR.

The data moat is the long-term asset: a platform with 3+ years of repair order history per shop owns the customer relationship and becomes increasingly difficult to displace. Parts ordering volume creates a potential B2B revenue stream. The shop-owner community becomes a self-reinforcing distribution channel.

This is the home services software playbook (Jobber, Housecall Pro) applied to auto repair — where no well-executed version has been built yet.
