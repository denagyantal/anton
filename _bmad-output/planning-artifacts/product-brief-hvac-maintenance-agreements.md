## Executive Summary

HVAC maintenance agreements represent **55% of US HVAC services revenue** — roughly $9.9 billion annually — yet small contractors (1–10 technicians) have no affordable, purpose-built tool to manage them. The only dedicated software for this function charges $500–$1,500+/month, built for 50+ employee enterprises. Every other tool either bundles agreement tracking as a shallow feature inside a full field service management (FSM) suite at $150+/month, or doesn't address it at all.

The result: 60,000–72,000 HVAC shops with active agreement portfolios manage their most profitable service line in Excel spreadsheets, missing contracted visits, losing renewal revenue, and operating blind to their total agreement ARR.

**AgreementDesk** is a standalone, purpose-built HVAC maintenance agreement tracker priced at $49–$79/month — the only tool in this price tier that offers missed-visit alerts, tech visit completion tracking, and a real-time Agreement ARR dashboard. It works *alongside* whatever FSM or dispatch tool the contractor already uses; it doesn't replace anything.

The market opportunity is real, the pricing gap is verified, and the MVP is buildable in 2–3 weeks. The go-to-market path (community launch → AppSumo LTD → SEO → ACCA partnership) is executable by a small team and requires no enterprise sales motion.

**One-line pitch:** "Never lose a contracted visit again" — standalone maintenance agreement tracker for HVAC shops at $49–79/mo vs. $500/mo+ for ServiceTitan or spreadsheet chaos.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small HVAC contractors (1–10 technicians) sell maintenance agreements as their primary recurring revenue engine — yet they have no affordable system to ensure those contracted visits actually happen. **1.4 contracted visits per asset go uncompleted annually** when managed manually. For a shop with 200 active agreements, that's 280 missed visits per year: lost service revenue, destroyed pull-through ($2 in repairs per $1 of agreement value), and eroded customer trust.

The root cause is a software pricing gap. The only tools that address maintenance agreement management comprehensively cost $500–$1,500+/month (ServiceTitan, FieldEdge) — designed for 50+ employee operations, not 3-tech shops. Affordable FSM tools like Housecall Pro ($49–$189/month) include a basic "maintenance plan" billing feature but offer no visit-completion tracking, no missed-visit alerts, and no Agreement ARR dashboard. The gap between "enterprise overkill" and "spreadsheet chaos" has no affordable standalone solution.

Every HVAC shop with 50+ active agreements is either:
- Overpaying for enterprise software (minority)
- Using Housecall Pro/Jobber + a side spreadsheet for agreement tracking (plurality)
- Managing everything manually in Excel and missing visits they don't know about (majority)

### Problem Impact

**Revenue Loss (Direct):**
- Average residential HVAC agreement: $150–$250/year per system
- Average missed visits per asset/year (unmonitored): 1.4
- For a shop with 200 agreements: ~280 missed visits/year = $42,000–$70,000 in lost agreement revenue annually
- Plus pull-through multiplier: every $1 of missed agreement value destroys $2 in associated repair/parts revenue
- Total annual revenue impact for a 200-agreement shop: **$126,000–$210,000 in foregone revenue**

**Renewal Loss:**
- Manual renewal tracking yields 70–80% renewal rates; automated systems achieve 90%+
- 10–20 percentage points of ARR left on the table from missed renewal outreach
- For a $40,000 ARR shop: $4,000–$8,000/year in avoidable churn

**Business Valuation:**
- Shops that can't quantify their Agreement ARR are undervalued when seeking financing or a sale
- "What's your recurring revenue?" is unanswerable from a spreadsheet
- AgreementDesk creates the first clean, credible ARR number most small shops have ever had

**Operational Inefficiency:**
- Tracking visits manually across hundreds of agreements wastes 2–4 hours/week for the office manager or owner
- Seasonal agreement visit scheduling requires manual calendar tracking
- Customer complaints about missed visits damage renewal probability and generate negative reviews

### Proposed Solution

**AgreementDesk** — A standalone, cloud-based HVAC maintenance agreement management tool priced at $49–$79/month for contractors with 1–10 technicians.

Core value proposition: AgreementDesk makes it impossible to lose track of a contracted visit. It automatically generates visit schedules from agreement terms, alerts the shop owner when a visit isn't completed on time, lets field techs confirm completion via mobile (with photo proof), and shows total Agreement ARR in a real-time dashboard.

Critically, AgreementDesk does **not** replace Housecall Pro, Jobber, or any other FSM. It is the agreement management layer on top of whatever dispatch/invoicing tool the shop already uses — or nothing at all. This eliminates the primary adoption objection ("I already have software") and positions the product as additive, not disruptive.

**Core product workflow:**
1. Contractor uploads customer + equipment roster (CSV import from existing spreadsheet)
2. Configures agreement types (annual/semi-annual/quarterly) + pricing
3. AgreementDesk auto-generates a visit schedule for every active agreement
4. Techs check in/out via mobile when they complete visits (photo + notes)
5. Owner sees real-time dashboard: visits completed vs. scheduled, renewal pipeline, Agreement ARR
6. Automated SMS/email reminders fire 90/60/30 days before each agreement renewal
7. Missed-visit alert fires if a scheduled visit isn't completed within 7 days of due date

### Key Differentiators

**1. Only standalone, affordable agreement tracker with missed-visit alerts**
No competitor under $100/month includes automatic missed-visit detection and owner notification. This single feature — which prevents the core revenue loss — is AgreementDesk's primary differentiator and the reason customers will pay for it even when they already have Housecall Pro.

**2. Non-replacement positioning ("works alongside everything")**
AgreementDesk does not ask contractors to abandon their current tools. It's an add-on, not a replacement. This removes the highest-friction adoption barrier for small business software and makes the sales conversation "do you want to stop losing contracted visits?" rather than "do you want to replace your current software?"

**3. Agreement ARR Dashboard — first real recurring-revenue visibility for small HVAC shops**
Most small HVAC contractors have never seen their total agreement ARR as a single number. AgreementDesk creates this visibility for the first time. The psychological impact of seeing "$67,240 in annual recurring agreement revenue" for the first time is a powerful retention hook.

**4. HVAC-specific data model**
Equipment roster with unit type, age, serial number, manufacturer, and location. Visit types mapped to HVAC service norms (spring A/C check, fall furnace check, filter changes). Seasonal visit scheduling logic that matches HVAC's natural two-visit-per-year rhythm. Competitors use generic "recurring job" constructs that don't match how HVAC agreements actually work.

**5. Price — 6–30x cheaper than comparable-feature competitors**
$49–$79/month vs. $225–$1,500+/month for the only tools with similar features. For a 200-agreement shop, AgreementDesk costs less than preventing a single missed $150 agreement visit per year.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver the complete "never lose a contracted visit" value loop in 2–3 weeks of development. Every feature included must serve this loop directly.

**Feature 1: Customer + Equipment Roster**
- Customer records: name, address, phone, email, contact preferences
- Equipment records per customer: unit type (furnace/AC/heat pump/etc.), manufacturer, model, serial number, install date/age, location (attic/basement/etc.)
- Supports multiple units per customer (common in commercial accounts)
- CSV import from any spreadsheet (flexible column mapping)
- Manual entry as fallback

**Feature 2: Agreement Type Configuration**
- Define agreement templates: name, visit frequency (annual/semi-annual/quarterly), price, included services
- Map agreement types to equipment types (e.g., "Annual Gold" covers furnace + AC, 2 visits)
- Assign agreements to customers with start/end date and price paid
- Support for custom pricing per customer (override template price)

**Feature 3: Auto-Generated Visit Schedule**
- On agreement creation or import, auto-schedule all visits through the agreement period
- Visit dates calculated from agreement start date and frequency
- Seasonal scheduling logic: furnace visits default to fall (Sept–Nov); A/C visits default to spring (Mar–May); user can override
- Schedule displayed as calendar + list view; filterable by tech, date range, agreement type

**Feature 4: Technician Assignment + Mobile Visit Completion**
- Assign visits to specific technicians
- Mobile-first tech interface (iOS + Android PWA at minimum; native app in Phase 2)
- Tech daily view: "Your visits today"
- Visit completion flow: Check in (GPS timestamp) → Add notes → Upload 1–3 photos → Mark complete
- Offline capability: log visit offline, sync when connected
- 5-tap maximum from visit list to completion confirmation

**Feature 5: Missed-Visit Alert System**
- If a scheduled visit is not marked complete within 7 days of due date → email + SMS alert to shop owner
- Alert includes: customer name, address, equipment, agreement type, days overdue
- Daily digest option for shops with high agreement volume (vs. individual alerts per visit)
- "Snooze" capability (reschedule directly from alert email)
- Dashboard badge showing total overdue visits

**Feature 6: Renewal Date Reminders**
- Automated reminder sequence: 90 days, 60 days, 30 days before agreement expiration
- Reminders sent via email + SMS to the shop owner (not to the customer in MVP — customer-facing renewal outreach in Phase 2)
- Renewal pipeline view: all agreements expiring in the next 90/30-day windows
- One-click renewal (extend agreement 1 year from expiration date)

**Feature 7: Agreement ARR Dashboard**
- Real-time metrics: Total active agreements, Total Agreement ARR, Visits completed vs. scheduled (current month), Upcoming renewals (30/60/90 day windows)
- Agreement list filterable by status (active/expired/pending renewal), type, tech, customer
- Visit completion rate trending (month over month)
- Export to CSV (for accountants)
- Mobile-accessible (responsive design)

**Feature 8: Account Management + Notifications**
- Email + SMS notification configuration (per-owner preference)
- User roles: Owner (full access) + Tech (visit completion only) + Admin (same as Owner)
- Basic account settings: company name, timezone, logo
- Billing management (Stripe integration for monthly subscription)

## Assumptions and Open Questions

### Validated Assumptions
- No standalone affordable (under $100/month) HVAC agreement tracker with missed-visit alerts exists (confirmed by competitive research)
- Target market size: ~72,000 HVAC shops with active agreement portfolios in the US (IBISWorld + industry estimates)
- Recurring agreement revenue = 55% of HVAC services revenue, growing at 8.3% CAGR (ServiceTitan, Calljolt)
- Spreadsheets are the dominant "solution" for 40–50% of small shops (industry consensus)
- Strong AppSumo market exists for niche B2B trades tools at $79–$129 LTD pricing

### Assumptions Requiring Validation in MVP
- Trial-to-paid conversion at 30% for niche B2B tools via community channels (assumption; needs empirical test)
- CSV import from Housecall Pro/Jobber is the dominant onboarding path (vs. manual entry) — dictates onboarding investment priority
- "1.4 missed visits per asset per year" statistic (from original shortlist; not independently verified; treat as directional)
- Shop owners will adopt mobile visit completion tech within their technician team — requires change management support

### Open Questions
1. **Onboarding depth**: How much handholding do first-week users need? Is self-serve sufficient or do we need a scheduled onboarding call?
2. **SMS costs**: At scale (100K+ agreements, multi-reminder sequences), SMS cost structure needs validation
3. **AppSumo LTD structuring**: What agreement-count cap makes LTD sustainable without underselling? ($79 cap at 50 agreements feels right but requires confirmation)
4. **Vertical expansion sequencing**: Plumbing vs. electrical vs. pool service — which has the highest willingness to pay and fastest adoption?
