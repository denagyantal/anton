# Product Brief: Residential Care Home Compliance Tracker (CQC-Ready)

---

## Executive Summary

Small residential care homes (5–30 beds) in the UK face a regulatory compliance crisis. The Care Quality Commission's 2024–2025 digital mandate requires providers to move away from paper-based records — yet the only affordable digital tools cover only a fraction of what a CQC inspection demands, and enterprise solutions start at £500/month, pricing out the 10,000–12,000 small independent homes that represent the majority of the UK market.

**The Residential Care Home Compliance Tracker** is a purpose-built, all-in-one digital compliance platform for small UK care homes. For £79–149/month (or a one-time LTD of $199–299), it gives registered managers and home owners everything they need to be CQC-inspection-ready: medication administration records (MAR charts), incident logging, staff training record tracking, inspection checklists, and one-click PDF inspection report generation — all with fully transparent pricing and no implementation complexity.

This is not a general-purpose care management platform. It is not designed for 200-bed nursing homes. It is designed for the 20-bed home in Somerset whose registered manager is currently tracking staff training expiry dates in a spreadsheet and handwriting MAR charts on paper, knowing full well that this creates regulatory exposure every day.

The market opportunity is strong: ~10,000–12,000 small UK independent care homes are the primary ICP; ~18,400 small US assisted living facilities are the follow-on market; and there is currently no product that combines all five compliance modules at under £150/month with transparent pricing. The closest direct competitor — InspectReady — is not yet live (expected mid-2026), creating a window for early-mover advantage.

**Score: 91/105 | Verdict: BUILD | Tier 1 Strong Opportunity**

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small residential care homes (5–30 beds) are legally required to maintain the same standard of digital compliance records as large facilities but have no affordable tool that meets this requirement in one place.

The CQC's Single Assessment Framework (2024) and digital record-keeping requirements create a non-negotiable compliance burden:

- **MAR charts** must be completed daily, in digital form; paper is now only permitted in exceptional circumstances
- **Incident reports** must be logged with specific fields and notified to the CQC under Regulation 16/17
- **Staff training records** — DBS checks, mandatory training, competency assessments — must be current and auditable; gaps are a high-frequency enforcement trigger
- **Inspection evidence packs** must be compiled and presentable on demand; managers currently spend 10+ hours preparing for each inspection
- **Compliance status** must be demonstrably monitored; the CQC SAF now expects continuous readiness, not periodic snapshots

The care home sector's paperwork burden is catastrophic: a Joseph Rowntree Foundation study found care homes regularly complete more than 100 pieces of paperwork, with one registered manager spending 20% of working time on paperwork alone. This crowding-out effect directly harms care quality.

### Problem Impact

**For care home managers:**
- Real risk of CQC enforcement action, ratings of "Requires Improvement" or "Inadequate", and in extreme cases, closure proceedings — all driven by documentation failures, not care quality failures
- 10+ hours of pre-inspection preparation per inspection cycle, compiled manually from paper and spreadsheets
- Ongoing anxiety about training record gaps, missed MAR chart entries, and undocumented incidents
- 83% of registered managers say their role is more pressured than when they started (Skills for Care survey)

**For small home operators (owners):**
- Regulatory non-compliance creates reputational risk that affects occupancy rates
- Enterprise software at £500+/month is simply not viable for a 15-bed home operating on thin margins
- Paper-based systems are now actively creating compliance liability post-CQC 2024 mandate

**Market-level impact:**
- 73% of providers cite set-up costs as a barrier to technology adoption (DHSC 2025 Survey, n=1,085)
- 40% of micro-providers (under 10 residents) use no care technology at all
- 20% of all UK providers (~5,000 homes) still have no digital record system

**The critical gap:** No product currently on the market combines MAR charts + incident logs + staff training records + inspection checklists + PDF report generation at under £150/month with fully transparent pricing. This combination is exactly what a small care home needs — and no one is selling it at the right price.

### Proposed Solution

The Residential Care Home Compliance Tracker is a focused, five-module digital compliance platform:

1. **MAR Chart Module** — Digital medication administration records with daily logging, missed dose alerts, and auditable history. Replaces paper MARs, satisfies CQC digital requirements.

2. **Incident Report Logger** — Structured incident recording with the specific fields CQC requires under Regulation 16/17. Tracks incident status, notifications, and outcomes.

3. **Staff Training Records** — Tracks DBS checks, mandatory training certificates, and competency assessments with automated expiry reminders. The #1 CQC enforcement trigger, automated away.

4. **Inspection Checklist Generator** — CQC Single Assessment Framework-aligned inspection readiness checklist. Tracks completion status across all evidence categories with one-click PDF generation.

5. **Monthly Compliance Report** — Auto-generated PDF summary of all compliance activity, formatted for CQC inspection presentation. The "10 hours of pre-inspection prep" reduced to one click.

The product's core promise: **"CQC-ready in a day."** A registered manager should be able to go from sign-up to a fully operational compliance system within one working day, with no implementation consultants required.

### Key Differentiators

1. **The only all-in-one compliance tool under £150/month with transparent pricing** — Bettal is cheaper but covers policies only; Log My Care needs a 10x upgrade for MAR; everyone else hides their pricing.

2. **Purpose-built for 5–30 bed homes** — No rostering, no billing, no family portal, no enterprise features that small homes pay for but don't need. Laser-focused on exactly what a CQC inspection requires.

3. **CQC-ready in a day** — Implementation is not a multi-week project. A registered manager with moderate digital literacy should be operational by end of day one. This is a design requirement, not a feature.

4. **Regulatory SEO moat** — "CQC compliance software small care home", "digital MAR chart UK", "CQC single assessment framework checklist" — high-intent, low-competition keywords with clear regulatory urgency. Content marketing to these queries builds organic acquisition that enterprise tools don't compete for.

5. **Speed-to-market advantage** — InspectReady is the most direct competitor with nearly identical positioning. They are not yet live. Launching before or alongside InspectReady, with a broader feature set (MAR + training + incident + checklist + PDF vs. their inspection checklist focus), creates first-mover advantage in a market where compliance tools become deeply sticky once adopted.

### Secondary Users

**Deputy Managers / Senior Carers (Day-to-Day Operators)**
These users interact with the product primarily for daily record entry — MAR chart completion, incident logging, daily notes. They are non-technical and mobile-first. Their interaction must require minimal training: icon-driven entry, confirmation prompts, and automatic save. If they find the interface confusing or slow, they will revert to paper. The entire value of the product depends on daily data entry quality.

**Care Home Owners (Passive Oversight)**
The owner/operator is typically the purchaser but not the daily user. They care about one thing: inspection readiness. The monthly PDF report and compliance dashboard are their primary touchpoints. The product must make it trivially easy for an owner to understand their compliance status in 2 minutes or less.

**CQC Inspectors (Indirect Users)**
Not system users, but the ultimate audience for the data. Every output — PDF reports, audit trails, evidence packs — must be designed with "what does a CQC inspector need to see?" as the guiding question. The product must produce outputs that inspectors recognise and accept, not internally-formatted reports that need explanation.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver all five compliance modules at launch. This is non-negotiable: the product's entire value proposition rests on being the only affordable all-in-one solution. Launching with three modules and adding the rest later cedes ground to InspectReady.

**Module 1: Medication Administration Records (MAR Charts)**
- Digital daily medication logging per resident per medication
- Configurable medication schedules (regular, PRN, variable dose)
- Missed dose alerts (push notification + dashboard flag) when entry is overdue by >1 hour
- Auditable history: timestamped entries with staff name, no edit-without-reason functionality
- CQC-aligned: digital MAR chart format matches expected evidence type

**Module 2: Incident Report Logger**
- Structured incident form with CQC Regulation 16/17 required fields: incident type, date/time, people involved, immediate actions, notifications made
- Incident status tracking: Open → Under Investigation → Closed
- CQC notification log: records whether CQC was notified (required for certain incident types)
- Filterable incident history for inspection presentation

**Module 3: Staff Training Records**
- Staff profile: name, role, start date, DBS certificate number and expiry
- Training record: certificate type, completion date, expiry date
- Automated alerts: 90/60/30 days before expiry (email + dashboard)
- Bulk CSV import for existing staff training data (critical for onboarding)
- Training compliance dashboard: % of staff current on all mandatory training

**Module 4: Inspection Checklist Generator**
- CQC Single Assessment Framework aligned checklist covering all five key questions (Safe, Effective, Caring, Responsive, Well-led)
- Completion tracking: each item marked as Complete / In Progress / Not Started / Not Applicable
- Evidence linking: attach notes and documents to each checklist item
- Self-audit mode: generates gap report showing outstanding items before an inspection

**Module 5: Monthly Compliance PDF Report**
- Auto-generated monthly summary including: MAR completion rate, incident summary, staff training compliance %, checklist completion %
- Professional formatting suitable for presenting to CQC inspectors and home owners
- One-click generation: no configuration required, just click "Generate Report"
- Historical archive: all monthly reports stored and downloadable

**Platform Foundations (cross-cutting requirements)**
- Multi-user access with role-based permissions: Manager (full access) / Senior Carer (record entry) / Owner (read-only dashboard)
- Mobile-first responsive design: all record entry workflows must work on a smartphone in one hand
- UK data hosting (required for GDPR/ICO compliance and Digitising Social Care listing eligibility)
- ICO registration completed pre-launch
- HTTPS/encrypted data storage (basic GDPR requirements)
- 14-day free trial, no credit card required
- Transparent public pricing page
