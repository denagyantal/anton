# Product Brief: Dental Patient Communication Platform

---

## Executive Summary

Dental practices across the US are bleeding revenue from no-shows, lapsing recall patients, and thin online review profiles — while paying $249–$399+/month to platforms like Weave and Solutionreach that bundle hardware, VOIP phones, and enterprise features most small practices will never use. The result: 135,000+ US dental practice locations are overpaying for communication tools or going without entirely.

**DentalPing** (working name) is a focused, affordable patient communication platform for solo and small-group dental practices. It delivers appointment confirmations, recall/recare campaigns, two-way SMS, and automated review requests at $79/month — no VOIP hardware, no setup fees, no annual contracts, no surprises. It integrates alongside any existing practice management system (PMS) rather than replacing it.

The pricing gap is validated and wide: Weave at $249–$399+/month vs. a clear white space under $149/month for a quality, HIPAA-compliant solution. With no-shows costing the average dental practice $105,000/year, the ROI pitch is a 33:1 return on tool spend. The market is $184–$395M (US patient communication sub-segment alone) growing at 11.2% CAGR.

This is a "boring business" in the best sense: unglamorous, recurring revenue, defensible through operational excellence, built for a $940K-average-revenue customer who has a clear, quantifiable reason to pay every month.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Dental practices need automated patient communication — appointment reminders, recare campaigns, two-way texting, and review requests — but every credible solution on the market either:

1. **Costs too much**: Weave ($249–$399+/mo), Solutionreach ($329–$400/mo), Lighthouse 360 (~$329/mo), and Adit ($399/mo) are priced for multi-location DSOs or practices with $2M+ revenue, not the 60,000–80,000 small practices that can't justify the spend
2. **Bundles what practices don't need**: Weave was built as a VOIP phone system with software added on. Practices that already have phone systems pay for hardware they can't use
3. **Locks practices in**: Annual contracts, $750+ setup fees, and proprietary integrations make switching painful and expensive
4. **Has terrible support**: The #1 Capterra complaint for Weave, Solutionreach, and Lighthouse 360 is not pricing — it's support quality. Practices get locked in and then abandoned

The one budget alternative (Emitrr, $149/mo) occupies the space but lacks dental-specific positioning, deep community trust, and the transparent, practice-first brand that the market is begging for.

Meanwhile, the pain is quantified:
- No-shows cost the average dental practice **$105,000/year**
- Recall rates of 60–70% mean **30–40% of patients are lapsing** and leaking hygiene revenue
- Google reviews are now the **#1 driver of new patient acquisition** for dental practices, and most have no systematic way to collect them
- Insurance eligibility verification saves **15–30 minutes per patient** — a workflow no affordable tool automates

### Problem Impact

**For the practice owner (dentist):**
- $105K/year lost to no-shows directly impacts personal income (average net: $207,980/year)
- A 30% reduction in no-shows recovers ~$31,500/year — a 33x ROI on a $79/month tool
- Every lapsed recall patient represents $471+ in missed hygiene + treatment revenue

**For the office manager (primary tool evaluator):**
- 2–3 hours daily spent on manual patient outreach, reminder calls, and insurance verification
- Front-desk burnout from repetitive communication tasks is a leading driver of turnover
- "I need something that just works without me babysitting it"

**For the dental practice as a business:**
- Practices with poor online review volumes lose new patients to competitors with more reviews
- Systematic recall campaigns can increase practice revenue by 30–55%
- Dental practices spending $0 on communication tools are especially vulnerable to competitor practices that do

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Problem | Incumbent Response | What's Actually Needed |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| High cost | "Our ROI justifies the price" | Transparent $79/month, no contracts |
| VOIP bundling | "It's an integrated platform" | Software-only, works with existing phones |
| Setup friction | "$750 setup fee, 4–8 week implementation" | Live in 15 minutes, $0 setup |
| Support failure | Tier-1 support → weeks to resolution | Humans available by phone/chat |
| Annual contracts | "We offer discounts for annual commitment" | Month-to-month from day one |
| Limited PMS integrations | "We support 400+ integrations" (at $329+/mo) | Core integrations at $79/mo |
| No pricing transparency | Custom quotes only | Published pricing, no surprises |

The key insight: the market is not under-served because the problem is hard — it's under-served because incumbents are optimizing for ARPU and upsell rather than small practice fit.

### Proposed Solution

**DentalPing** is a HIPAA-compliant patient communication platform that:

1. **Connects to the practice's existing PMS** (not a replacement — a communication layer on top)
2. **Automates appointment confirmations and reminders** via SMS + email — 22–34% no-show reduction, proven by every incumbent's marketing
3. **Runs hygiene recall campaigns** — automated recare sequences targeting lapsed patients based on last appointment date, triggered from PMS data
4. **Enables two-way SMS texting** — patients reply directly; front desk manages from a unified inbox
5. **Requests Google reviews automatically** — triggered 2–4 hours post-appointment, when response rate is 34% (vs. 12% at 48+ hours)
6. **Shows ROI clearly** — dashboard shows no-show rate trend, confirmation rate, recall reactivation, and review volume. Every practice can see exactly what they're getting

No VOIP hardware. No annual contracts. No setup fees. No custom quotes. $79/month, transparent, HIPAA-compliant from day one.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Price transparency**: The only dental communication platform that publishes real pricing without requiring a sales call
2. **No VOIP, no bundling**: Software-only — practices keep their existing phones and workflows
3. **Month-to-month, always**: No annual contracts, no cancellation penalties, no lock-in
4. **Zero setup fee**: $0 vs. Weave's $750 and Patterson's $3,600 data conversion fee
5. **Fast onboarding**: "Live in 15 minutes" vs. competitors' 4–8 week implementation timelines
6. **ROI dashboard**: The only platform that shows practices exactly how much revenue was recovered from no-show reduction and recall reactivation — in dollars, not percentages
7. **HIPAA-first, not HIPAA-checkbox**: BAA included at all tiers, encrypted two-way SMS, audit logs — built in from the start, not retrofitted
8. **Dental-specific brand voice**: Not a generic healthcare tool — every workflow, every template, and every integration is built for the dentist + office manager pairing

**Background:**
Sarah is 34, office manager for a solo general dentistry practice in a mid-sized suburban market. - Uses Dentrix's built-in reminder feature (included) but it sends generic texts with no two-way reply capability — patients can't confirm by text

- Google reviews: 47 total, last one posted 6 weeks ago. The dentist asked about getting more reviews but "there's no time to think about that"

**Pain Points:**
- The no-show rate is "around 8-10% on good weeks, 15% on bad ones" — the dentist constantly asks Sarah about it
- She knows there are patients in Dentrix who haven't been in for 2+ years but "I don't have time to work that list properly"
- When she sees competitors at ADA conferences with "350 Google reviews" she knows the practice is falling behind on new patient acquisition
- She's evaluated 3 communication platforms in the past year — all required "custom quotes" and "demo calls with sales teams," which she finds exhausting

- Confirmations happen automatically; she only sees exceptions (confirmed no / no response)

- The dentist stops asking about no-show rates because the dashboard shows them in real time

**Discovery Journey:**
Sarah finds DentalPing through a Google search for "Weave alternative dental" after the dentist asks her to look at options again. She sees transparent pricing immediately ($79/month, no setup fee) and books a demo without providing her phone number. She's live within a week.

Typically 23–35, part-time or full-time, responsible for answering phones, managing the schedule, and patient-facing communication. DentalPing's two-way SMS inbox is the feature they interact with most daily. Key need: the inbox must be simple enough to use without training. #### Dental Hygienist — Recall Workflow Beneficiary

The hygienist cares deeply about recall because their schedule is directly affected by lapsed patients. They're often the first to notice when recall rates improve ("my afternoons aren't empty anymore"). They're not a tool evaluator but they're a powerful internal advocate if the recall campaign results are visible.

#### Dentist-Owner (for multi-dentist practices) — ROI Validator

In practices with 2+ dentists, one is typically the managing partner who reviews expenses quarterly. They don't use the tool daily but they review the ROI dashboard. The dashboard must show revenue impact, not just vanity metrics like "messages sent."

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (4–6 Week Build)

**Feature 1: Appointment Confirmation + Reminder Campaigns (MUST-HAVE)**

The core ROI driver. Two-step campaign: 72-hour reminder → 24-hour confirmation request. Patient confirms via SMS reply or link. Unconfirmed patients trigger a front desk alert (not automatic cancellation — dental offices need human judgment for that).

- SMS + email delivery
- Customizable message templates (pre-built dental language)
- Confirmation tracking dashboard: confirmed / unconfirmed / no response
- PMS-synced: pulls appointments automatically, no manual upload
- HIPAA-compliant: no PHI in SMS body, patient-identifiable links are tokenized

**Feature 2: Hygiene Recall / Recare Campaigns (MUST-HAVE)**

The revenue recovery engine. Automated sequences targeting patients overdue for hygiene appointments based on last visit date from PMS.

- Configurable triggers: 5 months / 6 months / 12 months / 18+ months since last hygiene visit
- Multi-step sequence: text → email → text (spread over 2 weeks)
- Personalized by patient name and last appointment date
- One-click "book now" link to online scheduling or practice phone
- Reactivation dashboard: patients contacted, booked, no response

**Feature 3: Two-Way SMS Inbox (MUST-HAVE)**

The daily-use feature that creates stickiness and replaces phone tag. Patients reply to automated messages — or text in directly — and front desk manages from a single inbox.

- Unified inbox: all patient SMS conversations in one view
- "Reply as practice" from web browser
- Conversation threading by patient
- Assignment: mark conversations as handled / in-progress
- HIPAA: no clinical/PHI content — communication-layer only (appointment times, confirmation, directions)

**Feature 4: Post-Appointment Review Requests (MUST-HAVE)**

Automated review request sent 2–4 hours after appointment ends (when response rate is highest). Routes patients to Google Business Profile, Healthgrades, or Yelp based on practice configuration.

- Triggered by appointment completion (PMS sync)
- Configurable delay: 2 hours / 4 hours / next morning
- Smart routing: if patient has previously left a review → redirect to secondary platform
- Review dashboard: requests sent, click-through rate, new reviews detected (via Google Places API)

**Feature 5: Basic Analytics Dashboard (MUST-HAVE)**

The ROI visibility layer that justifies continued spend.

- No-show rate trend (week over week)
- Confirmation rate trend
- Recall campaign performance: contacted / booked / lapsed
- Review volume trend
- Estimated revenue recovered (calculated: reduced no-shows × average appointment value input by practice)

**Feature 6: PMS Integrations — Initial Set (MUST-HAVE)**

Without these, the product doesn't exist. MVP must support:

- **Open Dental** (open-source, free API, large community — easiest first integration)
- **Dentrix** (market leader, requires Henry Schein developer agreement — most coverage)
- **Eaglesoft** (Patterson Dental — ~7.7% of market, common in mid-Atlantic/Southeast)

Post-MVP (Month 7–12): Dentrix Ascend, Carestream Dental, Curve Dental, WinOMS, Dolphin

**Feature 7: HIPAA Infrastructure (TABLE STAKES — must be in place before any customer data is touched)**

- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) signed with customer at signup — automated workflow
- All patient data encrypted at rest and in transit (AES-256 / TLS 1.3)
- Audit log of all patient communication events
- Twilio HIPAA-enabled account (BAA with Twilio in place)
- No PHI transmitted in SMS body text — tokenized links only
- HIPAA Privacy Policy + Terms of Service reviewed by healthcare attorney

### Out of Scope for MVP

**Not in MVP (explicit decisions):**

- **VOIP phone system integration** — this is Weave's core moat, not our lane. We are explicitly not a phone system.
- **Insurance eligibility verification** — saves 15–30 min/patient and is a "killer app," but the API complexity and clearinghouse relationships required make this a Month 7+ feature. Document it as V2 priority.
- **Online scheduling widget** — practices use Zocdoc or rely on phone calls. A scheduling widget competes with dedicated scheduling tools and expands scope significantly. Use "call to book" or partner with Zocdoc.
- **AI conversational agent** — Arini.ai plays here; it's a different product. Out of scope for MVP.
- **Treatment plan follow-up automation** — high revenue impact but requires understanding of clinical workflow complexity. V2.
- **Multi-location reporting dashboard** — for small group (2–5 locations). V2 after solo practice PMF validation.
- **Payments / text-to-pay** — adjacent feature but not in the communication workflow. V2.
- **OSHA/HIPAA compliance binder** — validated as a separate product angle ($97–197 LTD). Build as a separate product after DentalPing MVP, not bundled in.
- **AppSumo launch** — explicitly ruled out. Dentists don't browse AppSumo. Not a go-to-market channel.

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when:

1. **10 paying beta customers** are live with the product and have processed at least 30 days of appointment data
2. **No-show rate improvement** is measurable: at least 6 of 10 beta practices show >15% reduction in no-shows vs. their reported baseline
3. **Recall campaign** has generated at least 1 reactivated patient per beta practice (measurable from PMS data)
4. **NPS > 45** from first 10 customers
5. **Churn = 0** in first 90 days post-launch — every beta customer renews
6. **G2 listing** with at least 5 verified reviews averaging 4.5+
7. **3 PMS integrations live**: Open Dental, Dentrix, and Eaglesoft

Go/no-go for scale: if 8 of 10 success criteria above are met, proceed to public launch and LTD or MRR pricing.
