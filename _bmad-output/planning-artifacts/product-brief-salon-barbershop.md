## Executive Summary

**BoothBook** is a no-commission, no-contract booking and management platform built specifically for independent barbershops and booth renters — the 40%+ of the US barbershop market that every major software platform either excludes, overcharges, or builds the wrong product for.

The November 2025 Fresha pricing change (adding a 20% new-client commission after years of "free forever" promises) created the largest mass-defection event the barbershop software market has ever seen. Hundreds of thousands of independent barbers are actively looking for an alternative. No current tool fills the gap: Mangomint explicitly excludes solos, GlossGenius has no walk-in queue, Vagaro crashes during peak hours, and every major platform forces client account creation — causing documented lost bookings.

BoothBook solves this with five focused features: a real-time walk-in queue with TV waiting room display, per-barber independent booking pages within a shared shop, friction-free client booking (phone number only — no account creation), tip tracking with daily income dashboards, and SMS reminders. Priced at $99/shop LTD on AppSumo with zero ongoing commissions.

**Positioning:** *"No commissions. No contracts. Just booking."*

**Market:** $1.12B (2024) → $1.8B (2029) salon management software market. US: 154,925 barbershops, ~60,000–100,000 in the independent/booth-renter target segment.

**Revenue path:** AppSumo LTD ($59–$199) → payment processing fees (2.6% + $0.30, passive MRR) → optional AI/premium tier ($19–$29/mo) targeting $50K+ MRR at 18 months post-launch.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Independent barbers and booth renters — self-employed professionals renting chairs in barbershops — have no software designed for how they actually work. Today's platforms were all built for the employee-model salon (one owner, multiple staff), and none of them accommodate the booth-rental model where each barber is an independent business operating under a shared roof.

The result: 40%+ of the US barbershop market (an estimated 60,000–100,000 independent shops and booth renters) is underserved by every major tool:

- **Fresha** promised "free forever," then reversed course in November 2025, imposing 20% commissions on new clients and monthly fees — triggering mass defection among the 450,000+ professionals on its platform.
- **Mangomint** (best-rated UX in the market at $165+/mo) **explicitly excludes solos** — "only teams allowed." The tool with the best experience locks out the largest segment.
- **Mindbody** charges $99–$599/month with mandatory 12-month contracts, price quadrupled in 4 years, 97 BBB complaints (50+ billing), and an interface independent barbers describe as "clunky."
- **Vagaro** crashes during peak hours, impacting front-desk queues and checkouts; 1-star BBB rating.
- **Boulevard** mobile app crashes on iOS and Android — a critical failure for booth renters who manage their entire business from their phone.
- **Every major platform** (Fresha, Booksy, GlossGenius, Vagaro) requires clients to create a platform account before booking. Barbers report losing bookings at this friction point.
- **No mainstream tool** has a barbershop-specific walk-in queue — the most common daily workflow in every barbershop.

The booth-rental model has a unique requirement no current software addresses: each barber in a shop is an independent contractor who needs their own booking page, calendar, payment processing, and income dashboard — while the shop owner needs an aggregate view. This combination does not exist anywhere in the market.

### Problem Impact

The daily cost of these failures is concrete and measurable:

- A busy barbershop losing 3 walk-ins per day at $35 average loses **$38,000+/year** — directly attributable to the absence of transparent wait-time management.
- **62% of walk-in customers leave** if they cannot see their wait time.
- Fresha's 20% new-client commission for businesses with high tourist/new-client volume translates to hundreds of dollars per month extracted directly from barber margins — on top of monthly platform fees.
- Platform account creation friction: every barber who can't get a client booked through a clunky flow loses that appointment permanently.
- A barber on Fresha doing $5,000/month in client revenue loses $1,000/month if 20% of clients come through the marketplace — plus now pays ~$15–$220/month in platform fees on top.

The emotional impact is equally significant: independent barbers built their client relationships over years and feel betrayed when platforms monetize those relationships through commission structures or client data lock-in.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Problem | Root Cause | No Current Fix |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Walk-in queue | All platforms assume 100% scheduled; no one built for walk-in + appointment hybrid | No mainstream tool |
| Booth rental model | Built for employee-model salons, not independent contractor model | No full solution |
| Client account friction | All platforms use account creation as growth/lock-in mechanism | No-account booking doesn't exist at scale |
| Commission extraction | Marketplace model is core business model for Fresha/Booksy | Structural conflict of interest |
| Mobile reliability | Complex feature sets = bloated mobile apps | Platform-level issue |
| Contract lock-in | Enterprise sales motion | Structural to enterprise pricing |
| Barbershop-specific features | Treated as subgenre of salon, not distinct workflow | Squire (higher-end), ValetVault (AU only) |

The most defensible gap is the combination: no tool offers walk-in queue + per-barber independent management + zero commission + no client account required. Solving all four creates a product that is clearly differentiated from every existing option.

### Proposed Solution

BoothBook is a barbershop-first management platform designed around the booth-rental model and the walk-in/appointment hybrid workflow that defines how real barbershops operate.

**The core product concept — "Booth Barber Model":**

- A **shop owner** creates a shop and sees an aggregate dashboard: all chairs, all bookings, all daily revenue
- Each **booth barber** gets their own independent booking page (shareable link + QR code), calendar, payment processing, and income dashboard
- **Clients** book with a phone number only — no account, no app download, no Fresha profile required
- The **walk-in queue** is a live TV display (TV mode) showing queue position, estimated wait times, and QR code to join the line
- **Tips** are captured at checkout and tracked in each barber's daily income report

This architecture natively supports the booth-rental model: barbers are independent (separate payments, separate booking pages, separate income), but the shop presents a unified front to clients and the owner. No other platform does this.

**Distribution:** AppSumo LTD launch targeting barber Facebook groups (highly active communities proven to drive software adoption), YouTube "best barbershop software 2025" search intent, and the direct anti-Fresha migration audience.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Barbershop walk-in queue with live TV display** — QR code to join queue, real-time wait times, waiting room TV mode. No mainstream competitor has this.
2. **Booth rental model natively supported** — Per-barber independent pages, calendars, and income dashboards within a shared shop. Mangomint excludes this segment; everyone else ignores it.
3. **No-account client booking** — Phone number only. No Fresha profile, no app download. Every major platform forces account creation; this directly attacks documented lost-booking friction.
4. **Zero commissions, zero contracts** — No marketplace commission (0% vs. Fresha's 20%, Booksy's 30%). No annual contract (vs. Mindbody/Boulevard's 12-month lock-in). Pure flat pricing.
5. **Anti-Fresha timing** — Launching directly into a confirmed mass-defection event. The Fresha trust vacuum is the single best distribution moment this market has seen.
6. **Mobile-first reliability** — Built for a phone-first workflow from day one, targeting the specific failure mode of Boulevard's buggy iOS/Android apps.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP is defined by the five features that directly address the documented unmet needs, produceable in a 4–6 week build cycle:

#### Feature 1: Walk-In Queue with TV Display

**What it does:**
- Shop owner activates walk-in queue for their shop
- Clients join queue by scanning a QR code (posted on shop door) or entering phone number at a kiosk URL
- Real-time queue display: TV mode is a full-screen web page (no app on the TV) showing queue positions, barber names, and estimated wait times
- Barber marks a client "done" → queue auto-updates → next client gets SMS notification "You're up next at [Shop Name]"

**Why it's MVP-essential:** This is the single most-cited unmet need. No mainstream tool has it. It's the primary reason shop owners will switch. It creates immediate, visible value on day one.

**Technical scope:** Real-time queue state (WebSocket or polling), QR generation, TV-mode URL (simple full-screen web page), SMS trigger on queue update.

---

#### Feature 2: Per-Barber Independent Pages Within a Shared Shop

**What it does:**
- Shop owner creates a shop and invites barbers (employees or booth renters)
- Each barber gets their own public booking URL: `boothbook.com/[shop-slug]/[barber-slug]`
- Barbers configure their own: services + prices, availability/hours, booking lead time
- Barbers have their own calendar view (can't see other barbers' client details)
- Shop owner has an aggregate view: all chairs, all bookings, all revenue

**Why it's MVP-essential:** This is the "Booth Barber" model that no competitor supports. It's the core architectural differentiator.

**Technical scope:** Multi-tenant within a shop. Role-based access: owner vs. barber. Each barber is effectively a mini-account within the shop.

---

#### Feature 3: Friction-Free Client Booking (No Account Required)

**What it does:**
- Client visits booking URL (e.g., from Instagram bio)
- Selects service + date/time → enters name + phone number → done
- No email, no password, no platform account, no app download
- Client receives SMS confirmation + reminder (24 hours before)
- On repeat visit, phone number auto-populates their history (optional: "remember me")

**Why it's MVP-essential:** Every barber who has used Fresha or Booksy has complained about losing clients at the account creation step. This directly addresses a documented lost-revenue problem.

**Technical scope:** No auth flow for clients. Phone-number-keyed client records. SMS confirmation via Twilio or equivalent.

---

#### Feature 4: Tip Tracking + Daily Income Dashboard

**What it does:**
- At checkout (or end of day entry), barber logs: service amount + tip
- Daily dashboard shows: total cuts, total revenue, tip breakdown, cumulative week/month
- For booth-renter shops: each barber's dashboard is private to them; owner sees aggregate totals only (no per-barber breakdown for renters — respects their independence)

**Why it's MVP-essential:** Independent barbers currently track income via cash + mental math. This is a daily pain point that creates immediate, personal value — and becomes a habit-forming daily touchpoint.

**Technical scope:** Simple payment log per barber. Aggregate calculations. No payment processing in MVP (log-only; processing can be Phase 2).

---

#### Feature 5: SMS Appointment Reminders

**What it does:**
- Automated SMS sent 24 hours before appointment: "[Client Name], reminder: you have an appointment with [Barber] at [Time] at [Shop Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."
- Barber can optionally enable a 2-hour reminder as well
- Client can confirm or cancel via SMS reply

**Why it's MVP-essential:** No-show reduction is one of the clearest ROI cases for booking software. A barber doing 8 cuts/day at $35 who eliminates 1 no-show/day recovers $35 × 250 days = $8,750/year. This makes the LTD price a no-brainer.

**Technical scope:** SMS via Twilio. Scheduled job per appointment. STOP/opt-out handling per TCPA compliance.

---

#### Feature 6: Client History + Service Notes

**What it does:**
- Per-client card: name, phone, visit history (date, service, barber, price, tip), notes field
- Barber can add free-text notes: "prefers #2 on sides, no taper," "allergic to [product]"
- On next booking, barber sees client card automatically

**Why it's MVP-essential:** Client relationship memory is a key reason barbers build loyal clienteles. This creates stickiness — clients get better service, barbers can't easily migrate to a tool without this history.

**Technical scope:** Simple client record per barber. Text notes. Visit log. No AI/analysis in MVP.

| Deferred Feature | Rationale |

| Custom branded client-facing pages | Vanity feature; defer. |

| Marketplace / client discovery | Explicitly NOT the product — this is an anti-positioning decision, not just a scope decision. |

| Android / iOS native app | Progressive web app in MVP. Native app in V2 if warranted by usage data. |
