# Product Brief: AI Receptionist for Home Service Businesses

## Executive Summary

Home service contractors — HVAC techs, plumbers, electricians, roofers, pest control operators — miss 60–80% of incoming calls while on the job. Each missed call costs an average of $1,200 in lost revenue, adding up to $45,000–$120,000 in lost annual revenue per business. The current solutions are either too expensive (a human receptionist at $3,750–$5,000/month), too generic (AI receptionists that don't understand trade terminology), or too enterprise-focused (Avoca, Netic) to serve the 200,000+ solo and small-shop operators who need help most.

**TradesAI** is a trade-specific AI phone agent — purpose-built for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, and pest control — that answers every call 24/7, understands trade terminology from day one, triages emergencies in real time, books jobs directly in the contractor's CRM (Jobber, HousecallPro), and dispatches on-call technicians for emergencies — all for a flat $99–$249/month with no overages and no surprise bills.

The market is validated ($4.6B virtual receptionist market, growing at 9.8% CAGR; voice AI growing at 34.8% CAGR). Well-funded players (Avoca $47.8M, Netic $43M) serve enterprises. The solo/small operator segment — the majority of the 500,000+ US home service businesses — is systematically underserved. TradesAI wins by being purpose-built for the tradespeople enterprise players ignore, priced for how small businesses actually operate, and distributed through the trade communities where these operators already trust each other.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Solo and small home service operators (1–15 trucks) are losing tens of thousands of dollars every year to a completely preventable problem: unanswered phone calls. While crews are on job sites, calls ring to voicemail. When 78–85% of callers who reach voicemail don't leave a message — they immediately call the next competitor on Google — every missed call is a lost customer.

The operators who feel this most acutely are the owner-operators running 1–8 truck shops. They're the ones answering calls from ladders, from under sinks, from attic crawl spaces. They lose emergency calls during heat waves and cold snaps — when a homeowner's AC fails at 9 PM and calls three companies in a row, the one who answers gets the $800 job. The others never hear from that homeowner again.

### Problem Impact

**For the individual contractor:**
- Average 62% call miss rate while on-site
- $45,000–$120,000 in lost annual revenue (assuming $1,200 average job value)
- After-hours and emergency calls — the highest-value work — go entirely unanswered
- Reputation damage: customers report an "unprofessional" experience when they reach voicemail
- The stress of always being "on call" for their own phone while trying to do technical work

**For the market:**
- 500,000+ US home service businesses face this problem daily
- The industry loses billions in aggregate to missed-call churn
- Labor shortages make hiring a human receptionist increasingly difficult and expensive
- 38% of contractors now report measurable AI business impact (ServiceTitan 2026) — adoption is accelerating

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Human receptionists** ($3,750–$5,000/month) cost 20–30x more than AI, only work 8 hours/day, 5 days/week, and still can't answer calls during the summer peak when the team is all on-site. For a solo operator grossing $500K/year, a receptionist is an unaffordable luxury.

**Generic AI receptionists** (Dialzara at $29/mo, Rosie at $49–$199/mo, AI Front Desk at $65–$150/mo) don't understand trade terminology. They can't distinguish between a service call and a maintenance plan. They don't know what "R-22 refrigerant," "no heat in winter," or "burst pipe" means in terms of urgency and pricing. The result: calls are handled poorly, customers get frustrated, and contractors churn after a few bad interactions.

**Enterprise trade-specific AI** (Avoca $47.8M, Netic $43M) is built for 15–50+ truck operations with ServiceTitan as the CRM and dedicated onboarding teams. Their sales process, pricing (per-minute, enterprise), and minimum requirements exclude the solo/small operator segment entirely.

**Human answering services** ($300–$500/month) handle messages but can't book jobs, quote prices, triage emergencies, or dispatch technicians. They're better than voicemail but create extra work for the operator who still has to call customers back.

**The gap:** No solution at $99–$249/month offers deep trade-specific knowledge, emergency triage, CRM job creation, and flat-rate pricing simultaneously. This is the unoccupied white space.

### Proposed Solution

TradesAI is a voice AI phone agent pre-trained specifically for home service trades. When a customer calls, TradesAI answers within two rings, identifies the trade, qualifies the issue using industry-standard terminology, books the job directly in the contractor's CRM (Jobber or HousecallPro), and — for emergencies — immediately dispatches the on-call technician via SMS.

**How it works:**
1. Contractor sets up TradesAI in under 10 minutes using a "Setup Wizard" (business name, service area, pricing ranges, on-call tech number, CRM connection)
2. Calls forward to TradesAI automatically (or as fallback after N rings)
3. TradesAI greets the customer, identifies the issue, qualifies urgency, and either books a job or escalates to the on-call tech
4. The job appears in the contractor's CRM within seconds
5. The contractor reviews a call log and transcript in the TradesAI dashboard

**Key capabilities:**
- 24/7/365 call answering with sub-2-second pickup
- Trade-specific knowledge base: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest control
- Emergency detection and human escalation (burst pipe, gas smell, no heat, flooding)
- CRM integration: Jobber (primary), HousecallPro (secondary)
- Flat monthly pricing — no per-minute overages, no surprise bills
- Bilingual English/Spanish for Sun Belt markets
- Full call logs, transcripts, and recordings in dashboard

### Key Differentiators

**1. Trade-native from day one** — not a generic AI retrained for trades, but an AI built ground-up with 200+ trade-specific intents, service types, emergency keywords, and pricing guidance. A contractor doesn't have to teach it what a "heat pump" or "main water shutoff" is.

**2. Emergency triage is non-negotiable** — The #1 reason a contractor needs a receptionist is to handle emergencies when they can't pick up. TradesAI's emergency detection → on-call SMS dispatch is rule-based, not AI discretion. It cannot miss a gas leak or burst pipe call.

**3. Flat pricing, no anxiety** — Per-minute billing creates bill anxiety for small operators, especially during summer peak when call volume spikes 3–5x. TradesAI's flat $99–$249/month removes that anxiety entirely. The operator always knows what they're paying.

**4. Built for Jobber operators, not just ServiceTitan** — Avoca and Sameday are ServiceTitan-native. Jobber has 200,000+ users — primarily the solo/small operator segment — and no dominant AI receptionist partner. TradesAI targets Jobber first, creating a defensible distribution channel that enterprise competitors don't prioritize.

**5. Community-led, not sales-led** — Home service operators trust peers in trade Facebook groups more than any advertising. TradesAI's go-to-market is built around being the AI receptionist that HVAC operators in the "HVAC Business Owners" Facebook group (40K+ members) recommend to each other. This creates viral acquisition at near-zero CAC.

## Target Users

#### Persona 1: "The Solo Operator" — Marcus, HVAC Technician & Owner

**Background:**
Marcus runs a 2-truck HVAC business in Phoenix, AZ. He started as a technician 12 years ago and bought his van at 31. **Day in the life:**
Marcus is on a job from 7 AM to 5 PM. His phone is in his pocket on silent. He checks it every hour or two during break and finds 8–12 missed calls with no voicemails. He calls back 3–4 that he recognizes and leaves the rest. At least two of those were homeowners whose AC failed in the heat — they already called his competitor and are booked.

**Pain points:**
- Loses 4–6 jobs per week during summer peak to missed calls
- His wife is burned out from being the "part-time receptionist" on top of everything else
- Tried a generic AI receptionist once — it confused a "thermostat replacement" with a "new installation" and quoted the wrong price; customer was furious
- Doesn't have time or patience to configure complex software
- Gets surprised by overage fees in his busy season

**What success looks like:**
"I want to finish a job and know that every call that came in while I was in that attic is already booked. I don't want to manage it. I just want it to work."

**Willingness to pay:** $99–$149/month flat. Will churn immediately if he sees an unexpected bill.

**CRM:** Jobber (set up 3 years ago, uses it daily for scheduling and invoicing)

### Secondary Users

**The On-Call Technician (Mike, Diana's employee)**
Mike is Diana's on-call plumber. When TradesAI detects an emergency, it sends Mike an SMS with the customer's name, address, issue description, and urgency level. Mike doesn't interact with the software directly, but his experience (getting clear, actionable alerts vs. vague "someone called" messages) determines whether the emergency dispatch feature works in practice.

**The Office Administrator (Sandra, Diana's assistant)**
Sandra reviews the call log dashboard each morning to catch anything TradesAI flagged for follow-up. She monitors transcript quality, flags any miscommunications, and updates the CRM if needed. For larger shops, the admin is the day-to-day TradesAI operator — the owner just sees the dashboard summary.

**Business Coaches and Trade Consultants**
EGIA, BDR, and similar trade business coaches who work with contractors on operational efficiency. These influencers recommend software tools to their client base of hundreds of contractors. A single endorsement from an EGIA consultant can generate 20–50 new accounts.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver the core value proposition completely and reliably: answer every call, understand what it's about, book the job or escalate the emergency, and sync with the contractor's CRM — without any configuration expertise required.

**Feature 1: Trade-Specific Call Handling**
- Pre-trained knowledge base for HVAC (primary), plumbing, and electrical
- 200+ service type intents (service call, maintenance visit, installation, emergency, estimate request)
- Service area confirmation ("Are you in [city/county]?")
- Pricing range responses ("A diagnostic visit is typically $89–$149")
- Natural conversation flow; callers do not realize they're speaking to AI

**Feature 2: Emergency Detection & On-Call Dispatch**
- Keyword detection: gas smell, no heat, no AC, burst pipe, flooding, no power, smoke, carbon monoxide
- Rule-based (not AI discretion) immediate escalation to on-call tech via SMS
- SMS to on-call tech includes: customer name, phone, address, issue summary, detected urgency level
- Fallback: if on-call tech doesn't respond within 5 minutes, re-SMS + call operator's cell

**Feature 3: CRM Job Creation (Jobber-first)**
- OAuth connection to Jobber in Setup Wizard
- Creates new client + job record with: customer name, phone, address, service type, requested date/time, notes from call
- Syncs within 60 seconds of call completion
- Duplicate detection (existing client by phone number)

**Feature 4: Setup Wizard (≤15 minute onboarding)**
- Business name, phone number, service area (zip codes or radius)
- Trade selection (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest control)
- Service types offered + typical price ranges
- Emergency keywords and on-call tech phone number
- CRM connection (Jobber OAuth)
- Call forwarding instructions (auto-generated for major carriers)

**Feature 5: Operator Dashboard**
- Call log with date, time, caller ID, duration, outcome (booked/emergency/follow-up/declined)
- Full call transcript for every call
- Call recording playback
- Job summary: "X calls handled today, Y jobs booked, Z flagged for follow-up"
- Recovered revenue estimate (jobs booked × average job value the operator sets)

**Feature 6: Billing & Subscription**
- 3 flat-rate plans: Solo ($99/mo), Pro ($149/mo), Growth ($249/mo)
- Annual billing at 20% discount (improves LTV, provides cash flow)
- 14-day free trial, no credit card required to start
- Stripe billing integration

**Technical Foundation:**
- Voice AI platform: Retell AI (recommended) or Vapi — both provide telephony + STT + LLM + TTS
- LLM: GPT-4o-mini for routine calls (cost), GPT-4o for complex/emergency triage (accuracy)
- TTS: ElevenLabs for voice quality, with phonetic customization for trade terminology
- Telephony: Twilio (via Retell/Vapi)
- CRM: Jobber API (OAuth)
- Backend: Node.js/TypeScript with PostgreSQL
- Dashboard: React

**Key "no" decisions for MVP:**
- No per-minute billing option (adds complexity, creates the anxiety we're differentiating against)
- No human escalation service (we're not a hybrid — we're AI-first with emergency rule-based dispatch)
- No AppSumo LTD (API cost economics make LTD unviable; monthly subscription only)
- No enterprise sales motion (self-serve only for MVP)
