# Product Brief: SoloCounsel — Legal Practice Management for Solo Attorneys

---

## Executive Summary

Solo attorneys represent 49% of all US private practice lawyers — approximately 450,000 active practitioners — yet every major legal practice management platform is priced and designed for firms with 5–20+ attorneys. The result: solos either overpay for bloated tools (Clio Complete at $139/user/month) or stitch together 3–5 fragmented applications averaging $150–214/month combined.

**SoloCounsel** is a purpose-built legal practice management platform for solo and micro-firm attorneys (1–3 users). It delivers matter management, one-click time tracking, client portal, invoicing, and — critically — full built-in IOLTA trust accounting, all at a flat $35/month regardless of how many users are added up to three. No per-user penalty. No premium-tier lockouts. No separate QuickBooks subscription needed.

The platform addresses four confirmed market gaps that no existing competitor has filled: (1) flat-rate pricing for small teams, (2) flat-fee matter budget tracking for fixed-fee practices, (3) built-in IOLTA trust accounting at an accessible price point, and (4) 2–3 day payment settlement (vs. the industry standard of 7–10 days).

The $3.14B legal practice management market is growing at 11.07% CAGR through 2032. SoloCounsel's US addressable market is ~$189M ARR at $35/month penetration across 450K solo attorneys. Target: 286 customers for $10K MRR; 1,000 customers for $35K MRR within 12 months of launch.

Go-to-market strategy centers on a $79/$149 Lifetime Deal launch on AppSumo, bar association listserv outreach, and high-intent SEO content targeting "Clio alternative for solo attorneys" searches — a market where Clio is explicitly moving upmarket, leaving the solo segment underserved by design.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Solo attorneys are systematically overcharged and underserved by legal practice management software. The dominant player, Clio, charges $139/user/month for its Complete tier — the only tier that includes the IOLTA trust accounting compliance every attorney is legally required to maintain. Meanwhile, per-user pricing means adding a paralegal doubles the monthly bill. The average solo attorney pays $150–214/month across fragmented tools (Clio + QuickBooks + DocuSign), losing 30–40% of their billable time to administrative overhead and experiencing 7–10 day payment processing delays that create serious cash flow problems in a practice with no firm backing.

The core problem is not a lack of legal software options — it is that all existing options were designed for law firms, not solo practitioners.

### Problem Impact

The financial and operational consequences of this gap are severe:

- **Revenue leakage**: 30–40% of billable time goes uncaptured due to inadequate time tracking integration. At $150–250/hour, this represents $40K–130K in annual lost revenue per attorney.
- **Compliance risk**: IOLTA three-way monthly reconciliation is mandatory. Errors lead to bar disciplinary action, financial penalties, and potential suspension. No affordable tool provides this correctly — solos either pay $139/month for Clio Complete or maintain error-prone manual processes.
- **Cash flow pressure**: The 7–10 day payment settlement cycle on leading platforms creates immediate cash flow stress for solo practices with no firm-level working capital buffer.
- **Tool fragmentation tax**: Maintaining 3–5 separate subscriptions means manual data re-entry between systems, error propagation, and time wasted on administrative coordination that should be automated.
- **Per-user pricing penalty**: A solo who adds a paralegal or associate pays a 100% price increase — a perverse incentive structure that punishes growth at the firm's most vulnerable stage.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Clio** (market leader, $1.6B+ raised): Explicitly moving upmarket with AI research tools, CRM, and enterprise marketing features. Trust accounting locked at $139/user/month. Clio Payments settles in 5–7 days. Reviews cite feature removals that "ruined entire firm accounting overnight," data loss incidents, and "price increases every single year." Mobile app is an afterthought.

**MyCase**: 100% of speed reviewers flag slow load times. Invoicing breaks for contingency-fee (PI) attorneys. Requires separate LawPay + Woodpecker subscriptions despite marketing itself as "all-in-one." Non-intuitive multi-menu editing.

**PracticePanther**: Competitive 2–3 day payment settlement, but "desktop far more functional than mobile" per Capterra. Per-user pricing persists.

**Smokeball**: $50K+ over 3 years for a 10-user firm. Annual price increases. Desktop-only strength. No trust accounting without separate QuickBooks.

**CosmoLex**: Built-in legal accounting is the right idea, but at $89–129/user/month — still pricing for larger firms.

**Budget tier (Lawcus, CaseFox, Time59)**: Billing-only tools or partial feature sets that still require QuickBooks and don't include flat-fee matter budget tracking.

**Critical gap confirmed**: No competitor at any price tier offers flat-rate pricing (non-per-user) that includes IOLTA trust accounting + client portal + workflow automation + flat-fee matter budget tracking. This is the unoccupied market position.

### Proposed Solution

SoloCounsel is a flat-rate ($35/month for up to 3 users), feature-complete legal practice management platform built ground-up for solo and micro-firm attorneys:

**Core platform:**
- **Matter management**: Kanban + list view, client and contact records, conflict check, document storage per matter
- **One-click time tracking**: Timer integrated directly into case records; automatic capture prompts for common activities; flat-fee matter budget tracking (first-to-market — no competitor offers this)
- **Invoicing and billing**: Matter-linked invoices, automatic time/expense pull, contingency-fee support (PI attorney-safe), online payment via integrated payment processing
- **Built-in IOLTA trust accounting**: Deposit, disbursement, three-way monthly reconciliation (bank statement + trust ledger + sum of client ledgers), California CTAPP compliance baseline — no QuickBooks required
- **Client portal**: Secure document sharing, messaging, invoice viewing and payment, e-signature for retainer agreements
- **Court deadline calendar**: Jurisdiction-specific rule sets for statute of limitations and filing deadlines

**Key operational advantages:**
- Flat $35/month for up to 3 users — add a paralegal for free
- 2–3 day payment settlement (vs. 7–10 days for most competitors)
- Setup in 30 minutes targeting paper/Excel migrators
- Mobile-first design for court and field use

### Key Differentiators

1. **Flat-rate pricing, not per-user**: $35/month for up to 3 users removes the per-user penalty that makes every growth decision feel expensive. No tier unlocking required — all features included.

2. **Built-in IOLTA trust accounting at launch**: Full three-way reconciliation built into the core product, not locked behind a premium tier or requiring a QuickBooks companion subscription. This is the moat — correctly implemented trust accounting creates extreme switching friction.

3. **Flat-fee matter budget tracking (first-to-market)**: No existing competitor tracks time entries and expenses against a fixed-fee matter budget, giving attorneys a live budget vs. actual view. Critical for PI, criminal defense, immigration, and estate planning attorneys.

4. **2–3 day payment settlement**: Meaningful competitive advantage over Clio's 5–7 days and most platforms' 7–10 days. For solo cash flow, this is a direct pain point with measurable dollar value.

5. **"Setup in 30 minutes" onboarding**: Explicitly designed for paper/Excel migrators (30% of solos don't have a website). Guided onboarding, pre-built templates, and migration assistance.

6. **Mobile-first design**: All competitors have weak mobile apps; solos doing court and field work are consistently frustrated. A genuinely functional mobile app is an unoccupied differentiator.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: Sarah — The High-Volume Solo (Primary Target)**

- **Background**: Sarah is a 38-year-old family law attorney in her 6th year of solo practice in Austin, TX. She handles 40–60 active matters at any given time — divorces, custody modifications, adoptions. She bills hourly and has a paralegal working part-time.
- **Current stack**: Clio Essentials ($79/month) + QuickBooks Online ($30/month) + DocuSign ($25/month) = $134/month. She needs trust accounting but can't justify Clio Complete at $139/month just for that one feature.
- **Pain points**: She loses track of billable time during client calls and court hearings. Trust accounting reconciliation takes her 4–5 hours per month and she's never entirely sure she's done it correctly. Her paralegal's Clio seat costs her the same as her own. Payment from clients via LawPay takes 8 days to settle.
- **Trigger**: Clio raises prices by 15% at renewal. Sarah opens Google and types "Clio alternative for solo attorneys."
- **Success vision**: "I can add my paralegal without paying double. My trust accounts reconcile themselves. I got paid in 2 days instead of 8."

**Persona 2: Marcus — The New Solo (High Lifetime Value)**

- **Background**: Marcus is a 31-year-old criminal defense attorney who left a mid-size firm 8 months ago. He's building his practice from scratch — currently using Gmail, Excel for billing, and a separate bank account he manually manages as his trust account.
- **Current stack**: $0/month in software (paper + Excel + email). Zero switching costs. Zero data to migrate.
- **Pain points**: He's been warned by a bar colleague that his trust account management is non-compliant. He has no formal intake process. He misses billable hours constantly because he never starts a timer.
- **Trigger**: Another solo recommends SoloCounsel on r/lawyers after someone asks "what software do you use for trust accounting without paying Clio's full price?"
- **Success vision**: "I went from Excel to fully compliant practice management in one afternoon. I'm actually tracking my billable hours now."

**Persona 3: Elena — The Frustrated Clio User (Displacement Target)**

- **Background**: Elena is a 52-year-old immigration attorney in Chicago. She's been on Clio for 4 years. Her practice is her and one part-time paralegal. She handles flat-fee immigration matters (I-130 petitions, naturalization, work visas) and is frustrated that she has no way to see whether a flat-fee case is profitable.
- **Current stack**: Clio Complete ($139/month per user × 1 full user + partial paralegal seat = ~$200/month effective).
- **Pain points**: "I have no idea if my $2,500 flat-fee cases are actually profitable until they're done and I look back." No competitor offers flat-fee matter budget tracking. She also resents Clio's annual price increases.
- **Trigger**: Clio raises rates again. She posts to a bar association listserv asking if anyone has made the switch.
- **Success vision**: "I can see at a glance that my I-130 case is 80% through its budget. I know when to have a conversation with the client about scope. I'm paying $35 instead of $200."

### Secondary Users

**Paralegals and Legal Assistants**: Non-attorney users who handle matter intake, client communication, scheduling, billing administration, and document management. In SoloCounsel, they get full functionality under the same flat-rate plan — no additional seat cost. This is a direct selling point to solo attorneys who currently avoid giving paralegals system access because it doubles their bill.

**Clients (Portal Users)**: Attorney clients who receive a portal invitation to view invoices, sign documents, share files, and make payments. The client portal is not priced separately — it is included in the flat-rate plan. Client experience directly influences attorney retention.

### User Journey

**Discovery:**
- Trigger: Clio price increase email, feature removal, bar association listserv discussion, r/lawyers post, or Google search ("Clio alternative solo attorney")
- First touchpoint: Landing page with headline "Legal practice management for solo attorneys. $35/month. Everything included."

**Onboarding:**

**Success Moment ("Aha!"):**
- For high-volume solos: First time they see the timer running in-app while on a client call and it automatically attaches to the right matter
- For new solos: First IOLTA reconciliation that completes without errors and generates a compliance report
- For flat-fee users: First time they see a matter budget dashboard showing 60% consumed, triggering a proactive client conversation

**Long-term Retention:**
- Trust accounting data creates strong switching friction — case history and trust ledger records are not easily migrated
- Flat-fee matter budgets become habit-forming for profitability management
- Bar association referrals and community recommendations compound over time

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP solves the core problems: compliance (trust accounting), revenue leakage (time tracking), and pricing resentment (flat-rate). Everything else is post-MVP.

**Feature Set (8-week build):**

**1. Matter Management**
- Matters list (kanban + list view toggle)
- Client and contact records with matter association
- Basic conflict check (name search across clients and matters)
- Document storage per matter (upload, organize, preview)
- Matter status workflow (Open → Active → Closed)

**2. Time Tracking**
- One-click timer per matter (start/stop from matter view and global)
- Manual time entry with matter assignment
- Flat-fee matter budget: set a budget at matter creation, track time/expense consumption vs. budget in real-time
- Expense tracking (mileage, filing fees, costs) attached to matters
- Time entry reports (by matter, by client, by date range)

**3. Invoicing and Billing**
- Invoice generation from matter time entries and expenses (one-click)
- Contingency-fee invoice support (no fixed billing amount required)
- Invoice PDF with firm branding (logo, address)
- Online payment link (integrated payment processing, 2–3 day settlement)
- Invoice status tracking (Draft → Sent → Paid → Overdue)
- Basic accounts receivable report

**4. IOLTA Trust Accounting**
- Trust account setup (bank name, account number, jurisdiction)
- Client trust ledger (one ledger per client per matter with trust funds)
- Trust deposit recording (retainer, settlement receipt)
- Trust disbursement recording (payment from trust, refund)
- Three-way monthly reconciliation wizard:
  - Bank statement import (CSV)
  - Auto-match trust ledger to bank transactions
  - Reconciliation report (bank balance = trust ledger total = sum of client sub-ledgers)
  - Reconciliation lock + compliance report download
- California CTAPP digital reporting baseline standard
- Trust accounting audit trail (immutable log of all trust transactions)

**5. Client Portal**
- Client invitation by email
- Secure document sharing (attorney uploads, client downloads)
- Secure messaging (matter-scoped)
- Invoice viewing and online payment from portal
- Retainer agreement e-signature (via DocuSign API integration or native basic e-sign)

**6. Onboarding and Setup**
- Guided 30-minute setup wizard (practice area, jurisdiction, trust account, branding)
- Client/matter import via CSV (for Clio and Excel migrators)
- Email/calendar sync (Google, Outlook — read-only calendar for court deadline visibility)
- In-app trust accounting setup walkthrough with jurisdiction-specific guidance

**Pricing at launch:**
- Monthly subscription: $35/month for up to 3 users (all features included)
- Lifetime Deal: $79 (1 user) / $149 (up to 3 users) — AppSumo launch

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following features are explicitly deferred. They are not hidden — they are communicated as the roadmap to build trust with early adopters:

| Deferred Feature | Reason | Target |
|-----------------|---------|--------|
| Court deadline calendar (jurisdiction rules) | High maintenance ongoing; Phase 2 | Month 3–4 |
| Native mobile apps (iOS/Android) | Responsive web MVP first; validate demand | Month 3–6 |
| AI document drafting | Upsell add-on; needs core product stable first | Month 6+ |
| Advanced workflow automation | Non-essential for solo MVP; reduces build time | Month 4–6 |
| Conflicts check (full, against open matters + closed) | Basic name search sufficient for MVP | Month 3–4 |
| International trust accounting rule sets | US market first | Month 9–12 |
| Native e-signature (no DocuSign dependency) | DocuSign API sufficient for MVP | Month 6+ |
| Client intake forms (website embed) | Phase 2 growth feature | Month 3–4 |
| Payment processing own rails | Use Stripe/LawPay API first; own rails later for margin | Month 12+ |
| Advanced reporting and analytics | Basic reports sufficient for MVP | Month 6+ |

**Phase 4 (Year 2–3): Platform and Market Expansion**
- International expansion: Canada (similar legal system, no market leader for solos), UK, Australia — with jurisdiction-specific trust accounting rule sets

**Long-term market position (Year 3):**

- The recognized market leader in the solo attorney segment — the position Clio is abandoning
