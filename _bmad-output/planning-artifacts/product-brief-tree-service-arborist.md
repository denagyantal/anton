# Product Brief: ArborDesk — Tree Service & Arborist Business Software

---

## Executive Summary

ArborDesk is a tree-native field service management platform for solo operators and small tree care crews (1–5 trucks) that sets up in under 30 minutes, syncs reliably with QuickBooks Online, and ships with the arborist-specific features that generic tools like Jobber completely lack — per-tree history cards, ISA A300/ANSI compliance checklists, and offline-capable mobile.

The US tree care industry generates $39.5 billion annually across 175,000 businesses, the vast majority of which are solo operators or small crews currently stuck in one of two unsatisfying options: (1) affordable generic tools like Jobber that have zero tree-specific features, forcing crews to maintain separate spreadsheets for tree records; or (2) expensive purpose-built platforms like ArboStar ($150+/user/month) and SingleOps ($550+/month base) that are over-engineered, plagued by documented QuickBooks sync failures and mobile data loss, and require weeks of configuration to get running.

ArborDesk occupies the completely unoccupied quadrant: **tree-specific + affordable at $79/month flat**. The product concept is validated by documented customer pain (direct Capterra quotes), a proven market of operators paying $250–$1,383/month for inferior tools, and a clear community channel (ArboristSite.com, r/arboriculture, TCIA Annual Conference, Facebook "Tree Care Business Owners" with 15K members) through which early customers can be acquired.

The go-to-market path begins with a $199 Lifetime Deal launch (the first LTD in this vertical — no incumbent currently offers one), targeting 300–700 units to generate $60K–$140K in upfront validation revenue, followed by a $79/month SaaS tier for ongoing MRR growth. The 12-month target is 200 paying customers at $79/month ($15,800 MRR / ~$190K ARR).

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small tree care businesses — the 120,000–160,000 US companies running 1–5 trucks — are operating their entire business on a broken patchwork of tools: Jobber or a spreadsheet for scheduling, paper or a separate app for tree records, manual QuickBooks entry because their FSM tool's sync keeps breaking, and a clipboard or generic form app for ISA compliance checklists. None of the tools designed for them actually understand what an arborist does.

The tree care operators who try to graduate to purpose-built arborist software (ArboStar, SingleOps, Arborgold) discover that these tools were designed for mid-market operations (5+ trucks), require weeks of configuration, cost $300–$1,383/month when all add-ons are included, and still have documented reliability failures — particularly QuickBooks sync and mobile data loss in the field.

**The core problem in one sentence:** No affordable tool (under $100/month) understands arborist work deeply enough to replace the paper-and-spreadsheet patchwork, and no purpose-built arborist tool is simple or affordable enough for the 80% of the market that runs 1–3 trucks.

### Problem Impact

**For the business owner/operator:**
- Time wasted on manual QuickBooks entry after jobs (30–60 minutes/week) because their FSM sync broke again
- Lost revenue from proposals not delivered (Arborgold's email deliverability cited at ~50% receipt rate in verified reviews)
- Data loss when crew submits job updates in the field and the mobile app drops data when the phone screen goes black (documented SingleOps complaint)
- Weeks invested in onboarding a specialized tool only to abandon it due to complexity (ArboStar)
- Inability to document tree health, hazard ratings, and ISA compliance in the same system as scheduling and invoicing — a liability and workflow friction problem

**For the crew in the field:**
- No offline capability means unreliable job data in dense canopy or rural areas with poor cell coverage
- Generic job forms don't capture tree-specific data (species, DBH, hazard rating, work history) — so nothing useful is recorded
- ISA compliance checklists exist only on paper, making documentation slow, lossy, and non-searchable

**For business growth:**
- Can't generate professional tree assessment reports for customers or insurance carriers
- No per-tree history means repeat customers start from zero every visit
- No recurring service automation means PHC (plant health care) upsells are missed

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**ArboStar ($150+/user/month):** Tree-specific and feature-complete, but priced for mid-to-large operations. Onboarding takes weeks; multiple reviewers abandoned it after 1–2 months. No Lifetime Deal, no month-to-month pricing. Over-engineered for solo operators.

**SingleOps ($550+/month base, annual contract):** Documented QuickBooks sync failures with direct Capterra quotes: *"I have nothing but problems with SingleOps syncing to QuickBooks"* (Anna M., Office Manager). Mobile app loses data when phone screen goes black. True all-in cost with add-ons reaches $1,383+/month. Acquired by private equity (Granum), with post-acquisition customer service decline cited in reviews. Forced annual contracts with documented auto-renewal disputes.

**Arborgold ($299–$599/month):** 25+ years in the market but dated UX. Email deliverability for proposals is ~50% per a verified review — operators are losing jobs because proposals don't arrive. Annual contract standard. Complex for small operators.

**Jobber ($69–$349/month):** Clean UX, reliable QBO sync, excellent mobile — but zero tree-specific features. Users must maintain separate spreadsheets for tree inventory, paper forms for ISA compliance, and manual workarounds for per-tree health records. Jobber explicitly acknowledges this gap on their tree care landing page.

**ArborNote ($50–$200/month):** The closest existing occupant of the target quadrant. Tree inventory, per-tree records, ISA compliance, QBO and Hubspot integrations, TCIA-recommended at annual conference. However: iOS-heavy with limited Android support, pricing escalates to $200/month, minimal community marketing presence, no LTD, and less robust business management (routing, crew scheduling, CRM) compared to Jobber.

**The critical gap:** No tool at $79–$100/month flat rate combines tree-native features (per-tree records, ISA compliance, species/DBH tracking) with reliable QBO sync, offline-capable mobile, and fast (<30 minute) setup for solo and small-crew operators.

### Proposed Solution

ArborDesk is a purpose-built arborist field service management platform that wins on three vectors simultaneously:

1. **Speed to value**: Pre-built job templates for pruning, removal, stump grinding, cabling, and plant health care — ready out of the box, no configuration week. Target: first job dispatched in under 30 minutes.

2. **Arborist-native workflows**: Per-tree history cards (species, DBH, work history, treatment log, hazard rating, photos), ANSI A300 / ISA standard inspection checklists on mobile, and PHC tracking built into the core product — not bolted on or maintained in a separate spreadsheet.

3. **Reliability where incumbents fail**: QuickBooks Online two-way sync built as a core investment (not an integration), with real-time sync status visible in the UI. Offline-first mobile architecture using local storage with background sync — data is never lost when the phone screen goes black or cell coverage disappears under a canopy.

**Positioning statement:** *"The arborist software that sets up in 30 minutes, syncs with QuickBooks reliably, and actually understands your trees — at $79/month flat."*

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: Marcus, the Owner-Operator (Solo / 1-Truck)**

Marcus runs a solo tree care business in a mid-size metro area. He's ISA-certified, does about $200K/year in revenue, and handles everything himself — climbing, quoting, invoicing, and QuickBooks. He currently uses Jobber ($69/month) for scheduling and invoicing because he heard it was easy and his landscaper friend uses it. He keeps a spiral notebook in the truck for tree records and uses paper ISA inspection forms that he fills out and files in a manila folder. He manually enters every invoice into QuickBooks on Sunday nights.

Marcus is not a technology enthusiast. He bought an iPad for the business once and never got around to setting it up. He discovers software through ArboristSite.com when someone asks for recommendations in a thread, and he's trusted by word-of-mouth more than by marketing pages.

*Pain*: Tree records are in a notebook, not searchable. He can't quickly find what he did on Mrs. Johnson's silver maple three years ago. When he quotes a job for a new customer who has 12 trees, he has no data on which ones were treated, when, or with what. ISA compliance documentation is paper and could be lost. His Sunday QuickBooks entry takes 2 hours because Jobber doesn't match the way QBO wants the data.

*Success vision*: Quotes a repeat customer while standing in their yard, pulls up the per-tree history on his phone, gives a professional assessment that references past work, and the invoice syncs to QuickBooks automatically. No Sunday night data entry. No spiral notebook.

*Discovery channel*: ArboristSite.com thread recommendation or TCIA conference attendee demo.

### Secondary Users

**Crew Members / Field Technicians**

The ground crew using the mobile app in the field. They need:
- Simple, clear job assignment view (what, where, when)
- One-tap job status updates (en route, started, completed)
- Ability to upload photos from the field (before/after tree work)
- ISA checklist completion on mobile, offline
- Time clock in/out

They are not the buyer but their friction is a primary reason companies churn from SingleOps and ArboStar. Making the mobile app simple and reliable (especially offline) is a retention driver for the primary buyer.

**Office Admin / Dispatcher**

For companies with 2+ trucks and an admin role (Sarah's persona), the office admin needs:
- Calendar/schedule view for all crews
- Quote and invoice management
- Customer communication history
- QBO sync status dashboard

**ISA-Certified Arborists (PHC/Consulting Specialist)**

A smaller but valuable secondary segment: ISA-credentialed arborists who do consulting, plant health care assessments, and hazard evaluations. They need robust documentation features — treatment logs, pesticide/fertilizer tracking, hazard ratings, and the ability to generate a professional tree assessment report to share with clients or insurance carriers. This segment would pay a premium for enhanced reporting and compliance features in Phase 2.

### User Journey

**Discovery → Decision:**
1. Operator hits a specific pain point (QBO sync fails, Arborgold proposal goes missing, crew loses field data)
2. Posts or searches in ArboristSite.com, r/arboriculture, or Facebook group
3. Peer recommends or a discussion thread surfaces ArborDesk
4. Operator visits landing page — headline "Sets up in 30 minutes. Syncs with QuickBooks. Built for arborists." resonates immediately

1. Signs up, imports QuickBooks customer list (one-click OAuth)
2. Selects job templates from pre-built library (pruning, removal, stump grinding, cabling)
3. Adds first customer, creates first job using template, assigns to self or crew

5. Completes job on mobile, photos uploaded, invoice generated, QBO synced automatically

**Core Usage Loop (Daily/Weekly):**
- Morning: Check schedule, see crew assignments
- Field: Crews update job status, complete ISA checklists, take photos

**Success Moment:**
A customer asks "What did you do on my oak last spring?" and the operator pulls up the per-tree card in front of the customer and walks through the work history, treatment notes, and hazard rating. Customer says "Wow, you're so organized." This moment triggers the referral.

**Retention:**
Once tree inventory, customer history, and job templates are populated (typically 60–90 days), switching costs are very high. The per-tree database is the data moat. Monthly churn drops below 2% after the 90-day mark.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver the "30 minutes to first job dispatched, QuickBooks synced, tree card created" promise. Eight features constitute the MVP:

**1. Pre-Built Arborist Job Templates**
- Out-of-box templates for: pruning, removal (full and partial), stump grinding, cabling & bracing, deep root fertilization, plant health care inspection
- Each template includes default line items, typical job duration estimates, and required materials checklist
- Templates are editable but work without modification
- *Why it's MVP*: This is the "30 minute setup" promise. Without pre-built templates, every new user faces a blank canvas. This is the reason operators abandon ArboStar after paying for it.

**2. QuickBooks Online Two-Way Sync**
- OAuth-based secure connection (no storing of QBO credentials)
- Customer sync: import existing QBO customers on setup
- Invoice sync: auto-push invoices to QBO on job completion
- Payment sync: mark invoices paid in ArborDesk when QBO records payment
- Sync status dashboard: green/yellow/red indicator always visible
- Sync error log with plain-language descriptions and one-click retry
- *Why it's MVP*: This is the #1 pain point driving users away from SingleOps. If we don't nail this in MVP, we have no differentiation from the tools that fail at it.

**3. Offline-Capable Mobile App (iOS + Android)**
- Local-first architecture: all job data stored on device, syncs to server when connectivity available
- Works without any cell signal (dense canopy, rural, underground)
- Background sync: automatically pushes pending changes when signal returns
- Clear UI indicator of sync state (synced / pending / offline)
- *Why it's MVP*: The documented SingleOps data loss problem is the #2 pain point. If crews lose data in the field, the business owner will cancel immediately.

**4. Per-Tree History Cards**
- Tree record fields: species (with common name dropdown), DBH (diameter at breast height), height estimate, location (attached to property/customer), installation date (if known), condition rating
- Work history log: automatically populated when a job is marked complete on that tree
- Manual notes field for treatments, observations, chemical applications
- Photo gallery (before/after) attached to each tree or each job entry
- Hazard rating field (ISA 1–4 scale) with last assessment date
- *Why it's MVP*: This is the core feature that differentiates ArborDesk from Jobber. Without it, ArborDesk is just another generic FSM tool. Per-tree cards are also the primary switching cost / data moat.

**5. ISA / ANSI A300 Inspection Checklists on Mobile**
- Pre-built inspection checklist templates for: general tree assessment, hazard evaluation, pre-work safety inspection, post-storm assessment
- Checklists attach to a specific tree card and job
- Completable offline
- Generates a PDF report for customer or insurance carrier (simple format)
- *Why it's MVP*: ISA compliance is a signal to the professional arborist community that ArborDesk understands their work. It's the "I knew this was built for me" moment during evaluation. It also has real professional value — ISA-certified arborists have liability reasons to document assessments.

**6. Customer CRM (Basic)**
- Customer record: name, address(es), billing address, phone, email, customer notes
- Property record: linked to customer, can have multiple properties per customer
- Tree records: linked to property
- Job history: all past jobs for a customer viewable in one place
- Import from QBO on setup (or CSV import)
- *Why it's MVP*: The minimum data structure required to link trees, jobs, customers, and invoices.

**7. Job Scheduling and Crew Dispatch**
- Create job: customer, property, job type (from template), scheduled date/time, assigned crew member(s)
- Calendar view: week view for scheduler/owner, day view for crew
- Job status flow: Scheduled → Dispatched → In Progress → Completed → Invoiced
- Push notification to crew when job is dispatched or updated
- *Why it's MVP*: Core workflow — without this, ArborDesk is not a FSM tool.

**8. Invoice Generation and Payment Collection**
- Auto-generate invoice on job completion using job template line items
- Editable before sending (add/remove/modify line items)
- Send invoice by email with professional template
- Accept payment via Stripe link in the invoice email (credit card / ACH)
- Invoice status: Draft, Sent, Viewed, Paid, Overdue
- *Why it's MVP*: Revenue collection is non-negotiable. This, combined with QBO sync, closes the loop between field work and accounting.

### Future Vision

**Phase 2 (Months 6–12): Community Moat + Premium Tier**

- **Route optimization**: Multi-stop day planning with Google Maps integration
- **PHC recurring service automation**: Schedule and track recurring plant health care visits with treatment reminders
- **Review automation**: Post-job SMS/email prompt to leave a Google or Capterra review
- **ISA credential badge on proposals**: ISA-certified arborist badge that populates automatically from the arborist's stored certification data — differentiates proposals from competitors
- **Customer portal (read-only)**: Customers can log in to view their tree records, past work, and upcoming scheduled jobs
- **Professional pricing**: $149/month tier for 5–15 users with all Phase 2 features

**Phase 3 (Months 12–24): Vertical Depth + Adjacent Markets**

- **GIS tree mapping lite**: Basic geo-referenced tree map overlay (not TreePlotter-level GIS, but useful for commercial properties with 20+ trees)
- **AI photo health assessment**: Upload a photo of a tree, get a preliminary health assessment and suggested treatments (powered by Claude API or specialized tree health model)
- **Insurance/hazard report generation**: Professional PDF hazard assessment report suitable for insurance carriers — leveraging ISA checklist data and hazard ratings
- **Pesticide/chemical application log**: Track chemical applications with product name, concentration, application method, target pest/disease — for ISA PHC specialists and compliance-focused operators
- **White-label partnership**: TCIA-endorsed "Tree Care Business Software" tier — TCIA distributes to 1,400+ members, ArborDesk provides platform

**Long-Term Vision (Year 3+):**

ArborDesk becomes the operating system for professional tree care — the tool that ISA-certified arborists point to when they want to demonstrate to customers that they're more than a chainsaw operator with a truck. When insurance carriers start accepting digital ArborDesk hazard assessments, when TCIA integrates ArborDesk certification verification into the member portal, and when drone/LiDAR canopy data can be imported directly into tree cards — ArborDesk becomes essential infrastructure for the professionalization of the tree care industry.

The market cap for this vision is substantial: 175,000 US tree businesses × $79/month × 20% market share = $33M ARR in the US alone, with Canada and Australia as natural expansion markets (same ISA standards, same QuickBooks dominance, same competitive vacuum).

## Strategic Summary

ArborDesk's path to success is tight, opinionated, and community-led:

1. **Build the MVP in 5–6 weeks** — 8 features, no scope creep, offline-first mobile, QBO sync as a non-negotiable investment
2. **Launch the LTD** — $199 lifetime deal, no incumbent has done it in this vertical, target ArboristSite/TCIA/Facebook community for initial seeding, goal 300–700 units

4. **Build the data moat** — Per-tree cards, customer history, and ISA compliance docs make switching painful after 90 days; high LTV follows naturally
5. **Scale up from community** — The TCIA/ISA community is small enough to be owned through authentic participation; conference presence at TCI Expo (Month 9) becomes the inflection point for word-of-mouth

The window is open. SingleOps is losing customers post-acquisition. ArboStar is moving upmarket. ArborNote has the right idea but hasn't captured it. No one has launched an LTD. The tree care industry is ready for a tool that respects their time and their trees.
