# Product Brief: Construction Billing & Compliance Tool (FieldBill)

---

## Executive Summary

Small general contractors and trade subcontractors — the 500,000+ businesses doing $500K–$5M in annual work — are getting crushed by a payment problem hiding in plain sight. They complete the work, they submit the invoice, and then they wait. And wait. And wait. In 2024 the construction industry lost an estimated $280 billion to slow payments. 74% of small contractors faced moderate-to-severe cash flow difficulties. 82% waited more than 30 days to get paid.

The root cause isn't just slow payers — it's that small contractors don't have the right tools to generate airtight pay applications. AIA G702/G703 progress billing forms, properly tracked retainage, documented change orders, and correctly issued lien waivers are what separate contractors who get paid on time from those who lose disputes and write off retainage. But the only tools that handle all of this correctly start at $186/month (Knowify) and run to $375–$1,000+/month (Procore). The budget alternatives ($49/month Contractor Foreman) are technically capable but practically unusable — cluttered, inconsistent, and not trusted for legally significant documents.

**FieldBill** closes this gap with a purpose-built construction billing and compliance tool for small contractors at $29–49/month (and $99 LTD on AppSumo). Not a watered-down project management suite. Not a complex ERP with billing bolted on. A fast, mobile-first tool that lets a contractor create an AIA-compliant progress billing on the jobsite in under 3 minutes, track every dollar of withheld retainage, document every change order, and generate lien waivers that actually protect their payment rights — at a price that doesn't require $3M in annual billings to justify.

This is the first purpose-built AIA billing tool at the $49/month tier. The AppSumo category is vacant. The market validation is overwhelming. The product builds one focused thing and builds it right.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small general contractors and specialty subcontractors (2–20 employees, $500K–$5M annual revenue) cannot afford the construction billing and compliance tools they need to get paid correctly and protect their lien rights. Progress billing using AIA G702/G703 forms, retainage tracking, change order documentation, and lien waiver generation are essential for anyone doing commercial or residential work above $100K — but every tool that handles these correctly costs $186–$1,000+/month. The result: small contractors lose money through underbilling, retainage write-offs, and rejected pay applications. They rely on Excel spreadsheets for legally significant documents. They don't know what they're owed until it's too late to file a lien.

### Problem Impact

The financial and operational consequences of this gap are severe and well-documented:

- **$280 billion** was lost to slow payments in the US construction industry in 2024 — a 35% increase from 2022
- **82%** of contractors experience payment waits exceeding 30 days (up from 49% two years prior)
- Only **5%** of subcontractors receive timely payments
- **74%** of construction businesses faced moderate-to-severe cash flow difficulties in 2024
- **1 in 3** subcontractors withdraw from personal or retirement savings to bridge payment gaps
- Pay applications are regularly rejected due to missing, inaccurate, or incomplete documentation — turning a billing dispute into a 30–60 day delay
- Lien waiver errors (wrong type, missing retainage exceptions) can legally extinguish payment rights, causing contractors to lose substantial sums they are legitimately owed
- Retainage (5–10% withheld per progress payment) is routinely undercollected or written off — because contractors don't track it systematically

The problem is acute, recurring (every billing cycle), financially material, and directly tied to a compliance document workflow that costs nothing to get right — if you have the right tool.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Enterprise tier (Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Buildertrend):** Technically capable but priced entirely out of reach for small contractors. Procore starts at ~$375/month and averages $76,000/year. Buildertrend has hit $799/month after documented 3–5x price increases over 5 years. These are platforms built for large GCs managing $10M+ projects. The feature sets are massive, the onboarding is complex, and the annual contracts create lock-in that small contractors resent. Procore's typical customer is a 100+ person firm. FieldBill's customer has 5 people.

**Mid-market tier (Knowify at $186/month, Buildxact at $133/month):** Knowify is the closest full-featured competitor with legitimate AIA billing at the lowest verified price point — but $186/month requires a contractor to be doing $500K+ in active billings just to justify the tool cost. The ROI math doesn't work for contractors below the $1M/year threshold. Buildxact is strong on estimating but weak on billing and financial compliance.

**Budget tier (Contractor Foreman at $49/month, Jobber at $39–$200/month):** Contractor Foreman technically has AIA billing but users consistently describe it as "clunky" — the mobile experience is inconsistent, the UI is cluttered with features from 35+ modules, and the AIA billing implementation doesn't inspire confidence for legally significant documents. Jobber has no AIA billing, retainage, or lien waivers at all — it's a field service dispatching tool. Neither solves the specific problem.

**The missing solution:** A focused, mobile-first, AIA-compliant billing and compliance tool at $29–49/month that small contractors can actually trust for legally significant pay applications. This product does not exist at this price point. Zero AppSumo entries in the category. Zero purpose-built AIA billing tools under $79/month. The gap is real and quantifiable.

### Proposed Solution

**FieldBill** — a mobile-first construction billing and compliance tool that does one thing exceptionally well: helps small contractors get paid correctly and on time.

**Core capabilities:**
1. **AIA G702/G703 Progress Billing** — generate compliant progress billing forms in under 3 minutes from the jobsite, with automatic retainage calculation and running total tracking
2. **Retainage Tracking** — per-project ledger tracking every dollar withheld, when it becomes due, and automated reminders at project completion
3. **Change Order Management** — documented change order log with owner signature tracking, billable amounts, and automatic inclusion in subsequent progress billings
4. **Lien Waiver Generation** — conditional and unconditional, partial and final — auto-populated from billing data, reducing the manual errors that cost contractors their payment rights
5. **QuickBooks Integration** — bidirectional sync with QuickBooks Online/Desktop; this is table-stakes and must be day-one
6. **Mobile-First Design** — built for iPhone/Android use on the jobsite, not desktop-first design ported to mobile

**Pricing:**
- $29/month (solo, up to 3 active projects)
- $49/month (team, unlimited projects)
- $99 AppSumo LTD (5 projects perpetual) — first mover in vacant category
- $149 AppSumo LTD (unlimited projects perpetual)

### Key Differentiators

1. **Purpose-built for billing compliance, not general project management.** FieldBill doesn't have scheduling, client portals, photo management, or 35 other modules. It does AIA billing and lien waivers better than anything at this price because that's all it does.

2. **Mobile-first AIA billing.** The only tool where creating a G702/G703 progress billing is optimized for a 5-inch screen on a dusty jobsite — not adapted from a desktop form. This is novel at any price point in the budget tier.

3. **Flat pricing with a no-surprise-hike brand promise.** Buildertrend did 3–5x price increases. Procore's renewals are notorious. FieldBill publicly commits to transparent pricing and contractual no-hike guarantees for annual subscribers. This is a brand differentiator, not just a feature.

4. **AppSumo LTD first mover.** No construction billing tool exists on AppSumo. Price-sensitive small contractors are an active AppSumo audience. First credible entry in the category earns outsized organic attention.

5. **Legally accurate lien waiver generation.** Most tools in this tier don't have lien waivers. Those that do have basic templates. FieldBill generates state-appropriate conditional and unconditional waivers auto-populated from billing data — reducing the errors that cause contractors to lose payment rights.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP delivers exactly the features that solve the primary pain — getting paid correctly — and nothing else. Every feature below maps directly to the problem of rejected pay apps, lost retainage, or lien waiver errors.

**1. Project Setup & Contract Management**
- Create project with contract amount, client, start date
- Schedule of Values (SOV) entry — line items matching the AIA G703
- Store retainage percentage per project (typically 5–10%)
- Track contract modifications (amendment log)

**2. AIA G702/G703 Progress Billing Generator**
- Generate standard AIA G702 Application for Payment form
- Generate AIA G703 Continuation Sheet from Schedule of Values
- Auto-calculate: Work completed to date, stored materials, retainage held, amount due
- Running total of previous billings, current billing, and balance to complete
- Carry forward data from prior billing periods automatically
- Export as PDF (print-ready, email-ready)
- Mobile-optimized data entry (update % complete per line item on phone)

**3. Retainage Ledger**
- Per-project retainage tracking: amount withheld per billing period
- Running total of total retainage held
- Alert at substantial completion: retainage release trigger
- Track partial retainage releases

**4. Change Order Log**
- Document each change order with: description, amount, owner approval status, date
- Pending change orders tracked separately from approved
- Approved change orders automatically added to next billing cycle SOV
- Running total of approved change order value vs. original contract

**5. Lien Waiver Generation**
- Four standard waiver types: Conditional Partial, Unconditional Partial, Conditional Final, Unconditional Final
- Auto-populate from current billing data (through-date, payment amount, project info)
- State-specific templates for top 10 construction states (CA, TX, FL, NY, IL, GA, NC, OH, PA, AZ)
- Export as PDF for signature
- Waiver log tracking which waivers were signed and when

**6. QuickBooks Integration (Day One)**
- Connect to QuickBooks Online (primary) and QuickBooks Desktop
- Push billing amounts to QuickBooks as invoices
- Pull payment records from QuickBooks to confirm receipt
- Map FieldBill projects to QuickBooks customers/jobs

**7. Mobile App (iOS + Android)**
- Full billing creation workflow on mobile
- Offline mode: enter % complete data without cell coverage, sync when connected
- Camera integration: attach photos to change orders

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following are explicitly deferred to prevent scope creep and maintain the product's focused positioning:

- **Scheduling / Gantt charts** — Buildertrend already owns this; not FieldBill's differentiator
- **Estimating / Takeoffs** — Separate problem, separate workflow, separate buyer moment
- **Client portal** — Adds complexity; email PDF is sufficient for MVP
- **Subcontractor management** (GC tools to manage their subs) — Phase 2 after validating primary sub/GC billing use case
- **Budgeting / Job costing dashboards** — Tracked in QuickBooks; FieldBill pushes data there
- **Bid management** — Different workflow, different trigger event
- **Daily reports / Field logs** — Buildertrend, Fieldwire, and others own this; not a billing problem
- **Document management / Drawing management** — Procore territory; not billing-adjacent enough for MVP
- **Team / Employee time tracking** — Jobber and Clockify own this
- **Multi-user collaboration / Permission levels** — Phase 2; MVP assumes owner-operator and one office admin

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is validated when:

1. **Product-market fit signal:** 100 active paying customers (LTD or MRR) with <5% support tickets about billing accuracy — indicating the core workflow produces reliable outputs
2. **QuickBooks integration stability:** >95% of QuickBooks syncs complete without error in the first 60 days after launch
3. **Retention baseline:** 70%+ of free trial users who create at least one complete billing convert to paid or continue to month 2
4. **Pay app acceptance:** In 90-day user survey, >80% report their FieldBill-generated pay apps were accepted on first submission
5. **AppSumo launch benchmark:** 300+ LTD buyers in the AppSumo launch window (30 days), indicating real demand in the price-sensitive segment

The go/no-go decision to invest in Phase 2 features (subcontractor management, expanded job costing, additional lien waiver states) should require: 500+ paying customers, <4% monthly churn, and at least 5 unsolicited feature requests per week from active users.

**Phase 2 (Months 7–18): Become the Sub-Contractor's Financial OS**
Once the billing core is validated and the user base reaches 500+ active customers:

- **GC-to-Sub Pay App Submission:** Subs submit pay apps directly to GC approval portals (GCPay, Textura, Procore) through FieldBill — removing email entirely for commercial subs
- **Subcontractor Payment Tracking for GCs:** GCs manage their sub pay applications and approvals through FieldBill — makes FieldBill valuable on both sides of the billing relationship
- **Joint Check and Conditional Payment Tracking:** Track payments tied to lien waiver conditions — a legally important workflow currently done manually
- **Expanded Lien State Coverage:** All 50 states with state-specific validation rules

**Phase 3 (Months 18–36): Platform Expansion**
- **Estimating Integration:** Connect FieldBill SOV to estimating tools (Buildertrend, Procore) or simple estimating built in — contract awarded → SOV auto-populated
- **AI Pay App Review:** Automatically check pay app against contract terms, flag common rejection reasons before submission
- **Construction Lending Integration:** Connect progress billings to construction lenders for automated draw requests (large GC and developer workflow)
- **International Expansion:** Canadian equivalents to AIA billing (CCDC forms), then UK/Australia

**Long-term Vision:**
FieldBill becomes the construction industry's billing and payment rails — the Stripe for construction payments — processing billions in construction pay applications and providing the compliance infrastructure that makes the industry's cash flow problem manageable for small contractors. Starting with the billing document workflow is the Trojan horse into the payment relationship itself.
