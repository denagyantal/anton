## Research Overview

The U.S. trucking industry loses **$15 billion annually** to driver detention — $3.6 billion in direct costs and $11.5 billion in lost productivity — yet fewer than 50% of detention fees are ever collected. The root cause is documentation failure: small carriers and owner-operators rely on paper logs, manual timestamps, and verbal agreements, leaving billions of dollars on the table every year.

This research validates a purpose-built opportunity at the intersection of two adjacent pain points: (1) automated detention tracking and fee recovery for small carriers (3–15 trucks), and (2) purpose-built CRM/dispatch tooling for independent freight dispatchers managing those same carriers. No standalone, affordable solution exists today for either segment. The competitive landscape is bifurcated between owner-operator apps ($49/month, basic) and enterprise TMS platforms ($5,000–50,000+/year), leaving the 3–15 truck carrier segment entirely underserved.

This report covers market size, customer segmentation, competitive landscape, pricing gaps, LTD viability, regulatory tailwinds, and a go-to-market roadmap. All data is sourced from live web research conducted May 2026.

## 3. Customer Insights and Behavior Analysis

### Customer Behavior Patterns

The target customer population is the **small carrier operator** — either an owner-operator with 1–5 trucks, or a small fleet owner with 3–15 trucks, often served by an independent dispatcher. Key behavior patterns:

- **80% of carriers refuse loads from facilities known for high detention** — they are already making business decisions based on detention but lack systematic data
- **Load selection is driven by rate-per-mile** but carriers with detention experience increasingly factor detention risk into acceptance decisions
- **Phone-first communication:** Most carrier–broker communication happens via phone and text. Documentation requests are resisted if they require separate apps or workflows
- **Reactive not proactive:** Carriers rarely submit detention claims on <$200 events due to administrative burden; they only fight for larger claims

_Source: [Trucker Tools — Detention Strategies for Owner-operators and Small Carriers](https://www.truckertools.com/detention-strategies-for-owner-operators-and-small-carriers-2/)_

**Owner-Operators:**
- ~923,000 independent owner-operators in the US (11% of all truck drivers)
- Average age: 56 years old (up from 50 two decades ago)
- Gen X dominant (40.8% of trucking workforce), Baby Boomers at 20.7%
- 55% of all US trucking companies are single-truck operations
- 97% of for-hire carriers operate 10 or fewer power units
- 23% of all semitrailer tractors are operated by carriers with <5 tractors

_Source: [AtoB — Owner Operator Statistics](https://www.atob.com/blog/owner-operator-statistics), [Rocky Transport — Trucking Industry Statistics](https://rockytransportinc.com/blog/trucking-industry-statistics-facts/)_

**Independent Freight Dispatchers:**

- Home-based, managing 3–15 owner-operator clients

- Active communities: Facebook groups with 10K–50K+ members

- Post-COVID surge continues: "a surge of new dispatch services continues to inspire the entry of new ones each day"

- Solo driver running 1–3 trucks, often with a spouse helping with admin

- Values: Systematic documentation, professional presentation to brokers, fleet-level visibility
- Tech attitude: Already uses TMS; open to specialized tools for pain points not covered by TMS
- Key fear: Losing money due to poor documentation across multiple drivers

**Segment C — The Independent Dispatcher**
- Manages 3–15 owner-operator clients, home-based, commission-driven
- Values: Load tracking, commission calculation, carrier contact management, rate confirmation storage
- Tech attitude: Uses WhatsApp + Google Sheets today; frustrated by fragmentation
- Key fear: Missing commission calculations, lost rate confirmations
- WTP: $29–79/month for a dedicated dispatcher CRM

## 4. Customer Pain Points and Unmet Needs

### Core Pain Points — Carriers

**Pain 1 — Documentation Failure (Critical)**
Carriers currently document detention with: manual paper logs, hand-written time notes, memory, or nothing. When submitting a detention claim, brokers routinely dispute timestamps. Without GPS-verified proof, carriers lose disputes or give up.
- "Carriers using manual processes often capture only around 40% of the detention they're owed" — Toro TMS
- $1.3 billion in annual driver wage loss attributable to undocumented detention — FMCSA

**Pain 2 — Collection Rate (<50%)**
94.5% of fleets charge detention fees. Fewer than 50% of those invoices are paid. The gap between charging and collecting is entirely a documentation and process problem — carriers can't prove their case.
_Source: [DAT Freight & Analytics](https://www.dat.com/blog/trucker-detention-is-still-a-15-billion-problem)_

**Pain 3 — Time Cost of Manual Claims**
Drivers report spending significant time on paperwork after detention events. For a $75 detention event, the administrative time cost often exceeds the recovery value — so carriers simply don't file.

**Pain 4 — No Dispatcher Visibility**
When a dispatcher is managing 5+ drivers across multiple loads, there is no single dashboard showing which drivers are currently detained, which facilities are chronic offenders, or which claims are outstanding.

**Pain 5 — Broker Relationship Fear**
Small carriers are reluctant to push detention claims aggressively because they fear losing future load opportunities from the broker. Professional, GPS-timestamped documentation (rather than driver word-of-mouth) shifts the dynamic from confrontational to factual.

### Core Pain Points — Independent Dispatchers

**Pain 1 — Communication Fragmentation**
"7 phone calls and 20 WhatsApp messages" per dispatch communication. Load information exists across WhatsApp, email, text, and phone — with no single source of truth.

**Pain 2 — Manual Commission Tracking**
Dispatchers manually copy driver pay from emails and spreadsheets into commission calculations, spending "a couple hours each week on this task."

**Pain 3 — No Carrier Contact Book**
No structured way to maintain broker contact databases, carrier preferences, or load history. Essential institutional knowledge lives in personal WhatsApp chats.

**Pain 4 — Rate Confirmation Storage**
Rate confirmations arrive via email or text; filing and retrieving them during disputes is cumbersome.

**Pain 5 — No Purpose-Built Tool**
Existing freight broker software (Aljex, AscendTMS, Rose Rocket) is built for freight brokers, not independent dispatchers. These tools are overpowered (and overpriced) for dispatchers who are not asset owners and not brokers.

_Source: [Trucking Lingo — Best CRM & Software Tools for Trucking and Freight Agents](https://truckinglingo.com/the-best-crm-software-tools-for-trucking-and-freight-agents)_

### Unmet Needs Summary

| Need | Current Solution | Gap |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| GPS-verified detention timestamps | None (manual logs) | App with background geofencing |
| Auto-generated detention invoice PDF | None | Template + GPS data auto-fill |
| Carrier/dispatcher shared dashboard | None | Multi-driver fleet view |
| Facility detention history | Detention Source ($5K+) | Lightweight crowdsourced database |
| Commission auto-calculation | Spreadsheet | Per-load commission tracker |
| Rate confirmation storage | Email inbox | Structured document repository |

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
- 3–15 truck carriers with independent dispatchers: estimated 80,000–120,000 companies

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1–3:**
- 2,000 accounts × 6 trucks × $29/month = $4.2M ARR (Year 3 target, ~1.5% SAM penetration)

### Strategic Recommendations

**Recommendation 1 — Lead with Detention ROI Framing**
Every marketing message should anchor to revenue recovered, not features. "$149 LTD = recover one detention event and you're paid for life." A detention pay calculator widget on the landing page showing annual leakage is essential.

**Recommendation 2 — Launch LTD on AppSumo for Validation**
AppSumo LTDs at $49–99/tier work well for niche B2B tools. Trucking is "boring business" (highest AppSumo conversion category for niche tools). Target 500–1,000 LTD sales as validation. Use LTD cohort for testimonials to drive MRR transition.

**Recommendation 3 — Target Independent Dispatchers as Distribution Channel**
Independent dispatchers manage 5–15 carriers each. Converting a dispatcher = automatic fleet adoption. Market to dispatcher Facebook groups (10K–50K members) where professional tools are actively sought. Dispatcher recommends tool to their entire carrier network.

**Recommendation 4 — Dual Product or Bundled Offering**
Carrier app (GPS detention tracking) + Dispatcher dashboard (fleet view + commission tracking) as a bundled suite. Dispatcher gets it free or heavily discounted; pays per truck under management. Aligns incentives: dispatcher wants all carriers to use it.

**Recommendation 5 — Facility Reputation Database**
Crowdsource detention history per facility (like Waze for detention). Each user contributes data; each user gets access to the database. Creates network effects and defensibility. Similar to what Detention Source charges $5K/year for, available to small carriers.

## 9. Risk Assessment and Mitigation

### Market Risk Analysis

**Risk 1 — DockClaim Adds Fleet Features** (Probability: Medium, Impact: High)
DockClaim has the core GPS geofencing infrastructure. Adding a dispatcher dashboard is a natural product extension. They're currently solo-focused and VC-backed, so there is runway for this move.
_Mitigation:_ Build dispatcher CRM deeper than a simple dashboard — include commission tracking, carrier contact book, rate confirmation storage, and dispatcher invoicing. Make the dispatcher angle the moat, not just the fleet view.

**Risk 2 — ELD Providers Enter (Samsara, Motive)** (Probability: Low, Impact: High)
Large ELD companies already have GPS data for dwell time. Adding invoice generation is one step away. However, their go-to-market is hardware-first and enterprise-focused; small carrier unit economics are unattractive to them.
_Mitigation:_ Speed to market. ELD providers are slow to launch new product lines. A focused product with strong community distribution has 18+ months of runway before they can respond.

**Risk 3 — Low Collection Rate Isn't Just a Documentation Problem** (Probability: Medium, Impact: Medium)
Some carriers don't collect because they fear relationship damage with brokers, not because of documentation gaps. Better documentation may not fully resolve the collection rate issue.
_Mitigation:_ Include broker-friendly communication templates (professional, not adversarial) and in-app tips for dispute resolution. Frame the product as "professional documentation" not "dispute tool."

**Risk 4 — Small TAM Within Small Carrier Segment** (Probability: Medium, Impact: Medium)
The 3–15 truck segment with active independent dispatchers may be smaller than estimated. Many small carriers don't use independent dispatchers — they self-dispatch.
_Mitigation:_ Design the dispatcher dashboard as optional. Solo carrier self-dispatching is equally valid as a use case. The dispatcher is a distribution multiplier, not a product requirement.

**Risk 5 — Regulatory Stall** (Probability: Low, Impact: Low)
If mandatory detention pay regulation stalls, the urgency driver weakens.

- **Shipper-side tooling:** As regulation evolves, shippers will need detention liability management tools — flip to a platform model serving both sides of the transaction
