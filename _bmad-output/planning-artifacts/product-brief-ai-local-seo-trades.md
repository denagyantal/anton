# Product Brief: AI Visibility & Lead Generation for Trades Contractors

---

## Executive Summary

**Product Name:** TradeVisible (working title) — AI search visibility and lead generation for independent trades businesses

**One-Liner:** Get your plumbing, HVAC, or electrical business recommended by ChatGPT, Gemini, and Siri — a purpose-built AI visibility tool for the 87% of independent contractors who are invisible when homeowners ask AI for a contractor.

**The Problem:** Google AI Mode, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Siri, and Alexa have fundamentally changed how homeowners find contractors. Instead of browsing 10 blue links, AI generates a summary and recommends specific contractors by name. Consumer adoption of AI for local service discovery surged from 6% to 45% in a single year — yet 87% of independent HVAC and plumbing contractors have zero AI citation share in their own metro, even with 800+ reviews and 30 years in business. National franchise networks (Roto-Rooter, ARS/Rescue Rooter, Mr. Rooter) dominate AI recommendations by default due to structural entity-strength advantages. Independent contractors have no tool to close this gap — until now.

**The Solution:** A trade-specific AI visibility platform that audits how AI assistants see a contractor's business, auto-generates the structured data and schema markup needed to get cited, manages reviews across platforms, and produces AI-optimized service page content — all from a simple dashboard built for non-technical trades business owners.

**Market Opportunity:** The US home services market is $842 billion with 1.5M+ addressable trades businesses. No dedicated "AI SEO for trades" product exists at any price point. Horizontal GEO tools start at $39-$745/month with no trade specificity. The open position is $99/month ($199 LTD) at the intersection of "local trades" + "AI search optimization" — a category that didn't exist 18 months ago and now faces 45% consumer adoption with no product to serve it.

**Launch Strategy:** Content-first launch targeting "Google AI Mode for plumbers/HVAC" keywords (currently low-competition, high-growth), free AI visibility audit as the freemium hook, followed by AppSumo LTD at $199 targeting 500+ sales. Primary trades: HVAC and plumbing (230K US businesses with validated 87% invisible stat). Expansion to electricians, roofers, landscapers post-validation.

**Confidence Level:** Very High — 87/105 signal score, upgraded from 75/105 with a major validation event: 87% of HVAC/plumbing contractors confirmed invisible in AI despite strong traditional SEO signals; consumer AI adoption 6% → 45% in one year; no direct competitor confirmed after exhaustive market research across 30+ sources.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Independent trades contractors — plumbers, HVAC technicians, electricians, roofers, landscapers — built their businesses on a simple promise: deliver great work, collect five-star reviews, and Google will send you customers. That promise is breaking.

Google AI Mode is now live, generating contractor recommendations by name rather than showing a list of links. ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Siri, and Alexa each generate their own single recommended contractor per query. A homeowner asking "Who's the best HVAC company near me?" no longer sees 10 results — they see one name. And 87% of the time, that name is not an independent contractor.

The problem is invisible to most contractors right now. They still see their Google reviews, they still rank on traditional search, and they don't yet realize that 45% of their potential customers are now starting the search on an AI platform that has never heard of them. But the referrals are quietly declining, and national franchise competitors they've never had to worry about are suddenly getting recommended by homeowners who never even opened a browser.

The contractors who will survive the AI search transition are the ones who act in the 12-18 month window before the market catches on. No tool exists today to help them do that.

### Problem Impact

**The Visibility Crisis in Numbers:**
- 87% of independent HVAC and plumbing contractors have zero AI citation share in their own metro area — even with 800+ Google reviews and decades in business
- Consumer adoption of AI for local service discovery: 6% → 45% in 12 months (7.5x growth)
- 22% of homeowners now go to ChatGPT first when they need a contractor — bypassing Google entirely
- Google clicks for contractors are down 42% year-over-year
- National franchise networks account for 19% of all HVAC/plumbing AI citations despite being a small fraction of the total market — an entity-strength advantage of roughly 8x

**The Revenue Impact for a Typical Contractor:**
A plumbing contractor with 50 service calls per month, losing 22% of new inquiry opportunities because AI sends prospects to franchise competitors instead, is losing roughly 11 jobs per month. At an average job value of $350-$600, that's $3,850-$6,600/month in lost revenue — $46,200-$79,200/year — from a problem they don't even know exists yet.

**The Education Gap:**
Most contractors don't know AI search is fundamentally different from Google search. They don't know that their 500 five-star reviews don't signal the same "trust" to ChatGPT that they signal to Google. They don't know that Siri and Alexa each recommend different contractors based on different data sources. They don't know that structured schema markup and entity-strength optimization are now as important as Google reviews once were. This education gap is an acquisition opportunity: the product that explains the problem first owns the category.

### Proposed Solution

TradeVisible is a trade-specific AI visibility platform with four interconnected capabilities:

**1. AI Visibility Audit Engine (the hook)**
Query ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode with "best [trade] in [city]" and 20+ related queries. Score the business's AI citation rate — showing exactly how often they appear vs. local competitors and national franchises. Make the invisible crisis visible and quantified in under 60 seconds.

**2. Structured Data & Schema Generator (the fix)**
Auto-generate LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, and Review schema markup pre-populated with the contractor's business data. Cover 20+ trade types (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, etc.) with trade-appropriate service taxonomies. One-click implementation via copy-paste or WordPress plugin. Entity-strength builder: guide contractors through setting up Wikipedia-equivalent trust signals (Wikidata, structured directory presence, cross-platform consistency).

**3. Review Management & AI-Response Hub (the amplifier)**
Aggregate reviews across Google, Yelp, Facebook, and HomeAdvisor into one dashboard. AI-draft responses for unanswered reviews in the contractor's voice. Automate post-job review request texts and emails. Track review velocity and sentiment trends that directly correlate with AI citation improvements.

**4. AI-Optimized Content Briefs (the moat)**
Generate trade-specific service page content optimized for AI citation signals — structured answers to "what is [service]," "how much does [service] cost," "how do I know if I need [service]" — the exact questions homeowners ask AI. Trade-specific FAQ templates for 20+ service types. Scheduled content suggestions tied to seasonal demand patterns.

**The Core Loop:** Audit reveals the visibility gap → Fix with schema + entity signals → Monitor AI citations improving → Review automation amplifies signals → Content briefs sustain momentum → Repeat audit shows progress.

### Key Differentiators

**1. Trades-Specific — Not Another Generic Tool**
Every schema template, content brief, FAQ structure, and audit query is built for trades businesses. A plumber gets plumbing service taxonomies, seasonal maintenance question sets, and cost-range structured data that matches how homeowners ask AI about plumbing. No horizontal GEO tool offers this depth.

**2. AI Citation Score Across All Platforms**
The only SMB-priced tool that shows a contractor's visibility score in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Siri, and Google AI Mode separately. Homeowners ask different AI platforms and get different recommendations — contractors need to know where they stand on each one. No competitor does this below $499/month.

**3. The "Franchise Gap" Benchmark**
Show how an independent contractor's AI citation share compares to local franchise competitors (Roto-Rooter, Mr. Rooter, ARS). This benchmark is emotionally powerful ("you have 200 reviews, they have 50, but AI recommends them 12x more often — here's why and here's how to close the gap"). No other tool surfaces this comparison.

**4. One-Click Schema Implementation**
Generating schema markup is developer-level work. No contractor can do it themselves, and agencies charge $200+ per page to implement it. TradeVisible generates correct, validated, trade-specific schema and delivers it as a WordPress plugin install or a two-line copy-paste for any website. The technical abstraction is total.

**5. First-Mover Content Moat**
"Google AI Mode for plumbers," "how to show up on ChatGPT as an HVAC company," "why Siri recommends my competitor" — these are real searches with no current content authority. The product that publishes definitive guides on these keywords first owns the category narrative for the window before incumbents respond.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP is built around the core audit → fix → monitor loop for HVAC and plumbing contractors (the validated-crisis segment). Every feature below is necessary to prove the core value proposition; nothing is optional for the loop to work.

**Feature 1: AI Visibility Audit Engine (The Hook — Freemium)**

The audit is both the acquisition tool and the core product feature. It must work as a standalone freemium tool that captures leads before paywall.

- Input: Business name + city (or Google Business Profile URL)
- Queries: 100+ AI queries per month across ChatGPT API, Gemini API, Perplexity API ("best [trade] in [city]," "cheapest [trade] near [city]," "emergency [trade] [city] reviews," etc.)
- Output: AI Citation Share score (0-100%) per platform, overall score, top 5 cited competitors in their market, estimated citation rank position
- Freemium gate: Full report (platform breakdown + gap analysis + competitor comparison) requires email → triggers drip sequence
- Paid unlock: Monthly monitoring, citation trend tracking, competitor alerts
- Success criteria: Audit completes in < 60 seconds for any US city; citation scores are accurate within ±5% of manual spot-checks

**Feature 2: Schema Markup Generator (The Fix)**

The highest-impact, lowest-effort citation signal improvement. Generates correct, trade-specific, validated schema that any contractor (or their nephew) can implement.

- Trade types covered in MVP: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping (5 core trades)
- Schema types generated: LocalBusiness, Service (with trade-appropriate serviceType values), FAQPage, Review aggregate
- Output format: Copy-pasteable JSON-LD code block + WordPress plugin (auto-installs schema with one click)
- Validation: Run through Schema.org validator and Google Rich Results Test before presenting to user
- Auto-population: Pre-fills from GBP data (business name, address, phone, categories, hours, review aggregate)
- Per-service pages: Generate service-specific schema for "plumbing services," "emergency plumbing," "drain cleaning" sub-pages
- Success criteria: 90% of generated schemas pass Google Rich Results Test on first implementation; contractors can implement without developer help

**Feature 3: Google Business Profile Optimization Wizard (The Foundation)**

Google AI Mode pulls directly from GBP signals. An incomplete or misconfigured GBP is the #1 reason AI ignores an otherwise strong local business.

- Integration: Google Business Profile API (read + write)
- Completeness scoring: Score the GBP across 25 elements (primary/secondary categories, service menu, service areas, hours, photos by type, Q&A, posts frequency, attributes)
- Priority queue: Rank the missing elements by their AI citation impact (categories > service menu > photos > Q&A > posts)
- Guided wizard: Step-by-step instructions for each missing element, written in non-technical language for the contractor
- Category optimization: Trade-specific recommended primary + secondary categories with AI citation correlation data
- Q&A seed generator: Generate 10 trade-appropriate Q&A pairs to populate the GBP Q&A section (a direct AI citation signal)
- Success criteria: Average GBP completeness score moves from 65% to 90%+ for users who complete the wizard; wizard completion rate ≥ 70%

**Feature 4: Review Management Dashboard (The Amplifier)**

Review velocity and response rate are AI citation signals. The dashboard centralizes multi-platform review management and automates review requests post-job.

- Platforms aggregated: Google, Yelp, Facebook (MVP); HomeAdvisor, Angi (post-MVP)
- Unanswered review queue: All unanswered reviews surfaced with AI-drafted responses in the contractor's voice (editable before posting)
- Review request automation: Contractor provides customer phone number/email post-job → system sends branded review request text or email with one-tap Google review link
- Review velocity tracking: Graph showing review count per month — leading indicator of citation improvement
- Sentiment analysis: Flag negative reviews for priority response; track sentiment trend over time
- Success criteria: 85%+ of users respond to reviews within 48 hours (vs. 30-40% industry baseline); review request open rate > 60%

**Feature 5: AI-Optimized Content Briefs (The Moat)**

The content that AI cites most often answers specific questions homeowners ask. Trade-specific FAQ content, structured as Q&A with cost ranges and local modifiers, is the highest-citation-value content type for local trades businesses.

- Content brief generator: Input trade type + service + city → get a complete content brief for a service page or FAQ page, including:
  - H1 suggestion with local keyword
  - 10-15 FAQ pairs in Q&A format (optimized for FAQPage schema)
  - Recommended cost ranges for the service in that metro
  - Seasonal timing notes ("best time to schedule X in Phoenix")
  - Entity signals to include (trade associations, certifications, license numbers)
- Template library: Pre-built FAQ sets for the 50 most common HVAC and plumbing service queries
- Implementation guidance: Instructions for how to add the content to WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, or static sites
- Success criteria: 60%+ of users generate at least 3 content briefs within first 30 days; brief quality rated 4+ out of 5 by users in post-generation survey

**Feature 6: Monthly Citation Score Dashboard (The Retention Anchor)**

The monthly citation score is the core retention mechanic. Contractors who see improvement stay. The dashboard makes progress visible and creates a habit of checking.

- Monthly automated audit reruns (100+ queries across all platforms)
- Citation trend chart: Score over time, per platform, vs. 3 top local competitors
- Progress attribution: Shows which actions drove citation improvement ("Schema implementation: +3% citation share")
- Alert emails: "Your citation score improved by X% this month" / "Competitor alert: [Name] is now being cited more often than you in [City]"
- Actionable next steps: Based on current score, recommends the 3 highest-impact next actions
- Success criteria: 80%+ of paid users view their monthly citation score report; users who see improvement have < 2% monthly churn

## Strategic Notes and Risks

### Go-to-Market Priority

1. **Content-first, not ads-first.** "Google AI Mode for plumbers," "why isn't my HVAC company on ChatGPT," and "how to get recommended by AI as a contractor" are real search queries with near-zero current content competition. The first 200 audit completions come from content, not advertising. Paid search is for after MRR is established.

2. **Free audit as the top-of-funnel.** The audit is the product's killer hook. Every marketing channel drives to the free audit — not to a pricing page. A contractor who sees their 0% AI citation score next to their 4.9-star Google rating is pre-sold. The audit makes the invisible crisis concrete and immediate.

3. **LTD as community-building, not just revenue.** AppSumo LTD customers become the testimonial bank, the beta feedback community, and the word-of-mouth engine in trade communities. The $99,500 in LTD revenue is secondary to the 500 motivated early adopters who will post their results in trades Facebook groups.

4. **Trades community infiltration as distribution.** Facebook groups for HVAC owners, plumbing contractors, and electrical contractors have 50K-200K members. A genuine, results-first presence in these communities (not spam) generates more qualified signups per hour spent than any paid channel at this stage.

### Critical Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| BrightLocal ships AI Mode features | High (6-12 months) | High | Trade-specific depth (schema templates, franchise gap analysis, trade content) is 12+ months to copy; lock in annual contracts and community brand before they move |
| Google AI Mode behavior changes | Medium (ongoing) | High | Multi-platform monitoring (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) means single-platform changes don't destroy value; schema and entity-strength signals are platform-agnostic |
| Customer education cost too high | High (early) | Medium | Lead with audit data, not feature descriptions; "your AI citation share is 0%" requires no education; number does the explaining |
| Citation improvement too slow for retention | Medium | Very High | "Get cited in 30 days" onboarding SLA; sequence schema first (fastest AI impact); over-communicate early wins via email alerts |
| Google provides native AI visibility in GBP | Low (12-24 months) | High | Tool value extends to ChatGPT/Perplexity/Siri — channels Google cannot control; content and review features retain value regardless |
| Venture-backed GEO startup targets trades | Low (6-12 months) | Medium | First-mover community brand + content authority + LTD customer base as moat; 500 vocal LTD customers are better defensibility than 2 months of head start |
| API costs at scale (OpenAI/Gemini/Perplexity) | Medium | Medium | Batch queries during off-peak hours; cache results for 30-day windows; target < $5/location/month in API costs at scale |

### Competitive Moat Building

The "first trade-specific GEO tool" positioning is the moat — but only if it's seized in the next 12 months. The combination of:
- Trade-specific schema library (20+ trades × 50+ service types = 1,000+ schema templates)
- Historical citation score data (contractors can see their trend going back to signup)
- Community brand in HVAC/plumbing/electrical Facebook groups and subreddits
- Trade press coverage (PM Magazine, Contractor Magazine, ACHR News)
- AppSumo review base (500 public reviews from verified trades users)

...creates a defensible position that a horizontal GEO tool pivoting to trades cannot replicate in under 18 months, and that an incumbent like BrightLocal would need a full product vertical to match.
