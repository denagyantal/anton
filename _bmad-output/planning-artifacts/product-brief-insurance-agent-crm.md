# Product Brief: Insurance Agent CRM & Policy Tracker

---

## Executive Summary

Independent insurance agents are trapped. They pay $350–$600/month for legacy platforms they openly despise — tools that force them to juggle 5–15 carrier portals daily, spend 57% of their day on admin instead of selling, and manually reconcile commissions from a dozen carriers every month using spreadsheets no one else can read.

The market has 39,000 independent P&C agencies in the US, writing 61.5% of all P&C premiums — and the dominant software, EZLynx, carries a 3.3/5 customer service rating with agents describing themselves as "locked into EZLynx hell." Enterprise alternatives like Applied Epic cost $500–$5,000/month with $10–25K setup fees. Nothing affordable and insurance-native exists.

**AgentPad** (working name) is a flat-fee, insurance-native CRM at $79/month for up to 5 agents. It combines the four workflows small agencies actually spend time on — renewal dashboard with automated outreach, multi-carrier commission tracking, ACORD form auto-fill, and client/policy management — into a single, modern product at 85–90% less than the incumbents.

The opportunity is validated by 12+ months of signal data, a confirmed pricing dead zone at $49–99/month flat, zero insurance-specific LTD products on AppSumo, and a November 2024 Momentum AMP rebrand that has created active churn among the incumbent's existing users. The window is open now.

**Recommended launch path:** $99 lifetime deal on AppSumo to seed 300–500 paying customers and reviews, then transition to $79/month MRR. Conservative 5% penetration of the 39,000-agency market = $1.8M ARR.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Independent insurance agents — typically running 1–5 person P&C shops — spend the majority of their working day on manual administrative work rather than selling or serving clients. The tools available to them are either:

1. **Enterprise AMS platforms** (Applied Epic, AMS360, EZLynx): $150–400+/user/month, designed for 50-person brokerages, with steep learning curves, poor customer support, and legacy UIs that haven't meaningfully changed since 2008.
2. **Generic CRMs** (HubSpot, Zoho, Bigin): Cheap but completely unaware of insurance concepts — they don't understand policies, carriers, ACORD forms, or renewal cycles.
3. **Sales-overlay CRMs** (AgencyZoom, BetterAgency): CRM on top of an existing AMS, adding another $149/month to an already expensive stack with no policy management.

The result: small agencies cobble together 3–4 disconnected tools, revert to spreadsheets for critical workflows, and pay $350–$600/month for software they openly describe as "handcuffs."

### Problem Impact

The documented consequences of this situation are severe and measurable:

- **57% of insurance professionals** spend more than half their day on administrative tasks (Vertafore 2024 survey, ~2,000 respondents)
- A typical 8-person agency managing 1,200 policies spends **20 hours/week** on manual renewal preparation — automation reduces this to 3 hours
- **Commission reconciliation** from 5–10 carriers monthly is entirely manual; a single person with a spreadsheet that no one else understands is the norm
- Agents log into **5–15 separate carrier portals daily** to service a single client
- "10–15 hours/week in admin tasks" is a direct quote from Reddit threads; confirmed across multiple independent sources
- Florida agents frustrated with EZLynx formed a dedicated **Facebook complaint group**; threads on Insurance Forums describe "EZLynx hell" as a shared experience

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Solution | Price (3 users) | Core Problem |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Applied Epic | $1,500–$15,000+/mo | Enterprise product; $10–25K setup; requires consultant |
| AMS360 | $450–$600/mo | Outdated, rigid, poor support, per-user pricing |

**The gap:** No flat-fee, insurance-native product under $99/month exists that combines renewal automation + commission tracking + ACORD form auto-fill + policy management for a 3–5 user agency. This is confirmed, not assumed.

### Proposed Solution

**AgentPad** is a lightweight, insurance-native CRM built specifically for independent P&C agencies with 1–10 agents. It is cloud-based, modern, and designed to replace the spreadsheets and disconnected tools agents currently use — not to compete with full enterprise AMS platforms.

**The core product delivers four insurance-specific workflows:**

1. **Renewal Dashboard** — A visual, date-driven policy renewal pipeline. See every policy expiring in the next 90/60/30 days. Automated email and SMS outreach sequences trigger at configurable intervals. Agents stop chasing renewals manually and start managing exceptions.

2. **Multi-Carrier Commission Tracking** — Upload carrier commission statements (CSV, PDF, Excel). Match to policies. Flag discrepancies and missing payments. Eliminate the single-person spreadsheet dependency. Track expected vs. received at the carrier and policy level.

3. **ACORD Form Auto-Fill** — Pull client and policy data from the system to pre-populate ACORD forms. Generate, save, and send forms without re-keying data. This is a meaningful differentiator vs. generic CRMs and a direct competitor to the incumbent's most-touted feature.

4. **Client & Policy Management** — An insurance-native data model. Not contacts and deals — clients, policies, carriers, effective dates, premiums, and coverage types. The difference between a product that "understands insurance" and one that doesn't.

**Pricing:** $49/month (Solo), $79/month (up to 5 agents), $129/month (up to 15 agents). LTD at $99 on AppSumo for launch acquisition.

**The positioning:** "All the features a small agency actually uses — renewal automation, commission tracking, ACORD forms, client management — without the enterprise complexity and the $500/month price tag."

### Key Differentiators

1. **Flat-fee pricing** — No per-user fees for the first 5 seats. A 3-agent agency pays $79/month vs. $450–600/month on EZLynx. The math sells itself.

2. **Insurance-native data model** — Built around the policy lifecycle (lead → quote → bind → service → renew), not generic CRM objects. Understands carriers, ACORD form types, premium amounts, and renewal cycles out of the box.

3. **Renewal automation as the core workflow** — Not a feature, the center of the product. The renewal dashboard is the first thing agents see. This directly attacks the #1 documented pain point from 2,000-respondent industry surveys.

4. **Commission reconciliation for small agencies** — No competitor in the affordable tier offers multi-carrier commission statement matching. The closest tools (Core Commissions, Commission Tracker) are standalone products at additional cost.

5. **ACORD form library** — Auto-populating forms from system data removes a major manual step. HawkSoft and Momentum charge $300–400/month to include this. AgentPad includes it at $79/month.

6. **Modern UX** — The incumbents run on 2008-era interfaces. Small agency agents are increasingly mobile-first; a product that looks like 2026 will win on aesthetic alone against legacy competitors.

7. **First-mover AppSumo presence** — Zero insurance-specific AMS/CRM products on AppSumo. The first credible entrant captures first-mover reviews, Sumo-ling word-of-mouth, and a permanent category anchor.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP is intentionally scoped to the four workflows that directly address the two highest-documented pain points: renewal management and commission reconciliation. Everything else is post-MVP.

#### Feature 1: Renewal Dashboard (Priority: Critical)

- **Policy expiration timeline view** — Visual pipeline of all policies expiring in next 90/60/30 days, sortable by carrier, agent, and policy type
- **Automated outreach sequences** — Configurable email (and SMS via integration) sequences triggered at 90, 60, 30, 14 days before expiration
- **Renewal status tracking** — Mark policies as: Renewed, Not Renewing, In Progress, Awaiting Client Response
- **Basic email templates** — 3–5 pre-built insurance-appropriate renewal outreach templates; editable
- **Manual override** — Suppress automation for specific policies or clients

**Acceptance criteria:** An agent can set up and activate renewal automation for their full book in under 30 minutes. Automated emails fire without manual intervention. Dashboard accurately reflects policy status.

#### Feature 2: Client & Policy Management (Priority: Critical)

- **Insurance-native data model:** Client → Policies (multiple per client) → Policy fields: carrier, policy number, type (auto/home/commercial/etc.), effective date, expiration date, premium, coverage summary
- **CSV import** — Import from EZLynx, AMS360 exports (standard field mapping + custom column mapping)
- **Client communication log** — Manual log of calls, emails, notes per client/policy
- **Policy status lifecycle** — Active, Expired, Cancelled, Non-Renewed, Pending
- **Basic search and filter** — Search by client name, carrier, policy type, expiration date range

**Acceptance criteria:** An agent can import their existing book from EZLynx in under 60 minutes. Full client + policy history is searchable. No data lost in migration.

#### Feature 3: Commission Tracking (Priority: High)

- **Statement upload** — Accept CSV, PDF (text-extractable), and Excel commission statements from carriers
- **Automated matching** — Match statement line items to policies by policy number; flag unmatched items for manual review
- **Expected vs. received tracking** — Set expected commission rate per carrier/policy type; flag when received deviates by >$X or >X%
- **Monthly reconciliation report** — PDF export of reconciled/unreconciled items for the month
- **Carrier profiles** — Store commission rate schedules per carrier (configurable by user)

**Acceptance criteria:** An agent can upload a carrier commission statement and get a reconciled/unreconciled breakdown within 5 minutes. Discrepancies are clearly flagged with policy-level detail.

#### Feature 4: ACORD Form Auto-Fill (Priority: High)

- **ACORD form library** — Initial launch: top 15–20 most-used personal and commercial lines ACORD forms (ACORD 125, 126, 130, 140, 25, 75, 80, 90, and key personal lines equivalents)
- **Auto-population** — Pull client and policy data from system to pre-fill form fields; editable before save/send
- **PDF generation** — Generate filled PDF for download, print, or email
- **Form history** — Track which forms were generated per policy; timestamp and agent

**Acceptance criteria:** An agent can select a policy, choose an ACORD form, review the auto-filled PDF, and download or email it in under 3 minutes. Data accuracy ≥95% (i.e., ≤5% of fields require manual correction for a fully-populated policy record).

#### Feature 5: Multi-User & Agency Setup (Priority: High)

- **Flat-fee team seats** — Solo (1 user), Agency (up to 5), Team (up to 15)
- **Role-based access** — Principal, Producer, CSR roles with appropriate permissions
- **Agency branding** — Logo and agency name on generated PDFs and outreach emails
- **Basic audit trail** — Log of who created/modified policy records and forms

#### Feature 6: Billing & Subscription (Priority: Critical)

- **Stripe integration** — Monthly and annual billing
- **LTD code redemption** — AppSumo one-time purchase code support
- **Plan management** — Self-serve upgrade/downgrade
- **Cancellation and data export** — Agents can export all data as CSV on cancel; no lock-in
