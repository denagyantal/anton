# Product Brief: GuardPost — Security Guard Scheduling & Compliance Platform

---

## Executive Summary

Small security guard agencies (10–75 guards) are the forgotten segment of a $47.8B US industry. They face some of the most demanding workforce management challenges in any sector — 24/7 post coverage requirements, high-turnover staff, overnight callout replacements, and contractual compliance documentation — yet they have no affordable, purpose-built software to manage it all.

Enterprise tools like TrackTik and Silvertrac start at $249–$2,000+/month and target agencies with 200+ guards. Generic workforce apps like Connecteam handle scheduling at $29/month but completely lack the four workflows that are non-negotiable for security operations: digital post orders, QR patrol checkpoint verification, mobile incident reports, and overtime compliance alerts. The result: 40,000+ small agencies cobble together Connecteam for scheduling, paper binders for post orders, phone calls for callout replacement, and paper forms for incident documentation — a fragmented stack that creates compliance exposure and operational chaos.

**GuardPost** fills this gap with a purpose-built scheduling and compliance platform priced at $99/month (up to 25 guards) and $149/month (up to 75 guards). The core product combines shift scheduling with open-shift automation, digital post orders with guard acknowledgment, and mobile incident reporting — the three features that eliminate the most acute pain points for owner-operators.

The market opportunity is validated: 112,124 US security services businesses, growing at 5.6% annually; the security guard management software segment is growing at 11.8–14.8% CAGR. No purpose-built software has ever appeared on AppSumo, creating a first-mover opportunity for LTD launch. Once post orders are digitized, agencies won't migrate — creating strong defensibility.

**Target outcome:** $10K MRR within 12 months through AppSumo LTD launch (months 1–3), followed by monthly SaaS subscriptions. Clear path to $30K MRR through upmarket expansion to 75–200 guard agencies in year 2.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small security guard agencies (10–75 guards, operating 3–20 client sites) have no purpose-built, affordable software solution that combines shift scheduling, digital post orders, guard patrol verification, and incident documentation. They are caught in an impossible gap: enterprise platforms are priced and scoped for 200+ guard operations, and generic workforce tools lack the security-specific workflows that are operationally and contractually essential.

This forces small agencies into a fragmented, liability-creating paper-and-phone stack:
- **Scheduling:** Connecteam or spreadsheets (callout replacement done by manually calling guards)
- **Post orders:** Paper binders at each site (frequently outdated, no proof guards read them)
- **Patrol verification:** Honor system or paper log sheets (no proof of service for client billing)
- **Incident reports:** Paper forms (no timestamp, no photo, no supervisor notification)
- **Overtime tracking:** Discovered at payroll (no proactive alerts before threshold breach)

### Problem Impact

The operational and financial consequences of this fragmented stack are severe and concrete:

**Operational impact:**
- When a guard calls in sick at 11 PM, the owner manually calls 8–10 guards to fill the post — a 45-minute fire drill that happens weekly
- A "no-show" is discovered when the client calls asking where their guard is — a critical relationship-damaging event
- Overtime surprises surface at payroll, not before scheduling — agencies pay 15–25% more in labor costs than necessary
- Guards show up at new sites without reading the site-specific instructions because the paper binder was at the office

**Compliance and liability impact:**
- Paper post orders provide no proof that guards were briefed on client procedures before their shift
- Paper incident logs have no timestamp integrity and can be challenged in litigation
- No QR patrol verification means agencies cannot prove guards completed their rounds to client auditors
- State overtime regulations (especially CA, NY, IL) expose agencies to wage claims when OT isn't tracked in real time

**Business development impact:**
- Agencies pitching new contracts against larger competitors cannot demonstrate professional-grade documentation
- Client-facing patrol reports require hours of manual compilation each month
- Without digital post orders, onboarding a new client site takes days, not hours

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Enterprise tools (TrackTik, Trackforce Valiant — $500–2,000+/month):**
- Priced for 200+ guard operations; effective per-guard cost is 5–10x what small agencies can absorb
- Require dedicated implementation time and administrative staff; owner-operators can't manage it
- Support and onboarding not designed for 10-person teams
- BBB complaints center on pricing opacity and poor small-account support

**Mid-market tools (Silvertrac $249+/mo, Guard Owl $300+/mo):**
- Silvertrac: $249/month minimum for 10 devices — too expensive for 10–25 guard agencies
- Guard Owl: Closest competitor at $300+/month — priced 3x above the market sweet spot
- Neither offers a self-service, month-to-month plan that owner-operators can trial without commitment

**Generic workforce tools (Connecteam $29/mo, When I Work $2.50+/user/mo):**
- Most widely used by small security agencies for scheduling — but these tools were designed for retail and hospitality
- No post order workflow (PDF upload, per-site versions, guard acknowledgment before clock-in)
- No patrol checkpoint verification (QR/NFC scan-based proof of rounds)
- No security incident report format (timestamped, photo-attached, supervisor-notified)
- Security teams using Connecteam still need paper for 3 of the 4 core workflows

**The result:** A $30–249/month purpose-built security software tier is completely unoccupied. No product has ever addressed this gap.

### Proposed Solution

GuardPost is a mobile-first scheduling and compliance platform purpose-built for small security agencies (10–75 guards). It replaces the paper-and-phone stack with four integrated workflows:

1. **Shift scheduling with open-shift automation** — create schedules, broadcast open shifts to qualified available guards (self-assign from app), supervisor gets coverage confirmation without manual calls

2. **Digital post orders with clock-in acknowledgment** — per-site PDF instructions; guards must confirm they've read the current post order before the app unlocks clock-in; every acknowledgment is timestamped and auditable

3. **Mobile incident reports** — guards submit photo + description + GPS location from the field; supervisor receives instant push notification; report is automatically formatted for client delivery

4. **Overtime threshold alerts** — before scheduling a shift that would push a guard past their OT threshold, the system flags the conflict and suggests an alternative

Phase 2 adds: **QR/NFC patrol checkpoint verification** — guards scan checkpoints during rounds; system auto-generates a client-ready patrol report after each shift.

Pricing: $99/month (up to 25 guards) | $149/month (up to 75 guards) | No long-term contracts

### Key Differentiators

1. **The only purpose-built security platform under $249/month** — occupies the unclaimed price tier between Connecteam's generic $29/month and Silvertrac's $249/month floor

2. **Digital post orders as a lock-in mechanism** — once agencies digitize their site-specific SOPs (each site has unique emergency contacts, access procedures, patrol requirements), switching to another tool means re-digitizing everything; creates extremely high switching costs

3. **Open-shift pool automation** — eliminates the #1 operational pain point (manual callout replacement) that no affordable tool currently addresses

4. **Mobile-first UX designed for the guard workforce** — average guard age 43, 42% hold a high school diploma; app must work on first use without training overhead; this is a product design bet that purpose-built tools can execute on and generic tools haven't bothered with

5. **AppSumo first-mover** — no security guard software has ever appeared on AppSumo; first listing creates category-defining awareness with zero paid acquisition cost

6. **"Looks professional to clients" narrative** — patrol reports, digital incident logs, and post order acknowledgment records let small agencies compete for contracts against larger, more established agencies; this is a direct revenue enabler, not just an efficiency tool

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### Segment 1: Owner-Operator ("The Solo Dispatcher")
**Profile:** Marcus runs a 15-guard security company he started after 12 years with a large agency. He handles scheduling, client relations, payroll, and dispatch personally. He wakes up to text messages about shift changes and goes to sleep doing tomorrow's schedule in his head.

**Context:** 10–25 guards, 3–8 client sites, 0–1 additional administrators. Uses Connecteam for scheduling, paper binders at each site, personal phone for callout replacement. Revenue: $350K–$1.2M/year. Makes software decisions alone; budget threshold: $100/month without hesitation if ROI is clear.

**Current pain experience:**
- Last Tuesday: guard called in sick at 10:45 PM for the 6 AM hospital lobby post; Marcus spent 40 minutes calling people before finding coverage
- His biggest client asked him to provide an incident report from 3 weeks ago; he found a partial paper form with no timestamp
- He accidentally scheduled a guard who was already at 36 hours for a 10-hour shift; discovered it at payroll

**Success vision:** "I want to go to sleep without worrying about whether the overnight posts are covered. If a guard calls out, I want the app to handle it."

**Value drivers:** Time savings (callout replacement automation), OT cost reduction (alerts before breach), peace of mind (automatic post coverage confirmation)

**Willingness to pay:** $99/month; decision made by owner alone within 1–2 weeks of trigger event

**Willingness to pay:** $149/month; 3–6 week evaluation cycle, free trial required

### Secondary Users

**Guards (Mobile App Users):** 10–75 guards per agency. Average age 43. Key interactions: clock in/out, acknowledge post orders before shift starts, submit incident reports from the field, self-assign to open shifts. Must require zero training — if guards need a tutorial, adoption fails and the owner churns. Success is measured by >80% of guards using the app for clock-in within 30 days of agency signup.

**Clients (Read-Only Portal, Phase 2):** Site managers at client locations who periodically receive patrol reports and incident summaries. Not active daily users but important for agency retention — agencies that deliver professional patrol reports to clients win contract renewals. Out of scope for MVP; added in Phase 2 as client-facing PDF delivery, Phase 3 as live portal access.

**Payroll Processors (Integration, Phase 3):** Agency staff using QuickBooks, Gusto, or ADP for payroll. Clock-in/out data from GuardPost needs to flow to payroll. Out of scope for MVP; handled via CSV export initially, then QuickBooks/Gusto integration in Phase 3.

### User Journey

#### Owner-Operator (Primary Journey)

**Discovery:** Trigger event (guard no-show discovered by client, OT shock at payroll, failed audit by client). Google search "security guard scheduling software small company" or Reddit post on r/securityguards asking for recommendations. Finds GuardPost via AppSumo listing or comparison article.

**Consideration:** Pricing page immediately visible ($99/month, no annual contract required). Reads 10–15 Capterra/G2 reviews filtered to similar-sized agencies. Watches a 3-minute demo video showing open-shift notification and post order acknowledgment. Signs up for 30-day free trial.

**Onboarding (Days 1–3):** Creates account, imports guard roster from CSV, creates first client site, uploads post order PDF. Schedules first week. Sends guard app invitation link via SMS. First guard clocks in and acknowledges post order — owner sees the acknowledgment timestamp in dashboard.

**Aha Moment (Week 1–2):** First callout handled without manual phone calls. Open-shift notification sent to available guards; one self-assigns within 15 minutes. Owner receives confirmation, goes back to sleep.

**Value Confirmation (Days 30–60):** Payroll runs 12% below previous month due to OT alerts. Client asks for an incident report; owner pulls it up in the app and emails the formatted PDF in 2 minutes. Renews subscription.

**Expansion (Month 3–6):** Adds QR patrol checkpoint tracking ($19/month add-on). Sends client their first auto-generated patrol report. Uses patrol report as a selling point when pitching a new contract.

**Advocacy (Month 6+):** Mentions GuardPost in r/securityguards thread asking for software recommendations. Refers a peer agency owner (referral credit applied).

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

**Guard adoption rate:** >80% of guards at an agency actively using the mobile app for clock-in within 30 days of that agency signing up. This is the single most critical early indicator — if guards don't adopt the app, the entire product fails for the owner.

**Callout replacement time:** Agencies using open-shift pool automation reduce average callout fill time from 40+ minutes (manual) to <15 minutes (automated notification + self-assign). Target: 60% of agencies report this improvement within 60 days.

**Post order compliance:** >95% of shifts have a guard post order acknowledgment before clock-in within 60 days of agency setup. This is the compliance audit trail metric that converts skeptics into evangelists.

**OT cost reduction:** Agencies using overtime alerts reduce overtime pay by 15–20% in the first full payroll cycle after adoption. Track and publish as a case study metric.

**Incident report completeness:** >90% of incident reports include photo + timestamp + location (vs. paper forms where 60% are incomplete). Guards submitting from mobile should achieve this automatically.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP is scoped to the three features that directly address the top three pain points identified in research. Every other feature, no matter how compelling, is deferred until these three are working flawlessly.

**Feature 1: Shift Scheduling with Open-Shift Automation**
- Owner/dispatcher creates weekly schedule by assigning guards to sites and shifts
- Drag-and-drop schedule builder with conflict detection (guard already scheduled, would exceed OT threshold)
- Overtime threshold alert: flag before booking a shift that would push a guard past their weekly limit (configurable: state-specific OT rules)
- Open-shift notification: when a shift is uncovered or a guard calls out, system broadcasts to qualified available guards via push notification + SMS
- Guards self-assign from mobile app; supervisor receives confirmation
- Guard schedule view: mobile-first view of upcoming shifts with site address and post order link
- Export schedule to PDF for posting (for guards without smartphones — edge case)

**Feature 2: Digital Post Orders with Clock-In Acknowledgment**
- Per-site post order management: upload PDF (or create from template) for each client site
- Version control: when a post order is updated, previous version archived, new version active
- Guard acknowledgment gate: app requires guard to scroll through and confirm post order before unlocking clock-in for that site
- Acknowledgment log: timestamped record of which guard acknowledged which version of which post order
- Supervisor dashboard: view acknowledgment status for all guards scheduled for upcoming shifts
- Export acknowledgment log as PDF (for client audits)

**Feature 3: Mobile Incident Reports**
- Guard-facing: one-tap "Report Incident" from mobile; captures photo (required), incident type (dropdown), description, GPS location, timestamp
- All fields auto-populated where possible (guard identity, site, shift)
- Supervisor notification: instant push notification + email when incident submitted
- Incident log: searchable by date, site, guard, incident type
- Export incident report as formatted PDF (for client delivery or insurance documentation)

**Supporting infrastructure:**
- Guard mobile app (iOS + Android via React Native or Flutter)
- Owner/supervisor web dashboard + mobile app
- SMS + push notifications for open-shift pool (Twilio)
- PDF storage for post orders and incident reports (Cloudflare R2 / AWS S3)
- Account management: add/remove guards, configure sites, set OT thresholds
- Basic billing: Stripe integration for $99/$149/month plans + $19/month GPS add-on (GPS tracking itself is Phase 2, but billing infrastructure is in from day 1)

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following features are explicitly deferred. They are important, but deferring them allows the MVP to ship in 6–8 weeks and validate product-market fit before adding complexity.

**Deferred to Phase 2 (Months 4–6):**
- QR/NFC patrol checkpoint scanning — requires physical QR sticker kits at each site, checkpoint configuration, and auto-generated patrol report assembly; complex but high-value; introduced as the $19/month GPS add-on
- Client-facing patrol reports — PDF report auto-generated and emailed to client contact after each shift; requires checkpoint data from Phase 2
- Basic client portal — read-only access for client site managers to view incident reports and patrol history

**Deferred to Phase 3 (Months 9–18):**
- Payroll integration (QuickBooks, Gusto) — initially handle via CSV export from clock-in/out data
- Guard certification tracking — state license expiration alerts per guard
- Multi-language guard interface — English-only for MVP
- Advanced analytics dashboard — operational reporting, client-level summaries
- White-label offering — for franchise security companies
- Biometric clock-in (face recognition) — future differentiator

**Explicitly not building:**
- Desktop-only workflows — entire product must be mobile-first; desktop is secondary
- Video verification of guard tours — AI/CCTV territory; out of scope for MVP and Phase 2
- Alarm monitoring integration — adjacent market, not scheduling and compliance
- Guard marketplace / staffing network — different business model

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when these gates are passed:

**Gate 1 — Adoption (30 days post-launch):** At least 10 agencies have signed up, imported their guard roster, and have >70% of guards actively clocking in via the mobile app.

**Gate 2 — Problem validation (60 days):** At least 5 agencies report a specific, documented improvement: faster callout replacement, successful client audit using digital post order acknowledgment log, or an incident report delivered to a client within minutes of the event.

**Gate 3 — Revenue validation (90 days):** AppSumo LTD campaign achieves 200+ sales. At least 20 LTD customers have converted to paid GPS add-on ($19/month) — confirming willingness to pay for ongoing monthly costs.

**Gate 4 — Retention (6 months):** Monthly churn is <5% among agencies that completed onboarding (guard roster imported, at least one site configured). If churn is >8%, pause marketing and investigate before scaling.

**Go/no-go decision:** If Gates 1–3 are passed, proceed to Phase 2 (QR patrol checkpoints). If Gate 2 fails (no specific documented improvements), run 5 user interviews before making any feature decisions. Gate 4 is a scaling gate — below 5% churn, aggressively invest in growth; above 8%, pause and investigate.

### Future Vision

**Phase 2: Proof of Service (Months 4–6)**
The next phase makes GuardPost the tool that agencies use to win and retain contracts by delivering professional proof-of-service documentation that small agencies couldn't produce before.

- QR/NFC patrol checkpoint system: print stickers (or use NFC tags) at each checkpoint location on client site; guards scan on rounds; app records time, guard identity, checkpoint ID
- Auto-generated patrol report: after each shift ends, system assembles a PDF showing all checkpoint scans with timestamps, any gaps, and a summary — automatically emailed to client contact
- Client portal (read-only): site managers can log in to view real-time patrol status, incident history, and scheduled guard information
- Advanced post order: video instructions per site (guard watches a 2-minute video and confirms), not just PDF

**Phase 3: Operational Excellence (Months 9–18)**
Transform GuardPost from a scheduling/compliance tool into the operating system for a small security agency.

- Payroll integration: sync clock-in/out data directly to QuickBooks, Gusto, or ADP; eliminate manual timesheet processing
- Guard certification management: upload state guard license, CPR cert, etc.; automatic alerts 30/60 days before expiration; prevent scheduling uncertified guards to sites that require specific certifications
- Multi-client dashboard: larger agencies (50–200 guards) with many client sites need a unified view of all sites, all guards, all incidents
- Predictive scheduling: AI suggests optimal schedule based on guard availability, OT threshold proximity, and certification requirements
- White-label mode: franchise security companies (10–20 agencies under one brand) pay 2–3x for their own branded app

**Long-Term Vision (Year 3+)**
GuardPost becomes the platform that defines how small security agencies operate — the way that Square defined payments for small retailers or Toast defined operations for restaurants. Expansion paths:

- International expansion: Canada, UK, Australia share the same market fragmentation and regulatory environment
- Vertical adjacency: parking enforcement, event security, loss prevention — all have similar non-desk workforce management needs
- Insurance partnership: security industry insurers offer premium discounts for agencies with digital audit trails; GuardPost becomes the documentation layer for insurance qualification
- Integration platform: GuardPost as the data source for property management systems (Yardi, AppFolio) — clients get guard status in their existing dashboards
- Acquisition target: as the category-defining small-agency tool, GuardPost becomes an attractive acquisition for TrackTik, Trackforce, or a workforce management platform entering the security vertical
