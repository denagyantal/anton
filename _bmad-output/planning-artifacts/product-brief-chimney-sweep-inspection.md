## Executive Summary

SweepHQ is a purpose-built scheduling, CRM, and compliance platform for chimney sweep companies — a $550M North American trade with ~8,000 US businesses, a proven $52,500/month MRR incumbent, and zero dominant software player offering an all-inclusive solution at the right price. The entire market currently runs on paper calendars, handwritten inspection forms, and Excel spreadsheets.

The core differentiator is regulatory and relational: NFPA 211 mandates annual chimney inspections and documented findings for every Level 2 inspection — this is non-discretionary, legally required, and no generic field service tool handles it. LayCor (the closest US competitor) charges $49–$99/month base but locks key features (photos, reminders, two-way texting) behind add-ons totalling $88–$102/month all-in. ChimSpect handles inspection reporting only at $99–$144/month but has no CRM or scheduling. The unidentified $52.5K/month incumbent demonstrates the market exists and will pay.

SweepHQ launches at $99/month all-inclusive — flat pricing, no add-ons — with a $249–$299 LTD offer seeded directly through the NCSG (National Chimney Sweep Guild) community and Facebook groups. The platform combines NFPA 211 inspection workflows, chimney-specific customer profiles, before/after photo documentation, annual reminder automation, and online self-booking into a single tool that works day one with zero configuration overhead. The north star metric is annual service reminders triggered per month — the highest-ROI feature that captures the structural recurring revenue chimney sweep businesses are currently leaving on the table.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Chimney sweep companies — the ~8,000 US businesses that clean, inspect, and repair residential and commercial chimney systems — operate almost entirely on paper. When a customer calls asking when they last had their chimney serviced, the owner digs through filing cabinets. Inspection findings from a Level 2 chimney inspection are recorded on handwritten forms, prone to loss, impossible to search, and insufficient for insurance and real estate documentation requirements. Before/after photos of chimney conditions are taken on personal phones and never tied to the customer record. Annual cleaning reminders — the single highest-leverage mechanism for recurring revenue — are sent manually (or not at all).

The result: chimney sweep operators with 60–70% recurring customer potential see 30–40% actual rebooking because there is no automated reminder system. A customer who gets their chimney swept in October 2024 should receive a reminder in September 2025 — but without software triggering that reminder, most operators lose that appointment to inertia.

NFPA 211 (the governing standard for chimney systems) requires Level 2 inspections at every real estate transfer, appliance change, or post-incident situation. These inspections must produce documented findings — findings that insurance carriers, real estate attorneys, and fire marshals increasingly require in professional PDF format, not handwritten on a yellow legal pad. No purpose-built chimney sweep software reliably delivers this compliance layer at an affordable price point.

### Problem Impact

**Revenue Leakage from Missed Annual Reminders:**
- A chimney sweep operator with 200 recurring residential customers who achieves 35% rebooking vs. 65% rebooking loses approximately $18,000–$26,000/year in first-call revenue (assuming $175/service average). The difference is a working annual reminder system.
- Seasonal concentration (September–November peak) means missed callbacks during the fall rush cost operators profitable jobs they can never recover — there is no off-season second chance.

**Compliance and Liability Exposure:**
- Level 2 inspection documentation is required by NFPA 211 at real estate transfer — the fastest-growing demand trigger as the real estate market recovers. Real estate attorneys request inspection records; handwritten forms create liability gaps.
- Insurance carriers increasingly require documented inspection histories for chimney-related claims. An operator who performed a Level 1 inspection 11 months before a chimney fire needs professional documentation to demonstrate they performed appropriate due diligence.
- CSIA (Chimney Safety Institute of America) certification requires ongoing professional practice documentation — digital records strengthen certification compliance.

**Operational Inefficiency:**
- Fall season surge overwhelms pen-and-paper scheduling: operators report double-booking, missed callbacks, and disorganized route planning during the 8–10 week peak window. Without digital scheduling and self-service online booking, profitable jobs are lost to scheduling chaos.
- Technicians in multi-truck operations have no shared access to customer history — when a different technician services an account, they start blind, unable to see what condition the chimney was in at the last service or what was recommended.

**Market Underinvestment:**
- The incumbent at $52,500/month MRR serves ~420 companies — approximately 5% of the market. LayCor serves 500+ more at lower price points. Combined, software-using chimney sweep companies represent ~12–15% of US operators. 85% of the market is on paper.
- This is not a mature SaaS market nearing saturation — it is a nascent vertical at the very beginning of its software adoption curve.

### Proposed Solution

**SweepHQ** is a purpose-built CRM and compliance platform for chimney sweep and specialty inspection businesses. The design principle is simple: operators run recurring annual appointments with compliance documentation requirements — so the software should be built around the annual service cycle, not adapted from a generic field service template.

1. **Chimney-Specific Customer Profiles** — Every customer record includes their chimney/fireplace configuration: flue type, liner material, fireplace type, fuel type, appliance, last service date, recommended service date, and condition history. When a technician arrives at a property, they see the full chimney history, not a blank screen.

2. **NFPA 211 Inspection Checklists (Level 1/2/3)** — Built-in inspection forms aligned with NFPA 211 standards. Level 1 visual checklist, Level 2 video camera inspection form, Level 3 destructive inspection documentation. Findings auto-populate a branded PDF inspection report delivered to the homeowner.

3. **Before/After Photo Documentation** — Technicians capture photos in the app; photos are automatically linked to the customer's service record, timestamped, and tagged with the chimney condition. No more photos lost on personal phones.

4. **Annual Reminder Automation** — The highest-ROI feature in the product. 11 months after each service, SweepHQ automatically sends the customer an SMS and email reminder to rebook. Operators set it up once; it runs indefinitely. This single feature can recover $15,000–$25,000/year in otherwise-lost recurring appointments for a mid-sized operator.

5. **Appointment Scheduling + Online Booking** — Calendar-based scheduling with a public booking widget (embed on website or share as a link). Customers book directly without phone tag. Route view shows the day's appointments in geographic sequence.

6. **NFPA-Compliant Inspection Report Generation** — Auto-generate professional PDF inspection reports from inspection form data, complete with before/after photos, findings, and recommendations. Replaces handwritten forms for real estate, insurance, and liability purposes.

7. **Invoice Generation + Payment Collection** — Job completion triggers an invoice with online payment link (Stripe). Recurring billing for service agreement customers. QuickBooks sync (Phase 2) for accounting integration.

8. **Camera Inspection Video Storage (Phase 2)** — Upload and store Level 2 camera inspection video files linked to the customer record. The only chimney-specific tool offering this.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**1. Chimney-Specific Customer Database (Must Have — Core Differentiator)**
- Customer profiles with contact info, property address, and chimney configuration fields: flue type (masonry/metal/prefab), liner material (clay tile/stainless steel/HeatShield/none), fireplace type (wood burning/gas/pellet/electric), fuel type, appliance make/model, date of last service, next recommended service date
- Service history timeline per customer: every job, date, technician, findings summary, photos
- Customer tags/status: "due for annual sweep," "sold/new owner," "level 2 required," "repair recommended"
- Quick search by name, address, or phone number
- Import from CSV (Excel migration support)

**2. NFPA 211 Inspection Checklists (Must Have — Compliance Differentiator)**
- Pre-built Level 1 inspection checklist (visual inspection: chimney exterior, crown, cap, interior, firebox, liner — all NFPA 211 Level 1 items)
- Pre-built Level 2 inspection checklist (all Level 1 items + accessible areas, video scan notation, appliance connection, transfer systems)
- Pre-built Level 3 inspection checklist (all Level 2 items + invasive inspection documentation)
- Pass/fail/marginal ratings per checklist item
- Free-text findings field per section
- Findings auto-populate the PDF inspection report
- Works offline; syncs when connectivity returns

**3. Before/After Photo Documentation (Must Have)**
- In-app camera capture linked directly to the service record
- Photo categorized by location: exterior crown, exterior masonry, firebox, smoke chamber, liner, damper, flashing
- Before/after comparison view per visit
- Photos displayed in the chronological service timeline
- Photo count visible on customer profile (e.g., "47 photos across 6 service visits")

**4. Annual Reminder Automation (Must Have — Revenue Recovery Feature)**
- Configurable reminder trigger: X months after last service date (default: 11 months)
- SMS reminder with direct online booking link
- Email reminder with service history summary and direct booking link
- Reminder delivery log per customer (sent, opened, clicked, booked)
- Override: operators can manually suppress or reschedule a reminder for individual customers
- Auto-cancels if customer books before reminder fires

**5. Appointment Scheduling (Must Have)**
- Calendar view (day/week/month) for scheduling jobs
- Drag-and-drop rescheduling
- Multi-technician schedule view
- Job types: sweep, Level 1 inspection, Level 2 inspection, Level 3 inspection, repair, dryer vent cleaning, estimate
- Geographic route view for the day's appointments (map showing job sequence)
- Online booking widget: embeddable on operator's website or shareable as a direct link; customers select service type, date, and time window

**6. NFPA-Compliant Inspection Report Generation (Must Have)**
- One-click generation from completed inspection checklist data
- Report includes: operator/company name, CSIA/NCSG certifications, customer name/address, inspection level, date, technician name, all checklist findings, before/after photos, summary notes, recommendations
- Professional branded PDF (operator can upload company logo)
- Delivered to homeowner via email from within the app
- Stored in customer record for future retrieval

**7. Invoice Generation + Payment Processing (Must Have)**
- Auto-generated invoice on job completion with line items from service type
- Online payment link via Stripe (credit card processing)
- Invoice history per customer
- Payment status: sent, viewed, paid, overdue
- Basic recurring billing for service agreement customers (monthly/annual)

**8. Mobile-First Technician App (Must Have)**
- iOS and Android apps with offline capability
- Today's schedule view with job details and customer chimney profile
- In-app navigation integration (Google Maps / Apple Maps)
- Job workflow: arrive → inspection checklist → photos → notes → invoice → complete
- Technician can see full customer service history before arriving

**9. Owner/Manager Dashboard (Must Have)**
- Jobs scheduled/completed today, this week, this month
- Upcoming annual reminders (next 30/60/90 days)
- Revenue summary: invoiced, collected, outstanding
- Customer list with next service dates
- Inspection reports generated (activity feed)
- Overdue accounts: customers past recommended annual service date without a scheduled appointment

Many chimney sweep companies already offer these services — they now use one tool for all of them instead of managing separate systems. The combined addressable market is 2–3x the chimney-only TAM (~$15M–$20M ARR total). Franchise support enables Midtown Sweeps (39 franchise units) to standardize on SweepHQ across their network, providing a bulk distribution channel. AI-assisted chimney condition assessment begins suggesting repair recommendations based on photo analysis (e.g., "Spalling brick pattern detected — recommend tuckpointing quote").

**Year 3 — Home Maintenance Record of Record:**
SweepHQ positions itself as the authoritative record of residential chimney and specialty system maintenance. Every Level 2 inspection report, every before/after photo series, every condition assessment creates a longitudinal property maintenance record. Real estate integration: Level 2 inspection reports accessible directly to real estate agents and buyers from the MLS listing. Insurance integration: annual inspection records shareable to insurance carriers as documentation of maintained chimney systems. The homeowner customer portal becomes a full property maintenance history — positioned not as a chimney app, but as "the maintenance record your home value depends on."

**Long-Term Moat:**
