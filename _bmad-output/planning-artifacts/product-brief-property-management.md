# Product Brief: Property Management for Small Landlords

## Executive Summary

The property management software market reached $3.61B in 2025 and is growing at 6-10% CAGR, yet the largest ownership segment — 10.6 million individual US landlords who control 70% of all rental properties — remains dramatically underserved. AppFolio charges a $298/month minimum and has replaced human support with AI chatbots. Buildium has imposed 6 price increases in 2.5 years. Free tools like TurboTenant and Innago pass costs to tenants through 2.9% + $0.30 transaction fees. The result: 40%+ of small landlords still manage properties on spreadsheets.

This product — **DoorKeep** — is the all-in-one property management tool purpose-built for self-managing landlords with 1-50 units. It covers rent collection with $1 flat ACH (saving landlords $5,000+/year vs. industry-standard fees), maintenance tracking, lease management, expense tracking with automatic IRS Schedule E categorization, and a mobile-first interface designed for landlords who manage properties as a side business, not a full-time job.

The go-to-market strategy launches via AppSumo at $79 (up to 10 units) / $149 (up to 25 units) / $179 (up to 50 units), targeting the deal-savvy real estate investor community. Distribution is community-led through BiggerPockets (2M+ members), Reddit's r/realestateinvesting (500K+) and r/landlord (65K+), and AppSumo's built-in audience of deal-oriented buyers. Transition to $19-39/month SaaS follows at month 6.

**Opportunity score: 99/105 — BUILD.**

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Self-managing landlords with 1-50 units face a punishing three-way trade-off when choosing property management software:

1. **Enterprise tools they can't afford**: AppFolio requires a 50-unit minimum at $298/month with annual contract lock-in. Buildium charges $55-375/month with serial price increases and has started removing integrations without notice. These tools are designed for 200-unit property management companies, not a landlord with 6 rental doors.

2. **Free tools that monetize through hidden fees**: TurboTenant, Innago, and Avail offer free landlord accounts but charge tenants 2.9% + $0.30 per ACH payment — meaning on a $1,500 rent payment, $44.10 disappears in fees every month. Across 10 units, that's $5,292/year in payment processing costs alone. Stessa provides excellent financial tracking but was acquired by Roofstock, raising platform continuity concerns.

3. **Spreadsheets and manual processes**: 40%+ of small landlords still track rent on spreadsheets, collect via Venmo/Zelle, manage maintenance via text messages, and scramble through shoeboxes of receipts at tax time. This works for 1-2 units but collapses at 5+.

With 80% of individual landlords self-managing (eschewing the 8-12% property management fee), and 75% owning 1-4 units, the market is screaming for a professional-grade tool at an individual-investor price.

### Problem Impact

- **Financial**: A 10-unit landlord loses $5,292/year to standard payment processing fees at 2.9% + $0.30. Disorganized expense tracking leads to missed tax deductions — the average landlord misses $2,000-5,000 in deductible expenses annually.
- **Operational**: Text-message maintenance tracking, paper leases, and Venmo rent collection create chaos that compounds with each added unit. Late payments go untracked. Maintenance requests fall through cracks.
- **Tax compliance**: Without Schedule E automation, landlords spend 10-20 hours at tax time manually categorizing expenses across properties. Many overpay taxes simply because they can't find receipts or properly allocate shared expenses.
- **Growth ceiling**: Landlords who want to grow from 3 to 10 units hit a wall — their manual systems break, but enterprise software is too expensive and complex. This creates an artificial ceiling on portfolio growth.
- **Quality of life**: Self-managing landlords work their day jobs, then spend evenings and weekends on property administration. The paperwork burden is the #1 reason landlords cite for hiring property managers (at 8-12% of gross rents) or selling rental properties entirely.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Competitor | Why It Fails Small Landlords |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **AppFolio** | $298/month minimum (50-unit floor), annual contracts with auto-renewal, AI-only customer support generating fierce backlash ("cannot call AppFolio — only AI help"), unauthorized charges reported with no human to resolve them. |
| **Buildium** | $55-375/month with 6 price increases in 2.5 years. Removed integrations "without any proactive communication." Mid-year price increases that "didn't allow any time to raise per-door fees." |
| **TurboTenant** | Free for landlords but tenants pay $2/ACH or 3.49% credit card. Limited reporting and accounting. Tenants resent the fees, creating friction in the landlord-tenant relationship. |
| **Stessa** | Excellent financial tracking (170K+ properties) but acquired by Roofstock — platform continuity uncertain. Limited PM features beyond accounting. Free tier constraining. |
| **Baselane** | Free with zero-fee ACH, but requires using their banking products. Different value proposition — banking platform, not property management tool. Limited PM features. |
| **DoorLoop** | Modern UX but expensive per-unit model ($49-69/month starting at 1 unit). Frustrating onboarding experience reported in forums. |
| **TenantCloud** | Broad features and AI capabilities but confusing dashboards, slow payment processing (7-day ACH), and overwhelming interface for small landlords. |

No existing competitor combines **all-in-one PM features + flat-rate pricing + $1 ACH + Schedule E automation + mobile-first UX** at a one-time or low monthly price.

### Proposed Solution

A mobile-first, all-in-one property management application with five core modules:

1. **Rent Collection** — Online payments with $1 flat ACH (vs. industry-standard 2.9% + $0.30). Automatic late fee calculation. Payment reminders via text/email. Tenant payment portal with multiple payment options. Real-time payment tracking dashboard.

2. **Maintenance Portal** — Tenant-facing request submission with photo/video uploads. Landlord triage dashboard with urgency categorization. AI-assisted maintenance triage suggesting priority levels and estimated costs. Communication thread per request. Contractor assignment and tracking.

3. **Lease Management** — Digital lease creation with state-aware templates. E-signature workflow. Lease renewal tracking with automated reminders. Document storage per unit. Move-in/move-out checklists.

4. **Expense Tracking & Tax** — Income/expense logging with receipt photo capture. Auto-categorization to IRS Schedule E categories. Per-property and portfolio-wide P&L reports. Tax-ready export for CPA or TurboTax. Mileage tracking for property visits.

5. **Property Dashboard** — Portfolio overview with key metrics at a glance. Tenant directory with lease status, payment history, and contact info. Vacancy tracking. Cash flow visualization. Multi-property consolidated view.

**Pricing principle**: One flat price. All features included, all tiers. No per-unit fees, no feature gates, no hidden transaction costs beyond the $1 ACH. $79-179 lifetime deal at launch, transitioning to $19-39/month for new customers.

**UX principle**: Mobile-first, built for landlords who manage properties between meetings, after work, or from the job site. Every screen optimized for quick action — log an expense in 3 taps, respond to a maintenance request in 30 seconds, check rent status at a glance.

### Key Differentiators

1. **$1 flat ACH rent collection** — The single most compelling differentiator. At $1/transaction vs. 2.9% + $0.30, a 10-unit landlord collecting $1,500/unit saves over $5,000/year. This alone pays for the lifetime deal in the first month.

2. **Flat-rate lifetime deal pricing** — $79 (up to 10 units) / $149 (up to 25 units) / $179 (up to 50 units). No monthly subscriptions, no per-unit scaling, no surprise price increases. Real estate investors think in deals — this framing resonates deeply with the target buyer.

3. **Automatic Schedule E tax categorization** — Expenses auto-categorize to IRS Schedule E line items as they're logged. At tax time, landlords export a tax-ready report instead of spending 20 hours with a spreadsheet and a shoebox of receipts. Only Stessa, Baselane, and Landlord Studio do this well among current players.

4. **Transparent zero-hidden-fee model** — Every feature included at every tier. No locked features, no per-unit upcharges, no mid-contract price increases. Directly contrasts with Buildium's pricing games and AppFolio's opacity.

5. **Human customer support** — Chat + email support staffed by humans, explicitly contrasting with AppFolio's AI-only support that has generated industry-wide backlash. Support included in the LTD price.

6. **Mobile-first architecture** — Not a desktop app with a mobile wrapper. Native mobile experience for landlords who check rent status from their phone, log expenses from the hardware store parking lot, and respond to maintenance requests from their day job.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: "Mike the Mid-Portfolio Landlord"**

- **Profile**: 42 years old, IT manager by day, owns 7 rental units across 3 properties (a duplex, a triplex, and 2 SFRs). Household income $140K ($85K salary + $55K net rental income). Based in suburban Atlanta.
- **Current tools**: Excel spreadsheet for tracking, Venmo/Zelle for rent collection, text messages for maintenance, paper leases from a template he found online, TurboTax at tax time with a shoebox of receipts.
- **Pain points**: Spends 8-10 hours/week on property admin. Missed a $3,200 insurance deduction last year because he couldn't find the receipt. Had a maintenance request fall through the cracks — a slow leak that became a $4,500 repair. Looked at Buildium but balked at $55/month when he's "just" a side-hustle landlord.
- **Goals**: Reduce admin time to under 3 hours/week. Never miss a tax deduction again. Professional maintenance tracking so nothing falls through. Grow to 15 units within 3 years.
- **Success moment**: When he exports his Schedule E report in February and hands it to his CPA, who says "this is the cleanest rental income documentation I've seen."

**Persona 2: "Sarah the Scaling Investor"**

- **Profile**: 37 years old, real estate investor building a portfolio. Currently owns 18 units across 6 properties. Active on BiggerPockets forums. Reads real estate investing podcasts during her commute. Household income $210K (primarily rental income + part-time consulting).
- **Current tools**: Stessa for financial tracking (loves it but worries about Roofstock acquisition), TurboTenant for rent collection (hates that tenants pay fees), individual lease templates from her attorney. Switched from Buildium 8 months ago after the 4th price increase.
- **Pain points**: Cobbling together 3-4 tools to do what one should handle. Stessa doesn't do maintenance tracking. TurboTenant's tenant-paid fees cause friction — two tenants have complained. Growing from 18 to 30 units means her current patchwork system will break entirely.
- **Goals**: One tool that does everything. Stop paying tenants' resentment. Scale to 30+ units with a system that grows with her. Clear financial reporting for potential property acquisition analysis.
- **Success moment**: When she adds her 25th unit and the system handles it without any additional cost or complexity, and her tenants actually prefer the payment portal over Venmo.

**Persona 3: "Tom the First-Timer"**

- **Profile**: 55 years old, high school teacher who inherited his mother's duplex. Also bought a small SFR as a rental last year. 3 total units. No real estate investing experience prior to the inheritance. Household income $72K ($58K teaching salary + $14K net rental).
- **Current tools**: A notebook and his personal bank account. Collects rent via check. Handles maintenance by calling the same plumber/electrician he uses at home.
- **Pain points**: Terrified of making a legal mistake with leases. Doesn't know what Schedule E is. Got overwhelmed when he tried Buildium — "too many buttons, I don't even know what half of them do." Needs simplicity above all else.
- **Goals**: Basic rent tracking so he knows who's paid. Lease templates that are legally compliant for his state. A simple way to track maintenance expenses for tax time. Peace of mind that he's not making costly mistakes.
- **Success moment**: When he sets up his 3 units in 20 minutes and realizes he can see everyone's payment status on one screen from his phone.

### Secondary Users

**Tenants** — Interact with the tenant-facing portal for online rent payment, maintenance request submission, and lease document access. Their experience directly impacts landlord satisfaction — if tenants hate the payment portal, landlords will churn.

**Accountants/CPAs** — Receive exported Schedule E reports and financial summaries at tax time. Clean, professional export formats build trust and create word-of-mouth referrals from CPAs to other landlord clients.

**Contractors/Handymen** — Receive maintenance work orders and communicate through the maintenance portal. Secondary users but their experience affects landlord efficiency.

### User Journey

**Discovery** → Landlord searches "Buildium alternative" or "best property management software for small landlords" after a price increase, tax-season chaos, or a maintenance disaster. Finds the product through a BiggerPockets forum recommendation, a Reddit thread, or an AppSumo deal listing.

**Evaluation** → Signs up for a trial or browses the AppSumo deal page. Immediately notices the $1 flat ACH differentiator and the flat-rate pricing. Uses the payment fee calculator tool to see how much they're currently losing. Reads 3-5 reviews from other small landlords.

**Onboarding** → Adds their first property in under 5 minutes. Imports units, sets rent amounts, and invites their first tenant. The system walks them through setting up rent collection and suggests relevant Schedule E categories for their expenses. Goal: first property fully configured in 20 minutes.

**Core Usage** → Monthly rhythm: check rent payment dashboard, respond to maintenance requests, log expenses as they occur, review property P&L. All from mobile during spare moments throughout the day.

**Aha Moment** → First month where they see the complete picture: all rent collected on time (with automatic reminders), maintenance requests tracked with full history, expenses auto-categorized, and a clean dashboard showing portfolio performance. Realized they spent 2 hours on property admin instead of 10.

**Advocacy** → Posts on BiggerPockets: "Found a Buildium alternative that's one payment, all features, and $1 ACH. AMA." Leaves a G2 review. Tells their real estate investing meetup group.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**1. Rent Collection** (Critical — Day 1)
- Online ACH payments at $1 flat fee per transaction
- Credit/debit card payment option (standard processing rates)
- Automatic payment reminders (text + email, configurable schedule)
- Late fee tracking and automatic calculation
- Tenant payment portal (branded, simple)
- Payment history and reporting dashboard
- Partial payment handling

**2. Property & Tenant Management** (Critical — Day 1)
- Add/edit properties with unit-level detail
- Tenant profiles with contact info, lease dates, rent amounts
- Vacancy tracking with status indicators
- Portfolio dashboard with at-a-glance metrics
- Multi-property consolidated view

**3. Maintenance Tracking** (Critical — Day 1)
- Tenant-facing request submission form with photo/video upload
- Landlord triage dashboard with status workflow (New → In Progress → Completed)
- Communication thread per request (landlord ↔ tenant)
- Priority/urgency categorization
- Maintenance history per unit

**4. Expense Tracking & Schedule E** (Critical — Day 1)
- Income and expense logging per property
- Receipt photo capture and attachment
- Auto-categorization to IRS Schedule E line items
- Per-property and portfolio P&L reports
- Tax-ready Schedule E export (PDF + CSV)
- Basic cash flow reporting

**5. Lease Management** (Critical — Day 1)
- Digital lease creation from templates
- E-signature workflow (landlord + tenant)
- Lease document storage per unit
- Lease expiration tracking with renewal reminders
- Move-in/move-out checklist templates

**6. Mobile-First Responsive Design** (Critical — Day 1)
- Responsive web application optimized for mobile screens
- Core workflows accessible in 3 taps or fewer
- Push notifications for payments, maintenance requests, lease expirations
- Works on iOS and Android browsers

### Out of Scope for MVP

- **Tenant screening/background checks** — Integrate post-MVP via TransUnion/Equifax API (Month 3-4)
- **State-specific lease templates** — Launch with general templates; add state-specific versions post-MVP (Month 3-6)
- **AI maintenance triage** — AI-powered urgency and cost estimation (Month 4-6)
- **Contractor directory/marketplace** — Matching landlords with local contractors (Month 6+)
- **Native mobile apps** — Responsive web first; native iOS/Android apps for v2 (Month 6-9)
- **Advanced financial reporting** — Cap rate calculators, ROI analysis, portfolio valuation (Month 4+)
- **Team/multi-user access** — Property manager roles, shared portfolio access (Month 4+)
- **Insurance integration** — Embedded coverage recommendations and partnerships (Year 2)
- **Open banking/API access** — Third-party integrations and developer API (Year 2)
- **Accounting software sync** — QuickBooks/Xero integration (Month 3-4)
