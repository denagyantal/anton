# Product Brief: HOA Community Self-Management Platform

---

## Executive Summary

Volunteer HOA board members across 224,000 self-managed communities in the US spend dozens of hours each month juggling Google Drive, Venmo, email chains, and spreadsheets to run their communities — tools that were never designed for this job. When a board member leaves, they often take folder access and financial records with them, leaving the next volunteer starting from zero.

Every serious HOA management platform on the market was built for professional property management companies, not volunteer boards. The cheapest purpose-built option starts at $39–$49/month — a price that requires a formal board vote and community budget allocation, a real barrier for a volunteer trying to solve a problem today.

The HOA Community Self-Management Platform is a permanently free (for communities under 50 units) platform that provides dues collection, document management, financial dashboards, and maintenance request tracking — designed from the ground up for volunteer board members, not professional managers. Revenue comes from payment processing fees (2.5%) on dues collection, meaning the platform scales with community activity rather than fighting the board vote barrier.

**The market moment is now:** TOPS Pro (a legacy platform used by thousands of communities) ended support in July 2025 and communities are actively migrating. Vantaca's $1.25B valuation confirms institutional conviction in HOA software. Two YC-backed companies entered the adjacent professional management space, but the self-managed DIY segment — the largest cluster of underserved communities — remains structurally open.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Self-managed HOA boards — volunteer homeowners who didn't sign up to run a small business — are forced to operate with a patchwork of consumer tools: Google Drive for documents, Venmo or Zelle for dues, email chains for communication, and Excel for financial tracking. These tools fail in two critical ways:

1. **They don't survive board turnover.** When a board treasurer leaves and revokes Google Drive access, or when a board president moves away taking vendor contacts in their personal email, the new board inherits nothing. This "data loss on departure" is the most acute and emotionally resonant pain point — boards fear it before it happens and scramble when it does.

2. **They're inadequate at scale.** Tracking dues payments from 50+ homeowners in a spreadsheet, reconciling a bank account manually, and compiling a treasurer report for the annual meeting from email records is a genuine operational burden that contributes directly to volunteer burnout and board turnover.

The problem is not a lack of available tools — it is that every tool designed for this problem is priced and scoped for professional property management companies. Volunteer boards cannot justify a $49–$280/month subscription from community funds without a formal vote, making adoption nearly impossible for the boards that need help most.

### Problem Impact

- **224,000+ communities** currently self-managing with no purpose-built software
- **2.5 million volunteer board members** spending 10–20+ hours/month on manual administrative tasks
- **Board turnover** creates a recurring cycle of crisis: new board members inherit no records, spend weeks reconstructing financial history, and either adopt bad workarounds or burn out and resign — perpetuating the cycle
- **Financial liability**: In many states, HOA board members carry personal legal exposure for financial mismanagement, making disorganized records a real legal risk, not just an inconvenience
- **Volunteer burnout** is described as a "pervasive issue" across the industry — administrative overhead is the primary cited cause

The emotional core of the problem is captured in a single scenario: a new board president logs into the community Google Drive folder they've been told has all the records, and finds it empty. The previous treasurer deleted their personal folder on the way out.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Every competitor in the market built their product for professional property managers — people who manage dozens or hundreds of communities full-time:

| Competitor | Monthly Start | Built For | Why It Fails Self-Managed Boards |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vantaca | Quote (~$300+) | Professional management companies | Enterprise product, enterprise price, enterprise complexity |
| AppFolio | $280 minimum | Professional managers (200+ units) | Requires a dedicated admin to operate; priced for portfolios |
| Buildium | $58–$600+ | Property managers | Designed for rental + HOA mix; complex onboarding |
| PayHOA | $49 (≤50 units) | Self-managed HOAs | Best current option, but $49/month still requires a board vote and budget allocation |
| HOA Start | $39/month | Self-managed HOAs | Flat monthly cost still creates adoption friction for small communities |
| TownSq | Free (very limited) | Community engagement | Free tier lacks accounting, dues collection, and financial reporting |

The deepest gap: no platform currently offers a **permanent free tier** for small communities (under 50 units) with genuine dues collection capability and document management. TownSq's free tier is too limited (no accounting, no ACH). PayHOA's $49 minimum — while reasonable — still creates the "requires a board vote" adoption barrier that prevents the most-desperate small boards from acting.

### Proposed Solution

A web-based HOA management platform purpose-built for volunteer boards, not professional managers.

**Core philosophy:** Make the board's job invisible. Dues collection should be set-and-forget. Documents should never be deletable by a departing board member. Treasurer reports should generate themselves.

**Business model:** Revenue flows from payment processing fees (2.5% on dues) rather than subscriptions — so the platform earns money when communities are active, not when volunteers remember to pay a bill. For small communities, this means free forever. For mid-size communities (50–200 units) collecting $5,000–$30,000/month in dues, the platform generates $125–$750/month in revenue per community without a monthly subscription fight.

**Free tier (under 50 units):** Full platform access with no monthly fee. Revenue from 2.5% transaction fees only. This eliminates the board vote barrier entirely — any board member can sign up and start collecting dues today without a formal community decision.

**Paid tier (50–300 units):** $49–$99/month subscription plus reduced transaction fees (1.5–2%). Justified by dues volume and feature depth.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Board-turnover-proof by design.** Documents, financial records, and owner data belong to the community, not any individual board member. Role-based access with seamless handoff — one click to transfer treasurer access to a new person. Departing members are removed without taking anything with them.

2. **Genuinely free for small communities.** Not a 30-day trial. Not a "free tier" with no useful features. Full platform access for HOAs under 50 units, funded entirely by payment processing fees. This is structurally impossible for competitors to match without abandoning their subscription revenue model.

3. **Auto-generated treasurer reports.** The most dreaded board meeting task — compiling the financial summary for the annual meeting — becomes a PDF download. One click, always current, always professional. This single feature is the "aha moment" that drives adoption and retention.

4. **Designed for non-technical volunteers.** Not for property managers with dedicated admin staff. Onboarding should take 15 minutes, not 15 hours. Every feature should be usable by a 60-year-old retired homeowner who has never used property management software.

5. **Transaction-fee revenue scales with community health.** The more a community collects in dues (a sign of organizational health), the more revenue the platform earns — without extracting more from communities in financial difficulty.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Persona 1: Margaret, the Reluctant HOA Treasurer**

Margaret is a 54-year-old accountant who owns a townhome in a 38-unit HOA in suburban Phoenix. When the previous treasurer resigned unexpectedly, the board asked Margaret to take over "temporarily" six months ago. She agreed because nobody else would.

She inherited a Google Drive folder with three years of disorganized spreadsheets, a Venmo account nobody remembers the password to, and a box of printed bank statements. Every month she manually matches dues payments from the bank feed to her owner list. Every quarter she spends a weekend building the financial report for the board meeting from scratch.

She is not frustrated with HOA management software — she has never heard of it. She is frustrated with the spreadsheet, the Venmo confusion, and the dread of the next annual meeting treasurer report. She would adopt any tool that solves these three problems today if she could sign up without spending community money.

**Job to be done:** Get dues collected reliably without manual matching; produce a professional-looking financial report for the board meeting; feel confident that when she eventually hands this role to someone else, they will have everything they need.

**Discovery path:** Google search for "HOA dues tracking spreadsheet alternative" during a late-night session trying to reconcile the bank statement. Or a post on r/HOA asking "is there a better way to track dues than a spreadsheet?"

**Adoption barrier:** Any monthly cost requires a board vote. She will not bring a $49/month expense to the board until she has used it herself and knows it works. A free tier allows her to test it privately before any formal proposal.

---

**Persona 2: David, the New Board President in Crisis**

David is a 41-year-old software engineer who just bought a condo in a 55-unit complex in Austin. Three weeks after moving in, the existing board president announced she was selling and moving out of state. David was the only owner who showed up to the emergency board meeting. He is now president.

He has been given login credentials to a shared Google Drive that contains nothing useful. There are folders with years-old documents but no financial records, no vendor contacts, no ongoing maintenance issues. The previous president managed everything from her personal email and personal phone.

David is technically sophisticated and has a clear-eyed view of what he needs: a single place where all community information lives that cannot be taken away by a departing board member. He is willing to pay, but wants to understand what he is paying for before committing.

**Job to be done:** Build an institutional memory for the community that does not depend on any individual. Start collecting dues immediately (he has discovered three owners have not paid in four months). Get a handle on the community's financial picture.

**Discovery path:** Searches "HOA management software self-managed" and reads comparison articles. Finds PayHOA, HOA Start, and possibly the platform through a Reddit mention. Evaluates two or three options before choosing.

**Adoption barrier:** Price is less of a barrier (55-unit HOA has real dues volume), but he wants to understand the product before committing. A free trial or free tier for the first month is key; so is clear, honest product documentation.

---

**Persona 3: Karen, the Board President of a Mid-Size Community**

Karen runs the board of a 140-unit planned community in suburban Atlanta. They have been using a legacy desktop software (TOPS Pro) for seven years. In July 2025, TOPS Pro ended all technical support. Karen is now being asked by the board to find a modern replacement.

She is an organized, experienced board leader who knows what she needs: the same functionality as TOPS Pro but in a cloud platform that works on her iPad. She has done this before and knows the migration will be painful. She is looking for a platform that understands HOA management, not just "property management."

**Job to be done:** Migrate from TOPS Pro to a modern platform without losing seven years of financial and community history. Maintain all the workflow her board has come to rely on. Ideally get something better than TOPS Pro in the process.

**Discovery path:** Actively searching "TOPS Pro alternative" and reading migration guides. High intent, limited time frame (TOPS Pro has no security patches as of July 2025). Will respond to content specifically targeting TOPS Pro migration.

**Adoption barrier:** Data migration is the primary concern. She will not switch platforms if it means manually re-entering seven years of owner and financial data. A clear, documented migration path from TOPS Pro is the conversion unlock for this segment.

### Secondary Users

**HOA Homeowners (Community Members)**

The 50–300+ individual homeowners in each community are secondary users who interact with the platform through the homeowner portal. They pay dues, submit maintenance requests, access community documents (CC&Rs, meeting minutes, rules), and receive community announcements.

They are not the buyer and do not drive adoption — the board does. But their experience matters because: (1) a homeowner portal that owners actively use reduces the board's communication workload, and (2) homeowner satisfaction with the platform affects board members' satisfaction with their own decision to adopt it.

Key needs: simple online dues payment (one click, autopay option); easy maintenance request submission with status updates; access to community documents without calling the board; notification of important community announcements.

**HOA Attorneys and CPAs**

A small but high-value secondary user group: professionals who serve self-managed HOA boards as advisors. HOA attorneys are consulted on disputes, rule enforcement, and governance. CPAs serving HOA clients review financial records and prepare tax returns.

Both groups can become referral sources if the platform produces documentation (financial reports, violation records, meeting minutes) that meets their professional standards. An HOA attorney who says "use this platform, it gives me what I need to represent you" is a powerful referral. This segment should be considered in document formatting and export standards.

### User Journey

**Discovery → Board Champion Adoption Journey (Margaret / Small HOA)**

1. **Trigger:** A specific frustration event — dues collection breakdown, preparation for annual meeting, or board member departure takes key records.
2. **Search:** Google search for the specific problem ("HOA dues tracking", "HOA treasurer report template", "TOPS Pro alternative"). Reddit/Facebook secondary.
3. **Landing:** Arrives at a landing page that speaks directly to their specific trigger ("replace Google Drive + Venmo", "treasurer report in one click").
4. **Sign-up:** Zero-friction free tier registration. No credit card. Community is active immediately.
5. **First value (Week 1):** Dues collection set up. First ACH payment received. Board member feels immediate relief.
6. **"Aha moment" (Week 2–4):** Generates first auto-formatted treasurer report. Shows it to the rest of the board. Other board members ask "how did you make that?"
7. **Advocacy (Month 2+):** Mentions the platform in r/HOA or a Facebook HOA group when someone asks for recommendations. Mentions it to another board member they know.
8. **Upgrade (Month 6–12, if community grows or needs more features):** Community surpasses 50 units, or board votes to support the platform with a budget allocation. Upgrades to paid tier.

**Crisis Adoption Journey (David / New Board President)**

1. **Trigger:** Takes over board presidency with no records, no financial history, three non-paying owners.
2. **Search:** "self-managed HOA software", "HOA management software free trial". Reads 2–3 comparison articles.
3. **Evaluation:** Tests free tier with no time limit. Imports owner list from spreadsheet. Sets up dues collection in first session.
4. **First value (Day 1–3):** Sends dues payment request to delinquent owners via the platform. Gets three payment notifications in 48 hours.
5. **Retention (Month 1):** Board sees the financial dashboard for the first time. Meeting takes 20 minutes instead of two hours because everyone can see the same numbers.
6. **Board vote for paid tier (Month 2–3):** David presents the platform at the next board meeting. 55-unit community approves $49/month budget allocation. Upgrades.

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

The platform is working for users when:

- **Dues collection reliability**: 90%+ of active communities collect dues via the platform (vs. Venmo/cash) within 60 days of signup. Boards stop using Venmo entirely within 90 days.
- **Board turnover survival**: When a board member role is transferred (treasurer, president), 100% of records remain accessible to the successor without any manual action by the departing member. Zero "access lost on departure" incidents for platform data.
- **Treasurer report generation**: 80%+ of active communities generate at least one financial report in the 30 days before their annual meeting. Meeting preparation time self-reported as reduced by 50%+.
- **Time savings**: Board members report spending less than 2 hours/month on dues administration (vs. industry average of 5–10 hours/month manual).
- **Maintenance request response**: Communities with active maintenance request tracking respond to requests within 7 days (vs. "falls through email" baseline).

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP must deliver on four use cases: (1) collect dues reliably, (2) store documents safely, (3) show financial status clearly, and (4) communicate with homeowners. These four solve the acute pains and create the retention lock.

**Feature 1: Dues Collection (ACH + Credit Card)**
- Recurring monthly dues setup per homeowner
- ACH payment (2.5% fee, or $1.50 flat for ACH) and credit card (3.2% + $0.30)
- Automated payment reminders (email to homeowners before due date)
- Real-time payment status dashboard for board (paid, pending, overdue)
- Manual payment recording for cash/check
- Delinquency tracking and automated late-fee calculation

**Feature 2: Document Library**
- Secure cloud storage for HOA documents (CC&Rs, bylaws, meeting minutes, financial records, vendor contracts)
- Folder structure with community-controlled naming
- Role-based access: board-only vs. all-homeowners
- Version history for key documents
- Board member role transfer: one-click reassignment of access without document deletion
- No individual board member can delete community documents unilaterally

**Feature 3: Financial Dashboard**
- Real-time dues revenue summary (collected, pending, overdue)
- Monthly income/expense ledger with category tagging
- Year-to-date financial summary
- Auto-generated treasurer report (PDF): monthly summary, dues collection status, expense breakdown, year-to-date comparison
- Bank account connection (read-only via Plaid) for automatic transaction import

**Feature 4: Owner Directory + Announcements**
- Homeowner roster with unit numbers, contact info, move-in dates
- Board announcement broadcasting (email to all homeowners)
- Document sharing with homeowners (view-only access to community documents)
- Welcome email to new homeowners with community documents and dues payment link

**Feature 5: Maintenance Request Portal**
- Homeowner-submitted requests with description, photo attachment, priority
- Board view of all open requests with status (New / In Progress / Resolved)
- Status update emails to homeowners when requests are moved
- Request history per unit (useful for documenting recurring issues)
- Vendor assignment and note field per request

**Feature 6: Onboarding Flow**
- 15-minute guided setup: community name → add units → import homeowner emails → set dues amount → go live
- CSV import for homeowner list
- Template library for first-time document upload (CC&R template, budget template, meeting minutes template)

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following are valuable features explicitly excluded from MVP to maintain focus and shipping velocity:

- **Violation tracking and fine management** — Important but not the acute crisis trigger; add in Month 3–4
- **Architectural review requests (ARC)** — Complex workflow; add in Month 4–6
- **Native mobile apps (iOS/Android)** — Mobile-responsive web (PWA) for MVP; native apps Month 6–9
- **Full accounting/QuickBooks integration** — The financial dashboard covers 80% of needs; deep accounting integration is a paid-tier differentiator for Month 4+
- **Voting and election management** — Annual use case, not crisis-driven; defer to Month 6+
- **Reserve fund analysis and planning** — High-value feature for established boards; defer to Month 6+
- **Vendor marketplace** — Interesting but not in the adoption loop; defer to Year 2
- **AI-powered features** — Violation detection, budget recommendations; defer to Year 2 after user base established
- **Multi-association management** — For property managers managing multiple HOAs; not self-managed segment target; defer
- **TOPS Pro data import tool** — High value for migrators but complex to build; deprioritize for MVP, build in Month 2–3 as dedicated migration sprint

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is validated and ready for investment beyond initial development when:

1. **Adoption signal**: 50+ communities actively using dues collection within 60 days of launch
2. **Retention signal**: 80%+ of those 50 communities still active at 90 days
3. **Revenue signal**: $3,000+ MRR from transaction fees + early paid upgrades at Day 90
4. **NPS signal**: Board NPS 40+ from first cohort survey
5. **Referral signal**: At least 20% of new community signups attributable to word-of-mouth from existing users by Month 3

If all five criteria are met at 90 days, the model is validated and the platform should accelerate acquisition investment and feature development.

**Year 1 Post-MVP: Become the Default Platform for Self-Managed HOAs**
- Violation tracking and notice management (automated compliance workflow)
- Architectural review request portal
- Native iOS/Android apps for board members and homeowners
- Reserve fund tracker and planning tool

- Possible acquisition pathway: Vantaca, AppFolio, or RealPage as eventual acquirer of the self-managed segment leader

## Go-to-Market Summary

**Primary channels (in priority order):**

1. **r/HOA + Reddit organic**: Authentic participation in HOA community discussions. Answer treasurer questions, board turnover questions. Build credibility before any mention of the platform. 2M+ community members; high-intent, self-selected pain sufferers.

2. **SEO content**: Target "self-managed HOA software", "free HOA management software", "HOA treasurer report template", "TOPS Pro alternative". Each content piece designed around a specific crisis moment.

3. **Facebook HOA Groups**: State-specific HOA board member groups (e.g., "HOA Board Members of Florida", "Texas HOA Boards") with 10K–50K members each. Authentic presence, not ads-first.

4. **Product Hunt launch**: Day-1 launch for initial spike and press coverage. Frame as "the free HOA tool that actually replaces Google Drive + Venmo."

5. **HOA professional referrals** (Month 3+): Target HOA attorneys and CPAs who advise self-managed boards. Becoming the "tool my attorney recommends" is a high-credibility referral channel.

6. **AppSumo LTD** (Month 4–6): After 100+ active communities and genuine reviews. Structure as $99 LTD (1 community, lifetime, up to 100 units) with transaction fees preserved on all LTD accounts. Seeded user base and social proof.

**Positioning statement:** "The HOA management platform that volunteer board members actually use — free for small communities, built for people who didn't sign up to be property managers."
