# Product Brief: Trade Safety Compliance

**Date:** 2026-06-11
**Author:** Root
**Project:** Trade Safety Compliance — Digital Safety Log for Small Landscaping & Construction Crews

---

## Executive Summary

Trade Safety Compliance is a purpose-built mobile-first safety compliance logging tool for small landscaping, lawn care, and construction crews (1–30 employees). It enables crew owners and foremen to capture safety training records, store certificates with expiry alerts, collect daily crew safety attestations via phone, document incidents, and export an OSHA-ready compliance report in a single click.

The product enters a market where ~700,000 US landscaping businesses and 200,000+ construction subcontractors manage all safety compliance via paper binders, spreadsheets, and PDFs — despite having a legal obligation to maintain training records and a rapidly intensifying commercial obligation from workers' compensation insurers. No purpose-built, affordable tool exists for this audience. The closest competitors (SafetyCulture at $24/user/month, Safesite at $16–$20/user/month) are generic, overbuilt, and price-prohibitive for a 10-person crew.

The go-to-market is a $149 LTD launch on AppSumo (zero LTD competitors exist in this niche) backed by organic community seeding in landscaping Facebook groups and r/lawncare. Long-term monetization is $29/month flat-rate for up to 20 crew members — a price point that undercuts every per-seat competitor by more than 90% at the crew level.

**One-line positioning:** _"Pass your workers' comp audit in 10 minutes — safety logs, certifications, and daily sign-offs for your whole crew, at one flat price."_

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Small landscaping and construction business owners are legally required to maintain safety training records, PPE documentation, and safety program evidence — but have no affordable, trade-specific tool to do it. The current state is a paper binder in the owner's truck and a folder of scanned OSHA 10 cards in a Gmail inbox.

This creates three converging failure modes:

1. **Regulatory exposure:** OSHA recordkeeping violations carry fines up to $16,550 per violation. For a 5-person landscaping company, a single paperwork audit failure can cost $5,000–$15,000 after small-business discounts.

2. **Insurance pressure:** Workers' compensation insurers are tightening safety documentation requirements as a condition of coverage. Landscaping carries one of the highest injury rates of any US industry (475 work-related deaths, 22,000+ severe injuries in 2021–22). Insurers are sending written audit letters requiring documented safety programs and training logs. This is the dominant near-term forcing function — it does not depend on any regulatory change.

3. **GC subcontractor mandates:** General contractors and commercial property managers increasingly require proof of safety training before allowing crew on site. A landscaping company bidding on institutional or commercial contracts cannot win without documentation.

The paper system breaks under all three pressures — records are at the wrong jobsite, certificates are expired without anyone knowing, and new-hire safety onboarding leaves no paper trail.

### Problem Impact

- **Financial:** Average OSHA fine for recordkeeping violation = $16,550 maximum; effective small-business fine = $1,800–$4,965 per citation. Workers' comp premium surcharges from poor safety records can double annual insurance costs.
- **Operational:** Every new hire (and landscaping has extreme turnover) requires documented PPE acknowledgment, safety training, and onboarding — manually tracked by the owner, who is also on the mowing route.
- **Growth barrier:** Companies that can't document safety compliance are locked out of commercial contracts that require it.
- **Language gap:** 40–60% of landscaping crews are native Spanish speakers. English-only safety briefings fail both OSHA's HazCom "train in understood language" requirement and practical crew safety.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Competitor | Why It Fails Small Trades |
|---|---|
| SafetyCulture / iAuditor | $24/user/month = $240–$360/month for a 10–15 person crew; generic templates; no landscaping-specific content; no Spanish support |
| Safesite | $16–$20/user/month = similar cost problem; construction focus, not landscaping |
| Procore | $500–$800+/month; enterprise construction only |
| FileFlo | $299/month flat; not trade-specific; complex for low-tech operators |
| Google Forms + Drive | No cert expiry tracking; no OSHA-ready export; no e-signatures; manual workflow |
| Paper binders | Free but fails all three compliance pressures; records unavailable when needed |

The market gap is not a technology gap — it is a product-market fit gap. The right tool at the right price for the right audience does not exist.

### Proposed Solution

A focused, mobile-first compliance logging app with five core capabilities:

1. **Safety Training Log** — record which crew member attended which training, with date and digital e-signature. Works on a $200 Android phone. Foreman adds a training session in 60 seconds.

2. **Certificate Tracker** — upload OSHA 10 cards, First Aid certificates, pesticide applicator licenses, and CDL medical cards with expiry dates. Automated 30/60/90-day alerts before expiry. Eliminates the "crew member turned away at the gate" incident.

3. **Daily Safety Attestation** — crew member opens the app, reads today's safety briefing (templated for landscaping-specific hazards: heat illness, chemical handling, rotating blades, roadside traffic), and signs with a tap. Owner has a timestamped record showing the briefing happened.

4. **Incident Report** — photo + description + timestamp + injured party info. Replaces the scattered phone photos and handwritten notes that currently document near-misses.

5. **OSHA-Ready Export** — one-click PDF showing crew roster, training history, certificate status, and incident log. Hand it to an OSHA inspector or workers' comp auditor in under 2 minutes.

Pricing: $29/month flat for up to 20 crew members. $149 LTD for AppSumo launch.

### Key Differentiators

**Differentiator 1: Flat-rate pricing**
$29/month for the whole crew, regardless of headcount (up to 20). Every competitor charges per seat. For a 15-person landscaping crew, this is $29 vs. $240–$360/month. This is the headline that wins the sale in a Facebook group post.

**Differentiator 2: Landscaping-specific content**
Pre-built toolbox talk templates for heat illness, pesticide/chemical handling, rotating blade equipment, roadside traffic safety, fall protection, and PPE sign-off. No competitor offers this. Generic "what are the hazards at your worksite" templates are useless for a foreman managing a mowing crew.

**Differentiator 3: Bilingual (Spanish/English) support**
All safety briefings, attestation forms, and training records available in both languages. OSHA HazCom requires training in the worker's understood language. First safety compliance tool to own this positioning in the trades.

**Differentiator 4: Workers' comp compliance framing**
Primary messaging is "pass your workers' comp audit" — not "OSHA compliance." This is a more immediate, commercial trigger for the target buyer and requires no regulatory change to be compelling.

**Differentiator 5: LTD timing**
AppSumo has zero competitors in this exact niche. A $149 LTD launch in this space is uncontested. First mover wins the "safety compliance LTD" search result.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP delivers one clear value: _a complete, auditable safety compliance record for a small trades crew, accessible on any smartphone, exportable in one click._

**Feature 1: Crew Roster Management**
- Add/remove crew members (name, role, phone number)
- Assign crew members to a company/team
- View active crew list at a glance
- Required for all downstream features

**Feature 2: Safety Training Log**
- Create a training session (topic, date, trainer)
- Add attending crew members
- Each attendee signs with digital e-signature on phone
- Training records stored with timestamp and signee identity
- Log visible per crew member ("last trained: heat illness — June 5, 2026")

**Feature 3: Certificate Tracker**
- Upload certificate image/PDF per crew member (OSHA 10, First Aid, pesticide applicator, CDL medical)
- Set expiry date per certificate
- Automated email/SMS alert to owner at 90, 60, and 30 days before expiry
- Certificate status visible on crew roster (green/yellow/red)

**Feature 4: Daily Safety Attestation**
- Owner/foreman selects a pre-built briefing topic (heat illness, equipment safety, chemical handling, roadside safety, etc.) or free-text entry
- Crew members receive SMS link or open in-app
- One-screen sign-off: "I attended today's safety briefing and understand the content" → tap to sign
- Bilingual: English and Spanish briefing text for each topic
- Timestamped record stored per crew member per session

**Feature 5: Incident Report**
- Structured form: date/time, location, crew member(s) involved, incident type, description, corrective actions
- Photo attachment (1–5 photos)
- Submitted reports stored in incident log
- Export per incident for workers' comp or OSHA reporting

**Feature 6: OSHA-Ready PDF Export**
- One-click export covering a selectable date range
- Export includes: crew roster, training session log with signatures, certificate status with expiry dates, incident report archive
- PDF formatted for presentation to OSHA inspector or workers' comp auditor
- Branded with company name/logo

**Infrastructure:**
- Web app (mobile-first responsive) + progressive web app (PWA) for crew sign-off on any phone
- Email + SMS notifications for cert expiry and daily attestation links
- Secure file storage for certificate uploads and signed attestation records

### Out of Scope for MVP

The following are explicitly deferred to post-MVP:

- **Native mobile app** (iOS/Android) — PWA covers the core use case; native app is a Month 6+ investment
- **Jobber / Service Autopilot / Housecall Pro integration** — high-value but requires API partnership negotiation; Phase 2
- **AI-generated toolbox talk content** — manual templates sufficient for MVP; AI generation is a Year 2 differentiator
- **Workers' comp compliance pack (white-label bundle)** — templated document bundle for insurer audits; Phase 2 feature
- **Advanced analytics / safety scorecard** — meaningful only with 6+ months of data; Phase 2
- **Team/admin roles** (owner vs. foreman vs. viewer) — single-role MVP first; multi-role permissions in Phase 2
- **API / webhook access** — Phase 2 for enterprise and integration partners
- **Canada / Australia / UK regulatory variants** — US-only MVP; international in Year 2
- **QR code-based crew sign-in** — streamlines attestation for low-tech crews; Phase 2

**Explicit "no" for MVP:**
- No scheduling or job management (that's Jobber's job)
- No payroll or HR features
- No geofencing or GPS tracking
- No equipment inventory or maintenance logs

### Future Vision

**Phase 2 (Months 4–9): Depth and Distribution**

- **Workers' comp compliance pack:** Pre-built written safety program template auto-populated with company info + crew training history. One-click "send to insurer" workflow. This transforms the product from a logging tool into a "compliance package" — higher perceived value, higher price point for a $49/month tier.
- **Jobber integration:** Safety status visible inside Jobber's job dashboard. When a job is scheduled, auto-prompt for safety briefing. This is the #1 FSM distribution channel for landscaping.
- **Multi-role access:** Owner, foreman, and crew member roles with distinct permissions. Foreman can run attestations without owner involvement. Critical for companies with 20+ employees.
- **QR code crew sign-in:** Laminated QR code posted at the work truck or job site. Crew scans → signs today's briefing → done without needing a link.

**Phase 3 (Months 10–18): Platform and Expansion**

- **AI toolbox talk generator:** Describe today's job conditions (location, weather, equipment, crew) → AI generates a customized safety briefing. Positions product as the "intelligent safety layer" for trades.
- **HVAC / pressure washing / janitorial expansion:** Hazard templates for adjacent verticals. Each adds 300K+ addressable businesses with the same compliance profile.
- **White-label for workers' comp insurers:** Insurer brands the product and recommends it to policyholders. Insurer gets safety data for underwriting; product gets distribution at zero CAC.
- **Construction vertical depth:** OSHA 300 log automation, Subcontractor Safety Program generation, ISNetworld/PEC/BROWZ compliance documentation.

**Long-term (Year 3+): Acquisition or Platform**

- The product's natural exit is acquisition by a larger FSM platform (Jobber, Service Autopilot, FieldEdge) or a workers' comp insurer/insurtech company seeking safety program data.
- Alternatively, pivot to "safety compliance layer" API that FSM platforms embed natively — B2B2C model at scale.
- Target ARR at acquisition/Series A consideration: $2M–$5M with strong retention data and one institutional distribution partnership in place.
