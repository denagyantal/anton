## Topliner — AI Operating System for Executive Search Agencies at $10K+/mo
- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/hitting-10k-mo-by-using-an-agency-as-both-testing-ground-and-distribution-FF8kooe4FWGH9sHjVrT3
- **Additional Links**: https://topliner.app/, https://thebigsearch.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: Featured in IH "For Tech Founders" section. Founder built the product inside a real executive search agency (The Big Search) before selling externally. Zero ad spend — all organic growth through content and founder network.
- **Revenue Data**: >$10K MRR and growing steadily. Two revenue streams: (1) platform revenue via revenue share on search work executed through the platform, (2) talent market mapping intelligence sold to VCs, PE funds, and enterprises. Built with a hybrid agency-as-distribution model.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Executive search and recruitment agencies
- **Core Value Prop**: Reduces 6 weeks of manual company research and candidate sourcing to 6 hours. "Glass box" AI approach — full transparency and evidence for every recommendation, critical in a trust-heavy industry. The founder notes: "Executive Search seems boring to many indie hackers. But think about it: The people these firms place run the companies that shape our future."
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Executive search is a $20B+ global industry still running on LinkedIn Recruiter, spreadsheets, and gut instinct. The big insight from this founder: build the tool inside an actual agency first, then sell to others. This "agency-as-testbed" model de-risks product development because you're your own first customer. The indie opportunity is NOT competing with Topliner head-on — it's applying the same pattern to adjacent recruiting niches that Topliner doesn't serve: (1) **Staffing agencies for trades** — electricians, welders, CDL drivers. These agencies place 100+ candidates/month but use paper applications and phone calls. A tool that matches trade-certified workers to job openings based on certifications, location, and availability would save 10+ hours/week per recruiter. (2) **Healthcare staffing for small facilities** — rural clinics, nursing homes, and dental offices struggle to find staff. A vertical recruiting tool with credential verification (nursing licenses, CPR certs, background checks) priced at $199/mo beats the generic ATS tools they can't afford or won't use.
- **Our Angle**: Apply Topliner's "build inside an agency" model to **trades staffing**. Partner with or build a small trades staffing operation, develop the internal tool, then sell it. Core features: (1) Worker profiles with certification tracking (welding certs, CDL classes, OSHA cards, EPA licenses), (2) Job matching based on trade, cert requirements, location radius, and pay rate, (3) Automated credential expiry alerts (a CDL driver's medical card expires = can't work = lost revenue), (4) Timesheet and payroll integration for temp placements. Price at $149/mo per recruiter seat. The certification tracking and expiry alerting is the moat — generic ATS tools don't understand that a welder's AWS D1.1 cert is different from their D1.5, or that an electrician needs both a journeyman card AND an OSHA-30. Trades staffing agencies have 50-200% annual worker turnover, meaning constant placement activity and high willingness to pay for efficiency tools.
- **LTD Potential**: 3

## Brickwise AI — YC-Backed AI Property Manager Validates Massive Landlord Pain Point
- **Source**: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/brickwise
- **Additional Links**: https://www.brickwiseai.com/, https://www.brickwiseai.com/blog/how-brickwise-raised-500k-in-48-hours-after-yc, https://x.com/ycombinator/status/1995870045117853752, https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46624067
- **Platform**: HN / YC
- **Type**: Show HN / YC Launch
- **Engagement**: Part of YC F25 batch. Raised $3M+ in 6 months. YC tweeted about them. HN discussion on property management data entry pain points drew engagement. Brickwise claims >$1M in committed ARR pipeline including enterprise contracts.
- **Revenue Data**: >$1M committed ARR (enterprise contracts). $3M+ raised. Founders: ex-LiveLink AI (raised $3M, 150K videos/month), ex-Dataform/Google (grew Dataform revenue from $0 to $100M at Google). Pricing not publicly disclosed but positioned as enterprise/premium.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Property management — landlords and estate agents
- **Core Value Prop**: AI automates the entire maintenance workflow: tenant submits photo + description → AI diagnoses the issue → troubleshoots remotely if possible → schedules contractors → follows up → resolves. Handles tenant calls and messages 24/7. Eliminates the #1 operational burden for property managers: maintenance coordination, which consumes 40-60% of a property manager's time.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Brickwise is going after enterprise property managers and estate agents with VC backing and premium pricing. This creates a huge gap for small landlords and self-managing property owners. There are 10.6M individual landlords in the US, most managing 1-4 properties themselves. Their maintenance workflow: (1) tenant texts/calls about issue, (2) landlord plays phone tag with 3-5 contractors to get quotes, (3) landlord coordinates access with tenant, (4) contractor no-shows, repeat from step 2. A separate HN thread (item 46624067) highlights another acute pain: tenant document data entry — scanned IDs, passports, and applications arriving as unstructured PDFs that need manual entry into CRM systems. OCR exists but requires "a lot of manual cleanup." The indie opportunity is a lightweight AI property assistant for individual landlords at $19-39/mo (vs. Brickwise's enterprise pricing). Core flow: (1) tenant texts issue to AI number, (2) AI diagnoses and asks clarifying questions, (3) AI finds local contractors from a pre-approved list or marketplace, (4) AI coordinates scheduling between tenant and contractor, (5) landlord approves via push notification, (6) AI follows up post-repair for tenant confirmation.
- **Our Angle**: Build "LandlordBot" — an AI maintenance coordinator for self-managing landlords with 1-10 units. NOT a full property management platform (AppFolio, Buildium already exist at $50-150/mo). Just the maintenance piece — the single biggest time sink. Core: (1) Dedicated phone number per property — tenants text maintenance requests, (2) AI triage — diagnoses urgency (emergency = immediate alert to landlord; routine = queued), (3) Contractor dispatch — maintains landlord's preferred contractor list, auto-contacts available contractors for quotes, (4) Scheduling coordination — finds time that works for tenant and contractor without landlord involvement, (5) Expense tracking — auto-logs all maintenance spend per property for tax time. Price at $9/unit/mo (a 4-unit landlord pays $36/mo). The killer insight from Brickwise's traction: maintenance coordination is SO painful that landlords will pay to automate it, but Brickwise is priced for enterprise. A solo landlord managing 4 rental units who spends 5 hours/month on maintenance coordination would gladly pay $36/mo to get that time back. Distribution: BiggerPockets forums (the #1 landlord community), landlord Facebook groups, local real estate investor meetups.
- **LTD Potential**: 5

## Schedo — Niche Appointment Scheduling at $5K MRR Validates Service-Business Scheduling Gap
- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/my-niche-appointment-software-makes-5k-month-e24aae2369
- **Additional Links**: https://schedo.app
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: IH post detailing growth from $0 to $5K MRR. First customer was a local window cleaning business that booked through the scheduling page and asked for website integration — paying $12/month. Over 50% of audience is solopreneurs: financial advisors, life coaches, personal trainers, accountants, photographers, sales professionals.
- **Revenue Data**: $5K MRR. Pricing: $12/month base, Pro version with unlimited meetings and integrations. Distribution via LinkedIn and Reddit communities, plus early PPC advertising.
- **Boring Business Score**: 3
- **Target Industry**: Service-based solopreneurs and small businesses
- **Core Value Prop**: Branded booking page with built-in calendar, Stripe payment integration, and customization for websites and social media. Simpler and cheaper than Calendly for service businesses that need payment collection at booking time.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Schedo is growing but targets a broad audience (solopreneurs across industries). The origin story is telling: the FIRST paying customer was a window cleaning business. Service trades need scheduling with a twist that Calendly/Cal.com don't handle: (1) multi-stop route scheduling (a cleaner books 6 homes across a city — need geographic clustering), (2) job duration estimation (a 2-bedroom clean ≠ a 5-bedroom clean, so slot lengths vary), (3) recurring service schedules (weekly/biweekly/monthly clients who auto-book the same slot), (4) deposit collection at booking (trades lose money on no-shows without deposits). A scheduling tool built specifically for recurring home service businesses (cleaning, lawn care, pool service, window washing) that handles variable-duration jobs, geographic routing, and recurring schedules would capture the long tail that Calendly doesn't serve and Jobber overserves. Price at $29/mo — more than Schedo's $12 but justified by trade-specific features. The recurring schedule feature alone is worth the premium: a cleaning company with 40 recurring weekly clients currently manages their schedule in a spreadsheet or paper planner.
- **Our Angle**: Build "RouteBook" — recurring service scheduling for home service businesses. Not a full FSM tool (no invoicing, no CRM, no dispatch). Just scheduling done perfectly for recurring route-based businesses. Core: (1) Recurring booking engine — clients book recurring slots (weekly Tuesday 10am, biweekly Thursday 2pm), (2) Route optimization — auto-clusters daily jobs by geography to minimize driving time, (3) Variable job durations — 1BR apartment = 1.5hr, 4BR house = 3hr, slots auto-size, (4) Online booking with deposit — clients book from a branded page and pay a deposit (configurable per service type), (5) Automatic rebooking — if a client cancels one occurrence, the recurring schedule continues. Price at $29/mo for solo operators, $49/mo for teams. The recurring schedule + route optimization combo is the wedge — every cleaning/lawn/pool service business with 20+ recurring clients struggles with this exact problem. Distribution: cleaning business Facebook groups, lawn care forums, Nextdoor business advertising.
- **LTD Potential**: 5

## Property Management Document Processing — HN Thread Reveals Acute Pain with Zero Solutions
- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46624067
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45073385 (property management software discussion), https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46554013 (noise evidence generator for tenants)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN
- **Engagement**: Jan 2026 thread asking "How do small property management teams handle data entry from tenant documents?" The sole responder gave a bleak assessment: "each organization handles their documents differently from everyone else" and the problem persists "until we get rid of phone, fax, and email." A separate Aug 2025 thread on property management project management software drew minimal responses, confirming the space is underserved. A Jan 2026 Show HN for a noise evidence generator (noiseevidence.com) got 24 upvotes and 28 comments but was criticized for inaccurate legal data and calibration issues — showing demand for tenant-facing tools but poor execution.
- **Revenue Data**: No existing product identified. Related market data: 300K+ property management companies in US. Average PM manages 40-100 units. Tenant onboarding involves 5-15 documents per tenant (ID, proof of income, application, lease, insurance, pet agreements, vehicle registration). At 10% annual turnover on 100 units = 100-150 documents manually processed per year per manager.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Small property management companies (managing 50-500 units)
- **Core Value Prop**: Tenant applications arrive as a chaotic mix: emailed PDFs, photographed documents, faxed pages, paper forms filled out in-office. Each needs to be parsed, verified, and entered into a property management system (AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager). Currently done manually by admin staff spending 15-30 minutes per application. AI document processing could reduce this to 2-3 minutes: tenant uploads documents → AI extracts name, SSN, income, employer, references → auto-populates the PM's system → flags discrepancies (e.g., stated income doesn't match pay stub) → admin reviews and approves.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The big PM platforms (AppFolio, Buildium) have basic online application forms but don't handle the messy reality of supplemental documents. Tenants submit pay stubs, bank statements, and IDs in random formats. The PM has to manually verify and cross-reference. An AI-powered tenant screening intake tool that: (1) accepts documents via text, email, or upload, (2) auto-extracts key data fields using LLM + OCR, (3) cross-references stated income with pay stub data, (4) auto-populates the PM's existing system via API, (5) generates a tenant profile summary for landlord review — would save 10-20 hours/month for a PM managing 100+ units. Price at $2/application processed or $99/mo flat. The per-application model aligns with the PM's workflow (they can pass the cost to applicants as an "application processing fee" — already standard practice at $25-75/application).
- **Our Angle**: Build "TenantParse" — AI document intake for property management. NOT a full PM platform (don't compete with AppFolio). A focused tool that sits between the tenant and the PM's existing software. Core flow: (1) PM sends tenant a unique upload link, (2) Tenant uploads/photographs ID, pay stubs, bank statements, references, (3) AI extracts all data, cross-references for consistency, flags issues, (4) PM gets a clean tenant summary with confidence scores on each data point, (5) One-click push to AppFolio/Buildium/Rent Manager via API. Price at $99/mo for up to 50 applications, $199/mo unlimited. The consistency-checking is the killer feature — automatically flagging when stated income of $5,000/mo doesn't match a pay stub showing $3,800/mo saves PMs from bad tenants AND liability. Distribution: property management associations (NARPM), PM-focused Facebook groups, AppFolio/Buildium app marketplace listings.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

## NicheNav CRM for Locksmiths — Hyper-Vertical FSM for 25K+ Mom-and-Pop Shops
- **Source**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/indie-hackers-saas-ideas-2025-10-you-can-launch-fast
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/what-niche-are-you-serving-f3ef238c3d, https://buildbite.com/insights/job-management-software-for-tradesmen
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Industry analysis
- **Type**: Discussion / Idea validation
- **Engagement**: Flowjam's "10 Indie Hacker SaaS Ideas for 2025" article specifically calls out a CRM for locksmiths as a validated opportunity, noting 25,000+ mom-and-pop locksmiths in North America. The article provides a concrete validation approach: "Cold-call 50 locksmiths from Google Maps; offer 30-day free trial." IH niche discussion confirms service businesses are underserved by vertical tools. BuildBite's 2026 guide on job management for tradesmen shows the broader trades FSM market is hot.
- **Revenue Data**: Suggested pricing: $49/user/month. Market: 25K+ locksmiths in North America, most are solo operators or 2-3 person shops. No established locksmith-specific software identified. Generic FSM tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) don't include locksmith-specific features. Locksmiths average $50-150/job, handle 5-15 jobs/day, with significant emergency/after-hours work.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Locksmiths (residential, commercial, automotive)
- **Core Value Prop**: Locksmiths have unique operational needs that generic FSM tools miss: (1) Key code lookup tables — locksmiths need instant access to key code databases for different lock brands (Schlage, Kwikset, Medeco, etc.) to cut keys without the original, (2) SMS ETA notifications — emergency lockouts require real-time customer updates ("your locksmith is 12 minutes away"), (3) Auto-invoicing to property management companies — locksmiths do recurring work for PMs who need invoices formatted with property address, unit number, and work order reference, (4) Lockout pricing calculator — prices vary by lock type, time of day (after-hours premium), residential vs. commercial, and whether key cutting or lock replacement is needed. A tool that integrates these trade-specific features with basic dispatch and invoicing would be worth $49/mo to operators currently juggling Google Sheets and their phone's notepad.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Locksmiths are an overlooked micro-vertical. They're too small for ServiceTitan, too specialized for Jobber. The emergency nature of locksmith work (50%+ of jobs are same-day emergency calls) creates specific software needs: (1) real-time dispatch with GPS tracking (customer wants to know when you'll arrive), (2) after-hours job logging (many locksmiths work 24/7 but forget to log 2am lockout calls), (3) property management contract tracking (commercial locksmiths rekeying 50+ units/month for PMs need to track which units are done). The key code database integration is the unique moat — a $49/mo tool that includes a searchable key code database saves locksmiths $20-30/month on standalone key code subscriptions PLUS gives them dispatch, invoicing, and customer management.
- **Our Angle**: Build "LockOps" — the all-in-one tool for locksmiths. Core: (1) Emergency dispatch — accept job from call/text, auto-send customer an ETA link with live GPS tracking, (2) Key code database — searchable lookup for major lock brands integrated into the job flow, (3) PM invoicing — auto-generates property-management-formatted invoices with property details, (4) After-hours job capture — voice memo that auto-transcribes into a job record (locksmith dictates details at 2am, gets a clean job record in the morning), (5) Pricing calculator — configure per-lock-type pricing, auto-calculates with after-hours and emergency surcharges. Price at $49/mo. The key code database + voice job capture are the wedge features that no generic tool offers. Validation: cold-call 50 locksmiths from Google Maps in 3 target cities, offer a 30-day free trial. If 5+ sign up, build it.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

Total: ~$19K MRR / ~$26.5K actual monthly. 13,000+ accounts. Pricing: hybrid subscriptions + one-time purchase options. The founder targeted "very specific low-volume keywords" for SEO — "you target these very specific low-volume keywords and you have very high conversion because there's nothing else there."
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Live events, video production, sports broadcasting, corporate presentations
- **Core Value Prop**: Remote countdown timer that syncs across multiple screens/devices during live events. Production teams (director, audio, lighting, presenter) all see the same timer. Simple concept, critical in live production where timing mistakes are visible to thousands of viewers.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Stagetimer is a masterclass in micro-niche SaaS — a countdown timer shouldn't be a $15K MRR business, but it is because: (1) live production teams NEED reliable synchronized timing, (2) no one else built a good web-based solution, (3) the SEO moat from low-competition keywords drives organic acquisition. The model is replicable: find a niche where professionals use a janky workaround for a critical workflow → build a clean, reliable web tool → own the SEO for hyper-specific keywords. Adjacent boring-niche timing/coordination opportunities: (1) **Church service flow management** — 300K+ US churches run weekly services with tight timing (worship set, sermon, announcements). Most use paper run sheets. A Stagetimer-like tool for church AV teams with integrated lyrics/slides timing would serve a massive, underserved audience. (2) **Auction timing and lot management** — livestock auctions, estate sales, auto auctions need lot timers, bidder tracking, and clerk integration. Auctioneer-specific software exists (Auction Flex) but it's desktop-based and expensive.
- **Our Angle**: This is more of a pattern signal than a single product idea. The "Stagetimer playbook": (1) Find a professional workflow using a janky workaround, (2) Build a clean web tool that solves exactly that one thing, (3) Dominate SEO for the 5-10 long-tail keywords that professionals search, (4) Offer both subscription AND one-time purchase (the hybrid model captures both recurring and one-off users). Applied to boring businesses, the top candidates: **Church service flow manager** ($29/mo, 300K+ US churches), **Auction lot timer and clerk tool** ($79/mo, 30K+ US auctioneers), **Court hearing scheduler for solo attorneys** ($49/mo, extremely specific workflow). The church opportunity is the most interesting for our stack — large addressable market, extreme loyalty (churches rarely switch tools), and minimal competition from VC-backed companies.

Screen Acquire.com and Flippa for micro-SaaS products in: pest control, property management, field service, cleaning, trades invoicing, and other boring niches. Target products doing $2-5K MRR with solid tech but poor marketing. Acquisition budget: $50-150K per product (3-4x annual revenue). Growth playbook: (1) improve onboarding and UX, (2) add AI features (AI-powered scheduling, auto-invoicing, chat support), (3) run an AppSumo LTD launch for cash infusion and user base growth, (4) layer on content marketing for long-tail SEO. ## Home Renovation Tracker — Contractor-Homeowner Collaboration Gap
- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/my-top-40-microsaas-ideas-for-2025-c779bf60ae
- **Additional Links**: https://bigideasdb.com/profitable-saas-micro-saas-ideas-2026, https://www.indiehackers.com/post/what-niche-are-you-serving-f3ef238c3d
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Discussion / Idea validation
- **Engagement**: Listed in IH's "Top 40 MicroSaaS Ideas for 2025" as "a collaboration platform for homeowners and contractors to manage renovation projects." BigIdeasDB's 2026 profitable SaaS list independently identifies home renovation project tracking as a gap. The IH niche discussion confirms contractors are underserved.
- **Revenue Data**: No existing indie product identified at this exact positioning. US home renovation market: $450B+ annually. 4.7M residential renovation projects started per year. Existing tools: Houzz (consumer marketplace, not project management), Buildertrend/CoConstruct ($199-449/mo, target professional builders not homeowner-contractor coordination), HomeZada (consumer-focused, limited contractor collaboration). The gap: affordable homeowner-contractor collaboration tool at $0/homeowner + $29-49/mo contractor.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Home renovation contractors (remodelers, kitchen/bath specialists, general contractors) and homeowners
- **Core Value Prop**: Renovation projects are communication nightmares. Homeowners don't know what's happening on their project. Contractors send sporadic text updates with blurry photos. Change orders are verbal agreements that lead to disputes. Payment milestones are unclear. A shared project dashboard where: (1) contractor posts daily photo updates with notes, (2) homeowner can see project timeline and upcoming milestones, (3) change orders are documented with before photos, scope description, and price — both parties sign digitally, (4) payments are released at milestone completion with photo verification. Think Basecamp but purpose-built for renovation with visual-first updates and change order management.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The renovation industry has a trust problem — the #1 complaint from homeowners is "my contractor doesn't communicate." The #1 complaint from contractors is "homeowners keep changing their mind and then dispute the bill." Both problems stem from poor documentation. Current workarounds: text threads (unsearchable, no structure), email (contractors hate it), Buildertrend (too expensive and complex for a $20K bathroom remodel). An indie tool that costs the homeowner nothing (the contractor pays) and focuses on three things — photo updates, change order documentation, and milestone payments — solves the trust problem for both sides. The contractor benefits because documented change orders = fewer payment disputes. The homeowner benefits because daily photo updates = peace of mind. Price at $29/mo for the contractor (unlimited projects). Homeowner access is free — this is the growth engine (homeowners who have a good experience recommend the tool to their next contractor).
- **Our Angle**: Build "RenoTrack" — renovation project transparency for contractors and homeowners. Core: (1) Photo journal — contractor takes 2-3 photos daily, app auto-timestamps and organizes by room/phase, homeowner gets a push notification with the update, (2) Change order flow — contractor photographs the situation, types one-line description and price impact, homeowner approves or discusses in-app, creates a signed digital record, (3) Milestone payments — contractor sets payment milestones at project start (e.g., demo complete = 25%, rough-in complete = 25%, etc.), homeowner releases payment with one tap when milestone is verified by photos. Free for homeowners. $29/mo for contractors. The change order documentation alone pays for itself: a single documented $2,000 change order that would have otherwise been disputed covers 5 years of subscription. Distribution: HomeAdvisor/Angi contractor profiles (partner with platforms), contractor Facebook groups, Google Ads targeting "renovation project management."
- **LTD Potential**: 5

## Trades Job Board Micro-SaaS — $250/Posting in an Underserved Hiring Channel
- **Source**: https://superframeworks.com/articles/best-micro-saas-ideas-solopreneurs
- **Additional Links**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/indie-hackers-saas-ideas-2025-10-you-can-launch-fast, https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22496129
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Industry analysis
- **Type**: Discussion / Market signal
- **Engagement**: Superframeworks' "Best Micro SaaS Ideas for 2026" independently identifies "Niche Job Board for Skilled Trades" as a top opportunity, noting the ICP as "HR managers at regional HVAC/plumbing companies (50-200 employees)." Flowjam's indie hacker SaaS ideas article validates niche job boards as proven revenue models. The classic HN "What industries are underserved by software" thread repeatedly cites hiring in trades as broken.
- **Revenue Data**: Suggested pricing: $250+ per job posting or $10-$30 per qualified applicant. Estimated revenue potential: $50K-$500K ARR. Market: US skilled trades labor shortage is 650,000+ unfilled positions. Indeed and LinkedIn perform poorly for trades hiring — a welder in Tulsa doesn't have a LinkedIn profile. Craigslist is the default but has zero filtering, verification, or matching. Niche job boards in other verticals (e.g., WeWorkRemotely for remote jobs, AngelList for startups) prove the model works at premium pricing.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, welding, construction trades hiring
- **Core Value Prop**: A job board where trade workers actually are. Core differentiators from Indeed/LinkedIn: (1) Certification-based matching — filter by specific trade certs (journeyman electrician, master plumber, CDL-A, OSHA-30, EPA 608), (2) Skill verification — candidates upload cert photos, platform verifies and displays badges, (3) Geographic radius — trades workers commute; match based on home zip code to job site within a configurable radius, (4) No-resume profiles — trade workers don't have resumes. Profile = certifications + years of experience + tools/equipment owned + availability. Companies post jobs, workers apply with their profile.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The trades hiring crisis is real and worsening: baby boomers retiring, not enough young people entering trades, demand rising.
