## AI-Powered Prior Authorization for Specialty Medical Practices

- **Category**: Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://automaiva.com/vertical-saas-ai-agents-2026/
  - https://wearepresta.com/vertical-ai-startup-ideas-2026-dominating-industry-specific-niches/
  - https://menlovc.com/perspective/software-finally-gets-to-work-the-opportunity-in-vertical-ai/
- **Description**: Generic AI tools fail at healthcare prior authorization because they don't know that United Healthcare requires a specific peer-to-peer review form for lumbar epidural injections that Aetna handles differently. One founder is already earning $41K MRR automating prior auth for physical therapy clinics — but orthopedics, cardiology, mental health, and dozens of other specialties remain untouched. Administrative staff outnumber physicians in US healthcare, and $740B flows annually to administrative services vs only $63B in IT.
- **Target Industries**: Medical/dental practices — orthopedics, cardiology, mental health therapy, dermatology, physical therapy
- **Market Timing**: Growing
- **Example Products**: None known for specialty verticals beyond PT; enterprise: Olive AI (dead), Waystar — but no SMB-focused tools
- **White Space**: Prior auth automation for any specialty practice other than PT. Carrier-specific form logic, peer-to-peer review scheduling, denial tracking — none of this is solved for small practices.
- **Speed to Market**: 4-8 weeks for a single specialty/carrier vertical using LLM APIs. Higher compliance overhead (HIPAA) but straightforward workflow.
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — practice managers want recurring reliability, not one-time purchases. But a lower-priced LTD could acquire early users to train the model.
- **Content Strategy**: "prior authorization software [specialty]", "[specialty] prior auth automation", "reduce claim denials [specialty practice]"

## Subcontractor Compliance Tracking for Construction (Beyond Insurance Certs)

- **Category**: Paper-to-Digital / Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://automaiva.com/vertical-saas-ai-agents-2026/
  - https://wearepresta.com/vertical-ai-startup-ideas-2026-dominating-industry-specific-niches/ (construction AI)
  - https://tracxn.com/d/companies/tradeserve/__bzgaZcxLeU6j7ltLXhgxHGCf7chKhgZ6THsRLITKAZY
- **Description**: General contractors manage dozens of subcontractors and must track not just insurance certificates, but permit histories, lien waiver management, certified payroll documentation (required for public projects), and prevailing wage compliance. "Tracking this manually across spreadsheets is how contractors lose surety bonds and get debarred from public projects." This is a high-stakes, low-glamour workflow that's still done in Excel and email chains.
- **Target Industries**: General contractors, construction project managers, public-sector construction, commercial real estate developers
- **Market Timing**: Early — insurance cert tracking (Compliance Depot, myCOI) exists, but full subcontractor compliance (permits + liens + payroll) is not integrated
- **Example Products**:
  - myCOI (https://mycoi.com) — insurance cert only
  - Compliance Depot — insurance cert only
  - Procore — has some compliance modules but enterprise-priced
- **White Space**: An integrated subcontractor compliance hub covering insurance + permits + lien waivers + certified payroll in one dashboard, priced for mid-market GCs ($50-200/month)
- **Speed to Market**: 4-6 weeks for MVP covering lien waiver tracking + expiration alerts; full suite 3-4 months
- **LTD Fit**: Yes — compliance tools are "set and forget" purchases that GCs would buy once to avoid ongoing headaches
- **Content Strategy**: "subcontractor compliance software", "lien waiver tracking software", "certified payroll tracking construction", "prevailing wage compliance software"

## Subscription Maintenance Plans for Home Service Businesses

- **Category**: SMB Platform Consolidation / Paper-to-Digital
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://www.bdrco.com/blog/home-service-industry-trends/ (Trend #19: Subscription-Based Maintenance Models)
  - https://www.housecallpro.com/industries/plumbing-software/
  - https://www.achrnews.com/articles/165646-7-predictions-for-hvac-in-2026
- **Description**: Home service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, pest control, cleaning) are rapidly shifting toward subscription/maintenance plan models for predictable revenue. But the software to manage these recurring service agreements — scheduling annual tune-ups, tracking which customers are on plans, automating renewal reminders, and handling escalating-tier plans — is either absent or buried inside expensive FSM platforms. This is a repeatable, high-LTV business model that small operators need to implement but can't.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC companies, plumbers, pest control, pool maintenance, gutter cleaning, appliance repair
- **Market Timing**: Growing — customer demand for subscription services is rising, but SMB software tools lag
- **Example Products**:
  - Virtually none at the micro-SMB level
  - ServiceTitan has a maintenance agreement module but costs $300-500+/month
- **White Space**: A standalone "maintenance plan manager" SaaS — sell recurring service agreements, auto-schedule the visits, send renewal reminders, track plan holders. Could sell for $49-99/month or as a LTD.
- **Speed to Market**: 2-3 weeks for a focused MVP (contract tracking + renewal reminders + scheduling)
- **LTD Fit**: Strong — trades owners understand the pain and would buy a tool that "sets up their maintenance plan business"
- **Content Strategy**: "HVAC maintenance plan software", "service agreement management software", "recurring maintenance scheduling tool for contractors"

## AI Administrative Automation for Clinical Documentation

- **Category**: Vertical AI / Paper-to-Digital
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://wearepresta.com/vertical-ai-startup-ideas-2026-dominating-industry-specific-niches/
  - https://menlovc.com/perspective/software-finally-gets-to-work-the-opportunity-in-vertical-ai/
  - https://lootmogul.medium.com/why-vertical-ai-will-build-the-next-billion-dollar-startups-57521660c6ec
- **Description**: Clinicians spend 30-40% of their time on documentation. AI medical scribes are emerging (Nuance DAX, Abridge) but target large health systems. Small practices — dentists, chiropractors, optometrists, mental health therapists — have no affordable option. These practices use generic EHR note templates and manual dictation. A vertical AI scribe built specifically for one specialty (e.g., chiropractic SOAP notes, therapy progress notes, dental procedure documentation) could command $99-299/month per provider.
- **Target Industries**: Chiropractic, mental health therapy, optometry, dentistry, physical therapy, urgent care
- **Market Timing**: Early — enterprise solutions exist but SMB-focused vertical scribes for specific specialties are rare
- **Example Products**:
  - Nuance DAX — enterprise only
  - Abridge — https://www.abridge.com (hospital-focused)
  - Freed AI — https://freed.ai (mental health focus, gaining traction)
- **White Space**: Specialty-specific AI scribes for chiropractic, optometry, or dentistry that integrate with the specific EHR those providers use (ChiroTouch, RevolutionEHR, Dentrix)
- **Speed to Market**: 4-6 weeks for a single-specialty scribe using Whisper + Claude; EHR integration adds 4-8 weeks
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — providers would pay ongoing for reliability, but a limited LTD could capture early adopters
- **Content Strategy**: "AI medical scribe [specialty]", "SOAP note automation [specialty]", "clinical documentation software chiropractor", "therapy progress note AI"

- **LTD Fit**: Low — mission-critical operations tools require ongoing support; MRR model fits better

## Skills Training & Workforce Development Software for Trades

- **Category**: Vertical AI / Paper-to-Digital
- **Signal Strength**: Moderate
- **Sources**:
  - https://www.bdrco.com/blog/home-service-industry-trends/ (Trend #24: Skilled Labor Shortage; Trend #25: Training & Certification)
  - https://wearepresta.com/vertical-ai-startup-ideas-2026-dominating-industry-specific-niches/ (VR/AR for trades training)
  - https://blog.hiringthing.com/2026-vertical-saas-trends (AI recruiting for trades)
- **Description**: The skilled labor shortage in trades is severe and growing — HVAC, plumbing, and electrical all face 20-40% technician shortages. Two software gaps emerge: (1) AI-powered recruiting that matches apprentices to trade contractors by skill/geography, and (2) digital training platforms for trades certifications (EPA 608, NATE, OSHA-10) that replace paper manuals and in-person-only courses. Pricing: AI recruiting commanding $99/user/month in similar verticals.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC contractors, plumbing companies, electrical contractors, vocational schools, apprenticeship programs
- **Market Timing**: Early
- **Example Products**:
  - HiringThing — https://blog.hiringthing.com (generic ATS with trades recruiting angle)
  - No dedicated trades training SaaS identified
- **White Space**: A digital training + certification tracking platform specifically for HVAC/plumbing/electrical apprentices. Let contractors assign EPA 608 study modules, track completion, and get notified when techs are due for recertification.
- **Speed to Market**: 4-6 weeks for a course + tracking MVP; content creation is the harder bottleneck
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — contractors would buy if priced per-technician; certification bodies might license it
- **Content Strategy**: "HVAC technician training software", "EPA 608 study app", "trades apprenticeship tracking software", "electrician continuing education app"
