## FieldFlow — Dead-Simple Job Management for Solo Contractors

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47272986
- **Additional Links**: https://fieldflow-nine.vercel.app/auth
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 1 point, 0 comments (posted ~85 days ago, early-stage)
- **Revenue Data**: Free (targeting $9/month once validated)
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Solo contractors (carpentry, handyman, trades)
- **Core Value Prop**: Invoicing + scheduling + client management with zero bloat. Built by a solo carpenter who refused to pay $39–150/month for tools designed for 20-person teams.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Existing tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan) are built for multi-person operations with fleet tracking, dispatching, etc. Solo operators don't need any of it — they need something that works in 5 minutes. This is a market signal that the low end is completely underserved.
- **Our Angle**: Charge $19–29/month (not $9). Target solo operators across all trades (not just carpenters). Differentiate on: SMS-first notifications to customers, offline-capable mobile PWA, and a Stripe-embedded payment link on every invoice. Add a one-tap "request a review" feature (Google/Yelp) after job close — trades live and die by reviews.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — $59–79 LTD price point, strong AppSumo match

## SecurVO — Compliance & Operations for Service Businesses

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45906762
- **Additional Links**: https://securvo.com
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 2 points, 0 comments (posted ~6 months ago)
- **Revenue Data**: Beta (free 90 days), flat-rate tier pricing model
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Property management, facilities management, field service businesses
- **Core Value Prop**: Recurring task management, document expiration tracking (COIs, licenses, permits), vendor compliance, asset maintenance, SOPs with attestation — all in one platform with no per-seat pricing.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Service businesses currently use spreadsheets or pay $50–200/user/month for generic PM tools not built for compliance work. The compliance burden is massive for property managers (inspections, certifications, vendor insurance tracking). SecurVO's scope may be too broad to achieve product-market fit quickly.
- **Our Angle**: Narrow to property management companies only (20–200 unit portfolios). Key workflows: vendor COI auto-expiry reminders, unit inspection checklists with photo capture, maintenance scheduling tied to lease dates. Offer white-label + property logo on all tenant-facing PDFs. Price at $99–199/month flat. LTD at $299 (property managers have long retention cycles).
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — strong niche but complex onboarding

## Inspec — FF&E Schedule Management for Interior Designers

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45692353
- **Additional Links**: https://inspec.design
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 13 points, 0 comments (posted ~7 months ago)
- **Revenue Data**: Not disclosed (early stage)
- **Boring Business Score**: 3/5
- **Target Industry**: Interior designers / architecture firms
- **Core Value Prop**: Replaces Excel for FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment) scheduling — the process of specifying everything that goes in a room before the furniture arrives. Real-time collaboration, version control, PDF exports, QR codes for on-site contractors.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 100% of interior designers currently use Excel for this. Only 2 known competitors exist. The founder watched his partner deal with this daily — strong personal pain validation. The QR-code-for-contractors angle is genuinely clever and solves a real coordination problem.
- **Our Angle**: Could extend to general design/build project specifications (not just interiors). Add an AI "spec assistant" that auto-suggests product specs from uploaded mood boards. Integrate with Houzz Pro or similar procurement tools. Pricing: $49–99/month per designer, or $199/firm.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — niche but design firms pay well for quality tools

## Construction Payment Management SaaS (Ireland)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47975571 (May 2026 Who Is Hiring thread)
- **Additional Links**: N/A (company name not disclosed in hiring post)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Hiring signal (Who Is Hiring)
- **Engagement**: N/A
- **Revenue Data**: Paying customers in Ireland and UK; 5-person team (3 engineers)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Construction — main contractors and subcontractors
- **Core Value Prop**: Tracking subcontracts, payment applications, and retentions. Replaces spreadsheets that main contractors currently use to manage what they owe to subcontractors and when.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Construction payment processes are notoriously complex (progress billing, retentions, lien waivers, certified payroll). - **Our Angle**: US-focused construction payment compliance tool. Include lien waiver tracking, AIA G702/703 document generation, certified payroll (Davis-Bacon compliance). Target general contractors with 5–50 active subcontractors. Price: $199–499/month. Vertical is extremely price-insensitive.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 — too compliance-heavy for LTD, strong MRR business

- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN

- **Revenue Data**: N/A (market research thread)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Multiple — agriculture, trucking, construction, welding, funeral, locksmith, pest control, automotive repair
- **Core Value Prop**: Community-validated list of software gaps in blue-collar industries. Key recurring themes from comments: (1) agriculture logistics — crop tracking, field management; (2) automotive repair shop management (schedulers, parts ordering); (3) funeral home management; (4) welding shop job tracking; (5) trucking dispatch and compliance (FMCSA/ELD rules).

Technician takes photos, creates annotated inspection report, customer approves/declines via text link. Integrates with labor guides (Mitchellpro). Target: 1–5 bay independent shops, $79–149/month. Strong review-generation angle.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — independent shop owners respond well to lifetime deals

- **Engagement**: N/A
- **Revenue Data**: Breezy (HVAC field service SaaS): 35 staff, 80,000+ active users, $3.8M+ in payouts. Proves the market is real.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential HVAC contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: HVAC-specific FSM — not generic field service. Equipment maintenance tracking (serial numbers, filter dates, warranty status), refrigerant inventory logging (EPA 608 compliance), seasonal service reminder automation, maintenance agreement billing.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: HVAC repair revenue grew 47% per job (2021–2025: $818 → $1,205). Market is booming but existing tools either too generic (Housecall Pro) or too expensive (ServiceTitan at $398+/month). Sweet spot: HVAC-only tool at $99–149/month with built-in refrigerant log compliance and maintenance agreement auto-renewal. Indie-friendly price point.
- **Our Angle**: "HVAC Brain" — refrigerant log + equipment history + maintenance agreements. The EPA 608 compliance angle alone justifies the price. Add automated text reminders ("Your AC filter is due for replacement") as a retention driver. Sell via HVAC association newsletters and Facebook groups.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — HVAC owners are used to paying for tools, LTD converts well at $149–199

- **Type**: Market validation
- **Engagement**: N/A
- **Revenue Data**: Pest control market: $22.7B (2024) → $29.1B (2026), 28% growth
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Independent pest control operators (1–10 technicians)
- **Core Value Prop**: Route optimization + recurring service billing + chemical usage logs (EPA compliance) + customer portal for service confirmations. Current tools (PestPac, FieldRoutes/ServiceTitan) target larger operators at $200–500/month.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Thousands of single-owner pest control companies operate in a $29B market with no affordable SMB-grade software. They track routes in Google Maps, bill via paper invoices, and schedule in spreadsheets. A tool at $49–79/month that handles recurring service routes, chemical logs, and text appointment reminders would convert well.
- **Our Angle**: Chemical application log compliance is a legally required record for licensed operators — makes it a "must-have" not a "nice-to-have." Add route-day planning (Mon=Zone A, Tue=Zone B) and auto-text reminders ("We're coming Thursday between 10–12"). LTD at $99 with $19/month after.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — recurring service business with mandatory compliance = perfect LTD target

- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
