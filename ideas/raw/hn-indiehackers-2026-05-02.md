## Vertical FSM for Single-Trade Operators (HVAC / Pool / Pest Control / Plumbing)

- **Source**: https://medium.com/@e2larsen/saas-for-home-services-a-solopreneurs-edge-de6cc095963b
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/ (SBA Q3 2025: 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools)
  - https://www.workiz.com/ (incumbent — too broad)
  - https://www.housecallpro.com/industries/hvac-software/ (incumbent — priced out of SMB)
  - https://www.fieldpulse.com (incumbent — overwhelming features for 1–3-truck shops)
- **Platform**: HN + Indie Hackers (discussed heavily across both)
- **Type**: Market gap / underserved demand signal
- **Engagement**: SBA Q3 2025 report cited across multiple IH posts; HN "boring businesses" thread surfaced FSM as a top pick
- **Revenue Data**: Incumbents like ServiceTitan report 95%+ gross retention — proving willingness to pay. Indie hackers report $79–149/mo per SMB customer for simpler tools.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Single-trade home service operators (pool cleaning, pest control, HVAC, plumbing) — specifically operators with 1–5 trucks
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, customer history, and route optimization designed *only* for one trade — no feature bloat from generic FSM
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Workiz are all priced for mid-market / enterprise. Solo operators feel overwhelmed. 89% of tradespeople still on spreadsheets/paper. Vertical-specific features (e.g., chemical tracking for pool service, pest-product records for pest control) are missing from generic tools.
- **Our Angle**: Pick one trade (e.g., pool service or pest control — lower competition than HVAC). Build *only* for them. Speak their language. Price at $49–79/mo. Distribution via trade Facebook groups and word of mouth.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — operators buy LTDs when they trust the tool; active trade communities validate fast

- **Type**: Revenue milestone / market validation
- **Engagement**: Multiple IH posts cited dental as top underserved vertical in 2025–26

- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Dental labs + dental practices
- **Core Value Prop**: Streamlines dentist→lab prescription/case communication; AI-assisted shade matching, case planning, and digital Rx interpretation to reduce costly remakes (industry remake rate: 5–8%)

## Youform Pattern: "Affordable [Industry Vertical CRM] Alternative"

- **Additional Links**: (same IH post — Youform $18K MRR story, March 2026)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / validated playbook
- **Engagement**: 100 upvotes, 9 bookmarks, active comments
- **Revenue Data**: $18K MRR (Youform — Typeform alternative). LTD launch: $35K in 40 days at $299→$399.
- **Boring Business Score**: 2/5 (Youform itself isn't boring, but the *playbook* applies to boring verticals)
- **Target Industry**: Any vertical with an overpriced incumbent raising prices (e.g., Mindbody for fitness studios, ServiceTitan for HVAC, Vagaro for salons, eSub for subcontractors)
- **Core Value Prop**: Apply the Youform formula to a boring vertical: find an incumbent that just raised prices or went enterprise, position as "affordable [Incumbent] alternative," launch to the community of people already searching for that incumbent's name
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Typeform raised prices → Youform found $18K MRR. This pattern repeats in every vertical where incumbents go upmarket. Current targets: Mindbody (fitness/wellness studios), ServiceTitan (HVAC), Cliniko (allied health), eSub (construction subcontractors).
- **Our Angle**: Research which vertical SaaS recently raised prices or pivoted upmarket. Build the simpler, cheaper version. Position explicitly as "affordable [name] alternative." Distribution via Reddit complaints + social listening.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — exact playbook used by Youform; validated model

## Market Trends Summary (HN + IH, May 2026)

**Key signals from today's research:**

1. **Vertical SaaS is eating horizontal.** The pattern from both HN and IH: solo founders who pick one specific trade/profession and build a surgical tool beat generic tools with 3x pricing power and better retention.

2. **"Affordable alternative" positioning still works.** Youform's Typeform-displacement play ($18K MRR, $35K LTD revenue in 40 days) is a repeatable playbook. Find a vertical incumbent that raised prices. Position explicitly as the affordable alternative. Launch into the Reddit/Facebook communities already complaining.

3. **89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools.** SBA Q3 2025 report. The home services market ($500B+ annually) is still dominated by pen, paper, and spreadsheets for small operators. ServiceTitan et al. serve mid-market; 1–3 truck shops are orphaned.

4. **Boring businesses outlast AI hype.** HN thread on boring businesses is still highly cited (70 points, 76 comments, Jan 2024). Government form-filling software example: $30M ARR. HN community increasingly bullish on "unglamorous" software.

5. **Micro-niches grew 340% vs. broad platforms** (Gartner Q4 2025 SaaS Market Report). Solo founders who build "surgical instruments, not Swiss Army knives" are hitting $5K–$50K MRR.

6. **Hotel PMS is a glaring gap.** Two incumbents, both described as "garbage" by an HN professional who works with them. Zero VC interest = permanent opportunity for a solo founder.
