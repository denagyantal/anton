## Pest Control Vertical SaaS (Field Operations)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://www.onhand.pro/p/i-wanted-to-build-vertical-saas-for-pest-control-i-took-a-technician-job-instead
  - https://tldr.tech/dev/2026-03-25 (featured the story)
  - https://gorilladesk.com/ (existing competitor — bootstrapped, 3,100+ customers)
  - https://www.fieldroutes.com/ (competitor)
  - https://www.pestpac.com/ (competitor, WorkWave)
  - https://www.briostack.com/ (competitor)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN / Founder story (posted ~March 25, 2026)
- **Engagement**: High engagement, featured in TLDR Dev newsletter
- **Revenue Data**: GorillaDesk (competing bootstrapped product) has 3,100+ companies, 437% YTD growth — market is validated
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control, lawn care, field service
- **Core Value Prop**: The HN post documents a founder who took a real pest control technician job (not a shadowing day) to learn the domain before building. Key pain points discovered: slow technician recruitment (2–3 months to ramp each hire), outdated dispatch systems, no internal innovation incentives, heavy paper-based workflows.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Incumbent tools (PestPac, GorillaDesk, FieldRoutes) exist but are generic or legacy. Opportunities: AI-assisted routing and chemical dosage recommendations, technician onboarding/training modules built in, regulatory compliance tracking (pesticide licensing per state), customer-facing service history portal, integration with GPS/fleet tracking. The founder concluded selling SaaS to existing operators is hard — they'd rather launch their own service business — which means software UX for operators is still very poor.
- **Our Angle**: Build specifically around technician retention & onboarding (the #1 pain point mentioned), plus AI route optimization with chemical inventory tracking. Target small independents (1–10 trucks) underserved by enterprise tools.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — Clear willingness to pay ($49–$149/mo/user is standard in the niche), LTD at $59–$99 could capture indie operators

## Locksmith CRM / Dispatch Tool

- **Source**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/indie-hackers-saas-ideas-2025-10-you-can-launch-fast (aggregated Indie Hackers signal)
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://www.getapp.com/all-software/locksmith/f/scheduling/ (market validation — 255+ tools, no dominant indie player)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers (community signal)
- **Type**: Market gap discussion
- **Engagement**: Recurring recommendation in multiple indie hacker forums
- **Revenue Data**: $49/user/month pricing validated; 25,000+ mom-and-pop locksmiths in North America alone
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Locksmith businesses (residential, commercial, automotive)
- **Core Value Prop**: Generic CRMs don't understand locksmith-specific workflows: 24/7 dispatch, key-code lookup tables, automotive lockout jobs, after-hours emergency pricing, property manager invoicing. Locksmiths use pen-and-paper or generic tools that don't fit their job types.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: No bootstrapped, locksmith-only CRM at a fair price point. Existing tools (ServiceTitan, Jobber) are designed for HVAC/plumbing and feel wrong. Key missing features: key-code database integration, SMS ETA to customers, auto-invoice to property managers, emergency job surge pricing, license/insurance document tracking.
- **Our Angle**: "Built by a locksmith, for locksmiths" — 3 core features only (dispatch, key-code lookup, invoicing) with a mobile-first UI. Cold-call distribution + free 30-day pilots. Integrate with QuickBooks and Stripe Day 1.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — Perfect LTD candidate, low churn once key-code library is populated, $59–$99 LTD maps well to $49/mo subscription

## HVAC-Specific Business Management Software

- **Source**: https://www.bdrco.com/blog/hvac-business-software-guide/ (industry signal)
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://www.housecallpro.com/industries/hvac-software/ (competitor — Housecall Pro)
  - https://www.getapp.com/operations-management-software/service-dispatch/f/scheduling/ (255+ dispatch tools, fragmented market)
- **Platform**: HN / industry research (market gap from multiple 2025–2026 HN discussions on vertical SaaS)
- **Type**: Market gap / industry analysis
- **Engagement**: Multiple HN threads on vertical SaaS referenced HVAC as the canonical example of an underserved trade
- **Revenue Data**: Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan serve 100k+ businesses at $49–$300/mo — market is $500M+; indie-sized slice is very accessible
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC contractors (1–10 trucks)
- **Core Value Prop**: Current tools are too complex and priced for large shops. Small HVAC operators (1–5 techs) need: equipment maintenance tracking per unit (serial number, install date, filter size, refrigerant type), seasonal maintenance reminders, refrigerant inventory/EPA logging, flat-rate pricing books, and simple dispatch. Generic field service tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) miss HVAC-specific workflows.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Refrigerant handling logs (EPA Section 608 compliance) are still done on paper at most small shops. Opportunity: compliance-first HVAC tool with equipment history, EPA log automation, and maintenance reminders — a feature no generic tool fully handles.
- **Our Angle**: Lead with compliance (refrigerant log + EPA 608) as the wedge — required by law, painful manually, zero existing indie solutions. Price at $39–$79/mo per tech.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — High willingness to pay for compliance tools; LTD $79 makes sense

## Vertical AI SaaS for Trades — "Has AI Killed Vertical SaaS?" (HN Discussion Signal)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46662857
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47040781 ("10 years building vertical software: are we cooked?")
  - https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46618911 ("The Future of Vertical SaaS Is Personal Software")
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Discussion / market signal
- **Engagement**: Multiple threads, high discussion volume in Q1 2026
- **Revenue Data**: Vertical SaaS companies see 35–60% higher retention than horizontal platforms; PE firms hit $15.3B in vertical SaaS deals in Q1 2025; 12x+ valuation multiples vs 5.2x for horizontal
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: All boring trades (HVAC, plumbing, pest control, landscaping, cleaning)
- **Core Value Prop**: HN consensus: AI hasn't killed vertical SaaS — it's accelerated the opportunity. Generic AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude) can't replace purpose-built vertical tools because the moat is workflow embeddedness, not data. A funeral home director with 500 event templates, or a repair shop with 2,000 parts transactions, won't leave. The opportunity: build AI *into* vertical tools (smart routing, auto-invoice, compliance checks) rather than compete with horizontal AI.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Nobody has built AI-native vertical SaaS for small trade businesses (1–10 employees). Current tools (ServiceTitan, Jobber) are adding AI features but too slowly and too expensively for small operators.
- **Our Angle**: Build a "vertical AI agent" for a specific trade — e.g., an HVAC business co-pilot that handles scheduling, sends maintenance reminders, drafts invoices, and flags low refrigerant inventory automatically.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Pool Service Route Management Software

- **Source**: https://www.getapp.com/industries-software/plumbing/f/scheduling/ (market signal)
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://launchingmax.com/micro-saas-ideas-2025/ (pool service mentioned as underserved)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers community (market gap signal)
- **Type**: Market gap / underserved niche
- **Engagement**: Recurring mention in 2025 micro-SaaS niche roundups
- **Revenue Data**: ~$500–$800/mo per pool route is typical for service techs; 60,000+ pool service businesses in the US; standard software pricing $29–$79/mo
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Swimming pool maintenance & service businesses
- **Core Value Prop**: Pool service technicians drive fixed weekly routes, test water chemistry, and perform maintenance. Current tools are generic field service apps that don't understand pool-specific data: chemical readings (pH, chlorine, alkalinity), equipment notes per pool (pump model, filter type, heater brand), recurring treatment schedules, and chemical inventory. Most pool techs use paper logs or spreadsheets.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: No purpose-built, indie-priced pool route management tool exists. Skimmer (the main competitor) charges $99+/mo and has poor mobile UX. Opportunity: mobile-first app with chemical log, customer pool profile (equipment details), route optimization, and auto-invoice. QR code on equipment for scan-to-log.
- **Our Angle**: Lead with the chemical log (required by many counties for licensed operators) + QR scan for pool equipment history. Target FL, TX, CA — highest density of pool service businesses. $39/mo or $149 LTD.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — Perfect LTD fit: predictable, recurring use case, high willingness to pay, clear switching cost from paper
