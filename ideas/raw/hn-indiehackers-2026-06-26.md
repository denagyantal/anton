## 1. ZenMaid — Cleaning Service Scheduling SaaS ($3M/yr)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/from-a-cleaning-side-hustle-to-a-3m-yr-saas-for-cleaning-services-suhsqkDZB1zIwRmXxrFm

- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5

## 5. Pest Control Vertical SaaS — Field Research Story (HN Discussion)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: https://www.onhand.pro/p/i-wanted-to-build-vertical-saas-for-pest-control-i-took-a-technician-job-instead
- **Platform**: Hacker News (linked discussion) + OnHand.pro article (March 2026)
- **Type**: In-depth industry research / founder story
- **Engagement**: Active discussion thread, 2026
- **Revenue Data**: Author closed $30k ARR within 21 days as a salesperson. Experienced reps doing $800k-$1.2M ARR/year.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control ($30B TAM in US)
- **Core Value Prop**: The author went undercover as a pest control technician and found: core system was Salesforce modified beyond recognition, techs complained constantly, onboarding required 10+ apps, quoting process nearly lost $24k deals due to multi-signature bureaucracy. Massive software pain.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Existing pest control software (PestPac, FieldRoutes, ServiceTitan) is expensive and enterprise-focused. Small independent pest control operators (1-10 trucks) need scheduling, route optimization, chemical usage logs (regulatory requirement), and recurring subscription billing — none of which cheap tools handle well. The author confirmed companies doing $800k-$1.2M ARR per rep with "very low churn." Extremely sticky, recurring-revenue businesses.
- **Our Angle**: Build pest control-specific vertical SaaS with regulatory compliance baked in (chemical application records, technician licensing tracking, state reporting). Undercut PestPac/FieldRoutes pricing for operators with 1-5 trucks. Very strong LTD candidate — pest control owners pay for tools that reduce regulatory risk.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Summary

**Total ideas found**: 12

**Top 3 most promising**:

1. **STR Cleaning Operations Tool (Idea #12)** — Fresh niche, no dominant player, Airbnb API integration creates a defensible moat, ZenMaid's success proves the parent market exists. LTD at $59-$99 is very realistic. Target: professional Airbnb cleaning companies.

2. **Solo Contractor FSM ($5-$49/month) (Idea #2/3, FieldFlow/FieldLedgr pattern)** — Multiple HN founders independently building this in early 2026 confirms real demand. Race to be the winner. Key differentiation: go trade-specific (HVAC, plumbing) rather than generic. Existing tools are $39-$150+/month for features solo operators don't need. LTD at $59-$79 would convert well.

3. **Pest Control Vertical SaaS for Small Operators (Idea #5/10)** — The $30B TAM pest control market has expensive enterprise software (PestPac, FieldRoutes) but nothing modern and affordable for 1-5 truck operators. The OnHand.pro undercover story validates massive operational pain. Regulatory compliance (chemical logs, license tracking) creates sticky retention. LTD at $99 is viable.

**Key themes observed**:

- **Solo/small operator gap is everywhere**: Every boring vertical has the same problem — enterprise software (ServiceTitan, Fleetio, Samsara) prices out small operators, generic cheap tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) don't go deep enough on trade-specific workflows. The $49-$99/month sweet spot is consistently underserved.
- **Sub-vertical specialization outperforms generic FSM**: ZenMaid ($3M/yr) beats generic tools for cleaning. Rentman ($15M+ ARR) beats generic tools for AV rental. The pattern holds across every niche — the winner goes specific, not broad.
- **Multiple indie builders converging on trades software in 2026**: FieldFlow (March 2026), FieldLedgr (April 2026), FleetFix (January 2026) — multiple Show HN posts in the same 4-month window confirms this is a zeitgeist moment. First-mover with distribution wins.
- **Airbnb/STR cleaning is the freshest sub-vertical**: Emerged from the STR boom (2018-2024), not well-served by existing cleaning SaaS. Airbnb API integration is a technical moat most bootstrapped competitors won't build.
- **Failure pattern confirmed by HN**: Generic "CRM for all trades" consistently fails. Deep specialization (pest control regulations, HVAC refrigerant tracking, STR photo checklists) consistently wins. Niche depth creates retention; breadth creates churn.
