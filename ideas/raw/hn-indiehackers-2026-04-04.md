## HVAC Field Service Software (Replace ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro)

- **Engagement**: Main HN hiring thread for April 2026 (hundreds of jobs)
- **Revenue Data**: N/A (startup signal, not indie hacker product) — but ServiceTitan reportedly charges $200–$500+/mo per user; legacy product; lots of churn
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential HVAC, plumbing, electrical
- **Core Value Prop**: "Breezy" is hiring an AI Product Engineer to build modern field service software for residential HVAC companies, replacing ServiceTitan and Housecall Pro with better UX + automation + applied AI. The incumbents are bloated, expensive, and hated.

- **Type**: Who is Hiring — demand signal

## Skilled Trades Recruiting Platform

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/ (Bootstrapped SaaS Niches 2026)
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/should-i-just-create-a-boring-b2b-saas-b6181991c0
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / research synthesis
- **Type**: Market opportunity / discussion
- **Engagement**: Referenced across multiple IH and industry reports
- **Revenue Data**: No specific data, but trades recruiting identified as major underserved niche
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC, electrical, plumbing, construction subcontractors
- **Core Value Prop**: Contractors can't find qualified electricians, plumbers, and HVAC technicians using Indeed or LinkedIn. Trades workers don't use LinkedIn. The market needs a vertical job board + recruiting tool specifically for licensed tradespeople.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Indeed/LinkedIn are too generic. Trades require license verification, journey/apprentice status, tool/equipment familiarity. The existing specialized options (e.g., Tradesmen International) are staffing agencies, not software. A SaaS that lets contractors post jobs, verify licenses, and run a talent pool for ongoing work is missing.
- **Our Angle**: Job board + lightweight ATS for trade contractors. Verify apprentice/journeyman/master licenses via state databases. Commission model ($200–$500 per hire) + monthly subscription ($99/mo) for active talent pool. Could expand to include skills assessments.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — commission model makes LTD awkward; subscription component can work at $59–$99

## Specialty Inventory Management for Niche Businesses

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.brewersassociation.org/ (9,778 craft breweries in 2025)
- **Platform**: Research synthesis / Indie Hackers discussion
- **Type**: Market opportunity
- **Engagement**: Referenced in bootstrapped SaaS niches research
- **Revenue Data**: Craft breweries lose avg $12,000/yr to inventory issues; 9,778 active US craft breweries
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Craft breweries, vintage clothing stores, artisan food producers, comic book stores
- **Core Value Prop**: Generic inventory systems (QuickBooks, Shopify) fail niche businesses with specialized inventory attributes. Craft breweries need batch tracking, fermentation stages, keg locations, and ingredient expiration. Vintage clothing needs era/brand/condition attributes. None of these map to standard SKUs.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 9,778 craft breweries × $12,000/yr in inventory waste = $117M in annual pain. Most use spreadsheets or generic systems with workarounds. A purpose-built solution charging $99–$199/mo is a clear win. Similar math applies for vintage retail and artisan food.
- **Our Angle**: Start with craft breweries specifically. Batch manager, raw materials inventory (hops, grain, yeast), keg tracking (full/empty/at distributor), taproom vs. cans sales. Integrate with BreweryDB. $149/mo per brewery. Word-of-mouth between brewers at festivals is strong.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — breweries will pay; LTD at $499–$799 for perpetual license is viable
