## Craftplan — Open-Source Production Management for Bakeries (577 Upvotes on Show HN)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46847690
- **Additional Links**: https://github.com/puemos/craftplan, https://craftplan.fly.dev
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 577 upvotes, 167 comments (massive engagement for a boring-business tool)
- **Revenue Data**: Open-source, no revenue yet. But 577 upvotes = massive validation signal. Built with Elixir + Ash Framework + Phoenix LiveView + PostgreSQL.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Micro-bakeries / small-batch food manufacturers
- **Core Value Prop**: Recipe management with versioned BOMs and cost rollups, inventory tracking with lot traceability and allergen tracking, demand forecasting, order management, production batch planning, and purchasing workflows. Built by a developer for his wife's micro-bakery because existing solutions were "either prohibitively expensive or overly generic."
- **Gap/Opportunity**: HN comments reveal the pain extends far beyond bakeries — 3D printing shops, artisan food producers, small manufacturers, and franchise operators all need affordable production management. Enterprise ERPs (SAP, NetSuite) are overkill. The key quote from the creator: "Data structure is almost everything, then comes business logic." Comments debate whether custom-built tools threaten traditional ERP vendors — consensus says small operators benefit most.
- **Our Angle**: Fork the concept (not the code) and build a hosted SaaS version targeting specific small-batch verticals: craft breweries, small cosmetics manufacturers, artisan food producers. Charge $49-99/mo. The open-source version validates demand but won't monetize well. A hosted version with onboarding, templates, and integrations (QuickBooks, Shopify) is the play. LTD at $199 for lifetime access.
- **LTD Potential**: 5

# Market Signals & Trends

## Key Themes This Week

1. **Small-batch manufacturing is desperate for affordable software** — Craftplan's 577 upvotes on Show HN is an extraordinary validation signal. Bakeries, craft breweries, cosmetics makers, and artisan food producers all need recipe/inventory/production management but can't afford enterprise ERPs. The open-source approach validates demand but leaves monetization wide open for a hosted SaaS version.

2. **The micro-SaaS acquisition model is maturing** — Pascal Levy-Garboua (Noosa Labs) at $120K MRR proves you don't need to build from zero. Boring SaaS products with neglected onboarding and pricing are acquisition targets. Sendtric (email countdown timers!) is his biggest earner — the boringest possible product.

3. **AI booking assistants are the next salon/service business battleground** — Softalon's AI booking assistant across messaging channels is the genuine innovation, not another scheduling UI. Multi-channel AI receptionist (WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, SMS) is applicable to any appointment-based service business. This is where salon tech is headed.

4. **"Build for one trade, go deep" keeps getting validated** — Spectora ($40M ARR in home inspection), Stagetimer ($15K MRR in event timers), Craftplan (577 upvotes for bakery management). The pattern is consistent: pick one boring trade, understand their exact workflow, build exactly what they need, show up to their communities daily.

5. **The invoicing flood continues with zero trade specificity** — Invox (Feb 2026) and Invoice Builder (Jan 2026) join the pile of generic invoicing tools on Show HN. None target trades. The persistent demand signal combined with persistent lack of differentiation = clear opportunity for trade-specific invoicing.

6. **Construction automation commands premium pricing** — Hive ERP sold $500K in contracts before open-sourcing. Construction companies will pay enterprise money for workflow automation but need pre-configured, industry-specific solutions, not configurable frameworks.
