## CATEGORY 7: SMB Platform Consolidation

### The Market Structure Problem
The trades software market has:
1. **Enterprise tier** (ServiceTitan, BuildOps) — complex, expensive, enterprise-grade
2. **SMB tier** (Housecall Pro, Jobber) — simpler but still broad/horizontal
3. **Micro-SMB tier** (Service Shark, Sera, FieldPulse, Workiz) — cheapest, underserved

**Market sizing:**
- Field service management market: $4.43B (2022) → projected $11.78B by 2030 (13.3% CAGR)
- ServiceTitan TAM estimate by leadership: $30B+
- HVAC service management software: $2.0B (2025) → $7.03B by 2035 (13.44% CAGR)
- Home services industry total: ~$487B by 2026; app-driven segment growing 18% annually

**ServiceTitan went public (NASDAQ, December 2024)**
- IPO signals institutional validation of trades software as a category
- ServiceTitan is now expanding aggressively into all trades (lawn, pest, etc.) post-IPO
- **URL:** https://www.achrnews.com/articles/164030-servicetitan-ipo-intends-dominance-amongst-all-trades

### Platform Consolidation Products

**Housecall Pro** — $147M raised, ~20K customers (2021 data: $37.2M revenue)
**Jobber** — Mid-market focus; 100K+ service businesses
**FieldPulse** — Budget alternative; estimates/scheduling/invoicing/payments
**Service Fusion** — $192/month unlimited users; HVAC/plumbing/electrical
**Workiz** — Scheduling + payments, mid-tier
**Sera (sera.tech)** — HVAC/plumbing/electrical specialist; $399/month base

### All-in-One Construction

**Contractor Foreman** — $49/month, all-in-one for general contractors — https://contractorforeman.com/
**Knowify** — Trade contractors; handles both multi-phase construction and single-day service calls, integrates with QuickBooks
**BuildOps** — Commercial trades; $127M, unicorn (see Category 3)

### Platform Consolidation Pain
- **URL:** https://www.planera.io/post/best-construction-management-software
- Contractors moving away from multiple software solutions (separate scheduling, invoicing, materials, communication tools)
- "Traditional spreadsheets and siloed tools unable to keep up" — driving demand for unified platforms

## CATEGORY 10: Key Macro Trends Synthesized

### Trend 1: AI Voice Agents Entering Every Trade Vertical
- "Larry" (HVAC/plumbing/electrical), AgentZap, Arch — AI phone answering tailored per trade
- 24/7 call handling = no missed leads = 5–25% revenue lift (claimed)
- This is now a product category, not just a feature

### Trend 2: Estimating/Quoting Automation Per Trade
- Rebar for HVAC/plumbing/electrical (blueprint reading, $14M raised)
- Equivalent gaps: residential HVAC, roofing, landscaping, pest control
- Current state: printed blueprints + spreadsheets + phone calls to distributors

### Trend 3: Materials Procurement Becoming Its Own Platform
- Kojo: $94M raised, $5B+ annual materials spend through platform, 3.5x ARR growth
- The "materials" workflow is separate from scheduling/FSM and is massive

### Trend 4: ServiceTitan Going Enterprise Post-IPO = SMB Gap Opens
- ServiceTitan now moves upmarket post-IPO
- 2.5M home service pros need simpler, cheaper tools
- Topline Pro, Sera, FieldCamp, Service Shark all targeting this gap

### Trend 5: Compliance + Documentation Becoming Software-Mandatory
- New state regulations (habitability, pesticide, OSSF) requiring digital records
- Food trucks, daycares, cleaning services — compliance documentation niche
- Same pattern: paper → software driven by regulatory requirement

### Trend 6: Niche Scheduling Emerging as Standalone Category
- Escape rooms, dog groomers, music teachers, mobile services
- Generic booking tools (Calendly, Acuity) don't handle industry-specific workflows
- GorillaDesk, FieldCamp, Fieldwork each serve only 1–3 sub-verticals

### Trend 7: "Vertical AI OS" — Full Stack Replacement
- Lupa (vet): replaces entire disconnected software stack with one AI-native platform
- Adit (dental): consolidates texting, payments, reminders, imaging, analytics
- Pattern: pick a vertical with 5+ fragmented tools, build the unified AI-native replacement
- Lupa's 50x revenue growth between rounds validates the "full replacement" approach

### Trend 8: Insider-Founder Advantage
- Rebar CEO was an HVAC estimator before founding the company
- BuildOps founders had commercial contracting experience
- Deep domain expertise = knowing *which* workflow to automate first
- IH community validates: generic founders struggle with trades sales; insiders succeed
