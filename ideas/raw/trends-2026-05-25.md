## AI Voice & Front-Office Agents for Home Services Trades

- **Category**: Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://fortune.com/2026/04/27/avoca-ai-agents-missed-calls-hvac-plumbing-roofing-kleiner-perkins-chen-shrivastava-braswell/
  - https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/avoca-raises-125m-at-1b-valuation-to-power-americas-services-economy-with-ai-302753962.html
  - https://www.inc.com/chloe-aiello/this-startup-gives-ai-superpowers-to-hvac-plumbing-and-roofing-companies/91266172
  - https://medium.com/lerer-hippeau-ventures/please-welcome-mura-the-ai-platform-revolutionizing-commercial-field-service-operations-e636c4317a8d
- **Description**: AI agents that answer missed calls, schedule jobs, follow up on estimates, and handle dispatch are becoming the top investment category in home services software. Avoca hit $1B valuation in April 2026 after raising $125M from Kleiner Perkins + General Catalyst. Netic raised a $23M Series B at $450M valuation in late 2025. These companies target HVAC, plumbing, roofing, and electrical — industries where 40–60% of incoming calls go unanswered during busy periods, costing thousands per week in lost bookings.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, pest control, appliance repair
- **Market Timing**: Growing — early adopters exist, mainstream adoption just starting
- **Example Products**:
  - Avoca (https://www.avoca.ai/) — AI phone agents + dispatch for trades; 800+ customers, $125M raised, $1B valuation
  - Netic (https://netic.com/) — AI revenue engine for home services; $23M Series B
  - Mura — AI back-office / order-to-cash for commercial HVAC
- **White Space**: Smaller operators (1–5 trucks/techs) can't afford Avoca's pricing. Niche verticals like pool service, irrigation, septic, chimney sweep have no AI phone agent yet. Bilingual (English/Spanish) voice AI for trades serving Latino markets. AI agents for *outbound* win-back campaigns in HVAC.
- **Speed to Market**: 3–5 weeks for MVP using Claude + Twilio + existing FSM webhooks (ServiceTitan, Jobber)
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — trades owners are price-sensitive but ROI is concrete (recovered calls = $$). Freemium or pay-per-call may work better than LTD.
- **Content Strategy**: "missed call HVAC", "answer calls while on job", "HVAC scheduling AI", "auto-book service calls"

ServiceTitan's 95%+ gross retention and $9.5B+ IPO valuation (late 2024) validate this market.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, electrical, plumbing, landscaping, commercial field service
- **Market Timing**: Growing — incumbents consolidating with AI features, gap opening for SMB-priced alternatives
- **Example Products**:

  - Jobber (https://getjobber.com/) — SMB field service, $50–200/mo
  - FieldEdge, BuildOps — mid-market trades FSM
- **White Space**: AI-native FSM priced for micro-operators (<5 employees) at $29–79/mo. Industry-specific FSM for underserved verticals: pool/spa service, chimney sweep, pressure washing, mobile car detailing. Voice-first job logging for techs who can't type on the job.
- **Speed to Market**: 4–6 weeks for a niche FSM MVP (one vertical, core scheduling + invoicing + AI notes)
- **LTD Fit**: Strong — trades owners love a one-time tool payment; compare to Jobber's recurring subscription friction
- **Content Strategy**: "HVAC software small business", "field service app free", "[trade] job management software", "replace ServiceTitan"
