## Cross-Cutting Observations

### What HN/IH Confirms About Boring Business SaaS in 2026

1. **Validation via acquisition**: PestRoutes ($300M acquisition), ZenMaid ($3M/yr bootstrapped) — both prove the boring niche SaaS model works at scale.

2. **The "too boring for VC, too unsexy for indie hackers" gap**: The July 2026 Show HN list had zero trades/field service tools in the top 100. This is the moat — no competition from the typical HN builder demographic.

3. **Small operator abandonment is universal**: Every field service vertical (pest control, HVAC, plumbing, lawn care, cleaning, junk removal) has the same pattern: enterprise tools too expensive/complex, generic tools don't fit workflows, small operators stuck on paper/spreadsheets.

4. **Stripe's own data confirms payment adoption**: FSM platforms went from 27% to 40% payment integration in one year (2024→2025) — the category is digitizing rapidly and early movers will capture market share before the window closes.

5. **LTD is highly viable**: Trades operators are price-sensitive and skeptical of recurring SaaS (they've been burned by unused subscriptions). LTD at $299-799 is a proven acquisition wedge in this segment (ZenMaid's first customer was a $1,000 lifetime deal).
