## Hyper-Local Business AI Agent for Home Services (Plumbers, HVAC, Salons)

## Key Trends & Signals from This Session

1. **AI-augmented boring tools** — The theme across IH and HN in 2025-2026 is not "build AI" but "add AI to boring workflows." Missed call response, route optimization, invoice drafting. The AI is invisible; the value is the workflow.

2. **Distribution is the hard problem** — Multiple IH commenters (April 2026) note that reaching tradespeople is the real challenge. They're not on Product Hunt or HN. They're in supply house parking lots, contractor Facebook groups, and local trade associations. Any winning product in this space needs an offline or community-based GTM.

3. **Solo-operator sweet spot** — The gap in almost every boring vertical is 1-10 employee businesses. Enterprise tools are too expensive/complex; consumer apps don't fit B2B workflows. Products priced at $39-$149/mo for this tier consistently show LTD and MRR success.

4. **Vertical depth beats horizontal breadth** — Stagetimer's lesson: own one workflow in one industry completely rather than building a general tool. HVAC refrigerant tracking + scheduling beats a generic field service platform. Dental medication reference beats a general drug database.

5. **FSM market validated and growing** — $5.64B → $9.68B (2025-2030). Pest control, lawn care, and pool service are the most underserved sub-niches with no dominant affordable tool below $100/mo.
