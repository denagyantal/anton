- **Additional Links**:

- **Type**: One-person SaaS testimonial + IH discussion
- **Engagement**: 192+ comments on profitable SaaS thread; multiple IH posts
- **Revenue Data**: HN commenter: "I make about $10k/month with a one-person SaaS... Some kind of project management/CRM type saas for a specific industry. Highly tailored to that industry from my dad's lifetime experience working in it. Currently have 34 paying companies with about 300 employees total using it daily." IH medium article: solo builder hit 80 plumber customers, $37k ARR in first year at $39/mo.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, landscaping — field service trades with <10 employees
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, and client management built for one trade. Not generic like Jobber or ServiceTitan (too expensive, too complex for micro-operators).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ServiceTitan costs $200-$700/month and is overkill for a 3-person plumbing shop. Jobber is the mid-market option but still too broad. A hyper-specialized tool for ONE trade (e.g., only plumbers, only HVAC) that mirrors how they actually work — truck dispatch, parts ordering, flat-rate pricing — would win on UX and price.
- **Our Angle**: Pick the single biggest trade by number of self-employed operators (plumbing has ~500k businesses in the US), go ultra-niche, undercut Jobber by 50%, build only the 4 features they actually use daily.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — trades love LTDs; they buy once, hate monthly fees, will pay $249-$499 upfront.

## Bank Statement / Financial PDF Parser SaaS

- **Additional Links**:
  - https://medium.com/@urano10/30-microsaas-niches-poised-to-explode-in-2025-82f0264d4457 (Idea #20: PDF-to-API for Legacy Industries)
  - https://twitter.com/tarasowski/status/1677197503018532865

- **Type**: Revenue milestone (shared link) + discussion

A friend works in a company doing bank statement conversion — hundreds of employees, brings in millions of dollars every month." PDF-to-API at $100-$300/month is the medium article target.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Mortgage brokers, loan officers, accountants, financial advisors — anyone who manually re-enters data from bank PDFs
- **Core Value Prop**: Upload a bank statement PDF → get clean CSV / structured JSON / QuickBooks-compatible export. AI handles every bank format. Saves hours per client file.

- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 — high-compliance, usage-based; LTD less appropriate here.

## Key Themes from Today's Research

1. **The HN crowd is not building for boring industries** — April 2026 "What are you working on?" thread is 100% AI tools, developer tools, and consumer apps. Zero mentions of trades, cleaning, or field service. This is the opportunity.

2. **$10K MRR from 34 customers** — The HN comment about a one-person industry CRM making $10K/month with 34 companies is the clearest signal. Only needs 34 businesses paying $300/month. That's 34 humans you can call by name.

3. **Validated categories with room for new entrants**: Commercial cleaning ($100K MRR proven), live event production ($20K MRR proven), bank statement parsing ($9.7K MRR proven). All have defensible micro-niches below the successful product.

4. **Review automation is severely under-served at the SMB price point** — Podium/Birdeye charge 5-10x what small service businesses can afford. A $59/month version with native trades integrations is a clear wedge.

5. **AppSumo/LTD sweet spot**: Tattoo CRM, cleaning management, compliance tracker, and review automation all score 4-5/5 on LTD potential. These buyers are on AppSumo.
