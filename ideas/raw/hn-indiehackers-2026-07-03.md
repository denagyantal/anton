## FieldFlow — $5/mo Jobber Alternative for Solo Contractors

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47294092 (Show HN, Mar 2026)
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47272986 (earlier post by same founder)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 1 point, 0 comments (very early, unpolished launch)
- **Revenue Data**: Pricing at $5/month flat, no per-user fees — no reported MRR yet
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Independent tradespeople — carpenters, HVAC, plumbers, electricians
- **Core Value Prop**: A solo carpenter was paying $39–$150/month for field service software designed for 20-person operations. He built FieldFlow — invoicing, scheduling, client management — stripped of everything solo contractors don't need. $5/month flat forever.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The existing market (Jobber at $39/mo, Tradify at $47/user, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan) is designed for larger operations with multiple technicians, dispatch boards, fleet tracking. Solo operators (the majority of plumbing, carpentry, HVAC, electrical businesses) are massively overcharged and over-featureed. Nobody is building *well* for the one-person shop.
- **Our Angle**: Purpose-built CRM + invoicing for solo tradespeople with dead-simple UX. Flat $9–$15/month LTD tier. Deep niche focus (e.g., HVAC-only or electrician-only) with industry-specific extras like job photos, equipment notes, seasonal reminders, refrigerant tracking, or compliance checklists. FieldFlow is nascent and unpolished — major execution opportunity.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — $59–$97 LTD is compelling for solo operators who hate subscriptions. AppSumo natural fit.

## Rentman — $15M+ ARR AV/Event Rental SaaS (Boring Business Validated)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q (IH, Apr 2026)
- **Additional Links**: https://rentman.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Case study
- **Engagement**: Published on IH, high visibility case study
- **Revenue Data**: $15M–$20M ARR target, 70+ countries, bootstrapped 8 years, multi-seat SaaS with per-product pricing
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 (event/AV rental — unsexy but very operationally complex)
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, staging, lighting, broadcast, film production companies
- **Core Value Prop**: Roy started an AV rental company at 16, frustrated with spreadsheets and bad on-prem software. Built Rentman to handle equipment availability, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, invoicing — the "messy middle" of running a production company.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The AV/staging/rental equipment market was massively underserved. This pattern — "I ran this business and the software was terrible, so I built my own" — is the clearest signal for boring business SaaS. The model is universally applicable: tool rental, party supply rental, wedding equipment, AV, furniture rental, medical equipment rental.
- **Our Angle**: Apply the Rentman pattern to adjacent equipment rental niches that remain unserved. Party/tent/furniture rental companies are still running on spreadsheets and outdated software. Tool rental shops (think independent competitors to Home Depot tool rental). Could be an LTD product at $97–$297.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (enterprise-complexity, per-seat pricing model — harder to LTD, but solid MRR business)

## Ask HN: Why Is Restaurant Software Still So Outdated?

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38750714 (Ask HN, Dec 2023)
- **Additional Links**: https://modernrestaurantmanagement.com/restaurant-tech-trends-that-will-define-2026/
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN — market demand signal
- **Engagement**: High enough to surface in searches; the *title itself* is a strong signal — this is a validated pain point the community was actively discussing
- **Revenue Data**: N/A — demand signal
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Restaurants, food service
- **Core Value Prop**: Restaurant back-office software (scheduling, food cost tracking, inventory, waste logs, staff scheduling, tip pooling, vendor management) is notoriously outdated, fragmented, and expensive. Small independent restaurants are stuck with Toast (expensive POS + everything locked in), or cobbling together 4–5 tools.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Independent restaurants (not chains) need a simple, affordable operations hub: food cost tracking, staff scheduling, waste/inventory, and basic reporting — all in one place without Toast-level pricing. There's no clean affordable tool for sub-$1M/year restaurants.
- **Our Angle**: "Restaurant OS for indie owners" — food cost + staff scheduling + inventory in one simple tool at $49/month. Focus on single-location independent restaurants that can't afford Toast + 7Shifts + MarketMan separately. Strong Reddit community (r/restaurantowners, r/KitchenConfidential) for customer discovery.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — single-location restaurants would love to pay once and own it. AppSumo fits.

## hvacAI.ca — Consumer HVAC Quote Explainer (Early Signal)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45869146 (mentioned in "Ask HN: What Are You Working On? Nov 2025")
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Work-in-progress mentioned in community thread
- **Engagement**: 1 comment mention
- **Revenue Data**: Not reported — early stage
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC — residential homeowners and contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: A website that helps homeowners understand HVAC quotes in simple terms — comparing cost and capability of proposed systems. Addresses the massive information asymmetry when homeowners get HVAC quotes.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: When a homeowner gets 3 HVAC quotes ranging from $4,000 to $12,000, they have no idea what to compare or whether they're being oversold. There's no trusted, neutral tool for this. Similar asymmetry exists in plumbing, electrical, roofing.
- **Our Angle**: "HVAC Quote Decoder" — paste in your quotes and get plain-English comparison of equipment specs, efficiency (SEER), warranty, brand reputation. Could expand to all home service quotes. Monetize via affiliate relationships with HVAC brands or a $9/one-time fee per quote. Could also serve contractors: auto-generate professional proposals from job specs.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (consumer-facing one-time payment model fits LTD well)

## EasyMiet.eu — German Rental Application Marketplace (Vertical Property Tech)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45869146 (mentioned in "Ask HN: What Are You Working On? Nov 2025")
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Work-in-progress
- **Engagement**: 1 comment, limited traction reported
- **Revenue Data**: No paying customers reported yet — monetization plan: charge applicants for premium profile features
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential property rental — landlords and apartment applicants (Germany market)
- **Core Value Prop**: German landlords often get 100+ applicants for a single listing. They require a custom self-disclosure form from each applicant. EasyMiet standardizes the process: landlords generate a form with QR code, applicants fill in their profile once and apply to multiple listings, system handles approve/dismiss workflow with automatic emails.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The monetization model was wrong (charging applicants instead of landlords), and the founder admitted low traction. BUT the problem is real. Landlord workflow automation for small private landlords (not property management companies) is underserved globally. Tools like Avail (US) exist but are US-only; European markets largely unserved.
- **Our Angle**: "Landlord OS for DIY landlords" — tenant screening, e-signature lease agreements, rent tracking, maintenance request management, and communication in one tool. Better monetization: charge landlords $19-$29/month per property. Works in any market: Germany, UK, France, or US small landlords who can't afford AppFolio.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (recurring per-property model is natural, but LTD at $79/property is plausible)

## Rob Walling / TinySeed: Boring B2B SaaS for Trades Confirmed Viable to 7 Figures

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/should-i-just-create-a-boring-b2b-saas-b6181991c0 (IH discussion, 2022)
- **Additional Links**: https://tinyseed.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Community discussion — validation signal
- **Engagement**: 12 upvotes, 26 comments, includes Rob Walling (MicroConf/TinySeed founder)
- **Revenue Data**: Multiple 6-figure and 7-figure bootstrapped boring SaaS companies confirmed by Walling
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 (meta-signal about boring SaaS viability)
- **Target Industry**: Home improvement contractors, electrical commissioning, CAD add-ons (confirmed TinySeed portfolio examples)
- **Core Value Prop**: Rob Walling explicitly confirms: "SaaS for home improvement contractors, a CAD add-on, and electrical commissioning — we believe all of them can get to 7 figures." He distinguishes between customer pain (hard to reach, offline, slow adopters, but low competition) vs. competitor pain (easy to reach, but crowded market). Trades fall in the "customer pain" category: hard to reach, but low competition and high willingness to pay once you get to them.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: TinySeed is investing in trade/contractor SaaS at the Series-A equivalent stage. This means the opportunity is validated at institutional level. The bootstrapper entry point is micro-niching further: not "contractor software" but "HVAC-only software" or "pest control route planning."
- **Our Angle**: Pick one trade, go extremely deep. Build features specific to HVAC (refrigerant tracking, equipment data, seasonal maintenance reminders), or specific to pest control (route optimization, chemical inventory, service agreements), or specific to cleaning services (key management, recurring schedule management, tip tracking). Depth over breadth.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5

## Centori — Failed SaaS Pivoted to $14k MRR Productized Service

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/services/from-failed-saas-to-14k-mrr-software-enabled-service-BsxsALYvpfriOYumgU0l (IH, Nov 2025)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Case study
- **Engagement**: IH feature article
- **Revenue Data**: $14k MRR as solo founder (SEO/content services)
- **Boring Business Score**: 2/5 (SEO services — not truly "boring trade" but pattern is transferable)
- **Target Industry**: SMB content marketing — but pattern applies to trades
- **Core Value Prop**: Pivot from failed SaaS → productized service using same software internally. The insight: SMBs often need the *service* more than the *tool*. Customers don't want to do SEO; they want someone to do it for them.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: This pattern applies directly to boring businesses. Instead of building scheduling software *for* HVAC companies, build a "HVAC scheduling-as-a-service" where you run their scheduling using your own software. Software-enabled services scale better and sell more easily than pure SaaS to offline/low-tech customer segments (trades, restaurants, small landlords).
- **Our Angle**: For trades, consider a hybrid model: sell the software ($49/mo) AND offer a managed tier where someone handles their scheduling/invoicing ($299/mo). The service validates the software and provides recurring revenue while the SaaS product matures.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 (service model doesn't suit LTD)

Use trade association directories, Facebook Groups for plumbers/HVAC, and YouTube (there are massive audiences of trade business owners learning business skills) as acquisition channels.

## DenchClaw — AI-Native Local CRM (HN Signal for AI-Local Business Tools)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47309953 (Show HN, Mar 2026)
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46838946 (Ask HN about OpenClaw usage)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: Low (early stage)
- **Revenue Data**: Open source / early-stage, no revenue reported
- **Boring Business Score**: 2/5 (AI-native CRM — tech-forward, not specifically boring business)
- **Target Industry**: Local businesses broadly — CRM + AI agents via Telegram
- **Core Value Prop**: Local CRM built on top of OpenClaw AI agent framework. Allows local business owners to interact with their CRM via Telegram. Uses DuckDB. Open source.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The interesting signal here is not DenchClaw itself but the trend it represents: AI-native interface for local business CRM. Tradespeople often prefer WhatsApp/Telegram over web apps. A CRM that works through voice/chat commands — "log job for John Smith, 3 hours HVAC maintenance, bill $350" — would dramatically lower the adoption barrier for technophobes in the trades.
- **Our Angle**: AI-first trade CRM that accepts natural language input via SMS/WhatsApp/voice. Technician on a job site says "log job done, charge $425, include parts $85, send invoice to Bob Jones" — AI handles the rest. This removes the typing/data-entry friction that kills adoption of web CRMs among tradespeople.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (if framed as "the CRM that actually works on job sites")
