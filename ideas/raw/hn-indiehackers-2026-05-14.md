## Key Themes & Market Intelligence (2026)

### Structural Tailwinds for Boring Business SaaS
1. **PE roll-up fragility**: Pest control, HVAC, and other trades are consolidating under PE, which degrades service quality and creates resentment among owner-operators. Software that helps independents compete is doubly valuable.
2. **89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools** (SBA Q3 2025 data): The adoption gap is enormous.
3. **Domain knowledge is the moat**: Most SaaS founders are tech-literate and build for tech-adjacent markets. Markets where you must learn the domain (USDA compliance, Coast Guard manifests, vet SOAP notes) repel competition naturally.
4. **Community-based GTM**: Trade Facebook groups, association newsletters, co-ops, and YouTube creators are underused channels. A typical growth hacker doesn't know how to operate here — but that's exactly where the customers are.
5. **AI is making boring software better, not replacing it**: AI scheduling, AI report generation, and AI follow-up automation are adding genuine value to workflow tools (not just chatbot wrappers).

### Price Points Validated in This Segment
- **Solo operators**: $29-$69/month (high LTD conversion at $149-$249)
- **Small teams (2-10)**: $99-$199/month
- **Mid-market (10-50)**: $299-$599/month

### Industries Showing Highest Unmet Demand (2026)
1. Solo tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, HVAC) — 1-5 employee shops specifically
2. Pest control (high engagement HN thread, PE-driven consolidation fragility)
3. Home inspectors (strong LTD match, legacy tools overpriced)
4. Tattoo studios (tight community, no dominant tool)
5. Fishing charter operators (Coast Guard compliance angle is unique moat)
6. Metal fabricators/industrial quoting (YC RFS validation, very high WTP)
7. Small farms/agriculture compliance (enormous regulatory complexity, zero modern tools)
