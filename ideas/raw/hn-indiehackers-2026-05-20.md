## Rentman — AV/Event Rental Operations Platform

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q
- **Additional Links**: https://rentman.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Founder interview / Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: 106 upvotes, 8 bookmarks, 15 comments
- **Revenue Data**: $15M–$20M ARR (>$1.25M/month); bootstrapped for 8 years before outside capital
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — AV/event production rental is deeply unglamorous; equipment logistics, crew scheduling, invoicing
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, event production, lighting/staging/broadcast/film
- **Core Value Prop**: One operations platform for rental companies — equipment availability, quoting, crew scheduling, project planning, invoicing. Replaces spreadsheets + bent-to-fit generic software.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Sub-niches still wide open: **DJ gear rental, photography equipment rental, construction equipment rental, party supply rental**. Rentman proved the model; these verticals have the same pain but no dedicated modern tool.
- **Our Angle**: Clone Rentman's model for a tighter niche (e.g., DJ/photo equipment rental; party supply rental). Cloud-first, simple UX, tight workflows. The AV rental world is small but every adjacent rental vertical has thousands of SMBs running on spreadsheets.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — Rental businesses are price-conscious; LTD for the "starter tier" with MRR for larger ops is viable. DJ gear rental shops, photographers, and party supply companies fit the $59–$99 LTD buyer perfectly.

## Stagetimer — Remote Countdown Timer for Live Events

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/turning-a-simple-b2b-solution-into-a-15k-mrr-saas-by-exploiting-a-market-niche-xIxrVwn24DVsN8b3PYD3
- **Additional Links**: https://stagetimer.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Founder interview / Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: 49 upvotes, 20 bookmarks, 17 comments
- **Revenue Data**: $15K MRR subscriptions + one-time payments; 13,000+ accounts; ~$20K/month combined
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — Live event production operations; not sexy but every conference, church service, and broadcast needs this
- **Target Industry**: Live events — conferences, churches, broadcast, production companies
- **Core Value Prop**: Synchronized, remotely controlled countdown timers visible on multiple screens for speakers and stage managers. Replaces manual per-device timers.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Proves that **deeply niche event-production micro-tools can reach $15K+ MRR**. Adjacent gaps: teleprompter SaaS, speaker confidence monitor apps, event run-of-show software, venue A/V checklist tools. All have the same narrow but high-willingness-to-pay audience.
- **Our Angle**: Identify the next "everyone in this industry uses a terrible workaround" moment in event or trades production. Stagetimer found SEO on ultra-low-competition keywords as primary growth; replicable in any boring vertical.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — Subscription suits this better; LTD works for individual users but teams prefer per-seat plans.

## Schedo — Niche Appointment Scheduling for Small Service Businesses

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/my-niche-appointment-software-makes-5k-month-e24aae2369
- **Additional Links**: https://schedo.app/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Founder interview / Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: Featured post (April 2025)
- **Revenue Data**: $5K MRR; first customer was a window cleaning company at $12/month
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — Serves window cleaning, DMV services, pet sitting, financial advisors, recruiters; deeply unglamorous verticals
- **Target Industry**: Solopreneur service businesses — cleaning, pet sitting, personal trainers, accountants, photographers
- **Core Value Prop**: Branded scheduling page showing all meeting types in one place with customization. Built-in calendar (missing from Calendly/competitors). Stripe payments integrated.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Generic scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) are built for tech workers, not for a window cleaner who needs: job site address capture, before/after photo upload links, seasonal service reminders, and recurring customer profiles. **A "Calendly for trades" with job-specific fields is wide open.**
- **Our Angle**: Narrow deeper than Schedo — build "Scheduling for cleaning/HVAC/pest control" with job-specific intake forms, technician dispatch, and route optimization baked in. This becomes a lightweight FSM, not just a calendar link.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — $59–$79 LTD is exactly the price range small service businesses will pay for a one-time tool. AppSumo-ready.

## InstaBuild — Invoicing and Payment Reminders for Construction Projects

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44246559
- **Additional Links**: (Show HN thread, June 2025)
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: Listed in HN Show HN index for June 2025
- **Revenue Data**: Not disclosed (early-stage launch)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — Construction project invoicing is pure unglamorous B2B back-office pain
- **Target Industry**: Construction contractors, subcontractors, specialty trades
- **Core Value Prop**: Professional invoices for construction projects, hour/materials tracking per project, auto-reminders for payment. Addresses the #1 cash flow killer for small contractors: getting paid late.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: General invoice tools (QuickBooks, FreshBooks) are overkill and not job-site-aware. Contractors need: progress billing, retention tracking, lien waiver management, and AIA billing format. **None of the indie tools target these construction-specific workflows.** InstaBuild is early — there's room for a more complete "contractor billing OS."
- **Our Angle**: Build "construction billing for subs under $5M revenue" — progress billing, retention holdback tracking, automated payment reminders tied to project milestones. Add a simple CRM for follow-ups with GCs. Target electrical/plumbing/HVAC subs specifically.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — Construction contractors are perfect LTD buyers; one-person operations, price sensitive, but will pay for something that stops payment headaches.

## Trade-Specific CRM / Field Service Gap (HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical)

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/ + https://superframeworks.com/articles/best-micro-saas-ideas-solopreneurs + SBA Q3 2025 Technology Adoption Report
- **Additional Links**: https://gorilladesk.com/ (existing competitor — pest control / lawn / cleaning), https://fieldworkhq.com/ (Fieldwork — pest control FSM)
- **Platform**: Industry research / Indie Hackers ecosystem
- **Type**: Market signal / validated gap
- **Engagement**: Multiple IH and analyst sources converging on same finding
- **Revenue Data**: GorillaDesk and Fieldwork are profitable businesses serving pest control and lawn. Adjacent verticals (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) have Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro — but all are expensive ($100–$400+/month), enterprise-leaning, and over-featured for 1–5 person shops.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — HVAC, plumbing, electrical are the definition of "boring but essential"
- **Target Industry**: 1–5 person HVAC, plumbing, or electrical contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025). The gap isn't that there's no software — it's that everything costs too much and is too complex for a two-truck plumbing company. A **dead-simple scheduling + dispatch + invoice tool at $49–$79/month** beats Jobber on simplicity and price.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ServiceTitan charges $200–$600/month; Jobber $99–$249. A solo dev can build 20% of the features that serve 80% of the use cases and charge $49/month. The market is: ~500K small HVAC/plumbing/electrical businesses in the US alone. Even 1,000 subscribers = $49K MRR.
- **Our Angle**: "Jobber Lite for HVAC" — scheduling, dispatch, customer history, invoice, payment collection. Mobile-first. No feature bloat. Target via HVAC technician Facebook groups and trade association forums. Referral flywheel: one satisfied tech tells 3–5 others.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — A $99 LTD for a simple tool a plumber can use on their phone is the perfect AppSumo product. Sub-$100 is impulse buy territory for tradespeople.

## Pest Control Software — Fragmented Market with Indie-Sized Gaps

- **Source**: https://www.pestbase.ai/blog/best-pest-control-software-in-2026-what-service-businesses-need + https://gorilladesk.com/ + https://www.pestpac.com/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.capterra.com/pest-control-software/
- **Platform**: Industry / market research
- **Type**: Market validation signal
- **Engagement**: Pest control software market projected to exceed $2B by 2027; 68% of pest control operators using at least one software tool (2024)
- **Revenue Data**: GorillaDesk and Fieldwork are indie-founded FSM tools that built real businesses serving pest control. PestPac is the incumbent (enterprise). PestBase is a new entrant targeting the mid-market.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — Literally pest extermination. Does not get more boring.
- **Target Industry**: Small pest control operators (1–10 technicians)
- **Core Value Prop**: Route optimization, recurring service scheduling, chemical usage logs (compliance), customer reminders, invoice/payment.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The 32% who haven't adopted software yet + dissatisfied GorillaDesk users who outgrew the free tier. Key unmet need: **chemical usage compliance logging with state-specific regulatory reporting** — this is required by law but no indie tool handles it well. Regulatory compliance = lock-in.
- **Our Angle**: "Pest control software with built-in compliance logs" — state chemical use reporting, technician license tracking, service record keeping. Charge a premium for the compliance angle. Target operators in states with strict pesticide regulations (CA, NY, FL).
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — Compliance tools are subscription-sticky; LTD works for early adopters but subscription is better long-term. Consider LTD for core scheduling + compliance module as subscription upsell.

## Ask HN Signal: Small Business Appointment Reminders — Still Unsolved

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46512745
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44477078 (Ask HN: Anyone interested in improving scheduling?)
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Ask HN — demand signal
- **Engagement**: Appeared in Jan 2026; recurring theme across multiple Ask HN threads
- **Revenue Data**: N/A — demand signal only
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — Hair salons, med spas, HVAC providers, cleaning companies
- **Target Industry**: Small local service businesses handling repeat appointments
- **Core Value Prop**: The problem HN users surface: small businesses (hair salons, med spas, HVAC, cleaning companies) rely on SMS and phone calls for reminders. Generic reminder tools (Twilio, SendGrid) require dev work. Scheduling platforms are overpriced. The gap is a **sub-$30/month SMS reminder + no-show management tool** for non-technical service business owners.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: No-shows cost service businesses an estimated 10–20% of revenue. A dead-simple "text your customer 24h before their appointment, let them confirm/cancel, auto-fill the slot if they cancel" tool is not well served at sub-$30/month. Calendly doesn't do this for trade-style jobs. Acuity is too expensive.
- **Our Angle**: "No-show eliminator for service businesses" — SMS reminders, 1-click confirm/cancel, automatic waitlist fill. $19–$29/month. No calendar integration required; works with any existing booking method (even just a phone call log). Sell via Facebook groups for salon owners, HVAC businesses, cleaning companies.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — Perfect LTD product. Simple value prop, immediate ROI (stop losing $500/month to no-shows), low perceived risk.
