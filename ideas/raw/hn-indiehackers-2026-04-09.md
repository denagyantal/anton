## Key Themes & Market Signals

### What's Working in Boring Business SaaS (2025-2026)

1. **Undercover customer development** — The pest control founder getting a technician job generated massive HN engagement (448 pts). The market values authentic domain expertise. This approach is a differentiation strategy, not just research.

2. **Open-source → paid SaaS gap** — Craftplan (577 pts) is open-sourced and has no paid version. Every niche open-source tool without a commercial version is an opportunity. Pattern: build the hosted, managed SaaS version of an open-source tool.

3. **Billion-dollar incumbents abandoning SMBs** — Spectora's $30M ARR and PE acquisition means it's raising prices and going upmarket. Every maturing vertical SaaS creates a downmarket gap. Pattern: find the "Spectora of X industry" that's been acquired and start a cheaper, simpler alternative.

4. **Price point matters enormously** — Motara vs shop software incumbents: $20/month vs $100-300/month. Mechanics talk about software pricing in Facebook groups constantly. Same dynamic exists in pest control, landscaping, cleaning. Affordable + simple beats feature-rich + expensive for small operators.

5. **Geography arbitrage** — Most successful US-centric vertical SaaS tools (ZenMaid, Spectora, etc.) have weak international versions. Building localized equivalents for UK, Australia, Canada, Brazil, India represents a repeatable playbook.

6. **Distribution channel = Facebook Groups** — Both Spectora and ZenMaid explicitly cite Facebook groups as their primary early distribution channel. Pest control operators, cleaning business owners, home inspectors all have large, active Facebook communities. This is a consistent, low-cost channel for boring business SaaS.
