# HN & Indie Hackers Scanner — 2026-03-19

## Summary

Today's scan surfaced strong signals in field service software for solo tradespeople, event/production tech with ugly legacy incumbents, industrial IoT for quality control, local-business CRM, and contractor invoicing tools. The recurring theme: trades and field service businesses are still massively underserved by modern software, and founders building simple, affordable tools for plumbers/carpenters/HVAC techs are finding traction. The vertical SaaS market is projected to hit $720B by 2028 (25.89% CAGR), and micro-SaaS specifically is growing ~30% annually — boring niches are the sweet spot.

# Market Signals & Trends

## Key Themes This Week

1. **Solo tradespeople are dramatically underserved** — FieldFlow's $5/mo positioning against Jobber's $39/mo shows the price gap. Solo operators don't need CRM, fleet management, or team scheduling — they need invoicing, a calendar, and client contact info.

2. **"1999 software" is everywhere in boring industries** — Stagetimer's founder nailed it: industries with expensive hardware often have terrible software that people pay $10k/year for. This applies to AV/events, dental, manufacturing, and construction.

3. **Voice-first is the killer interface for trades** — Multiple sources flagged that tradespeople can't type during their workday. Voice-to-invoice, voice-to-job-update, and voice-to-status are natural but unbuilt.

4. **Customer communication is the new frontier** — TurboOps (job status tracking) and PostalAgent (direct mail) show that the next wave of field service tools isn't about internal ops — it's about customer-facing communication.

5. **Vertical AI for specific industries outperforms generic AI tools** — DenchClaw (CRM for law firms) and the broader indie hacker consensus: AI tools for one boring industry beat generic AI tools in conversion and retention.
