- **Description**: Voice AI agents that answer calls 24/7, book jobs into CRM, and triage service requests are exploding in the trades. Avoca (HVAC/plumbing/roofing/electrical) hit a $1B valuation on $125M in April 2026, led by Meritech and General Catalyst (seed by Y Combinator, Series A by Kleiner Perkins). Avoca is on track to book $1B in jobs in 2026 for 800+ customers. The model: AI answers every missed call, captures job details, books directly into the dispatch calendar. Also: niche verticals not yet covered (chimney sweep, appliance repair, window treatment installation).

## HVAC A2L Refrigerant Compliance & Certification Tracking
- **Category**: Compliance & Regulation Tech
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://www.getjobber.com/academy/hvac/hvac-industry-trends/
  - https://www.bdrco.com/blog/hvac-business-software-guide/
  - https://www.bdrco.com/blog/home-service-industry-trends/
- **Description**: The mandatory phase-out of R-410A refrigerant is forcing ~120,000 HVAC businesses to retrain technicians, track new certifications, manage different refrigerant types in inventory, and document A2L handling compliance. New A2L refrigerants (R-454B, R-32) require technician certification records, safety training logs, and equipment documentation. Most HVAC shops are tracking this in spreadsheets. The HVAC service software market is growing from $1.5B (2026) to $6.3B (2035) at 17.3% CAGR. PE consolidation of HVAC shops increased 88% YoY through mid-2025 — PE operators need compliance documentation tools even more.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC (specifically the ~120,000 independent HVAC businesses in the US)
- **Market Timing**: Early — the regulatory forcing function (A2L mandate) is creating urgency *right now* but compliance software for this specific requirement barely exists.
- **Example Products**:
  - No purpose-built A2L compliance tracker exists — the whitespace is open
  - Adjacent: ServiceTitan (enterprise FSM with compliance modules), Field Edge
- **White Space**: A focused app for HVAC shops to: (1) track technician A2L certifications and renewal dates, (2) log refrigerant inventory by type, (3) generate compliance reports for DOT/EPA audits, (4) send cert renewal reminders. Could be a standalone micro-SaaS or a plugin for Jobber/ServiceTitan shops.
- **Speed to Market**: 2–3 weeks for MVP (cert tracking + alerts). Compliance report PDF generation adds 1 week.
- **LTD Fit**: Strong — HVAC shop owners will pay a one-time fee for a compliance tool that protects their license. $199–$399 LTD is reasonable.
- **Content Strategy**: "A2L refrigerant compliance tracking", "HVAC technician certification software", "R-410A phaseout compliance tool", "EPA 608 certification tracker for HVAC shops"

- **Target Industries**: Independent/accidental landlords, small real estate investors, short-term rental operators expanding to mid-term
- **Market Timing**: Growing — the market is fragmenting rapidly. Multiple new entrants in 2025–2026 signal active competition but also validated demand.
- **Example Products**:
  - Innago — https://innago.com (free, all-in-one)

  - Landlord Studio — https://landlordstudio.com (mobile-first, accounting-focused)
  - Hemlane — https://hemlane.com (local agent network + software)

A landlord with 10 units receives a maintenance request, needs to get 2–3 quotes from local handymen, and coordinate access. This workflow is still done by phone/text. A tool that auto-routes maintenance requests to pre-vetted local vendors, collects bids, and lets the landlord approve with one tap is missing.
- **Speed to Market**: 3–5 weeks for MVP (tenant portal + maintenance request + vendor notification). Bid collection and payment coordination adds 2–3 weeks.
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — landlords are cost-conscious; a $149–$299 LTD for a maintenance coordination tool (not full PMS) could work.
