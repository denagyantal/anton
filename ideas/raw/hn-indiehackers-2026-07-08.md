## Summary

### Top 3 Opportunities

**1. Compliance Tracking for Licensed Trades (Finding #12 + #5 combined)**
The convergence of EasyCheck's early validation, IH analyst consensus on compliance tools being "impossible to churn out of," and the US trades market being entirely unserved makes this the highest-conviction opportunity. Electrical contractors, HVAC companies, and food service operators all have mandatory compliance requirements (OSHA, EPA, health dept) with zero affordable digital tools. A trades-specific compliance tracker at $79-149/mo is a genuine white space. Vertical compliance tools command 68% margins. LTD score: 5/5. First mover in any single trade niche (start with electrical or food service) wins the market before VC attention arrives.

**2. AI Voice Agent / Inbound Call Handler for Micro Home Service Businesses (Finding #2 + #15)**
Avoca AI reaching a $1B valuation validates the market. But Avoca targets mid-to-large operators. The gap is the 1-5 truck HVAC, plumbing, or pest control business that currently pays $600/closed lead from Google ads and closes 20-25% of calls. A simple, affordable ($99-149/mo or $699 LTD) AI phone agent that answers after-hours calls, books jobs, and texts confirmations would generate instant ROI. This is the clearest "Avoca for the little guy" opportunity, and the LTD model is perfect for this buyer persona (trades owners hate recurring software fees).

**3. Niche Scheduling for Recurring Home Service Contracts (Finding #11)**
The $35K-$450K ARR range is validated. The specific gap: no tool owns "recurring maintenance contract scheduling" for HVAC tune-up plans, pest control quarterly routes, or gutter cleaning annual contracts. Calendly and Acuity are built for one-off bookings. Route scheduling tools are enterprise-priced. A $59-99/mo recurring route scheduler with built-in reminder sequences, customer self-booking, and tech dispatch would find immediate product-market fit with the thousands of HVAC companies offering maintenance plans and pest control companies with monthly/quarterly route customers.

**Honorable mention**: The Rentman-for-microbusiness angle (Finding #3/#8) — a $29-49/mo or $249 LTD tool for freelance AV techs, DJs, and small event companies — is an extremely clean LTD/AppSumo play with validated market ceiling and no well-funded direct competitor in the micro tier.
