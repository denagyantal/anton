Charge for outcomes, not access.
- **Our Angle**: Build "BooksDone" — AI-powered bookkeeping-as-a-service for home service contractors. Connect to their bank account and QuickBooks. AI categorizes transactions, reconciles monthly, generates P&L and tax-ready reports. The contractor never opens QuickBooks. $149/mo or $99/mo with annual commitment. Not a tool — a result. Competitors: Bench ($299/mo, shutting down consumer bookkeeping) left a gap. 1-800Accountant charges $200+/mo. The trades-specific angle: understands job costing, material expenses, subcontractor 1099s, and seasonal cash flow patterns.

- **Revenue Data**: Suggested pricing: $2K per grant application or $499/mo subscription. US cities and towns number 19,000+ — many with small staffs that lack grant-writing expertise. Federal infrastructure bills (IIJA, IRA) have massively expanded available grants since 2022. Professional grant writers charge $5K-15K per application.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Municipal governments, small towns, rural communities, school districts
- **Core Value Prop**: Cities leave $3 billion in federal money on the table annually because small-town staff hate writing grants — or don't know how. Federal grants require specific narrative formats, compliance language, budget justifications, and data citations. A 2-person town clerk office doesn't have a grant writer. No tool specifically helps municipalities apply for federal grants using AI. The data advantage: municipal demographics, infrastructure data, and previous grant awards are all public — an AI tool could pre-populate 60-70% of a typical application.
- **Our Angle**: Build "GrantBot" — AI-powered federal grant application assistant for small municipalities. Auto-match eligible grants from grants.gov based on municipality profile. Pre-fill applications with public demographic/infrastructure data. Generate compliant narrative sections. Review checklist for common rejection reasons. $499/mo or $2K per completed application. Target the 10,000+ US municipalities under 25,000 population. LTD doesn't fit this model — go subscription.

# Market Signals & Trends

## Key Themes This Week

1. **Trade-specific CRMs beating generic field service tools** — QuoteIQ expanding aggressively across 50+ trades with AI features (photo-based estimating, voice commands, self-scheduling). But breadth = shallow vertical coverage. Deep single-trade CRMs (pest control, locksmith, HVAC) with industry-native workflows can charge premium and win on specificity. GorillaDesk proves 3,100+ companies will pay $165-375/mo for pest control-specific software.

2. **Software-Delivered Services model going mainstream** — IH's trending post on "selling outcomes not tools" signals a paradigm shift. Instead of selling a scheduling tool to a plumber, sell "booked appointments." Instead of accounting software, sell "books done." AI makes this economically viable at indie scale. The biggest gap: nobody is applying SDS to boring industries yet — it's all tech-adjacent (support, recruiting).

3. **Dental practice tech ripe for indie disruption** — $1.97B market growing at 9.5%. Dominated by legacy PMS (Dentrix, Open Dental) that small practices are locked into. The indie opportunity isn't replacing the PMS — it's building lightweight AI layers on top: smart scheduling, patient communication, review automation. $49-99/mo complements, doesn't compete.

4. **Therapist note-taking converging across all idea lists** — Three independent 2026 micro-SaaS roundups feature AI clinical note generation. HIPAA compliance is the moat. Generic AI transcription tools can't output SOAP/DAP/BIRP format. The market: 198,000+ licensed psychologists + 370,000+ licensed counselors in the US alone.

5. **Back-office automation for trades is the next wave** — Faraday's 300% growth and VC backing validates that the opportunity after scheduling/CRM is automating permits, warranties, rebates, and compliance paperwork. This is the unsexy layer that nobody wants to build — which is exactly why it's valuable.

6. **Homeowner-facing renovation tools barely exist** — $420B+ home improvement market, and homeowners managing $30K+ renovations coordinate via text messages. Buildertrend ($500+/mo) is contractor-first. Remodelum is expense-only. No homeowner-first project coordination tool exists at consumer price points ($29/mo).
