## Pest Control Vertical SaaS / Tech-Enabled Pest Control Operator

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: Comments in same thread reference petroleum equipment service and other field service industries still running pen-and-paper
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN / Story post (extremely high engagement, 1100+ comments)
- **Engagement**: Hundreds of upvotes, 1100+ comments, very active discussion
- **Revenue Data**: One commenter mentioned charging a restaurant $8k for 2-3 days of pest exclusion work ($500 in materials). Route service businesses generate reliable recurring revenue
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control, field services
- **Core Value Prop**: Founder took a real technician job at a pest control company to understand operations from the ground up before building. Key finding: existing vertical SaaS (e.g. PestRoutes) is "decent, cheap, and ubiquitous" but ops are still chaotic; the opportunity is a *tech-enabled operator* model — acquiring/growing a pest control company with internal software that gives a competitive edge, not generic SaaS sold to incumbents.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: "Pen and paper still dominates" parts of field service industries. Incumbent software vendors (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, PestRoutes) are bloated. Blue-collar decision makers are now being bombarded by inbound pitches — window for early access is closing. AI layers for upselling, hands-free CRM data entry, and smart traps/stations could differentiate. Thread commenter also mentioned petroleum equipment service — bought a business, building internal tools, pen-and-paper still the norm.
- **Our Angle**: Lightweight, AI-augmented back-office (scheduling → invoicing → route optimization → customer messaging) purpose-built for a single trade vertical (pest control, or pool service, or chimney sweep). Sell as affordable FSM with opinionated workflows vs. bloated ServiceTitan.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (recurring service businesses want subscriptions, but LTD could work for smaller operators to get initial traction)

## Craft Brewery Inventory Management (Proven $12k/yr Problem)

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/ (citing Brewers Association 2025 Industry Report)
- **Additional Links**: https://www.brewersassociation.org/
- **Platform**: Research article (IH / bootstrapper audience)
- **Type**: Market validation via industry data
- **Engagement**: Industry report + indie hacker community discussion
- **Revenue Data**: 9,778 active US craft breweries (2025). Average $12k/yr lost to inventory management issues. Pricing at $79-199/mo justified by operational savings.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Craft brewing
- **Core Value Prop**: Purpose-built inventory for breweries — batch numbers, fermentation stages, keg locations, ingredient inventories, TTB compliance reporting. Generic inventory systems (QuickBooks, Square) can't model brewing-specific workflows.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 9,778 breweries × $12k/yr loss = ~$117M/yr in preventable losses. A $99/mo tool that saves even $500/mo pays for itself. Adjacent: craft distilleries (similar batch tracking needs), kombucha/cider producers, meaderies. Existing solutions (Ekos, BreweryDB) are expensive ($300-800+/mo) or old.
- **Our Angle**: Modern, affordable brewery inventory + TTB reporting + recipe costing at $79-149/mo. Free trial, AppSumo LTD launch to get first 200 breweries. Nail recipe costing (understated pain point) as differentiator.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 (breweries are small businesses, LTD at $99-149 would convert well; clear ROI story)
