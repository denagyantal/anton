## Pest Control Vertical SaaS — Tech-Enabled Operator Model

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: N/A
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN / founder story
- **Engagement**: 448 upvotes, 182 comments
- **Revenue Data**: Commenter disclosed their pest control SaaS runs at $2M ARR; smaller operations cited at $50K MRR potential
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control / extermination
- **Core Value Prop**: Founder took an actual technician job to learn the industry before building. Key pain points: hands-free CRM data entry (techs wearing gloves, driving), intelligent scheduling, upsell prompts calibrated to individual tech personality/comfort level, smart trap monitoring.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The incumbent VSaaS is described as "decent, cheap, and ubiquitous" — suggesting a mature but complacent market. One operator notes building their own CRM that "no big company will ever touch," signaling micro-operator needs are unmet. Voice-first field input is an emerging gap.
- **Our Angle**: AI voice-first data capture for pest control technicians — hands-free CRM updates via mobile while driving between jobs. Could license as add-on module to existing pest control software or build standalone.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (operational software, but field-specific AI features could command LTD)

## Craftplan — Production Management for Micro-Bakeries

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46847690
- **Additional Links**: https://github.com/craftplan (open-source)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 577 upvotes, 167 comments — exceptionally high for a niche tool
- **Revenue Data**: None — currently open source, no commercial model
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Artisanal food production / micro-bakeries / small-batch manufacturing
- **Core Value Prop**: Recipes with versioned bills of materials and automatic cost calculations, inventory with lot traceability and allergen tracking, order processing, production batch planning, demand forecasting. Built with Elixir/Phoenix LiveView/PostgreSQL.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The high engagement (577 points, 167 comments) with zero commercial model is a massive signal. Enterprise ERPs cost $10K–$50K/year. Spreadsheets don't handle multi-level BOMs. Commenters said "BOM with cost rollups is the feature that would have saved me hours." No SaaS specifically built for micro-bakeries at affordable price points.
- **Our Angle**: Commercialize this exact category: $49–$149/month SaaS for micro-bakeries and small-batch food producers. Craftplan proves the market exists. Could expand to cottage food, jam-makers, specialty food producers. Compliance/nutrition labeling add-on is adjacent upsell.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (clear LTD at $59–$99, strong AppSumo potential from food-entrepreneur communities)

## Manufactured Home Dealership SaaS — Underserved Niche

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=35573937
- **Additional Links**: https://everlogic.com/manufactured-home-dealership-management-software/
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN / indie launch
- **Engagement**: Moderate (posted April 2023)
- **Revenue Data**: 15 paying customers at time of post; EverLogic (incumbent) charges $129/month/license with 3-license minimum
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Manufactured / mobile home dealerships
- **Core Value Prop**: CRM + DMS (Dealer Management System) specifically for manufactured housing retailers — inventory, deal desking, service tracking, financing. After 2008–2009 financial crisis, multiple incumbent software companies went under leaving ~1,500 US street dealerships with legacy or no modern software.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The HN Ask discussion (item 22496129) confirmed "no modern SaaS since industry contraction." EverLogic is 20+ years old. Cirrus is the only other competitor. A modern, mobile-first DMS at $99–$199/month could capture even 200 of 1,500 dealerships for $20K–$40K MRR.
- **Our Angle**: Modern cloud-first DMS for manufactured home dealers: mobile-responsive, integrated financing workflow, digital signatures, and payment processing. Undercut EverLogic on price, win on UX and mobile.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (niche but viable for LTD on core features; ongoing MRR is better fit)

## DocuSketch — Insurance Claims Floor Plan Software

- **Source**: https://www.docusketch.com/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/27-micro-saas-examples-that-actually-print-money-in-2025
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Bootstrapped case study
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / validated product
- **Engagement**: 30,000+ restoration professionals, 1 million+ claims processed; PE-backed (Summit Partners, Jan 2024)
- **Revenue Data**: $90K MRR cited (bootstrapped before PE investment); founder was a former insurance adjuster
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Insurance restoration / property claims / damage assessment
- **Core Value Prop**: 360-degree camera + AI generates instant 3D floor plans for insurance claims and restoration scoping. Eliminates manual measuring and hand-drawing. 8 of top 10 largest restoration franchises use it.

- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (strong LTD candidate at $99; adjusters are cost-conscious but need the tool)

## Kickserv — Field Service Invoicing for Plumbers / Trades

- **Source**: https://www.kickserv.com/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/27-micro-saas-examples-that-actually-print-money-in-2025
- **Platform**: Bootstrapped / Indie case study
- **Type**: Revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: Established product since 2006, under 10 employees
- **Revenue Data**: $120K MRR — bootstrapped, refuses to scale headcount
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, general field service
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, CRM for trades. Founded by plumbers for plumbers. Integrates with QuickBooks Online/Desktop and Stripe.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Kickserv proves that boring trade software can sustain $120K MRR with fewer than 10 people. The gap is: mobile-first design (Kickserv's interface is dated), AI-assisted estimating, and voice-to-invoice features. Also: Kickserv doesn't serve non-English speaking immigrant contractor owners well.
- **Our Angle**: Mobile-first, AI-assisted field service tool targeting immigrant contractors (Spanish-language first, then Portuguese/Haitian Creole) — a segment completely ignored by Kickserv, Jobber, and ServiceTitan. Multilingual invoicing and customer comms as core differentiator.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (strong AppSumo/LTD potential for a Kickserv alternative with better mobile UX)

- **Engagement**: N/A (documented case study)
- **Revenue Data**: $29K MRR in 12 months with 45 locations at $650/location/month; CAC $280 via newsletter; monthly churn 0.9%
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Funeral homes / death care industry
- **Core Value Prop**: Event scheduling, logistics coordination, client management built on Airtable + Zapier. Distributed via funeral director industry association newsletters. Market: ~19,000 funeral homes in US; competitors (FrontRunner, Tribute Technology) charge $400–$900/location/month.

- **Our Angle**: Build a polished, mobile-first funeral home management SaaS at $299–$499/month targeting independently-owned funeral homes (still the majority). Lead with a free trial via NFDA (National Funeral Directors Association) member newsletters — same distribution channel as the case study.

- **Additional Links**: N/A
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / case study

- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5

- **Core Value Prop**: Vehicle maintenance tracking and DOT compliance management for small and mid-size fleet operators. Scheduling preventive maintenance, tracking inspections, managing driver logs.
