## Pest Control Vertical SaaS (Go Undercover to Build)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: https://www.onhand.pro/p/i-wanted-to-build-vertical-saas-for-pest-control-i-took-a-technician-job-instead
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN / founder blog (linked from HN, March 2026)
- **Engagement**: High engagement — dozens of comments praising the approach, HN community endorsing the "take the industry job first" strategy
- **Revenue Data**: Author reports closing $30k ARR personally in 21 days as a sales tech; market has top reps doing $800k–$1.2M ARR each. Author is now acquiring a small residential pest control operator to build tooling around.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control / home services
- **Core Value Prop**: The industry's core system is typically Salesforce bent beyond recognition, with 10+ additional apps on onboarding, broken fleet management (fuel cards taking 5+ weeks), no upsell tooling for techs while on-site, and recruitment software that ghosts applicants. The entire ops stack is duct-taped.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Purpose-built pest control ops software with: clean mobile-first tech interface, built-in upsell workflows, streamlined fleet ops, AI-assisted scheduling and quoting. Existing tools (ServiceTitan, GorillaDesk) are either enterprise-heavy or very basic. The founder is pursuing a rollup-then-SaaS model — acquire a small operator, build the tooling, prove it, then license it.
- **Our Angle**: Build the software as you operate the business — same moat. Even without the rollup, building a modern, lightweight alternative to the bent-Salesforce stack has a clear wedge. Start with fleet + onboarding pain, expand to scheduling and quoting. $30B TAM, recurring (pest control is subscription by nature), regulated (licensing creates stickiness), fragmented.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (recurring model fits subscription better, but LTD for solo operators is viable at $299–$499)

## AV & Event Production Equipment Rental — Rentman ($15M+ ARR)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q

- **Type**: Founder interview / revenue milestone (April 2026)
- **Engagement**: Featured IH interview; Rentman is on its way to $20M ARR
- **Revenue Data**: >$1.25M/month ARR. 8 years fully bootstrapped before taking outside capital. 70+ countries, tens of thousands of users.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, event production, staging, broadcast, lighting, film
- **Core Value Prop**: Equipment rental availability, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, invoicing — all in one tool purpose-built for event/media production companies. Existing tools were either spreadsheets or generic software bent into shape (sound familiar?).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Rentman now owns the AV/event space. BUT: the same pattern applies to dozens of adjacent rental verticals that have no purpose-built tool. Party rental, medical equipment rental, construction equipment rental, tool rental (DeWalt-level), staging/furniture rental for real estate, costume rental. The playbook is proven — Roy built from scratch and hit $15M ARR by solving the "bent generic software" problem.
- **Our Angle**: Pick an adjacent rental vertical (medical equipment, construction tool rental, party rental) where no Rentman-equivalent exists. Party/event rental (tents, linens, tables) is particularly underserved — different workflow from AV, much more fragmented market of small regional operators.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 (per-seat SaaS is the proven model here; LTD would need heavy feature gating)

## Distribution-First SaaS — Launch Fast ($30k MRR in 90 Days)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-product-in-48-hours-and-hitting-30k-mrr-as-a-non-technical-founder-wWtWIH5tmwASUbxKaLT9
- **Additional Links**: http://launchfastlegacyx.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / founder interview (June 2026)
- **Engagement**: 128 upvotes, 23 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $10k MRR day 30, $17–18k day 60, $21.8k day 90, $30k MRR now. 330 active Chrome extension users.
- **Boring Business Score**: 2/5 (Amazon seller tools aren't traditional boring, but the lesson is universal)
- **Target Industry**: Amazon FBA sellers (product research, catalog management, ad optimization)
- **Core Value Prop**: Hasaam Bhatti built an Amazon product research tool and MCP agent using Cursor in 48 hours, leveraged an existing coaching community (Legacy X — thousands of active Amazon sellers) for immediate distribution, and hit $10k MRR in 30 days.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The meta-lesson here for boring-business SaaS: distribution-first beats product-first. Find an existing community with a problem, pitch the community owner a partnership before writing a line of code, build in 48 hours, launch to a warm audience. Boring business communities that fit this model: trade association newsletters, franchisee groups (lawn care, pest control), service business Facebook groups with 20k+ members.
- **Our Angle**: Identify a boring-niche community leader (e.g., pool route broker, HVAC franchise association) who has a large engaged audience. Build a simple tool for their audience's #1 pain point. Pitch the partnership (rev share or equity) before building. This de-risks the entire distribution problem that kills most boring-business SaaS attempts.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (the distribution partner model works perfectly with AppSumo — community owner promotes the LTD, earns affiliate rev)

## Ask HN: Boring Businesses for Hackers — Niche Gap List

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38882314
- **Additional Links**: N/A
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN (Jan 2024, still actively referenced in 2025–2026)
- **Engagement**: 70 points, 76 comments
- **Revenue Data**: N/A (discussion thread)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Multiple
- **Core Value Prop**: Community-sourced list of markets with sleepy/stagnant incumbents. Key callouts from comments:
  - **Hotel management software**: "There are like two, both garbage" — entrenched but terrible UX, no modern challenger
  - **Access control / biometric office entry**: Cheap Chinese hardware, terrible management software, zero competition
  - **Bookkeeping for small businesses**: CPA shortage (65% of CPAs are 55+), huge demand for software-assisted bookkeeping
  - **Restaurant menu sync**: Menus out of sync across DoorDash/UberEats/website — losing sales daily, no clean tool
  - **Web presence for trades**: Plumbers/landscapers/roofers with terrible or no websites, don't understand SEO
  - **MSP tooling for SMBs**: Ticketing, invoicing, onboarding automation — still done manually at most small MSPs
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The "restaurant menu sync" idea is particularly interesting — small/mid restaurants are losing revenue daily from inconsistent menus across 3–5 delivery platforms. No indie SaaS handles this well. Estimated TAM: hundreds of thousands of US restaurants.
- **Our Angle**: Restaurant menu sync as a micro-SaaS: connect Squarespace/Wix/GoDaddy website + DoorDash + UberEats + Grubhub and keep menus/prices/hours in sync. $29–$49/mo. Clear ROI story (they lose more per month in missed orders than the subscription costs). Bootstrappable solo.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (great AppSumo candidate — restaurant owners respond to one-time deals)
