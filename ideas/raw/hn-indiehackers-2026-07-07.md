## Finding 1: Zirco.ai — AI Dental Front Desk

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47385090
- **Additional Links**: https://zircoai.vercel.app
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 1 upvote, 3 comments (low visibility but early stage with 30+ practices interviewed)
- **Revenue Data**: None disclosed; beta stage
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Dental practices
- **Core Value Prop**: AI that replaces the dental front desk — handles inbound calls, insurance benefit verification (2–3 hrs/day manually), appointment booking, and patient reminders via SMS/email. Front desk employees cost $40–50K/year with 40% annual turnover.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Insurance verification is genuinely painful and time-consuming. Avoca AI just hit $1B valuation doing the same for HVAC/plumbing — dental is a parallel market that's wide open. No dominant AI-native player yet.
- **Our Angle**: Narrower scope than Avoca: focus only on insurance verification automation for dental. Lower price point ($200–400/month vs. Avoca's $2K/month) could capture the 80% of independent dental practices not willing to pay enterprise pricing.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (recurring value is high; LTD less natural for voice AI with ongoing compute costs)

## Finding 2: Pest Control Vertical SaaS / Tech-Enabled Operator

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: N/A
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN / founder story
- **Engagement**: Active thread with substantive community discussion
- **Revenue Data**: Commenter cited "$2M ARR for a few years now" from incumbent pest control SaaS; founder personally passed pest control certification exam in 13 days (company record)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control services
- **Core Value Prop**: The founder embedded himself as a technician to understand the business, then pivoted from building generic pest control software to tech-enabling an acquired operator. Key insight: existing software is "decent, cheap, and ubiquitous" — the gap is operational execution, not software.

- **Our Angle**: AI-powered voice-to-CRM for pest control field techs. No typing required: technician says what they found and did on-site, AI logs it into the CRM. Targets the "lots of antiquated operators not having newer technology" segment.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 (clear per-tech pricing model; LTD for small operators who hate SaaS subscriptions)

## Finding 4: HVAC / Plumbing SaaS Market — ServiceTitan Gap for Small Operators

- **Source**: https://www.saasmag.com/vertical-saas-niche-beats-horizontal-2026/ | https://startup.unitelvoice.com/servicetitan-alternatives | https://ctacquisitions.com/guides/private-equity-hvac-2026/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.signalfire.com/blog/vertical-ai-in-trades-and-construction
- **Platform**: HN discussion (https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41828896) + market research
- **Type**: Market validation / discussion
- **Engagement**: HN thread about "America's new millionaire class: Plumbers and HVAC entrepreneurs" — active discussion
- **Revenue Data**: ServiceTitan: $685M ARR, $9.6B valuation (Dec 2024 IPO). Avoca AI: $1B valuation, $125M raised, 800+ customers, 8-figure ARR, on track to book $1B in jobs for clients (2026). Market: ~$2.1–2.2T US trades/construction annually.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: ServiceTitan costs $245+/technician/month with high onboarding fees — too expensive for solo and 1–5 tech shops (the majority of the market). Technicians spend ~30% of working hours on non-billable admin tasks. Paper, phone calls, and PDFs still dominate.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The 1–10 tech HVAC/plumbing shop is systematically underserved. They can't afford ServiceTitan, don't want complex onboarding, and need mobile-first simplicity. Jobber/Housecall Pro exist but have their own gaps (no AI, clunky estimates, weak QuickBooks sync). The QuickBooks integration pain is acute — HVAC operators constantly manage two systems.
- **Our Angle**: AI-native HVAC/plumbing ops tool for 1–10 tech shops. Core pitch: "Everything ServiceTitan does for 1/5 the price, works on your phone, set up in a day." Embedded voice-to-estimate generation differentiates from Jobber/Housecall Pro.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 (this niche is the sweet spot — operators hate monthly fees, love one-time deals, buy on AppSumo, share in Facebook groups)

## Market Context: Why Boring Business SaaS Is Winning in 2026

- **ServiceTitan** (HVAC/plumbing/electrical): $685M ARR, $9.6B valuation — proof the market is massive
- **Avoca AI** (voice agents for trades): $1B valuation in 18 months — AI is unlocking new price points
- **ZenMaid** (cleaning): $3M ARR bootstrapped — the playbook works for solo founders
- **Vertical SaaS market**: $130B (2025) → $499B (2035), growing 16–22% annually
- **Gross retention advantage**: Vertical SaaS retains 91% vs. 78–85% for horizontal SMB SaaS
- **The underdigitization fact**: Technicians spend ~30% of working hours on non-billable admin; 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025)
- **PE rollup signal**: 27+ active PE platforms buying HVAC companies in 2026 — inflating valuations and degrading service quality, opening market for independent alternatives

## Top 3 Opportunities Ranked by Potential

### #1 — AI Call Answering + Job Booking for Small HVAC/Plumbing Shops
**Why**: Avoca AI just proved the market ($1B valuation, 800+ customers) but at $2K/month they miss 90% of shops. The 1–5 technician segment has zero good options. AI voice + booking at $149–299/month with a $499 LTD offer is the wedge.
**Distribution**: HVAC Facebook groups (hundreds of thousands of members), AppSumo, YouTube HVAC business channels.
**Revenue target**: 500 customers x $200/month = $100K MRR in 18 months.
**LTD Potential**: 5/5

### #2 — Trades Job Costing + Bookkeeping (QuickBooks Alternative for Contractors)
**Why**: Every HVAC/plumbing/electrical contractor is running two systems (field service software + QuickBooks) and neither tells them per-job profitability. ZenMaid proved cleaning software works at $3M ARR; job-costing for general trades is a bigger market with no clear indie winner.
**Distribution**: Trade association newsletters, contractor Facebook groups, content marketing ("How much did that HVAC job actually make you?").
**Revenue target**: 1,000 customers x $79/month = $79K MRR.
**LTD Potential**: 5/5

### #3 — Pest Control Technician AI Tools (Voice-to-CRM, Smart Trap Integration)
**Why**: Existing pest control software is "decent, cheap, and ubiquitous" (per HN thread) but has no AI. Regulatory compliance (chemical usage reporting, treatment documentation) is mandatory and still paper-based. $2M ARR already proven for incumbent — there's room for an AI-first challenger.
**Distribution**: Pest control Facebook groups (PestVet, Pest Control Operators), direct outreach to independent operators being squeezed by Terminix/Rentokil PE rollups.
**Revenue target**: 300 customers x $149/month = $44K MRR.
**LTD Potential**: 4/5
