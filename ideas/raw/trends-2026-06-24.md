## Executive Summary

Boring business software is the hottest unsexy category in tech right now. Vertical SaaS captured 53% of all VC deal volume in 2025 ($56.2B deployed across 4,395 deals), and the Q4 data shows the trend accelerating — 55% of all new venture-backed companies in Q4 2025 were building for a specific industry. AEC & Trades specifically saw 42% of deals fall in the $5–15M tier, the highest of any vertical, signaling the category has moved past early formation into mainstream legibility.

Landmark signals: Avoca (AI voice agents for HVAC/plumbing) hit a $1B valuation in April 2026 with $125M raised. CompanyCam (contractor photo documentation) raised $415M at a $2B valuation, becoming Nebraska's first unicorn. ServiceTitan IPO'd in late 2024. Fleetio raised $454M at $1.5B for fleet/auto repair. These exits and rounds are attracting a wave of new seed-stage founders into the category.

The field service management market is projected to grow from $6.26B in 2026 to $23.61B by 2035. Vertical SaaS commands a 41% M&A premium over horizontal SaaS, with PE firms executing 73+ transactions worth $15.3B in Q1 2025 alone. Boring business software is becoming PE's favorite category.

**Bottom line for founders:** Pick a specific trade, go deep, win the workflow. The whitespace is in sub-vertical specificity (roofing vs. HVAC vs. plumbing), AI-native front-office automation, and the long tail of micro-verticals that are too small for ServiceTitan but too complex for generic tools.

## Trend 1: AI Voice Agents for the Trades Front Office

- **Category**: Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:

  - https://contractortoolstack.com/software/avoca-ai/
- **Description**: Trades businesses (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical) miss huge-dollar inbound calls because they're in the field — no receptionist, no answering system. When a restaurant misses a call, it's a $30–40 order. When an HVAC company misses a call, it can be a $30,000–40,000 install. Avoca built AI voice agents that answer every inbound lead in seconds, book jobs into the CRM, follow up on outstanding estimates, and run outbound campaigns. They hit $1B valuation in April 2026, with 800+ customers, on track to book $1B in jobs this year. Kleiner Perkins partner described it as "a necessary bridge between Silicon Valley and Main Street." The formula: trades workers are the main characters, AI does the front-office admin.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, pest control, moving companies, auto service
- **Market Timing**: Growing — Avoca has first-mover advantage but the market is large enough for multiple entrants. Only one unicorn so far in a $50B+ HVAC market alone.
- **Example Products**:
  - Avoca (https://avoca.ai) — $1B valuation, Kleiner/Meritech/General Catalyst backed
  - AutoLeap AIR (https://autoleap.com) — AI receptionist for auto repair shops
  - TriFetch (https://trifetch.ai) — clinic workflow automation, $1.9M pre-seed 2026
- **White Space**: Sub-vertical AI voice agents for smaller niches (pool service, landscaping, pest control). Estimate follow-up automation. Dispatch optimization integrated with AI scheduling. "AI office manager" that handles inbound + outbound + estimate follow-up for shops under $1M revenue (Avoca focuses on 800+ customer operators; the solo/small shop market is unaddressed).
- **Speed to Market**: 4–8 weeks for MVP using Twilio Voice + LLM + ServiceTitan/Jobber webhooks. The integration complexity is the moat, not the AI itself.
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — trades owners are value-focused but the ROI story ($30K+ job from a captured call) makes monthly subscription the natural fit. LTD works for smaller operators who won't pay $500+/month.
- **Content Strategy**: "missed call HVAC cost," "AI receptionist plumbing," "how to never miss a service call," "HVAC answering service alternative"

## Key Themes & Cross-Cutting Insights

**1. Founder-Market Fit is Decisive**
Every major winner in AEC & Trades has a founder who worked in the industry: Luke Hansen (CompanyCam) from his family roofing business, Avoca founders embedding themselves with an HVAC company for months before shipping. The HN pest control SaaS founder took a technician job before building. The distribution advantage of knowing the community — trade shows, industry associations, Facebook Groups — is as important as the product.

**2. The $1M–$5M Revenue Sweet Spot is Unaddressed**
ServiceTitan targets $2M+ revenue businesses. Generic tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) serve sub-$500K shops adequately. The 1–3 person operation doing $500K–$1.5M in revenue is in the gap — too complex for basic tools, too small for ServiceTitan. This is where the LTD / affordable SaaS opportunity lives.

**3. AI's Role is Admin, Not Labor**
Avoca's Tyson Chen said it explicitly: "AI functions as a tool that makes good mechanics more productive, not a replacement." The AI pitch that lands in boring business is: AI handles the phone, the follow-up emails, the invoice reconciliation, the compliance paperwork — so the tradesperson can stay in the field. Front-office automation is the Trojan horse into these businesses.

**4. Vertical SaaS Commands Premium Exits**
