## ZenMaid — Scheduling SaaS for Maid/Cleaning Services

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/from-a-cleaning-side-hustle-to-a-3m-yr-saas-for-cleaning-services-suhsqkDZB1zIwRmXxrFm

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Case study (Feb 2025)
- **Engagement**: 28 upvotes, 10 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $250K/month MRR → $3M/yr ARR. Bootstrapped. 11+ years running. Lifestyle business.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential maid/cleaning services

## Dental Practice Compliance SaaS (Auxpanel model)

- **Source**: https://medium.com/@urano10/vertical-saas-micro-niches-beyond-the-obvious-01e0adf16a98
- **Additional Links**: https://auxpanel.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers ecosystem / Medium research
- **Type**: Bootstrapped case study
- **Engagement**: Referenced in vertical SaaS deep-dive article (Dec 2025)
- **Revenue Data**: Auxpanel reached profitability in year 2 with ZERO marketing spend, purely word-of-mouth. Estimated $30K–$50K MRR (conservative: 100+ dental practices × $300–$500/month). Margins ~85%.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Dental practices (OSHA, HIPAA, state dental board compliance)
- **Core Value Prop**: Business intelligence + compliance tracking + practice management. Dental practices face real legal exposure without proper compliance tracking — the ROI is undeniable.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 200,000 dental practices in the US. Even 1% penetration = 2,000 customers × $350/month = $700K MRR. Most dentists still use paper checklists or generic tools. OSHA compliance logs, sterilization records, and infection control documentation are required by law — this is a recurring-need product.
- **Our Angle**: Focus on the most painful compliance pain (OSHA log, sterilization records). Start with one state, build dental association partnerships. Sell via dental CE course platforms. Build in AI to auto-generate compliance reports from photos. LTD to build base.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Fleet Management SaaS for Small Fleets (10–50 vehicles)

- **Source**: https://medium.com/@urano10/vertical-saas-micro-niches-beyond-the-obvious-01e0adf16a98
- **Additional Links**: https://getlatka.com/companies/industries/i-hvac-software
- **Platform**: Medium / Indie Hackers ecosystem analysis
- **Type**: Market analysis + validated model (Dec 2025)
- **Engagement**: Widely cited in indie hacker SaaS strategy content
- **Revenue Data**: Navifleet (incumbent) serves 1500+ clients and saw 25% MRR surge after pricing optimization. Solo-founder model projection: $80/vehicle/month × 30 vehicles avg × 100 fleets = $240K MRR. Churn: ~1.2%/month (high switching costs). CAC: ~$1,200 via trade shows and forums.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Small fleet operators — plumbing, HVAC, landscaping, delivery companies with 10–50 vehicles
- **Core Value Prop**: GPS tracking, DOT compliance logs, maintenance reminders, driver log books, fuel cost tracking. Smaller than enterprise fleet tools, cheaper and simpler than Samsara/Verizon Connect.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Enterprise players (Samsara, Verizon Connect) ignore fleets under 50 vehicles. Small operators pay $50–$100/vehicle/month for bloated enterprise tools or use spreadsheets. The 10–50 vehicle SMB segment is massively underserved by modern, affordable software. DOT regulations mean this is legally required record-keeping — not optional.
- **Our Angle**: No hardware required (phone GPS), DOT compliance built-in from day 1, flat pricing per vehicle (no per-seat chaos), simple mobile app for drivers. Market via trucking/HVAC Facebook groups, AppSumo LTD. Differentiate: AI-powered preventive maintenance reminders that actually reduce breakdown costs.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 (hardware-adjacent products are harder LTD plays — focus on software-only)

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers ecosystem / market research (Jan 2026)
- **Type**: Market signal / underserved segment analysis
- **Engagement**: Cited in multiple 2026 bootstrapped SaaS guides
- **Revenue Data**: FSM market $5.1B (2025) → $9.17B by 2030 (12.5% CAGR). 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025). Leading indie player Jobber: 300K+ users, starting at $29/month. Opportunity for niche-within-niche plays.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Solo/small HVAC, plumbing, electrical contractors (1–5 techs)
- **Core Value Prop**: Job scheduling, customer history, mobile invoicing, payment collection, equipment service history. Jobber/ServiceTitan are too expensive and complex for solo operators. Most 1-2 person shops use pen and paper.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Jobber starts at $29/month but has 50+ features solo contractors never use. Workiz/ServiceFusion still feel like enterprise tools. The genuine gap: an ultra-simple, mobile-first tool for single-tech operations with flat pricing ($19–$39/month), WhatsApp integration for customer updates, and a 5-minute onboarding. Trade-specific versions for each niche (HVAC: refrigerant tracking; plumbing: fixture catalog; electrical: circuit diagrams).
- **Our Angle**: Pick ONE trade (e.g., HVAC). Build the world's simplest scheduling + invoicing + customer history tool. Integrate with QuickBooks for tax time. AppSumo LTD to get first 500 users. Join HVAC Facebook groups for distribution.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5

## Craft Brewery Inventory & Batch Management SaaS

- **Additional Links**: https://www.brewersassociation.org/statistics-and-data/national-beer-stats/

- **Type**: Market signal / underserved niche analysis
- **Engagement**: Cited in bootstrapped SaaS strategy content
- **Revenue Data**: 9,778 US craft breweries (Brewers Association 2025). Breweries lose avg $12K/year to inventory management issues. Target pricing: $79–$199/month. At 10% penetration = ~980 customers × $99/month = $97K MRR.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Craft breweries, cideries, meaderies (10–500 barrel/year production)
- **Core Value Prop**: Batch tracking (start-to-tap), ingredient inventory, fermentation schedule, keg location tracking, ABV/IBU calculations, compliance reporting for TTB. Generic inventory tools force breweries into wrong-shaped workflows.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Generic tools (QuickBooks, Excel) fail because they can't handle batch numbers, fermentation stages, ingredient lot tracking, and TTB compliance simultaneously. Existing brew-specific tools (OrchestratedBEER, Ekos) are expensive enterprise plays ($500+/month). No affordable indie option exists for 1–3 person nano/microbreweries.
- **Our Angle**: $59–$99/month flat pricing for breweries under 1000 barrels/year. Focus on batch tracking and TTB compliance report generation. Sell via Brewers Association, homebrewer-to-pro communities. Strong LTD candidate — brewers are passionate, community-driven, and willing to pay for good tools.

- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5

- **Additional Links**: https://www.entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/

- **Type**: Market gap signal (cited as "real money" niche by indie experts)
- **Engagement**: Consistently cited as underserved in January 2026 indie SaaS content
- **Revenue Data**: ~28,900 licensed residential care homes in the US (small/medium). Target: $99–$199/month. Even 1% penetration = 289 customers × $149 = $43K MRR.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Small residential care homes, adult foster care, assisted living (4–24 beds)
- **Core Value Prop**: CMS/state inspection compliance checklists, resident care plan documentation, medication administration records (MAR), incident reporting, staff training logs, fire drill records.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Large facilities use enterprise tools ($500–$2000/month). Small 4–10 bed homes can't afford enterprise software and use paper binders. State regulators are increasing enforcement — operators are actively seeking better compliance tools. Regulatory requirement creates very low churn (operators can't stop tracking compliance). Almost no indie/bootstrapped competition — VCs avoid this space entirely.
- **Our Angle**: State-specific compliance modules (start with one state). $99/month for homes under 10 beds, $149/month for 10–24 beds. Sell via state care home associations, adult care licensing consultants. AppSumo LTD possible for awareness. Strong word-of-mouth in tight care home operator communities.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5
