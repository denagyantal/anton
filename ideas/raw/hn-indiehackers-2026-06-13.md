## Rentman — AV/Event Production Operations Platform

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q
- **Additional Links**: https://rentman.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder interview
- **Engagement**: 110 upvotes, active comments (April 2026)
- **Revenue Data**: $15M–$20M ARR, bootstrapped for 8 years before taking outside capital
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — AV rental/event production is unsexy and operationally complex
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, event production, staging, broadcast, film crew
- **Core Value Prop**: All-in-one ops platform for production companies: equipment rental, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, invoicing
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Founder Roy van den Broek built it because existing tools were spreadsheets or software "bent into shape" from other industries. Started with 5 design-partner companies. Took 8 years bootstrapped to get to tens of thousands of users in 70+ countries. Product-led growth via tight-knit AV industry word-of-mouth and freelancers who brought it to new employers. Currently targeting US expansion. AI layer next.
- **Our Angle**: Rentman owns the mid-market. There's a massive underserved long tail: **church AV teams, wedding AV companies, school/university production departments, small festival staging companies**. These are too small for Rentman but too complex for spreadsheets. A lighter-weight "Rentman Lite" or specifically "church AV operations tool" could capture a very loyal, high-LTV niche.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — MRR model better, but church/school market may prefer one-time licensing

## Trades Compliance & License Tracking

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/ + HN underserved industries signals https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22496129
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/should-i-just-create-a-boring-b2b-saas-b6181991c0
- **Platform**: Hacker News (Ask HN 2020, still highly relevant) + IH discussion
- **Type**: Ask HN demand signal + market gap
- **Engagement**: Ask HN thread had 169 comments; "trades/blue-collar software" was the top recurring answer
- **Revenue Data**: No validated indie product yet — gap confirmed by 89% of tradespeople lacking adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025 Tech Adoption Report)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — contractor licensing/compliance is brutally unsexy
- **Target Industry**: Licensed trades: plumbing, electrical, HVAC, roofing, pest control — any trade requiring state licenses, insurance certificates, bonding
- **Core Value Prop**: Track expiration dates for contractor licenses, liability insurance, surety bonds, vehicle registrations, EPA certifications, worker comp policies — and send renewal reminders before they lapse
- **Gap/Opportunity**: A lapsed license = can't work = immediate revenue loss. Contractors track this in spreadsheets and miss renewals regularly, exposing themselves to fines or job loss. No purpose-built lightweight tool exists at sub-$50/mo. Existing solutions are buried inside field service management platforms that cost $200+/mo.
- **Our Angle**: Build **ContractorCompliance.io** — a dead-simple dashboard where a contractor enters all their licenses, certs, and insurance policies; gets email/SMS reminders 90/60/30 days before expiry; and can share a "compliance profile" URL with general contractors who want to verify subs. Priced at $19/mo or $99 LTD.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — perfect AppSumo product: simple, single-purpose, saves real money

## Key Market Themes (2026)

**From HN/IH signal analysis:**

1. **Vertical SaaS moats are real** — Rentman's $15M+ ARR built purely in AV rental demonstrates that going deep in one boring industry beats going broad. "Horizontal players cannot cross over without a domain shift that breaks their UX." (Gregory Scott Henson, IH comment, 2026)

2. **The 80,000+ HVAC software user signal** — Breezy's rapid growth proves trades software demand is not yet saturated. Legacy tools (decades-old desktop software) are being displaced by modern SaaS with mobile apps.

3. **Deathcare consolidation = opportunistic window** — PlotBox acquiring Batesville's HMIS in Oct 2025 signals the legacy funeral tech is being rolled up. Specialty niches (pet cremation, green burial, memorial livestreaming) have a 2-3 year window before they're absorbed.

4. **"Pay for one thing" anti-subscription trend** — JustFax Online's success with one-time payments for a specific action resonates on HN. Contractors, tradespeople, and SMBs are fatigued by recurring software costs. LTD or per-use models are differentiators.

5. **89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools** (SBA Q3 2025 Tech Adoption Report) — this is a massive underserved market. The key is not building another "field service platform" but solving one specific unbearable pain (license expiry, inspection reports, chain of custody) with a focused tool.
