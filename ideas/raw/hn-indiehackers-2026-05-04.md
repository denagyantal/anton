## Tap-to-Report App for Home Inspectors

- **Source**: https://blog.startupstash.com/7-boring-micro-saas-ideas-making-2k-month-the-developers-blueprint-8beec91d4cd0
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/5-product-ideas-with-proven-demand-e470cee6a9
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Medium
- **Type**: Validated idea breakdown with MVP spec
- **Engagement**: High — article widely shared in IH circles (Jan 2026)
- **Revenue Data**: Concept stage — inspectors charge ~$500/inspection, 2 per day bottlenecked by report typing. 3rd inspection = $500/day more revenue for inspector → high WTP for tool
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Home inspection
- **Core Value Prop**: Native mobile app (offline-first) that lets inspectors tap to fill pre-loaded comment snippets and markup photos on-site, eliminating the 2–3 hour "second shift" of report writing
- **Gap/Opportunity**: No dominant micro-SaaS in this space — most inspectors use generic tools or paste from Word docs. The moat is a pre-loaded library of 500+ standard inspection comments for every defect type (roofing, electrical, HVAC, foundation). Offline-first is table stakes (basements, rural properties have no signal)
- **Our Angle**: Build a pre-loaded comment library from real inspector templates + a "show the client what your old reports look like vs. ours in 10 minutes" cold outreach play. Sell the time savings to unlock a 3rd daily inspection
- **LTD Potential**: 4 — inspectors are solo operators who love one-time purchases; recurring can work at $49–79/month given the direct revenue uplift

## HVAC EPA Section 608 Compliance Sidecar

- **Source**: https://blog.startupstash.com/7-boring-micro-saas-ideas-making-2k-month-the-developers-blueprint-8beec91d4cd0
- **Additional Links**: https://www.workyard.com/compare/hvac-software, https://clarro.ca/blog/hvac-field-service-management-software-guide/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Medium
- **Type**: Validated gap analysis
- **Engagement**: Referenced across multiple 2025/2026 boring-SaaS listicles
- **Revenue Data**: EPA Section 608 violations = $44,539/day/violation. Insurance-angle pitch removes pricing objection. Comparable niche compliance tools: $79–199/month
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC — refrigerant-handling technicians and business owners
- **Core Value Prop**: Compliance workflow tool that sits alongside ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro. Hard-stop UI blocks "Finish Job" until refrigerant weight + photo of scale is logged. OCR scans unit model plate. One-click EPA audit export in the correct format
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ServiceTitan is too broad — handles scheduling/billing but not granular EPA 608 compliance fields. No indie-scale competitor exists specifically for this. HVAC business owners lose sleep over compliance; this is a "don't-get-sued tool" not a productivity tool
- **Our Angle**: Sell via HVAC business insurance brokers ("recommend this to your clients, reduce their liability, you look like a hero"). Partner with local trade associations. Not competing with ServiceTitan — running alongside it
- **LTD Potential**: 3 — compliance tools need ongoing updates as regulations change; MRR model is stronger. LTD at $199–299 to seed early customers

## Automated Shift Bidding for Staffing/Security Guard Agencies

- **Source**: https://blog.startupstash.com/7-boring-micro-saas-ideas-making-2k-month-the-developers-blueprint-8beec91d4cd0
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=13368013 (Staffjoy — abandoned, similar concept), https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=10433521
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Medium + HN historical context
- **Type**: Validated idea breakdown (Staffjoy tried and failed at scale; micro-niche version untapped)
- **Engagement**: Staffjoy was on HN twice — real demand signal; company pivoted away from this market
- **Revenue Data**: Target $99–299/month per agency. Agencies run on group texts today — zero software spend
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Staffing agencies — especially security guard, cleaning, and healthcare temp staffing
- **Core Value Prop**: SMS-based shift fill system (no app download for workers). Tiered blast: Gold-rated staff first → 5 min wait → everyone else. "Yes/Yeah/I'll take it" parser. "Banned from site" list prevents legal exposure when sending wrong person to a client site. Removes manager favoritism drama
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Staffjoy was open-sourced after abandoning the market. No dedicated micro-SaaS for small staffing agencies (20–200 workers). Generic tools like WhenIWork miss the "banned list" and impartial auto-selection logic that agencies legally need
- **Our Angle**: Niche down to security guard agencies first — highest regulatory exposure, clearest pain around fairness and banned-worker lists. Cold email Sunday nights at 8pm ("Stop texting staff to fill Monday morning shifts")
- **LTD Potential**: 3 — recurring is better given evolving staff lists, but LTD at $299 gets first 50 agencies

## Client Intake Portal for Estate Planning Lawyers

- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/should-i-just-create-a-boring-b2b-saas-b6181991c0

- **Type**: Validated idea breakdown with sales channel insight
- **Engagement**: Legal-tech is consistently cited in IH "boring SaaS" discussions
- **Revenue Data**: Lawyers bill $300–500/hour — any tool saving 2+ hours/week is an easy $199–299/month sell
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Solo/small estate planning law firms
- **Core Value Prop**: Secure intake portal with form validation (catches client typos in names, dates before they become legal errors). "Finish Later" email recovery for 80% who abandon mid-form. Auto-generates formatted Word doc in the lawyer's letterhead style. Pushes data into Clio/PracticePanther via Zapier or direct API

- **LTD Potential**: 3 — compliance and integration updates mean MRR is better; LTD at $249–399 possible

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Medium

- **Revenue Data**: Real estate photographers charge $150–400/shoot. This enables a "Marketing Agency" upsell doubling their revenue → high WTP. $49–99/month per photographer
- **Boring Business Score**: 3

- **Core Value Prop**: Upload once → auto-generate branded link (with agent contact) AND unbranded link (MLS-compliant). Video hosting via Vimeo/Mux API. Lead-capture form on branded site. Automatic social media flyer PDFs from photos

- **LTD Potential**: 4 — photographers are freelancers who love one-time tools; LTD at $99–199 works well for initial traction

- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Trades — electricians, plumbers, HVAC, locksmiths
- **Core Value Prop**: AI agent that handles inbound web chat, SMS, and missed calls 24/7 for trade businesses — qualifies the lead (emergency or scheduled?), books the appointment, sends confirmation. Tradespeople can't answer the phone while under a sink
