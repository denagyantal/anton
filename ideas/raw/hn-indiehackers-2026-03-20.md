# HN & Indie Hackers Scanner — 2026-03-20

## Summary

Today's scan highlights the massive validation happening in "boring business" vertical SaaS: Spectora (home inspection) hitting $30M ARR from a $5K MVP, Rashid Khasanov's $42K MRR portfolio of "boring database products," and a fresh wave of invoicing tools flooding Show HN (Invox, Sidepay, PlainInvoice, Atrium — signaling persistent unmet demand in freelancer/trades billing). The field service management space continues to show a clear gap between enterprise players (ServiceTitan at $200+/mo) and solo tradespeople stuck on paper. Construction payment tracking, salon/hairdressing tech, and food business inventory management surfaced as industries still running on spreadsheets. Multiple sources converge on the same thesis: boring micro-SaaS targeting 10,000+ potential customers at $50-200/mo pricing, built by solo founders, routinely hits $5K-$50K MRR with 70%+ margins.

## Spectora — Home Inspection Software ($30M ARR Boring Business Masterclass)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/hitting-30m-arr-with-the-first-product-he-built-oJ1E1ukp60M7OZZXl2AJ

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Interview
- **Engagement**: Featured IH interview (high visibility)
- **Revenue Data**: $30M ARR (2025), projecting $40M by end of 2026. PE partial exit at $90M valuation (2023), then $110M (2024). Founder retains 12%. SaaS + usage-based pricing. MVP cost $5,000 total ($2,500 each from two co-founders).
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Home inspection / residential real estate
- **Core Value Prop**: All-in-one inspection software — report generation, scheduling, payment processing, client communication. Built on Rails + Vue.js. Replaces paper checklists and Word docs that inspectors were using.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The founding story is the playbook: interview industry practitioners (they bought inspectors Starbucks), build a focused MVP, then show up to Facebook groups every day for years answering questions. - **Our Angle**: The Spectora story validates the "pick one boring trade, go deep" approach. Similar untapped niches at the same scale: pest control inspection reporting, pool service compliance tracking, fire safety inspection software. Any trade that requires inspection + reporting + scheduling has this same playbook available. Can't compete with Spectora directly, but the pattern is replicable in adjacent trades.

# Market Signals & Trends

## Key Themes This Week

1. **The "Spectora Playbook" is the gold standard** — $5K MVP → interview practitioners → show up to industry Facebook groups daily → scale to $30M ARR. This pattern is replicable in any trade that requires inspection + reporting + scheduling. The key was distribution (community presence, SEO) not product sophistication.

2. **Invoicing is the most-built, least-solved problem** — 5+ invoicing tools launched on Show HN in 4 months. Everyone builds generic invoicing. Nobody builds trade-specific invoicing with materials markup, job photos, permit tracking, and warranty management. The gap isn't "simple invoicing" — it's "invoicing that understands my trade."

3. **"Curated databases are harder to kill"** — Rashid Khasanov's $42K MRR portfolio proves that well-maintained, curated data products have natural moats that AI wrappers don't. This applies to any boring industry: licensed contractor databases, supplier directories, equipment specification databases.

4. **The $50-100/mo field service gap persists** — ServiceTitan is too expensive and complex for small crews. Solo-contractor tools are too simple for 2-5 person teams. Repair-CRM at $89/mo for 5 users is actively positioning in this gap with aggressive SEO content.

5. **Construction remains the most-cited underserved industry** — Appears in every HN "underserved industries" thread, every micro-SaaS roundup, and multiple indie hacker discussions. Subcontractor payment tracking is the most actionable sub-niche: clear pain, regulatory drivers (lien waivers), spreadsheet-dependent workflows.

6. **Salon tech has an untouched AI angle** — Scheduling is solved (Vagaro, Fresha). Visual AI consultation (showing clients realistic previews) and color formula management are completely unaddressed.
