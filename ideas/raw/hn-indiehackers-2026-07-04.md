## ZenMaid — Scheduling Software for Maid/Cleaning Services

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/from-a-cleaning-side-hustle-to-a-3m-yr-saas-for-cleaning-services-suhsqkDZB1zIwRmXxrFm
- **Additional Links**: https://get.zenmaid.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Interview
- **Engagement**: 28 upvotes, 10 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $3M/yr ($250k/mo MRR). Bootstrapped since 2013, 13+ years old.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential cleaning / maid services
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling software specifically for maid/cleaning service businesses — booking, routing, staff management, client retention. Founder Amar Ghose ran a cleaning business himself, discovered the gap, built ZenMaid with a co-founder.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ZenMaid dominates this niche but has been around 11+ years with legacy UX. The market is validated at scale ($3M/yr from a single focused product). Modern challengers could win with: AI-powered route optimization, WhatsApp/SMS customer comms, built-in review generation for Google/Yelp, instant quote calculators, and better mobile apps for cleaners. Competing cleaning verticals (commercial cleaning, Airbnb turnovers, post-construction) are adjacent and underserved.
- **Our Angle**: AI-native cleaning ops platform focused on Airbnb/short-term rental turnovers — a huge exploding niche with unique scheduling needs (back-to-back cleanings, linen tracking, damage photo reports). ZenMaid doesn't focus here. $59–$99/mo LTD target.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Rentman — Operations Platform for AV/Event Production Companies

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q
- **Additional Links**: https://rentman.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Interview (published April 2026)
- **Engagement**: 111 upvotes, 11 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $15M–$20M ARR ($1.25M+/mo). Bootstrapped 8 years before raising. 70+ countries.
- **Boring Business Score**: 3/5
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, lighting, staging, broadcast, film production companies
- **Core Value Prop**: Equipment rental management + crew scheduling + quoting + logistics + invoicing for production and rental companies. Founder started an AV rental company at 16 and built the tool for himself.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Rentman is the worldwide market leader but is primarily cloud SaaS and focuses on larger production companies. Smaller AV integrators (install side, not rental) and regional A/V companies in emerging markets (Southeast Asia, Latin America) are underserved. Also: smaller sub-niches like photography equipment rentals, music gear rentals, party supply rentals. The core lesson: any niche where businesses still use spreadsheets + a "bent" generic tool = Rentman-sized opportunity waiting.
- **Our Angle**: Equipment rental + scheduling for a different niche vertical — e.g., power tool/equipment rental yards (SMB construction gear rental), costume/prop rental companies for film, or party rental (tents, tables, bounce houses). Each is $50k–$500k/yr market opportunity. LTD at $79/site feasible.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5

## Stagetimer — Remote Countdown Timers for Live Events

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/turning-a-simple-b2b-solution-into-a-15k-mrr-saas-by-exploiting-a-market-niche-xIxrVwn24DVsN8b3PYD3
- **Additional Links**: https://stagetimer.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Interview (published August 2024)
- **Engagement**: 50 upvotes, 20 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $15k MRR subscription + one-time payments, $20k/mo combined, 13,000+ accounts. Founder Lukas Hermann, solo bootstrapped.
- **Boring Business Score**: 3/5
- **Target Industry**: Live events, conferences, churches, broadcast productions, corporate AV
- **Core Value Prop**: Web-based synchronized countdown/clock timer visible on stage monitors and speaker screens at live events. Browser-based, no hardware needed. SEO-driven growth off low-volume niche keywords.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Stagetimer proves ultra-niche event production tools can quietly build $15k+ MRR. Adjacent underserved tools: broadcast graphics lower-thirds software, church presentation management (beyond ProPresenter), greenroom speaker queue displays, event check-in + badge printing, backstage rundown/cue sheets. Conference/event production runs on spreadsheets + generic tools.
- **Our Angle**: "Stagetimer for churches" — a dedicated church service flow management tool (song lyrics, sermon notes, countdown, volunteer scheduling, giving kiosks). 300k+ US churches, most still use spreadsheets or expensive legacy tools (Planning Center dominates but is complex). Simpler $29/mo option is viable. Good LTD story.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Carbon — Open-Source ERP for SMB Manufacturers

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44792005
- **Additional Links**: https://carbon.ms/ | https://biggo.com/news/202508050714_Carbon_ERP_Self-Hosting_Challenges
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Show HN (August 2025)
- **Engagement**: High engagement (hundreds of comments with detailed technical feedback from ERP professionals)
- **Revenue Data**: 5 early customers using it to run operations. Open-source model, commercial license available.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Small-to-mid-size manufacturing (job shops, custom parts, furniture, food manufacturing)
- **Core Value Prop**: API-first, open-source ERP combining ERP + MES + QMS for manufacturers who find SAP/Odoo too bloated or expensive. Built by a solo dev over 3 years. Targets job shops and startups (sub-200 employee manufacturers).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: SAP is for enterprises ($500k+/yr). Odoo is complex to self-host. The SMB manufacturing gap (10–100 employees) is huge and underserved. Thread feedback shows strong demand: furniture makers, custom parts manufacturers, food producers all struggle. **The key gap**: Carbon still lacks accounting/GL integration, which is the #1 blocker for purchase decisions. Also, self-hosting complexity is a serious UX barrier (multiple microservices, Supabase dependency). A simpler, hosted-first manufacturing ops tool (inventory, BOM, work orders, purchase orders, basic invoicing) targeting one sub-niche (e.g., food/beverage manufacturers or custom furniture shops) could win.
- **Our Angle**: "QuickBooks + Simple MRP" for 5–50 person food/beverage manufacturers. Manage recipes (BOM), production batches, inventory, and basic invoicing in one tool. No SAP-level complexity. $99/mo or $499 LTD. Food manufacturing is huge (every city has small craft breweries, sauce companies, bakeries).
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5

UK/EU regulatory pressure around allergen labelling (Natasha's Law) creates a compliance hook. Target 10,000+ UK small bakeries at £29/mo. LTD at $59–$79 maps well to AppSumo.

## PastureEgg — Farm Management for Pasture Poultry Producers

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46414570 (Ask HN: What are you building as a side-project in 2026?)
- **Additional Links**: https://www.pasturegg.com/
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Ask HN side project mention (January 2026)
- **Engagement**: Individual comment in thread, positive reactions from other HN users ("Vertical SaaS for poultry producers is not something I was expecting to see! Awesome, best of luck!")
- **Revenue Data**: Pre-revenue at time of posting. Founder has "one very happy daily user: me" and aims for revenue in 2026.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Small-to-medium pasture poultry farms (free-range eggs, broiler chickens)
- **Core Value Prop**: Record-keeping and work organization for pasture poultry operations. Tracks flock health, production records, labor tasks. Replaces messy Google Sheets.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 50,000+ small poultry farms in the US, most managing everything in spreadsheets or paper logs. Compliance, flock rotation tracking, mortality tracking, egg production logging — all manual. Broader farm management software (FarmLogs, Granular) targets row crop farmers, not livestock/poultry. Very low competition in this specific niche. Community angle: pasture-raised poultry producers have active Facebook groups and communities.
- **Our Angle**: Pasture livestock management SaaS (poultry + pigs + sheep) for small regenerative farms. Include: flock tracking, medication records, certification logs (organic/humane certifications), basic financials, CSA customer management. Target the "regenerative agriculture" movement — high-engagement community with willingness to pay for tools that help them scale. $29–$49/mo or $149 LTD.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5

- **Type**: Discussion / Solo founder story
- **Engagement**: Mentioned in broader IH community search around "underserved niche" stories
- **Revenue Data**: Early stage — no specific MRR disclosed.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Industrial facilities, manufacturing plants, construction sites, utilities, oil & gas, mining
- **Core Value Prop**: Digital platform to replace paper forms and Excel spreadsheets for industrial risk assessments, hazard identification, safety management, and disaster preparedness. Founder came from an electrical technician background — saw the problem firsthand.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Massive compliance burden in industrial sectors (OSHA, ISO, local regulations). Most companies manage safety documentation in spreadsheets, paper binders, or clunky enterprise software (SAP EHS, Intelex) that costs $50k+/yr. Core features: digital safety checklists, incident reporting, corrective action tracking, OSHA 300 log automation, toolbox talk scheduling. Target US construction SMBs first (1.5M+ companies). $79/mo or $299 LTD for small teams.

## Fleet Management for SMB Fleets (10–50 Vehicles)

- **Source**: https://medium.com/@urano10/vertical-saas-micro-niches-beyond-the-obvious-01e0adf16a98 (referenced in broader HN/IH discussion synthesis)
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41828896 (America's new millionaire class: Plumbers and HVAC entrepreneurs)
- **Platform**: HN Discussion / IH Community
- **Type**: Market analysis + HN discussion signal
- **Engagement**: HN thread on HVAC/plumbing millionaires had strong engagement (not quantified here)
- **Revenue Data**: Enterprise players (Samsara, Verizon Connect) dominate large fleets (100+ vehicles). SMB segment (10–50 vehicles) is underserved. Estimated viable: $80/vehicle/mo → $240k MRR at 100 fleets of 30 vehicles.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Local service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, pest control, delivery), construction contractors, property management companies
- **Core Value Prop**: GPS tracking + maintenance scheduling + driver behavior + fuel cost tracking for fleets of 10–50 vehicles. No hardware required (uses smartphone GPS). Simple pricing, no enterprise contracts.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Samsara starts at $33/vehicle/mo with annual contracts and hardware installation. Smaller businesses resent the complexity and commitment. A simpler, smartphone-first, no-hardware option at $15–$25/vehicle/mo with month-to-month billing would take significant market share. The HVAC/plumbing millionaire trend means more of these businesses have 5–30 vans that need basic tracking.
- **Our Angle**: "Samsara Lite" — smartphone GPS fleet tracker for local service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, pest control). No hardware, no annual contracts. Basic location + mileage + maintenance reminders + driver reports. $19/vehicle/mo. AppSumo LTD at $99 for 5 vehicles perpetual.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Vertical SaaS for Skilled Trades Job Boards / Matching

- **Source**: https://medium.com/@urano10/vertical-saas-micro-niches-beyond-the-obvious-01e0adf16a98 + https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41828896
- **Additional Links**: https://www.housecallpro.com/resources/field-home-service-industry-trends/ | https://www.servicetitan.com/blog/home-services-industry-trends
- **Platform**: HN + IH synthesis
- **Type**: Market gap / Discussion signal
- **Engagement**: Multiple HN threads discussing trades labor shortage and the "plumbing/HVAC millionaires" phenomenon
- **Revenue Data**: ServiceTitan IPO'd at $9.6B valuation (HVAC/plumbing management, not job boards). Skilled trades have 500k+ unfilled jobs in the US.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, welding, carpentry (skilled trades employers and job seekers)
- **Core Value Prop**: Niche job board and candidate matching specifically for skilled trades — where certifications, license types, local availability, and hands-on skills matter more than resumes. Employers waste $500–$2,000 per bad-fit hire from generic boards (Indeed/LinkedIn don't understand trades context).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Indeed and LinkedIn don't surface plumbing license types, union membership, equipment certifications, or local availability windows. Trades employers spend a lot on bad hires. Niche job boards (Bluecollar.com, Tradesmen International) are incumbents but have poor tech/UX. An AI-matching layer (match candidates to jobs based on cert types, location radius, pay range) is an obvious win.
- **Our Angle**: AI-assisted skilled trades hiring platform — employers post jobs with specific certification requirements (EPA 608, OSHA 10, CDL types), candidates list verified credentials. Automated match-and-notify. $99/mo per employer or $49 job post credits. LTD at $299 for lifetime employer access (10 active jobs at a time).
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 (harder to make LTD work for a marketplace, but possible with credit model)

## Lawn Care & Landscaping Vertical SaaS

- **Source**: https://www.servicetitan.com/blog/home-services-industry-trends + IH/HN synthesis on vertical SaaS pricing power
- **Additional Links**: https://www.ownedandoperated.com/post/4-major-home-service-trends-shaping-2025 | https://integrateiq.com/blogs/top-crm-for-lawn-care-pest-control/
- **Platform**: IH + HN synthesis
- **Type**: Market gap / Trend signal
- **Engagement**: "A kanban board built only for landscaping companies can charge 3× more simply because it speaks the user's language" — widely cited insight in 2026 IH discussions
- **Revenue Data**: Jobber ($100M+ ARR), Housecall Pro (large), but generic. Niche vertical tools like RealGreen (lawn care specific) command higher pricing. Landscaping is a $105B US industry.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Lawn care companies, landscaping contractors, tree service, irrigation companies (1–20 employee businesses)
- **Core Value Prop**: All-in-one operations for lawn care businesses: route optimization (weekly mowing schedules), estimate builder, recurring job scheduling, crew timesheets, chemical application logs (legally required in many states), customer portal, automated billing.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Jobber and Housecall Pro are horizontal field service tools. They don't have: chemical application rate tracking (required by pesticide license holders), property measurement tools for lawn estimation, seasonal service bundling (spring cleanup + summer mowing + fall aeration), HOA bid management. These gaps mean lawn care companies pay for Jobber but also maintain separate spreadsheets for compliance and estimating.
- **Our Angle**: Lawn care + landscaping specific platform with built-in chemical application log (differentiator for licensed applicators), drag-and-drop recurring route builder, and one-click HOA proposal templates. $79/mo for solo operators, $149/mo for teams. AppSumo at $79 LTD for first year then converts.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5
