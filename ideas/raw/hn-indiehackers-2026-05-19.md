## Rentman — AV/Event Production Operations Platform

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder interview
- **Engagement**: 105 upvotes, 16 comments
- **Revenue Data**: $15M–$20M ARR (>$1.25M/month), bootstrapped for 8 years before outside capital. Serves 70+ countries.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — AV/staging/event production is unglamorous operational work
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, event production, film, lighting, staging, broadcast companies
- **Core Value Prop**: All-in-one operations platform handling equipment rental tracking, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, and invoicing for production companies. Replaces "5 tools duct-taped together" or expensive clunky on-prem software.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Founder Roy van den Broek (started at 16 with his own AV rental company) couldn't find software built for this world — everything was generic or overpriced legacy systems. Built it for himself; 5 other companies backed the idea early. US market still underpenetrated. AI for planning, quoting, and coordination not yet deeply integrated.
- **Our Angle**: Build a leaner, AI-native version targeting the long tail of smaller AV/staging/event companies (1–5 person operations) who can't afford Rentman's enterprise pricing. Could include AI-assisted quoting, crew scheduling from availability pools, and gear tracking. UK/EU markets still underserved for smaller operators.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 — recurring operations software; could offer LTD for small operators to bootstrap early users

## Cohesive AI — Agentic CRM for Blue-Collar Service Businesses

- **Source**: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/cohesive
- **Additional Links**: https://getcohesiveai.com/ | https://www.linkedin.com/posts/y-combinator_cohesive-ai-yc-x25-is-the-agentic-crm-for-activity-7332842967365234689-KWGz
- **Platform**: Hacker News / YC (Spring 2025 batch)
- **Type**: YC-backed launch / market validation
- **Engagement**: YC X25 company — strong institutional validation
- **Revenue Data**: Early stage (YC batch), $500K YC investment
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — janitorial, HVAC, landscaping, pest control, pressure washing
- **Target Industry**: Blue-collar local service businesses (HVAC, janitorial, landscaping, pest control, pressure washing)
- **Core Value Prop**: Agentic CRM that fully automates sales and marketing outreach for local service businesses — scrapes Google Maps for prospects, acquires owner emails, runs AI-managed email campaigns so field crews can grow while servicing clients.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Blue-collar businesses have almost zero CRM/outreach tooling designed for them. Generic CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot) are built for inside sales teams. These owners are in the field all day and need automation that works while they're on the job. YC funding this area validates massive unmet demand.
- **Our Angle**: Focus on a single vertical (e.g., pest control or cleaning companies) with a simpler, cheaper alternative — full job management (scheduling, invoicing, customer history) plus automated follow-up/review requests. No-code setup, mobile-first. Could skip the AI-outreach angle and focus on retention/review generation instead, which is less saturated.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — LTD for solo operators (single-truck HVAC, 1-person pest control) would resonate well on AppSumo

- **Additional Links**: https://toanoa.com/

- **Target Industry**: On-demand and hyperlocal delivery businesses (food, pharmacy, retail, courier)

Market through real estate investor Facebook groups and BiggerPockets.

## Craft Brewery Inventory & Batch Management

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Additional Links**: https://superframeworks.com/articles/untapped-underserved-micro-saas-niches
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / market research synthesis
- **Type**: Market research / underserved niche analysis
- **Engagement**: Published analysis citing Brewers Association 2025 Industry Report
- **Revenue Data**: Breweries lose average $12,000/year to inventory management issues. 9,778 active US breweries. Sweet spot pricing $79–$199/month.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — craft brewing is niche but the operational side (raw materials, fermentation, keg logistics) is pure boring-business territory
- **Target Industry**: Craft breweries (nano and microbreweries especially)
- **Core Value Prop**: Inventory management built for craft brewing: track batch numbers, fermentation stages, hop/grain inventory, keg locations, taproom vs. distribution allocation, and ingredient usage per recipe. Generic tools force brewing into SKU-centric frameworks that don't match how a brewer thinks.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Most nano/micro-breweries use spreadsheets or paper. Specialized tools like OrchestratedBEER exist but are expensive and over-engineered for sub-1,000 barrel operations. 9,778 breweries × $100/month = ~$12M TAM at 10% penetration, achievable for a solo founder.
- **Our Angle**: Mobile-first batch tracker with recipe scaling, fermentation log, and keg inventory. Include TTB (federal excise tax) reporting automation — breweries must file quarterly and it's a pain. That single compliance feature justifies the subscription. Market at craft brewing conferences (CBC) and state brewer guild newsletters.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — brewery owners are frugal; LTD could work for the "homebrewer going pro" segment

## Key Market Signals & Themes (2026)

### Broad Trends Observed

1. **Vertical SaaS beats horizontal** — Rentman ($15M ARR) and Cohesive (YC) both validate that industry-specific depth beats generic tools for boring industries. The more specific the workflow, the higher the willingness to pay and the lower the churn.

2. **89% of tradespeople lack adequate tools** (SBA Q3 2025) — The skilled trades gap is enormous and validated across multiple sources. HVAC, pest control, plumbing, electrical — each is a viable standalone market.

3. **Non-English markets = 3–5 year opportunity lag** — What seems boring/solved in the US (basic invoicing, scheduling) is genuinely underserved in LATAM, Eastern Europe, SEA, and Africa. A hyper-localized version of a "solved" problem can be a first-mover win.

4. **AI in boring operations is just starting** — Rentman, Cohesive, and the funeral industry AI discussion all point to AI being applied to unsexy operational problems (scheduling, quoting, compliance paperwork). This is not yet saturated in most niches.

5. **QuickBooks integration = recurring demand** — Multiple threads mention small business owners wanting accounting integration built in. Whoever solves this cleanly for a specific niche (landlords, pest control, breweries) will have extremely low churn.

6. **Testimonial/review generation is underserved for field service** — Businesses like HVAC, cleaning, and pest control know reviews matter but have no frictionless way to get them. Dedicated tools here have strong LTD appeal.
