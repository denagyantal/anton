## Key Themes & Market Intelligence

### What's Working in Boring SaaS (2025–2026)

1. **Vertical specificity beats horizontal features** — ZenMaid ($3M/yr) wins by being the *only* tool that thinks about maid services specifically. Generic tools adapted for a vertical consistently lose to purpose-built ones.

2. **Per-user pricing is hated at scale** — Jobber's top complaint. Flat-rate pricing ($99–$299/month unlimited users) is a sustainable differentiator in field service.

3. **Word-of-mouth is faster in boring niches** — One customer in a tight trade community refers 3–5 colleagues. Acquisition cost drops dramatically vs. tech-adjacent markets.

4. **Non-tech companies are building their own software** — RINSE (laundry logistics), Apex Dental (practice management), and SPADE Agriculture (dairy farm data) are all hiring engineers to build in-house because off-the-shelf fails them. Each signals a product gap.

5. **SaaS market growth is slowing; micro-niches are growing 340%** — Per Gartner Q4 2025 data, micro-niche SaaS dramatically outperforms the broader market. The prize is in the unsexy verticals VCs ignore.
