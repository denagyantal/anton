## HVAC/Plumbing Software for Small Operators (1–5 Trucks)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41828896
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://www.wsj.com/business/entrepreneurship/plumbers-hvac-skilled-trades-millionaires-2b62bf6c
  - https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Discussion (high-traffic article link)
- **Engagement**: 186 upvotes, 260 comments (Oct 2024)
- **Revenue Data**: ServiceTitan dominates at enterprise tier ($300–$700/mo+). Jobber and HouseCallPro serve mid-market. No great sub-$100/mo option for true mom-and-pop operators.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical — small independent shops (1–5 trucks)
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, and customer management without the complexity or price of ServiceTitan
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 100,000+ privately-owned HVAC businesses in the US alone (highly fragmented). Private equity is rolling them up, but the small independent operator is completely underserved. They use WhatsApp, spreadsheets, or pen-and-paper. ServiceTitan requires a sales call and costs more than they're willing to pay. Jobber is close but still overly complex for a 2-truck shop.
- **Our Angle**: Build the "Shopify for HVAC" — a one-page setup, mobile-first, $49–$79/mo, no contracts, no sales calls. Bundle basic CRM + scheduling + invoicing + review request automation. Word-of-mouth spreads fast in local trades communities.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — high MRR fit, LTD viable at $199–$299 for single-location ops

## ZenMaid / Cleaning SaaS Model — Adjacent Niches Not Yet Captured

- **Additional Links**:
  - https://www.indiehackers.com/product/myclean/revenue
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / case study (Feb 2025)
- **Engagement**: 28 upvotes, 10 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: ZenMaid — $250k MRR ($3M/yr), bootstrapped, 11-year-old company. Still growing. Maid/cleaning SaaS vertical is taken.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Adjacent verticals to cleaning: window washing, pressure washing, gutter cleaning, carpet cleaning, pool cleaning, lawn care (non-landscape) — all have similar workflow needs (recurring jobs, route scheduling, client portals, automated billing)
- **Core Value Prop**: ZenMaid proves the model works for cleaning. Adjacent verticals are fragmented and still use spreadsheets or generic tools.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ZenMaid serves maid services specifically. Pool cleaning, pressure washing, and window washing companies have nearly identical operational needs but no purpose-built software. GorillaDesk covers pest control/lawn/cleaning but is broadly positioned and not hyper-specialized. The most profitable angle is owning one sub-vertical completely.
- **Our Angle**: Pick the single most underserved adjacent vertical (e.g., pool service or pressure washing). Build a ZenMaid clone with that vertical's specific branding, terminology, and workflows. Market through vertical-specific Facebook groups and trade associations.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — low CAC via community marketing, strong LTD appeal for small operators

## Trades Invoicing & Job Management for UK/EU Market

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46307973
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38882314
  - https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Platform**: HN + market research
- **Type**: Market gap (identified via multiple threads)
- **Engagement**: HN $500/month thread (485 upvotes, 560 comments, Dec 2025) — multiple participants building niche tools for local services
- **Revenue Data**: Jobber (Canadian) is the leading player but US-focused. UK trades market is underserved — Tradify, Tradesman Pro exist but are limited. Checkatrade is referral-only, not management.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: UK/EU tradespeople — plumbers, electricians, builders, decorators (sole traders and 1–5 person crews)
- **Core Value Prop**: Quote → Job → Invoice → VAT return — the complete flow for a UK sole trader tradesperson, with HMRC Making Tax Digital (MTD) compliance built in
- **Gap/Opportunity**: UK MTD (Making Tax Digital) compliance is now mandatory for VAT-registered trades businesses. This creates a hard regulatory driver for trades software adoption — they must use digital tools to submit VAT, creating a captive market. Most UK-targeted options are generic accounting (Xero/QuickBooks) which don't understand job/site workflows.
- **Our Angle**: MTD-compliant from day one. Quote builder with labor + materials. Job photos. WhatsApp-friendly invoice sending. £39–£59/mo. Target via UK trades Facebook groups and subreddits (r/DIYUK). LTD appeal high given the compliance hook.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — regulatory compliance creates mandatory demand, UK market underserved

## Access Control / Biometric Management Software for Small Offices

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38882314
- **Additional Links**:
  - https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38882314#38889565
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN discussion (Jan 2024)
- **Engagement**: 70 upvotes, 76 comments. Specific comment: "access control devices (biometric, NFC) used in small offices have terrible software and useless interfaces. The devices can be procured from China."
- **Revenue Data**: Large enterprise: Lenel, Genetec. SMB: Brivo, Kisi. Hardware from China (HikVision, ZKTeco) is cheap but software is terrible. No great bootstrapped player targeting SMB with sub-$100/mo pricing.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Small offices, co-working spaces, storage facilities, dental/medical practices — anywhere with controlled access needs (5–50 employees)
- **Core Value Prop**: Modern cloud software to manage ZKTeco/HikVision/Suprema biometric devices — schedule access, manage employees/visitors, generate audit logs, integrate with HR tools
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The hardware is commodity and cheap from China. The software that ships with it is horrible (Chinese UX, Windows-only, no cloud). Kisi and Brivo are modern but $150–$500/mo — too expensive for a 15-person office. There is a genuine opportunity to build the "good" software layer on top of cheap commodity hardware.
- **Our Angle**: Companion SaaS for ZKTeco/Suprema devices specifically (huge installed base). $49–$79/mo. Cloud dashboard, mobile app, employee self-service, visitor management. Hardware-agnostic over time. LTD priced at $199–$299 per door.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — niche and B2B, LTD works for co-working/small office segment
