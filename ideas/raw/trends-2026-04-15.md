# Boring Business Vertical SaaS — Trend Research
**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Agent:** trend-spotter
**Focus:** Recent product launches, funded startups, and market gaps in boring business verticals

---

## Summary

Active investment and launch activity in boring business vertical SaaS continues in 2025–2026, with the clearest signal being that specialty trades, healthcare practice management, and construction back-office tooling remain structurally underserved. YC W2026 alone backed multiple companies in residential contractor software, electrical estimating, and real estate AI. The dominant theme is AI layered on top of workflows that still run on spreadsheets, paper, or legacy on-premise systems from the 1990s–2000s.

## INDUSTRY: HVAC, Plumbing, Field Services

### Market overview
The field service management market reaches $8.6B by 2026. Established platforms (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, FieldEdge, FieldPulse, Zuper) dominate. No new venture-backed pure-play HVAC/plumbing startup identified for 2025–2026, but clear gaps exist:
- **89% of plumbers and electricians lack adequate digital tools** (Entrepreneur Loop data)
- Pricing gap: ServiceTitan is priced for large businesses; SMB segment ($29–$149/mo) is contested but not won
- Vertical-specific CRM (HVAC equipment maintenance tracking, seasonal reminders) remains underbuilt

### Key incumbents and their gaps
- **ServiceTitan** — Enterprise-focused, high cost, steep learning curve; not good for shops under 5 techs
- **Housecall Pro** — Strong mobile, automation; #2 for small HVAC/plumbing
- **Jobber** — Generalist field service; lacks deep trade-specific features
- **FieldEdge** — HVAC/plumbing specific with flat-rate pricing; mid-market
- **Zuper** — Mid-sized, mobile-first; not trade-specialized
- **BuildOps** — Commercial operations focus

**Opportunity signal:** Trade-specific CRM with equipment history per unit, seasonal maintenance scheduling, and LTD-friendly pricing ($59–$149/mo) — not yet cleanly addressed for the 5–25 tech shop.

## KEY MARKET GAPS / OPPORTUNITIES IDENTIFIED

Based on all research, these verticals show the weakest current software coverage relative to market size and pain:

### 1. Specialty Trade Payroll / Back Office (validated by Trayd's 600% growth)
- Union deductions, prevailing wage, certified payroll still done manually at most shops
- Market: 400:1 ratio of specialty contractors to GCs
- Trayd proved the wedge; opportunity in adjacent trades not yet served (masonry, painting, fireproofing)

### 2. Residential Contractor All-in-One (validated by Foreman YC W2026)
- Fragmented: spreadsheets for estimates, email for plans, phone photos, copy-pasted Word proposals
- Sub-$1M to $5M revenue contractors lack any true system of record
- Opportunity for AI-native platform doing estimate → proposal → job management → invoicing

### 3. Electrical / Mechanical Contractor Estimating (validated by Bidflow YC W2026)
- Takeoff still done manually from floor plans
- Adjacent: mechanical (HVAC ductwork), fire suppression, low-voltage

### 4. Dental Practice AI Modernization (validated by Archy's $47M and 300% growth)
- Most dental software is 20+ years old; AI-assisted claims, notes, eligibility verification barely deployed at scale
- Regional/DSO segment not yet fully penetrated

### 5. Veterinary Practice Cloud Migration (structural shift underway)
- Server-based legacy systems aging out; cloud-native alternatives maturing
- Sub-niche opportunity: exotic/avian vet practices, equine clinics — completely underserved

### 6. HOA Management AI (validated by Assembly HOA YC S2024, Aldara YC S2022)
- 370,000+ HOAs in the US; most run on email, spreadsheets, or 20-year-old software
- AI-native management (violation detection, vendor coordination, meeting minutes, financial reporting) barely started

### 7. Construction Back Office (payment, AP, compliance)
- Payra proved AR automation is a wedge ($15M funding)
- IronLedger.ai proving AP automation is a wedge
- Certified payroll / prevailing wage compliance still largely manual in states beyond Trayd's coverage

### 8. Vertical-Specific CRM for HVAC/Plumbing SMBs
- 89% of small trades contractors lack adequate digital tools
- ServiceTitan too expensive; Jobber too generic; gap at $79–$149/mo with equipment-level tracking
- Seasonal maintenance reminders, equipment asset management per customer address — validated pain point

### 9. Pool/Spa Service (Skimmer at 35,000 pros proves market; competitors thin)
- Skimmer dominates but hasn't locked in the market
- Chemical tracking compliance, LSI water chemistry logging, state regulatory reporting — underdeveloped

### 10. Funeral Home Compliance & Memorial Technology
- State-mandated paperwork (death certificates, FTC compliance, price disclosures) largely paper
- Memorial livestreaming, digital obituaries, grief aftercare follow-up — emerging but no dominant player
