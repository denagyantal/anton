## Key Themes & Emerging Opportunities (Meta-Analysis)

### 1. PE Roll-Up Backlash Creates Indie Software Opportunity
Private equity is rolling up HVAC, plumbing, pest control, dental, and veterinary practices. HN community is vocal about customer distrust of PE-owned service companies. **Opportunity: Software that helps independent operators prove they're NOT the PE shop** — transparent pricing tools, photo documentation, honest diagnostic workflows.

### 2. Vertical AI Receptionist is Early but Real
The IH community is actively building and selling AI voice receptionists to trades businesses. The wedge is appointment booking + call answering. $300-800 MRR per client is validated. The gap: most builders are selling generic AI; trades-specific training (HVAC terminology, emergency triage, seasonal demand patterns) is underexplored.

### 3. Cleaning/Maid Service Software Adjacencies
ZenMaid at $3M/yr proves the cleaning-software model works. Identical opportunity exists in: pool service management, window cleaning, pressure washing, carpet cleaning — each niche is large enough to support a $500K-$2M ARR business and small enough to avoid VC attention.

### 4. Vertical SaaS Moats Are Real and Defensible
Multiple IH community comments validate: horizontal SaaS players cannot easily enter niche verticals without breaking their UX. Vertical SaaS moats compound over time. HVAC, dental, restoration are specifically called out as where moats are being quietly built.

### 5. Compliance as a Lock-In Mechanism
Pest control EPA logs, industrial safety ISO compliance, OSHA records — any niche with mandatory regulatory compliance is a natural SaaS lock-in. Once the audit trail lives in your software, switching means losing it.
