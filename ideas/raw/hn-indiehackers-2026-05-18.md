## Rentman — AV & Event Production Operations Platform

- **Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q
- **Additional Links:** https://rentman.io/
- **Platform:** Indie Hackers
- **Type:** Revenue milestone / Case study (April 2026)
- **Engagement:** 105 upvotes, 8 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data:** $15M–$20M ARR ($1.25M+/month), bootstrapped for 8 years before raising external capital, 70+ countries
- **Boring Business Score:** 4/5
- **Target Industry:** AV rental, event production, staging, broadcast, film
- **Core Value Prop:** All-in-one ops platform for production companies: equipment rental tracking, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, invoicing — handles the "messy middle" that spreadsheets and generic tools can't.
- **Gap/Opportunity:** Founder was running an AV rental business himself and couldn't find adequate software — every tool was either built for other industries or expensive clunky on-prem. He built it for himself, five other companies backed him early. Now the world market leader in category. The US market is still wide open per founder.
- **Our Angle:** Adjacent verticals at same maturity level as early Rentman — photography equipment rental, film gear houses, staging companies, broadcast fleets. These operators have the same pain (gear tracking + crew scheduling + invoicing) but no dedicated modern SaaS. Rentman itself is complex; a simpler, self-serve, LTD-friendly tool targeting one sub-niche (e.g., photo/video rental studios under 20 items) is buildable. Strong SEO moat from niche keywords.
- **LTD Potential:** 3/5 (B2B subscription model works better here, but one-time deals for small shops viable)

## Stagetimer — Remote Countdown Timer for Live Events

- **Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/turning-a-simple-b2b-solution-into-a-15k-mrr-saas-by-exploiting-a-market-niche-xIxrVwn24DVsN8b3PYD3
- **Additional Links:** https://stagetimer.io/
- **Platform:** Indie Hackers
- **Type:** Case study (Aug 2024, still active/growing)
- **Engagement:** 49 upvotes, 20 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data:** $15k–$20k MRR, 13,000+ accounts, mix of subscriptions and one-time payments
- **Boring Business Score:** 4/5
- **Target Industry:** Live events, conferences, TV production, AV/production crews
- **Core Value Prop:** Browser-based remote countdown timer synced across multiple screens for stage managers, event producers, and broadcast crews. Solves the "manually counting down on each device" pain point.
- **Gap/Opportunity:** Simple, overlooked B2B tool. SEO was the superpower — low-volume but high-intent keywords. Competing tools were desktop-only or required expensive hardware. Zero awareness of this market among most indie hackers. Similar "boring event production" opportunities: digital stage cue sheets, speaker slide remote, AV signal routing status dashboards.
- **Our Angle:** Pattern here is: one narrow event-production workflow → solve it perfectly → SEO captures all intent searches. Apply this to: band set-list display software, production call sheet apps, venue tech-rider management. Each is similarly niche with real paying customers (event companies have budgets).
- **LTD Potential:** 4/5 (Event companies respond well to AppSumo; clear one-time value)

- **Additional Links:** https://www.fullbay.com/
- **Platform:** Referenced at Stripe Sessions 2026

- **Boring Business Score:** 5/5
- **Target Industry:** Commercial trucking repair shops
- **Core Value Prop:** Full-stack management software for heavy-duty and diesel repair shops — invoicing, parts tracking, work orders, fleet customer portals, compliance.
- **Gap/Opportunity:** Fullbay targets commercial trucking; the light-duty auto repair shop space (small garages, independent mechanics) is still dominated by ancient shop management software (Mitchell1, Shop-Ware, etc.) with terrible UX and $250-$500/month price points. ## Quipli — Equipment Rental Lead Generation from Permits

- **Source:** https://stripe.com/blog/vertical-saas-insights-sessions-2026
- **Additional Links:** https://quipli.com/resources/introducing-quinn-the-ai-revenue-growth-engine-for-equipment-rental/
- **Platform:** Stripe Sessions 2026 (case study)
- **Type:** AI-enhanced vertical SaaS
- **Engagement:** Featured by Stripe as AI innovation example
- **Revenue Data:** Not disclosed; referenced as notable vertical SaaS example
- **Boring Business Score:** 4/5
- **Target Industry:** Equipment rental companies (excavators, lifts, forklifts, etc.)
- **Core Value Prop:** Auto-generates leads for equipment rental dealers when new building/construction permits are filed nearby — turning public permit data into a prospecting engine.
- **Gap/Opportunity:** The permit-to-lead idea is a pattern that works across other boring industries: HVAC dealers could get leads when new HVAC permit pulls happen; electricians when electrical permits are filed; plumbers when plumbing permits appear. This is a B2B data/CRM feature that could be a standalone product or embedded in FSM tools. No one has built a generic "permit monitoring → automated outreach" tool for trades specifically.
- **Our Angle:** "TradeLeads" — pull permit data by ZIP code, match to relevant trade type, generate outreach email/SMS. Subscription model at $99/month per trade type. Serve HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roofing, landscaping. High LTD potential — one-time setup with recurring value.
- **LTD Potential:** 4/5 (Strong appeal to trade businesses as customer acquisition tool)

- **Engagement:** n/a
- **Revenue Data:** Kickserv reported at $120k MRR (general FSM); ServiceTitan valued at $9.5B but priced out of small operators. FieldPulse, Workiz targeting small FSM businesses with some traction.
- **Boring Business Score:** 5/5
- **Target Industry:** Pool & spa service companies (route-based chemical service), pressure washing, window cleaning
- **Core Value Prop:** Route-based recurring service management — pool/spa operators need: chemical dosing logs per visit, recurring route scheduling, customer portals for service records, chemical inventory tracking, and invoicing. - **Gap/Opportunity:** Pool service is one of the most underserved sub-niches in FSM. - **Our Angle:** "PoolRoute" — mobile-first app for pool service technicians with chemical log, route optimization, recurring billing, and customer portal. Target via pool service Facebook groups (100k+ members), Pool & Spa News, and regional pool trade associations.
- **LTD Potential:** 5/5 (Pool service operators are exactly the LTD buyer profile — small business, value-conscious, recurring need)

- **Additional Links:** n/a
- **Platform:** Hacker News
- **Type:** Ask HN (July 2025)
- **Engagement:** Thread about complex scheduling rules for multi-resource, multi-constraint environments
- **Revenue Data:** n/a
- **Boring Business Score:** 3/5
- **Target Industry:** Staffing agencies, event production companies, construction project managers
- **Core Value Prop:** Multi-resource scheduling with complex constraints (certifications required, availability windows, location, skill matching) — not project management but operational scheduling. The poster specifically asked about scenarios with "hundreds to thousands of resources, significant budgets, multi-year projects."
- **Gap/Opportunity:** General scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) are for appointments. Complex resource scheduling (like "which certified HVAC tech is available Tuesday in Zone 3 who also holds EPA 608 certification") is solved by spreadsheets for mid-sized trade businesses. A rules-based scheduler that sits between calendar apps and full ERP would serve plumbing/HVAC/electrical companies with 10-50 techs who've outgrown spreadsheets but can't afford ServiceTitan.
- **Our Angle:** "TradeDispatch" — resource scheduling for service businesses with 5-50 field techs. Constraint-based dispatch (skills, zones, certifications, availability), mobile app for techs, simple Gantt for dispatchers. $199/month, 14-day trial. Target via PHCC (plumbing/HVAC trade group) and electrical contractor associations.
- **LTD Potential:** 3/5 (B2B scheduling tools can do LTD; $399 one-time viable for smaller operators)
