## Stagetimer — Remote Countdown Timer for Live Events ($15k MRR)
- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/turning-a-simple-b2b-solution-into-a-15k-mrr-saas-by-exploiting-a-market-niche-xIxrVwn24DVsN8b3PYD3
- **Additional Links**: https://stagetimer.io (implied)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder interview
- **Engagement**: Featured post on IH
- **Revenue Data**: $15k MRR (subscriptions), ~50% higher including one-time payments. 13,000+ accounts. Also co-founded Rundown Studio ($4k MRR) for event planning. Founder Lukas Hermann, solo + 1 hired dev.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Live events, video production, conference management
- **Core Value Prop**: Remote countdown timer management for live events. Teams can start/stop timers, communicate delays, and coordinate timing across multiple screens without physically touching equipment. Replaced manual timer operation in video production workflows.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The live events industry is massive but fragmented — conferences, corporate events, church services, live streams all need timing management. 13,000 accounts at $15k MRR means very low ARPU (most on free/one-time plans), suggesting significant upgrade potential. The one-time payment option was a revenue breakthrough — event operators who only do 2-3 events/year don't want subscriptions. Key lesson: Hermann's SEO strategy captures 40%+ of traffic through low-volume, high-intent keywords that big players ignore. The event planning companion product (Rundown Studio at $4k MRR) shows expandability within the same customer base.
- **Our Angle**: The playbook here isn't to clone Stagetimer — it's to apply the same "boring niche + SEO + one-time payment option" formula to adjacent event verticals. Think: equipment rental checkout tracking for events, AV setup checklists and documentation tools for production crews, event staffing scheduling for temp crews. The one-time purchase option alongside subscription is critical for industries with irregular usage patterns — trades businesses that do seasonal work have the same pattern. The tech stack ("really vanilla" JS/Vue/Node) proves you don't need bleeding-edge tech for boring SaaS.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

## Expense Management for Field Workers — Mobile Receipt Capture
- **Source**: https://microsaasidea.substack.com/p/micro-saas-ideas-veterinary-management-ecommerce-store-builder-expense-management-ai-review-ideas
- **Additional Links**: Rydoo (competitor, ~$64M annual revenue)
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Newsletter
- **Type**: Market analysis / Validated idea
- **Engagement**: Part of newsletter with 36k subscribers
- **Revenue Data**: No direct revenue data. Competitor Rydoo estimated at $64M annual revenue — validates massive market. Target: $1k-10k MRR for micro-SaaS version.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Field service workers — technicians, sales reps, delivery drivers, trade contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: Mobile app for receipt capture that auto-extracts merchant name, amount, date, and category. Policy compliance checks. Mileage tracking. Automated expense reports for employer reimbursement.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Rydoo at $64M ARR proves massive demand, but it's enterprise-priced and enterprise-complex. Field workers at small companies (5-50 employees) currently stuff receipts in their truck console and submit expenses on crumpled paper monthly. The gap: a dead-simple mobile app for the worker + a dashboard for the small business owner to approve and reimburse. No enterprise features, no SAP integration — just photo receipt → auto-categorize → submit → approve → reimburse. Specifically for trades: plumbers buying parts at supply houses, HVAC techs purchasing materials mid-job, electricians picking up supplies from Home Depot. These workers make multiple purchases daily and lose/forget receipts constantly.
- **Our Angle**: Build "receipt snap" for trade workers specifically. Snap photo of receipt → auto-extract details → tag to specific job/customer → submit to owner. Owner sees expenses by job (critical for job costing), approves reimbursement, exports to QuickBooks. The job-tagging feature is the key differentiator for trades — knowing that $47 at Ferguson Plumbing Supply was for the Johnson water heater job enables accurate job costing. Price at $5-10/user/month (per field worker). At 20 employees = $100-200/mo — affordable for small operators. Integrate with popular trade accounting tools.
- **LTD Potential**: 4
