## Craftplan — Production Management for Micro-Bakeries & Small-Batch Manufacturing
- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46847690
- **Additional Links**: None
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 577 upvotes, 167 comments
- **Revenue Data**: No revenue disclosed. Open-source project (Elixir/Phoenix LiveView), no SaaS offering yet.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Small-batch manufacturing — bakeries, soap makers, candle makers, breweries
- **Core Value Prop**: Open-source micro-ERP handling recipes with versioned bills of materials, cost calculations, inventory with lot traceability and allergen tracking, order management, production batch planning, purchasing workflows, and demand forecasting. Developer built it for his wife's bakery after finding existing tools "either expensive or way too generic."
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The 577-point engagement is the highest in this scan by a wide margin — massive latent demand for production tools tailored to artisan/micro-manufacturers. - **Our Angle**: A SaaS wrapper around this concept — hosted, with onboarding for non-technical bakery/food production owners — is a clear opportunity. The recipe-to-cost-to-production pipeline is the key workflow. Target cottage food producers and small commercial bakeries first. Price at $49-99/mo. The open-source project validates the feature set; a managed version with mobile app, onboarding wizards, and integrations with suppliers (US Foods, Sysco) would capture the market that can't self-host Elixir apps. The bakery angle alone represents 35,000+ commercial bakeries in the US.

# CROSS-CUTTING THEMES & SYNTHESIS

## Strongest Demand Signals (Convergence Across Multiple Sources)

### 1. Field Service Management for Micro-Operators (1-5 Trucks)
**Evidence**: FleetFix (HN), Kickserv ($120K MRR), OttoField (IH), multiple Ask HN threads about underserved trades. ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro/Jobber charge $200-500+/mo and are built for larger operations. The 1-5 truck operator is dramatically underserved.

### 2. AI Phone/SMS Answering for Service Businesses
**Evidence**: DentistAI (HN), OttoField (IH), Ask HN threads about missed calls. Service businesses lose 30-60% of inbound calls. The AI receptionist market is early and vertical-specific implementations don't exist yet.

### 3. Job Costing & Financial Visibility for Trades
**Evidence**: Ask HN accounting threads, Onedesk ($100K MRR cleaning), Carbon (manufacturing ERP), Bookie/bookkeeping discussions from 2026-02-27 scan. The #1 pain point for growing trades businesses is "I don't know which jobs are profitable."

### 4. Vertical-Specific Production/Operations Management
**Evidence**: Craftplan (577 upvotes, micro-bakery ERP), Carbon (322 upvotes, manufacturing ERP), Uniform Rental Software ($50K MRR). Micro-manufacturers and specialty businesses need purpose-built operations tools, not generic ERP.

### 5. Procurement & Parts Management for Trades
**Evidence**: Quickinim (HN), Itemgrid (HN), Ask HN construction threads. Trades businesses manage hundreds of parts across multiple suppliers using spreadsheets or memory.

### 6. AI Engine Optimization (AEO) for Local Businesses
**Evidence**: Chatalyst (HN) — 92% of local businesses invisible to AI assistants. Emerging category with zero established competitors.
