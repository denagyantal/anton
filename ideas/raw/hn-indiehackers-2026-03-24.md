## Summary

Today's scan surfaces strong signals around AI support agents for small businesses (Recursive's Show HN targeting 3-person companies too small for Zendesk), the continued dominance of the boring micro-SaaS acquisition playbook (Noosa Labs steady at $120K MRR across email timers and recruitment tools), and a growing wave of construction/contractor-specific software demand. The "What Will Work in SaaS in 2026" HN discussion crystallizes the trend: winners automate decisions not just tasks, embed into workflows, and reduce cognitive load. Vertical SaaS for trades and field service businesses remains the most consistent opportunity pattern — every 2026 micro-SaaS roundup features cleaning, plumbing, HVAC, and pest control as underserved.

Upload your docs/FAQ, connect to your help desk email, and the AI handles L1 support (common questions, how-tos, billing inquiries). Escalates to the founder only for complex issues. $29/mo for up to 500 conversations. Unlike Recursive's technical focus, target the broader micro-SaaS market — every solo founder drowning in support tickets. LTD at $99 for AppSumo launch.

## Courier/Delivery Fleet Analytics — Spreadsheet Replacement Opportunity

- **Source**: https://www.rockingweb.com.au/micro-saas-revenue-analysis-2025/
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/how-to-choose-the-right-courier-software-for-your-business-17dde0d977, https://www.shipday.com/post/best-courier-software-route-planning-apps-2025, https://www.softwareadvice.com/fleet-management/courier-comparison/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Market research
- **Type**: Market analysis + Indie Hackers discussion
- **Engagement**: IH has dedicated courier software discussion threads; multiple 2025-2026 roundups highlight this gap
- **Revenue Data**: 50% of courier companies still use spreadsheets for performance data. Basic reports take 3-5 hours weekly. Existing players: Onfleet ($500+/mo for 2000 tasks), Shipday (freemium but limited analytics), Ontime360 (legacy). Gap: affordable analytics for small/mid fleets (5-50 vehicles).
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Small courier/delivery companies, last-mile delivery fleets
- **Core Value Prop**: Operations managers at small courier companies cannot extract meaningful delivery metrics, driver performance data, or operational efficiency indicators from their current tools. They compile data manually from dispatch software, GPS trackers, and customer feedback into spreadsheets. A simple analytics dashboard that pulls from existing dispatch/GPS tools and shows delivery times, driver performance, route efficiency, and customer satisfaction would save 3-5 hours/week.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Enterprise fleet solutions (Onfleet, Bringg, DispatchTrack) cost $500-2000/mo and are built for large operations. Small courier companies with 5-50 drivers need just the analytics layer — they already have dispatch tools and GPS trackers but can't make sense of the data. No one builds for the 5-50 vehicle fleet size.
- **Our Angle**: Build "FleetPulse" — a lightweight delivery analytics dashboard that connects to popular GPS trackers and dispatch tools via API/CSV import. Show: on-time delivery %, driver scorecards, route efficiency heat maps, cost-per-delivery trends. No dispatch features, no route planning — just the analytics layer missing from every small fleet's stack. $49/mo for up to 25 vehicles. LTD at $149.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

## Construction Subcontractor Payment Tracking (Spreadsheet-to-SaaS Gap)

- **Source**: https://blog.startupstash.com/6-boring-industries-begging-for-micro-saas-with-zero-competition-9e626316ddf1
- **Additional Links**: https://ww3.gcpay.com/, https://www.procore.com/pay, https://www.jobtread.com/, https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46962356 (Hive ERP's $500K construction contracts)
- **Platform**: Multiple (Startup Stash blog, HN, market research)
- **Type**: Market analysis + validated demand signal
- **Engagement**: January 2026 Startup Stash article on "boring industries begging for micro-SaaS"; Hive ERP's $500K in construction contracts before open-sourcing
- **Revenue Data**: GCPay and Procore Pay serve enterprise. JobTread serves mid-market ($50-200/mo). Gap: general contractors managing 3-10 subcontractors per project with no affordable payment tracking. Subcontractors wait 30-90+ days for payment with zero visibility. Construction companies paid $500K for Hive ERP's workflow automation.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: General contractors / small construction companies
- **Core Value Prop**: General contractors manually track payments across subcontractors using spreadsheets. Subcontractors have no visibility into payment status. Lien waiver tracking is manual. The process: GC receives payment from owner → calculates sub shares → manually sends checks → tracks who's been paid in a spreadsheet → chases lien waivers by email. A simple "payment waterfall" dashboard showing: owner payment received → sub payments released → lien waivers collected would save 5+ hours per project per month.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Procore Pay and GCPay require Procore/enterprise subscriptions ($500+/mo). JobTread bundles payment tracking into full PM software. No standalone "subcontractor payment tracker" exists for GCs managing $500K-$5M/year in projects. The construction terminology matters — software must speak in "draws," "retainage," "lien waivers," and "pay applications," not generic invoicing terms.
- **Our Angle**: Build "SubPay" — a standalone subcontractor payment tracker for small GCs. Track draw schedules, calculate retainage, generate pay applications, collect lien waivers digitally, and show subs real-time payment status. QuickBooks integration for accounting sync. $59/mo per project. LTD at $199 for unlimited projects. Construction companies already validated willingness to pay ($500K+ for Hive, $50-200/mo for JobTread).
- **LTD Potential**: 5

- **Revenue Data**: Revenue potential: $19-49/mo per business. No dominant player for trade-specific referrals. Generic review/referral tools (Birdeye $300/mo, Podium $249/mo) are overkill for solo tradespeople. Senja.io hit $1M ARR with a focused testimonial collection tool.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Plumbers, cleaners, HVAC technicians, landscapers, pest control
- **Core Value Prop**: After every completed job, automatically send a review request. After every 5-star review, automatically send a referral discount code to the customer. Track which referrals convert. Home service businesses live and die by word-of-mouth but have no systematic way to trigger and track referrals. The entire workflow is manual: ask for review verbally → hope they leave one → never follow up with referral incentive.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Birdeye and Podium do review management but cost $250-300/mo and are designed for multi-location businesses. Solo plumbers and 2-person HVAC teams need a $19/mo tool that does one thing: turn completed jobs into reviews and reviews into referrals. The trigger-based automation (job completed → review request → 5-star received → referral code sent) doesn't exist as a standalone affordable tool.
- **Our Angle**: Build "ReviewLoop" — automated review-to-referral pipeline for home service pros. Connect to their scheduling tool or manually mark jobs complete. Auto-send review request via SMS. If 5-star review received, auto-send referral code with custom discount to the reviewer. Dashboard shows: reviews collected, referral codes sent, referrals converted. $19/mo for solo, $39/mo for small teams. LTD at $59. The "after 5-star → auto-send referral" trigger is the novel hook that no generic review tool offers.
- **LTD Potential**: 5

- **Additional Links**: https://blog.startupstash.com/6-boring-industries-begging-for-micro-saas-with-zero-competition-9e626316ddf1, https://www.servicetitan.com/blog/home-service-business-ideas

- **Type**: Market analysis
- **Engagement**: Cited across "underserved markets 2026" and "boring industries for micro-SaaS" roundups
- **Revenue Data**: Skilled trades employ millions with high turnover. Revenue model: commission-based ($500-2000 per placement) or subscription ($99-299/mo per contractor). Indeed/LinkedIn charge $250+ per job posting but trades workers don't use LinkedIn. Market Clarity ranks skilled trades recruiting as a top underserved niche for 2026.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, welding contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: Contractors face severe worker shortages. Generic job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn) don't work for trades — electricians and plumbers don't have LinkedIn profiles or check Indeed. Recruitment happens through word-of-mouth, union halls, and supply house bulletin boards. A trades-specific job board with SMS-first applications, skill verification, and location-based matching would fill a massive gap.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The skilled trades worker shortage is a $1T+ infrastructure problem. Contractors post on Craigslist and Facebook groups. No "LinkedIn for trades" exists. The key UX insight: trades workers are mobile-first, often on job sites — the application process must be SMS/text-based, not resume-upload-based. Skill verification (licenses, certifications) is critical and missing from generic boards.
- **Our Angle**: Build "TradesBoard" — SMS-first job matching for skilled trades. Contractors post jobs with required skills/certs. Workers apply via text message with their trade, years of experience, and certifications. Auto-match based on trade, location, and availability. $99/mo for contractors to post unlimited jobs. Workers are free. LTD at $299 for contractors. The SMS-first application flow (no app download, no resume, just text "HVAC 8yrs" to apply) is the killer differentiator.
- **LTD Potential**: 3

# Market Signals & Trends

## Key Themes This Week

1. **AI support agents going downmarket** — Recursive's Show HN proves the demand for AI-powered customer support at the micro-business level (3-10 person companies). The enterprise support tool market (Zendesk, Intercom) has no affordable small-business equivalent. Every micro-SaaS founder is drowning in support tickets and would pay $29/mo for an AI handler.

2. **Property management software still missing basics** — Active HN threads asking "what is PM software missing?" in late 2025 signal that even with AppFolio and Buildium, small landlords (1-10 units) remain underserved. AI tenant communication, IoT integration, and post-lease management are the gaps.

3. **Construction/trades willing to pay premium** — Hive ERP sold $500K in contracts before open-sourcing. GCPay and Procore Pay serve enterprise but nothing exists for small GCs tracking 3-10 subcontractors. Construction-specific terminology (draws, retainage, lien waivers) matters — generic invoicing fails here.

4. **Courier fleet analytics is the next spreadsheet-to-SaaS conversion** — 50% of courier companies still compile performance data in spreadsheets, spending 3-5 hours weekly on basic reports. Enterprise fleet tools ($500+/mo) are overkill for 5-50 vehicle fleets. The analytics-only layer (not dispatch, not routing) is the gap.

5. **Review-to-referral automation for home services** — Every micro-SaaS roundup for 2026 features automated referral systems for trades. The trigger-based flow (job complete → review request → 5-star → referral code) doesn't exist as a standalone affordable tool. Birdeye/Podium at $250-300/mo are 10x too expensive for solo tradespeople.

6. **"What Will Work in SaaS in 2026" consensus** — HN discussion (Jan 2026, 46612377) crystallizes the winning pattern: automate decisions not tasks, embed into workflows, reduce cognitive load, earn trust over time. The implication for boring SaaS: don't build dashboards, build tools that make the next decision obvious.
