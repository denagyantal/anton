## Craftplan — Bakery / Food Production Management

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46847690
- **Additional Links**: https://craftplan.fly.dev
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 577 upvotes, 167 comments (strong signal)
- **Revenue Data**: Not disclosed; open-source side project built by developer for his wife's micro-bakery
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Food production — bakeries, confectioneries, small-batch manufacturers
- **Core Value Prop**: Production management for micro-bakeries: versioned recipes with auto cost rollups, lot-traced inventory, allergen tracking, batch scheduling, demand forecasting, purchasing reorder, and labor accounting. The founder couldn't find anything affordable or not over-engineered.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Existing solutions (e.g., Craftybase, Katana) are expensive or generic. The 577 upvotes signal massive latent demand. Commenters mentioned similar needs in biscotti production, jam-making with semi-products, 3D printing manufacturing, and restaurant operations — broader than just bakeries. No mobile app. Hard-coded forecasting logic is a pain point.
- **Our Angle**: Purpose-built SaaS for small-batch food producers (bakeries, jam makers, chocolatiers, hot sauce brands) at $49–$99/month. Add mobile app, configurable forecasting, AppSumo launch to capture cottage food industry. Huge cottage food / food entrepreneur community on Reddit (r/cottagefoods, r/ArtisanFood).
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

- **Source**: https://www.flowjam.com/blog/27-micro-saas-examples-that-actually-print-money-in-2025

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers / Micro-SaaS research
- **Type**: Validated competitor / revenue milestone
- **Engagement**: Listed in multiple micro-SaaS roundups as proof case
- **Revenue Data**: $120k MRR, under 10 employees
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Field service — plumbing, HVAC, electrical, general contracting
- **Core Value Prop**: Job scheduling, customer management, invoicing, and field dispatch for service businesses. Simple enough that a plumber can use it.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025 report). Jobber ($100/mo+) is too expensive and complex for solo operators. No existing solution serves ultra-micro operators (1–3 person shops) with clean mobile-first UX + WhatsApp/SMS quoting.
- **Our Angle**: Mobile-first field service app for solo tradespeople (1–3 person shops) in emerging markets or rural US. WhatsApp-based quoting and job dispatch. Priced at $29/month. Target locales where Jobber hasn't penetrated. Facebook Groups are the acquisition channel.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## ZenMaid — Maid Service Business Software

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/stories (multiple references)
- **Additional Links**: https://get.zenmaid.com, https://vev.co/blog/jobber-vs-zenmaid
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / validated competitor
- **Engagement**: Widely cited in IH community as "unsexy niche done right"
- **Revenue Data**: $200k MRR (started 2013, founder Amar Ghose)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Residential cleaning / maid services
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, automation, cleaner tracking built specifically for how residential cleaning companies work (not generic field service).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: ZenMaid dominates residential cleaning but has weak multi-location support, real-time team coordination (page refresh needed when multiple staff work simultaneously), and no strong mobile app for cleaners. - **Our Angle**: Post-construction / commercial cleaning software with mobile-first cleaner app, photo documentation for move-out inspections, and client-facing cleaning reports. B2B focus on cleaning companies serving real estate agents (move-out cleans). LTD at $99.

## Craft Brewery Inventory Management

- **Source**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Platform**: Research / curated SaaS niche analysis
- **Type**: Validated gap analysis
- **Engagement**: N/A
- **Revenue Data**: Market: 9,778 active breweries (Brewers Association 2025); estimated $12,000/year loss per brewery from poor inventory management
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Craft brewing / beverage production
- **Core Value Prop**: Batch tracking, fermentation stage management, keg location tracking, ingredient inventory, and purchasing for small craft breweries.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Generic inventory tools (QuickBooks, spreadsheets) can't handle brewing-specific concepts (batches, hops/grain bills, fermentation timelines, keg tracking, taproom vs. wholesale). - **Our Angle**: Affordable brewery ops software ($79/month or $999 LTD) for nano and microbreweries (under 1,000 barrels/year). Core features: batch logs, fermentation tracking, keg inventory, and taproom POS integration. Reach via homebrewing communities (r/homebrewing, BrewUnited) and craft brewery Facebook Groups.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5

## Home Care Agency Coordination (AI-Assisted)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47601859 (Who is Hiring April 2026 — Caribou hiring)
- **Additional Links**: https://www.cariboucare.com
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Who is Hiring — demand signal from a VC-backed startup in this space
- **Engagement**: Who is Hiring thread (April 2026)
- **Revenue Data**: Caribou is VC-backed; indicates a large market being targeted
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Healthcare — home care agencies (non-medical personal care, companionship, ADL assistance)
- **Core Value Prop**: Matching caregivers to clients, shift scheduling, compliance documentation, payroll, and family communication for home care agencies.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Caribou is building AI coordination for home care. The market has 30,000+ home care agencies in the US. ClearCare/WellSky dominates but costs $500+/month. - **LTD Potential**: 4/5

- **Revenue Data**: Apex Dental has 59 practices. DSOs (dental service organizations) are consolidating the industry.

- **Type**: What Are You Working On discussion
- **Engagement**: Featured in WAYWON thread; founder built for their own children with severe disabilities
