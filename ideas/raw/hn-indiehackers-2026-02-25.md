## InvoxZero — Lightweight Invoice Workflow Tracker for Suppliers
- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46764526
- **Additional Links**: https://invoxzero.com
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 3 upvotes (very early, posted ~1 month ago)
- **Revenue Data**: Pre-revenue. Running costs under €4/month on Hetzner VPS via Coolify. Built in "about half a day, fully vibe coded" with Next.js, Prisma, PostgreSQL, Stripe.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Small businesses, freelancers, and supplier/vendor operations
- **Core Value Prop**: Structured invoice creation + sending with status lifecycle tracking (Draft → Sent → Viewed → Paid). Built to replace the workflow of giving multiple suppliers access to expensive accounting platforms like Xero or QuickBooks. Basic revenue analytics included.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: This validates that small businesses want lightweight invoicing without the overhead of full accounting software. The lifecycle tracking (knowing when a client *viewed* your invoice) is an underappreciated feature — particularly for trades businesses where chasing payments is a major pain point. Currently too generic and bare-bones to compete. The "viewed" tracking is a real differentiator most invoicing tools lack — construction contractors and field service businesses would pay for payment visibility. No mobile app, no payment collection, no industry-specific templates.
- **Our Angle**: Take the "invoice lifecycle tracking" concept (Draft → Sent → Viewed → Paid) and build it for a specific trade vertical. The "viewed" notification alone is a killer feature for contractors chasing payments — "I can see they opened my invoice on Tuesday but haven't paid." Add automatic payment reminders triggered by view-but-no-pay events, integration with Stripe/Square for instant payment collection, and trade-specific invoice templates (progress billing, retainage, change orders). Price at $19-29/mo.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

## Recurring Patterns (Updated)

- **"The $50k/mo solo founder" pattern**: Chimney sweep software ($52.5k/mo), party rental booking ($53.4k/mo), uniform rental tracking ($50k/mo) — three independent boring-business SaaS products all converging on ~$50k/mo revenue with solo founders. - **Asset/rental tracking is underserved**: Uniform rentals, party equipment, linen services — any business that tracks individual items across client accounts has terrible software. The rental lifecycle (available → reserved → delivered → in-use → returned → cleaned → available) is a pattern that can be built once and verticalized.
- **Price points cluster at $79-149/mo for B2B boring SaaS**: Most successful boring-business tools land in this range. Below $49/mo = hard to sustain as solo founder. Above $200/mo = enterprise sales cycle. The $79-149 sweet spot enables self-serve purchase by business owners without formal procurement.
- **"Revenue recovery" positioning beats "software" positioning**: HVAC service agreement tracking, dental patient recall, automated review/referral systems — all positioned as "we help you capture revenue you're currently losing" rather than "we help you manage your business." Revenue recovery ROI is immediately calculable and justifies higher price points.
- **SEO dominance in niche keywords** remains the #1 growth channel: Stagetimer ($20k/mo), Khasanov portfolio ($42k/mo), JustFax Online — all SEO-driven. Niche keywords have near-zero competition and high buyer intent.
- **LTD as launch mechanism**: Kleo sold out 500 LTD spots twice to fund initial development. ZenMaid's first customer paid $1,000 LTD. The LTD launch → subscription conversion pipeline is a proven playbook for boring-business SaaS where operators hate monthly subscriptions.
- **The "service first, software second" approach** (Centori lesson): Offer the service manually, learn the workflow deeply, then build software to automate it. De-risks product-market fit entirely.
- **"Founded by [tradesperson]" is the ultimate positioning**: Kickserv was "founded by plumbers," which resonates with their target market. When building for a boring industry, either partner with a domain expert or invest deeply in understanding the trade before building.
