## AI Voice Agents & Front-Office Automation for Trades

- **Category**: Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://fortune.com/2026/04/27/avoca-ai-agents-missed-calls-hvac-plumbing-roofing-kleiner-perkins-chen-shrivastava-braswell/
  - https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/avoca-raises-125m-at-1b-valuation-to-power-americas-services-economy-with-ai-302753962.html
  - https://aiautomationglobal.com/blog/avoca-ai-voice-agent-trades-unicorn-2026
  - https://contractortoolstack.com/software/avoca-ai/
- **Description**: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, and electrical companies miss an estimated 30-40% of inbound calls during peak hours — customers call once, then call a competitor. Avoca AI reached unicorn status ($1B valuation, $125M raised) in April 2026 by building AI voice/chat agents that handle inbound calls, book appointments, follow up on estimates, and run dispatching 24/7. The company is on track to book $1 billion in jobs in 2026 with 800+ service operator customers including Turnpoint, 1-800-GOT-JUNK?, and Goettl. The HVAC industry alone is worth ~$50B with projections to $75B by 2032.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, auto services, moving companies
- **Market Timing**: Growing — major funding just happened, category is being created now
- **Example Products**:
  - Avoca AI — https://www.avoca.ai/ (unicorn, $125M raised)
  - Infinity Sky AI — https://infinitysky.ai/blog/ai-automation-plumbing-hvac-2026
- **White Space**: Avoca targets larger operators (800+ customers at scale). The 90% of the market — solo operators and micro-businesses with 1-5 trucks — still can't afford enterprise AI voice. A lighter-weight, self-serve, LTD-friendly AI receptionist specifically for solo trade operators is uncrowded.
- **Speed to Market**: Yes — AI voice + booking can be built on existing LLM + calendar APIs in 2-4 weeks MVP
- **LTD Fit**: High — trades owners pay for tools that directly book more jobs; a one-time "never miss a call again" tool is an easy sell
- **Content Strategy**: "missed calls plumbing", "HVAC answering service", "24/7 call answering for contractors", "AI receptionist for trades"

## AI-Native Field Service Dispatch & Scheduling

- **Category**: Vertical AI / Trades Going Digital
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://roboticsandautomationnews.com/2026/03/19/fieldcamp-introduces-ai-dispatcher-for-field-service-skills-matching-route-optimization-and-emergency-reshuffling-built-for-the-trades/99895/
  - https://www.commercialintegrator.com/news/simpro-group-launches-lightning-ai-platform-for-field-service-trades/148010/
  - https://brocoders.com/blog/ai-in-field-service-management/
  - https://medium.com/brocoders-team/best-scheduling-systems-for-hvac-plumbing-companies-2026-068928b10e7a
- **Description**: Roughly 30 million field service workers in the US still receive daily assignments via whiteboards, spreadsheets, and text messages. Two major launches in Q1-Q2 2026 signal the AI-dispatch wave is here: (1) Simpro Group launched Lightning (May 2026), an AI-native platform with Cooper AI operating layer and four dispatch/documentation AI agents across Simpro, AroFlo, and BigChange. (2) FieldCamp launched an AI dispatcher handling skills matching, route optimization, real-time scheduling, and emergency reshuffling. Companies using AI dispatch report 15-25% more jobs completed per day with the same truck count. The global FSM market is projected to grow from $5.64B in 2025 to $9.68B by 2030.
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, cleaning, pest control, any field service business
- **Market Timing**: Growing fast — category is 1-2 years behind AI voice; two major products launched Q1 2026
- **Example Products**:
  - Simpro + Lightning AI — https://www.simprogroup.com/
  - FieldCamp — https://fieldcamp.ai/features/ai-job-scheduling/
  - Jobber — https://fieldservicesoftware.io/software/jobber/ (200K+ users, still largely manual dispatch)
- **White Space**: AI dispatcher that integrates with the existing FSM tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) rather than replacing them — an add-on AI layer vs. platform replacement. Small operators don't want to switch tools; they want their current tool to get smarter.
- **Speed to Market**: Medium (4-8 weeks for integration layer MVP)
- **LTD Fit**: High — "pay once, dispatch smarter forever" is compelling for owner-operators
- **Content Strategy**: "AI dispatcher for HVAC", "field service scheduling automation", "smart dispatch software", "reduce windshield time for technicians"

## Legacy Trade Software Replacement Wave (2026 Tipping Point)

- **Signal Strength**: Strong

  - https://www.capterra.com/field-service-management-software/
  - https://atiba.com/legacy-software-vulnerabilities-the-2026-risk-assessment-guide
  - https://www.skmgp.com/legacy-software-modernization-strategies---complete-guide
  - https://brocoders.com/blog/hvac-plumbing-scheduling-systems/
- **Description**: Industry analysts are calling 2026 a "tipping point" for legacy software replacement across boring industries. The driver: the gap between customer expectations (real-time tracking, self-service portals, same-day booking) and what 20-year-old on-premise software can deliver has become impossible to ignore. In field service, even adding a field to a form in legacy systems requires custom development. In HVAC specifically, older platforms built for Windows XP-era workflows are being replaced by mobile-first, cloud-native tools. The replacement cycle is accelerating with AI-native tools showing dramatically better ROI — companies report 15-25% more jobs per day using AI dispatch vs. manual scheduling. ServiceTitan (HVAC/plumbing), FieldEdge, and BuildOps are winning the enterprise transition; the SMB transition is largely unfinished.
- **Target Industries**: Any trade/field service business still on legacy systems (Windows-only software, no mobile app, no real-time customer visibility)
- **Market Timing**: Growing / accelerating — 2026 called as the year legacy migration decisions are made
- **Example Products**:
  - ServiceTitan — https://www.servicetitan.com/ (enterprise HVAC/plumbing, expensive)
  - BuildOps — https://buildops.com/ (commercial contractor focus)
  - FieldBoss — https://www.fieldboss.com/ (mid-market field service)
  - Brocoders — https://brocoders.com/blog/best-scheduling-systems-for-hvac-plumbing-companies-2026 (analysis)
- **White Space**: "ServiceTitan for small shops" — the 1-5 tech HVAC/plumbing company that can't afford ServiceTitan ($200-500/mo+) but has outgrown Jobber. There's a specific gap in the $75-150/mo range with mobile dispatch, customer texting, and basic inventory management. Pricing power in this middle market is significant.
- **Speed to Market**: Medium (4-8 weeks for core dispatch + mobile workflow)
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — trades owners switching from legacy software are used to paying; LTD can work as an acquisition hook
- **Content Strategy**: "ServiceTitan alternative small business", "affordable HVAC software", "plumbing software mobile app", "field service software under $100/month"

## Top Opportunities for Quick MVP

1. **AI Estimate Follow-Up for Pest Control / Lawn Care** — Build an AI that automatically contacts unconverted estimates via SMS after 24/48/72 hours. Measurable ROI, small build, strong LTD appeal.
2. **AI Receptionist for Solo Trades (LTD-priced)** — Lightweight version of Avoca AI for owner-operators. Answering + booking via AI, flat LTD pricing. Avoca targets enterprise; nobody owns the solo operator.
3. **Food Safety HACCP AI for Single-Location Restaurants** — AI generates HACCP plan instantly + mobile daily compliance logging. Regulatory pressure is high, paper logs are still the norm, and the build is straightforward.
4. **Mobile-First Estimate-to-Invoice for Solo Contractors** — Phone-only job tracking for GCs that can't be bothered with software. Estimate → accept → daily log → invoice. No enterprise features, minimal UI, LTD-friendly.
