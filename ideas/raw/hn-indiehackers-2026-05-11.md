## FieldFlow — $5/mo Jobber Alternative for Solo Contractors

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47294092
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47272986 (earlier iteration)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 1 point, 0 comments (both submissions)
- **Revenue Data**: Priced at $5/mo flat, no per-user fees — builder is using it himself daily
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Solo/small-crew carpenters, handymen, general contractors
- **Core Value Prop**: Invoicing + scheduling + client management for one-person trades operations at $5/mo. Jobber starts at $39, Tradify at $47/user — both bloated for solo operators.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The 1–5 person trades operator is completely underserved. Enterprise FSM tools (ServiceTitan $200-500/mo, Housecall Pro $49-$149/mo) are built for 20+ employee shops. Solo tradespeople pay for complexity they never use.
- **Our Angle**: A genuinely trade-specific, mobile-first tool at $29–49/mo with deep vertical features (material markup calculator, permit tracking, photo documentation, warranty notes) rather than a generic form builder at $5. Lean into AI estimating to reduce quoting time.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5

## Wedding Vendor Coordination — The 47-Person Group Text Problem

- **Source**: https://superframeworks.com/articles/untapped-underserved-micro-saas-niches
- **Additional Links**: https://superframeworks.com/articles/profitable-micro-saas-niches
- **Platform**: Research / Analysis
- **Type**: Market Gap Analysis
- **Engagement**: Research article (March 2026, Superframeworks). Gap Score 9/10.
- **Revenue Data**: Existing tools (The Knot, Zola) focus on the couple's side, not vendor coordination. No mainstream vendor-side tool.
- **Boring Business Score**: 3/5
- **Target Industry**: Wedding vendors — photographers, florists, caterers, DJs, planners (vendor side, not couple side)
- **Core Value Prop**: Shared event timelines, vendor-to-vendor messaging, document sharing (contracts, floor plans, shot lists), payment milestone tracking, day-of coordination dashboard — at $25–49/mo per vendor
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The average wedding has 12–15 vendors who must coordinate without any shared platform. The current solution is "a 47-person group text" (direct quote from industry surveys). Each vendor maintains separate timelines, leading to frequent miscommunication. The couple-facing platforms (The Knot, Zola) have no interest in building vendor-side tools.
- **Our Angle**: Distribution advantage — one wedding photographer on board reaches the caterer, DJ, florist, and planner automatically through the platform. Each new vendor is a distribution channel. Land with photographers (highest pain, most tech-savvy wedding vendors) and let the network effect pull in the rest.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5

## Local Government Permit Tracking — $50K Enterprise Tools for $500 Town Budgets

- **Source**: https://superframeworks.com/articles/untapped-underserved-micro-saas-niches
- **Platform**: Research / Analysis
- **Type**: Market Gap Analysis
- **Engagement**: Research article (March 2026, Superframeworks). Gap Score 9/10.
- **Revenue Data**: Accela and Tyler Technologies charge $50K–$500K for enterprise civic tech. Small municipalities (<50,000 population) cannot afford implementation.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Small municipalities, townships, county code enforcement offices
- **Core Value Prop**: Online permit submission, automated status notifications, inspection scheduling, fee collection, compliance deadline tracking — at $99–199/mo per department
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Small town government offices track building permits in filing cabinets or 15-year-old Access databases. Residents complain about lost applications and missed deadlines. The gap between current reality and what's possible is enormous. Long-tail market: 35,000+ municipalities in the US under 50,000 population.
- **Our Angle**: Start with a specific use case (building permits only) in one state to handle the state-specific requirement complexity. Partner with state municipal leagues for distribution — they have direct relationships with every member municipality and actively seek affordable tools to recommend.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 (government = subscription contracts; LTD not appropriate)
