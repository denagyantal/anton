## Vertical AI Voice Agents for Trades (Avoca Model)

- **Category**: Vertical AI
- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:
  - https://leapingai.com/blog/voice-ai-platforms-compared-leaping-ai-vs-avoca
  - https://getdriive.com/alternatives/avoca
  - https://lunacal.ai/blogs/ai-receptionist-for-HVAC
  - https://myquoteiq.com/best-24-7-answering-service-software-hvac-2026/
  - https://golmtech.solutions/blog/best-ai-answering-services-home-services/
- **Description**: Avoca raised $125M in April 2026 (Kleiner Perkins, Meritech, General Catalyst) to build always-on voice AI agents that answer calls, book jobs, and run SMS drip campaigns for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and roofing companies. Now valued at $1B, it proved that trade businesses will pay serious money for AI phone agents because missed calls = missed revenue. A wave of competitors is now entering the space: Driive (drive-time-aware scheduling, CRM-agnostic), QuoteIQ (call booking + field CRM bundle), Leaping AI, and ServiceAgent ($39/mo entry-level bundle).
- **Target Industries**: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest control, garage door, drain services
- **Market Timing**: Growing — Avoca proved the market, competitors racing in
- **Example Products**:
  - Avoca AI: https://www.avoca.ai (unicorn, $125M raised)
  - Driive: https://getdriive.com
  - QuoteIQ: https://myquoteiq.com
  - Leaping AI: https://leapingai.com
  - ServiceAgent (bundled, $39/mo entry)
- **White Space**: Niche-within-a-niche AI voice agents targeting single verticals Avoca doesn't own well (e.g., pool/spa service, chimney sweeps, appliance repair). Also: **outbound AI** for re-booking seasonal customers (HVAC tune-ups in spring/fall) — Avoca focuses on inbound. CRM-agnostic version for shops not on ServiceTitan (90% of small trades).
- **Speed to Market**: 4-6 weeks for a targeted voice agent with booking integration using existing voice AI APIs (Vapi, Retell, ElevenLabs)
- **LTD Fit**: Moderate — service businesses prefer monthly recurring but an LTD at $199-$299 with usage caps could work for the 1-3 van segment
- **Content Strategy**: "AI receptionist for [trade]", "never miss an HVAC call", "24/7 answering service for plumbers"

- **Signal Strength**: Strong
- **Sources**:

  - https://fieldcamp.ai/cleaning-business-software/best-software/

They serve 350+ commercial cleaning, landscaping, and pest control owners. The core insight: these owners are in the field all day and have zero time for CRM hygiene or sales follow-up. An agent that prospcts, follows up, and books new commercial accounts on autopilot is extremely high-value. Similar to how Avoca owns the inbound call, Cohesive owns the outbound growth motion.

- **Market Timing**: Early — Cohesive is small (350 customers), large market untapped
- **Example Products**:
  - Cohesive: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/cohesive

  - FieldCamp: https://fieldcamp.ai

Also: post-job review solicitation + re-booking automation as a standalone micro-SaaS ($49/mo) targeting the 1-5 employee service company that can't afford Cohesive.
- **Speed to Market**: 3-4 weeks for a review + re-booking automation tool; 6-8 weeks for a full agentic outbound prospecting flow
- **LTD Fit**: Yes — $199-$399 LTD for the standalone review/rebooking tool; monthly for the full agentic CRM
- **Content Strategy**: "cleaning business CRM", "landscaping customer follow-up software", "how to get more commercial cleaning clients"

  - https://serfy.io/blog/the-2026-guide-to-digital-proof-of-work-for-contractors
  - https://www.repair-crm.com/2026/06/25/paperless-solutions-for-contractors-the-2026-guide-to-field-efficiency
  - https://formaflow.io/blog/construction-safety-inspection-software
  - https://servicebusinessacademy.org/top-10-safety-compliance-software-contractors-2026/
  - http://cdn.hl.com/pdf/2026/field-and-frontline-operations-management-software-mar2026.pdf
- **Description**: Over 50% of field workers still use paper and spreadsheets. The average construction worker wastes 14 hours/week on preventable inefficiencies. Firms using digital safety platforms report 73% fewer OSHA citations (AGC 2025 data). The gap for 3-10 technician crews (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) isn't enterprise safety software — it's timestamped job photos, custom inspection checklists, OSHA 300 log generation, and a defensible paper trail for insurance claims and client disputes. Enterprise tools (Fieldwire by Hilti, Procore) are too expensive and complex. FormaFlow and Serfy are emerging in this gap.
- **Target Industries**: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, general contracting (3-15 person crews)
- **Market Timing**: Growing — OSHA enforcement tightening, general contractors increasingly requiring documentation from subs
- **Example Products**:
  - FormaFlow: https://formaflow.io
  - Serfy: https://serfy.io
  - GoBuid: https://gobuid.com
  - Fieldwire (enterprise): https://www.fieldwire.com
- **White Space**: **Trade-specific safety vault** — not generic construction safety, but a tool designed for a specific trade (e.g., "HVAC technician compliance kit") with pre-built checklists for refrigerant handling logs, EPA certifications, and service records. Or a **"digital job binder"** app for sub-contractors that bundles photos, signatures, and OSHA logs into a sharable PDF for the GC — priced at $29-$49/mo per crew.
- **Speed to Market**: 2-3 weeks for a focused mobile form + photo + PDF report tool
- **LTD Fit**: Strong — trade business owners buy one-time tools; $99-$199 LTD makes sense
- **Content Strategy**: "OSHA compliance for HVAC contractors", "digital job photos for insurance claims", "paperless field service for electricians"

  - Axon (legacy/mid-market)

A $79/mo mobile-first app combining FMCSA-compliant ELD, load management, invoice generation, and IFTA fuel tax reporting specifically for solo/tiny carriers. Existing tools force owners to juggle 3-4 apps. Also: **AI load matching for small fleets** that auto-routes based on driver HOS windows.
