## Rentman — AV/Event Production Operations Platform

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-15m-arr-saas-from-a-gap-he-found-at-his-brick-and-mortar-HFriCBQLHukAmdXVEj1q
- **Additional Links**: https://rentman.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder case study
- **Engagement**: 111 upvotes, 18 comments
- **Revenue Data**: $15–20M ARR ($1.25M+/month), bootstrapped 8 years before taking outside capital, 70+ countries
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — AV/event production is unglamorous but every concert, corporate event, and film shoot runs on this stuff
- **Target Industry**: AV rental, event production, staging, lighting, broadcast, film production companies
- **Core Value Prop**: Equipment rental tracking, crew scheduling, quoting, logistics, and invoicing in one cloud platform for production companies that previously used spreadsheets or expensive on-prem software built for other industries
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Rentman owns AV/staging globally but the same problem exists in adjacent rental verticals — construction equipment rental, party/event rental (tents, tables, chairs), medical equipment rental, film/photo gear rental — all still heavily spreadsheet or legacy desktop software. Small operators (5–50 employees) are underserved by Rentman (priced for production companies) and cannot afford enterprise tools.
- **Our Angle**: Clone the Rentman model for a specific adjacent vertical — construction equipment rental or party/event rental — targeting small operators with a $49–99/month SaaS that replaces their spreadsheet. The "built by someone who ran the business" angle is repeatable.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — small operators in these niches are LTD-friendly; AppSumo has audience overlap

## ZenMaid — Maid Service Scheduling SaaS

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/from-a-cleaning-side-hustle-to-a-3m-yr-saas-for-cleaning-services-suhsqkDZB1zIwRmXxrFm

- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder case study
- **Engagement**: 28 upvotes, 7 comments
- **Revenue Data**: $3M/yr ($250k/month), bootstrapped since 2013, still growing as a lifestyle business
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — maid services are about as boring as it gets, but extremely high demand
- **Target Industry**: Residential and commercial cleaning / maid services
- **Core Value Prop**: Scheduling, dispatching, customer management, and payment processing built specifically for maid services — not adapted from generic project management tools

- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — artisan food production is unglamorous but a huge and growing market

## Pest Control & Field Service Inspection Gap

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/top-5-pest-control-and-equipment-inspection-apps-in-2026-b48a5f6d0f
- **Additional Links**: https://getlatka.com/companies/industries/i-pest-control-software
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Industry analysis / market gap post (November 2025)
- **Engagement**: Industry roundup post
- **Revenue Data**: Current market pricing $30–150/user/month. PestPac (WorkWave) is enterprise-focused. No clear winner for 1–5 truck operators.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — pest control is about as unglamorous as it gets but is recession-proof
- **Target Industry**: Pest control companies (especially small operators, 1–10 technicians); equipment inspection for field service businesses
- **Core Value Prop**: Current options are either expensive enterprise platforms (PestPac $150+/user) or generic field service tools that don't understand pest control compliance. Small operators need: digital inspection checklists, offline functionality, chemical usage/compliance tracking, and automated customer communication — at $30–50/month flat, not per-seat pricing.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The per-seat pricing model kills small pest control operators — a 3-tech shop can't justify $450+/month for PestPac. Flat-rate pricing at $49–79/month for up to 5 technicians would capture an enormous underserved segment. Same dynamics apply to pool service, lawn care, and pressure washing businesses.
- **Our Angle**: Build "Pest Control Pro" — flat-rate pricing for small operators, mobile-first, with offline capability, OSHA compliance checklists built in, and automated customer SMS ("Your tech is 30 min away"). Sell on Facebook Groups where pest control owners actually hang out.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — pest control owners are extremely LTD-receptive; they hate subscriptions and the compliance angle makes it a "must have not a nice to have"

## Lightweight FSM CRM for Small Home Service Contractors

- **Source**: https://www.saasmag.com/vertical-saas-niche-beats-horizontal-2026/ ; https://www.signalfire.com/blog/vertical-ai-in-trades-and-construction
- **Additional Links**: https://www.genailabs.agency/blog/underserved-saas-niches-2025-2026-analysis ; https://ninefour.vc/2023/09/28/the-vertical-saas-opportunity-in-home-services/
- **Platform**: Industry research / HN discussions (ongoing)
- **Type**: Market gap / structural opportunity
- **Engagement**: N/A — structural finding across multiple sources
- **Revenue Data**: ServiceTitan (the market leader) is at $685M ARR, went public Dec 2024 at $9.6B valuation. Median vertical SaaS gross retention: 91% vs 78–85% for horizontal. 89% of tradespeople lack adequate digital tools (SBA Q3 2025 report).
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing = core sweaty startup territory
- **Target Industry**: HVAC technicians, plumbers, electricians, roofers — specifically operators with 1–10 employees who cannot afford ServiceTitan ($500+/month)
- **Core Value Prop**: ServiceTitan is the right product but priced for mid-market. Small operators (1–5 trucks) need: job scheduling, customer history, invoicing, and payment collection in one mobile app — priced at $49–99/month, not $500+. Jobber exists but lacks deep trade-specific workflows (refrigerant tracking for HVAC, permit tracking for electrical, material cost tracking for roofers).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Go one level deeper than "field service CRM" — build for a single trade. "HVAC Pro" with refrigerant inventory management, seasonal tune-up reminder campaigns, and equipment service history per unit. These details justify 3x pricing vs generic tools. A solo dev built Neuclis targeting this exact space (spotted in IH comments) — market is pre-crowded but not yet won.
- **Our Angle**: Pick one trade (HVAC is largest), build the single killer feature that no generic FSM tool has (e.g., refrigerant tracking + EPA compliance logs), offer a free tier for 1-tech shops, charge $79/month for 2–5 techs. Find customers in HVAC Facebook Groups (very active) and ACCA/HARDI trade forums.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — HVAC business owners are extremely LTD-friendly; compliance features make it sticky

## Appointment Scheduling + Recurring Service Management for Small Service Businesses

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/micro-saas-trends-automation-trades-pdf-and-8-more-niches-db94d9c5a6
- **Additional Links**: https://calendesk.com/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Micro SaaS trend
- **Engagement**: Micro SaaS trend roundup (IH community)
- **Revenue Data**: Calendesk (generic scheduling) hit $15k MRR after 4 years. ZenMaid ($250k/month) proves niche scheduling beats generic. 78% of online bookings now made on mobile (2026 data).
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — scheduling is boring but the recurring-service model (pool cleaning weekly, pest control quarterly, lawn care bi-weekly) is underserved by generic tools
- **Target Industry**: Service businesses with recurring schedules — pool cleaning, lawn care, window washing, house cleaning, gutter cleaning, junk removal
- **Core Value Prop**: Generic booking tools (Calendly, Acuity) are built for one-off appointments. Service businesses with recurring routes need: route optimization, automatic rebooking, seasonal pause/resume, and recurring billing integrated — not a calendar widget bolted onto Stripe.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: "Recurring route management + billing" is a specific unmet need. Pool service companies run 50–200 weekly stops and coordinate chemical levels per pool, skimmer notes, gate codes. Lawn care companies run bi-weekly routes with crew assignments. No affordable tool handles this end-to-end at $49–99/month. Yardbook (lawn care) exists but is dated and has weak mobile UX.
- **Our Angle**: Build "Route + Recurring" — a mobile-first recurring service management tool with built-in route optimization (Google Maps API), per-customer service notes, and automated invoicing. Start with pool service or lawn care as the beachhead. Compete on UX vs Yardbook's dated interface.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — small pool/lawn service owners are exactly the LTD customer; route-based software with a one-time fee is very appealing

- **Type**: Revenue milestone + Show HN signal
- **Engagement**: My AskAI: 99 upvotes, 20 comments; AI calls Show HN: high engagement
- **Revenue Data**: My AskAI at $40k MRR ($500k ARR), 2 founders, 75k+ support chats/month. AI phone handling market growing rapidly.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5 — AI customer intake isn't boring, but the end customer (plumber, HVAC company, pest control) is pure boring business
- **Target Industry**: HVAC companies, plumbers, electricians, pest control — any home service business that gets inbound calls they miss (after hours, during jobs, on weekends)
- **Core Value Prop**: A trade business misses 30–40% of inbound calls when techs are on jobs. Each missed call is a lost $200–500 job. An AI front desk that answers calls 24/7, qualifies leads, books appointments, and sends confirmations — priced at $79–149/month — pays for itself with one recovered job per week.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Generic AI voice tools (Bland AI, Vapi) are built for developers. The IH Show HN "Companies use AI to take your calls, I built AI to make them for you" (HN item 44716414) signals this is a hot space, but nobody has nailed the "boring business vertical" angle yet.
- **Our Angle**: Build "Trade Desk AI" — an AI phone answering and scheduling tool specifically for home service businesses. Pre-built scripts for HVAC, plumbing, pest, and electrical. Integrates with Google Calendar or Jobber. Charge flat $99/month. Find customers in HVAC/plumbing Facebook Groups where "I missed too many calls today" complaints are posted daily.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — harder for LTD because it's usage-based (call minutes) but a flat-rate LTD tier (e.g., 500 mins/month lifetime) is feasible

## Vertical SaaS Structural Signal: Boring Industries Still Running on Paper

- **Source**: https://www.signalfire.com/blog/vertical-ai-in-trades-and-construction ; https://www.saasmag.com/vertical-saas-niche-beats-horizontal-2026/
- **Additional Links**: https://entrepreneurloop.com/bootstrapped-saas-niches-solo-founders/
- **Platform**: Industry research (SaaS Mag, SignalFire VC)
- **Type**: Structural market analysis / investment thesis
- **Engagement**: N/A — macro signal
- **Revenue Data**: Vertical SaaS cohort averages 91% gross retention vs 78–85% for horizontal. ServiceTitan $685M ARR at IPO (Dec 2024). Median vertical SaaS ARR growth 31% in 2025 vs 28% for horizontal peers.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5 — the entire finding is about boring industries
- **Target Industry**: All trades and local services — roofing, restoration, tree service, pool service, pressure washing, septic, chimney sweep
- **Core Value Prop**: The data consistently shows that trades businesses have 89% no/poor digital tooling (SBA report), prefer domain-specific tools over generic CRMs, pay $30–150/user/month without blinking if the tool solves their exact problem, and churn at very low rates once onboarded (high switching costs due to data).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The next wave of boring-business SaaS winners will be single-trade specialists who build 2–3 killer workflow features that no generic tool offers. Specific underserved niches identified: (a) tree service / arborist management — no clear leader, (b) chimney sweep scheduling + inspection report generation, (c) septic pumping route + compliance records, (d) restoration (water/fire damage) — job costing and insurance claim documentation. Each of these is a $1M+ ARR business waiting to happen.
- **Our Angle**: Pick the most underserved single-trade niche, build the 3 features that matter most to operators in that trade (use Reddit/Facebook Group research to identify), launch with a simple LTD, and grow via trade association forums.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — all of these niches are LTD-friendly and largely untapped on AppSumo

## Summary of Key Patterns

### Industries with Confirmed Revenue Validation
| Product | Industry | Revenue | Model |
|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| Rentman | AV/Event equipment rental | $15-20M ARR | Per-seat SaaS |
| ZenMaid | Maid/cleaning services | $3M/yr | Per-business SaaS |
| ServiceTitan | HVAC/plumbing (enterprise) | $685M ARR | Enterprise SaaS |
| Calendesk | Appointment scheduling | $15k MRR | Per-seat SaaS |
| My AskAI | AI customer support (any vertical) | $40k MRR | Usage SaaS |

### Hottest Gaps Identified Today
1. **Single-trade HVAC SaaS** — refrigerant compliance + scheduling at $79/month vs ServiceTitan's $500+
2. **Pest control flat-rate tool** — compliance checklists + offline mode for 1-5 truck operators
3. **Recurring route management** — pool cleaning / lawn care with route optimization (Yardbook is dated)
4. **AI phone answering for trades** — 24/7 intake that books appointments automatically
5. **Food production management** — allergen-compliant batch production for small food manufacturers (Craftplan model)
6. **Equipment rental for adjacent verticals** — party rental, construction equipment rental (Rentman model replicated)

### Key Structural Insight
Boring industries punish bad software immediately — the feedback loop is brutal but honest. Vertical SaaS moats are strongest here because horizontal players can't cross over without breaking their UX, and operators don't have time to self-configure generic tools. The best entry point: find a niche where the existing solution is a spreadsheet or a 15-year-old desktop app.
