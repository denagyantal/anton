## Stagetimer — Validated $22K/mo from "Boring" Single-Purpose Event Timer
- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/turning-a-simple-b2b-solution-into-a-15k-mrr-saas-by-exploiting-a-market-niche-xIxrVwn24DVsN8b3PYD3
- **Additional Links**: https://stagetimer.io, https://www.somethingsblog.com/2026/01/24/real-indie-hacker-success-stories-that-prove-its-still-possible-in-2026/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Founder interview
- **Engagement**: Featured post on Indie Hackers tech section; widely cited in 2026 success story roundups
- **Revenue Data**: $15K MRR subscriptions + ~$7.5K/mo one-time payments = ~$22.5K/mo total. 13,000+ accounts. Founder: Lukas Hermann. Also runs Rundown Studio (event planning software) at $4K MRR. Hybrid pricing model (subscriptions + one-time purchases for occasional users) increased total revenue ~50% over subscription-only. SEO drives 40%+ of acquisition through low-volume, high-intent keywords.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Live events and video production — stage timing / countdown management
- **Core Value Prop**: Remote countdown timer for live events. Multiple team members (directors, lighting/audio techs) can view and control synchronized timers across multiple screens/devices. Born from a real pain: "A friend does video recording and he would run to physically start that timer, then run back to his recording equipment." Expanded to event updates (cancellations, delays, messages to presenters).
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Validates the "insanely narrow niche, insanely well-served" model. A countdown timer sounds too trivial for $22K/mo, yet there's almost no competition because the niche is too small for funded startups to care. Key pricing insight: adding one-time purchase option for occasional users expanded revenue 50% — many event professionals only need the tool a few times per year. The pattern is replicable: find professional contexts where a "simple" tool hasn't been properly digitized. Candidates: (1) **Auction bid clock** — 40K+ US auctioneers need timed bidding displays with lot tracking, (2) **Church sermon timer** — discreet pulpit-visible timing for pastors, (3) **Courtroom proceeding timer** — attorneys tracking deposition/hearing time limits, (4) **Sports scoreboard controller** — amateur leagues without dedicated hardware.
- **Our Angle**: Don't clone Stagetimer — it owns its niche with 13K accounts and strong SEO moat. Apply the pattern to adjacent single-purpose professional tools. The auction bid clock is most promising: iPad-based bid clock with remote control, lot tracking, bidder registration, and results reporting. Price at $49/mo for regular auctioneers, $19/single-event for occasional users (mimic Stagetimer's hybrid model). The one-time pricing for occasional users is a crucial learning — it dramatically expands TAM beyond frequent subscribers. Build, SEO-optimize for "[niche] timer/clock software", and let inbound do the work.
- **LTD Potential**: 3

## Angel Match — "Boring Database" Strategy Printing $37K MRR

- **Type**: Revenue milestone / Portfolio breakdown
- **Engagement**: Featured story on Indie Hackers tech section
- **Revenue Data**: Angel Match: $37.3K MRR (down from $41K peak Sep 2025). Investor Hunt: $4,557 MRR. Journalist Hunt: $180 MRR. Pur Social: $491 MRR. Total portfolio: ~$42K MRR. Founder: Rashid Khasanov. Tech stack: Node.js, DigitalOcean, Stripe, Klaviyo. Built on Figma → code workflow.
- **Boring Business Score**: 3
- **Target Industry**: Startup fundraising (Angel Match), PR/media relations (Journalist Hunt)
- **Core Value Prop**: Curated databases of industry contacts — 110K+ angels/VCs in Angel Match, 100K+ journalists in Journalist Hunt. Includes CRM, email outreach tools, and calculators. The founder's thesis: "Databases are boring. The products we run are not flashy, but they solve boring problems." Value comes from aggregation and curation, not novel technology.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The "curated database + outreach" pattern is highly replicable for boring industries. Consider: (1) **ContractorScout** — database of subcontractors by trade/region for general contractors (GCs spend hours finding reliable subs for each job), (2) **InspectorDB** — database of home/building inspectors by specialty and jurisdiction for real estate agents, (3) **PermitPro** — database of permit requirements by municipality for contractors (every city has different rules, currently looked up manually). Each follows the Angel Match template: aggregate hard-to-find professional contacts, add basic CRM/outreach, charge $49-99/mo. The moat is in the data collection — once you have 50K+ verified contacts, competitors need years to replicate.

GCs perpetually struggle to find reliable, licensed, insured subcontractors (electricians, plumbers, framers, drywallers, roofers) for each project. Currently they rely on personal networks, referrals, and Craigslist. A curated database with verified licenses, insurance certs, availability calendars, and past project ratings would be worth $99/mo to any active GC. Distribution: construction industry associations, builder Facebook groups, construction Slack/Discord communities. The database-first approach means low development cost — the product is essentially structured data with search and outreach tools. Programmatic SEO for "[trade] subcontractor [city]" landing pages (à la Journalist Hunt's approach) drives organic acquisition.

## SMB Finance Tracker + CRM Combo — Validated Demand, Poor Execution Gap
- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44725376
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44047324, https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42997397
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Show HN (multiple posts)
- **Engagement**: Multiple Show HN attempts at SMB finance/CRM tools in 2025: (1) "Simple finance tracker, CRM and task manager for small business" — 2 points, minimal traction, (2) "Invoice and Expense Tracker with AI Advisors for SMBs" — 1 point, early MVP seeking validation, (3) "Personal CRM for Founders" — 1 point, 1 comment with critical UX feedback. All had low engagement, suggesting either poor execution or oversaturated positioning.
- **Revenue Data**: None — all pre-revenue MVPs. But the repeated attempts signal persistent demand. The commenter on the CRM post nailed the core problem: "Too much text on the landing page. Try to get it to much simpler value. Pick a single ideal customer."
- **Boring Business Score**: 3
- **Target Industry**: Small businesses (generic horizontal positioning — the weakness)
- **Core Value Prop**: Integrated finance + CRM + task management for small businesses. The AI invoicing tool adds cash flow analysis and expense reduction suggestions. These are all "QuickBooks + spreadsheet replacements" attempts.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Three independent founders launched nearly identical "simple SMB finance/CRM" tools on HN in 2025, all with near-zero traction. This pattern tells us two things: (1) **demand is real** — multiple builders independently identified the same problem, (2) **horizontal positioning is death** — "small business" is not a niche, it's a category. The HN commenter's feedback is spot-on: pick ONE type of small business. The winning move: take this exact product concept (simple invoicing + expense tracking + customer management) and vertical-ize it for a specific trade. "Invoice tracker for HVAC contractors" beats "invoice tracker for small businesses" every time because you can pre-build line item templates (furnace install, AC tune-up, duct cleaning), integrate with HVAC-specific supplier catalogs, and market in HVAC Facebook groups rather than competing in the noise of generic SMB tools.
- **Our Angle**: Don't build another generic SMB tool — the three failed Show HN attempts prove this doesn't work. Instead, fork the concept into a trade-specific variant. Best candidate: **HVAC Invoice & Expense Tracker**. HVAC is ideal because: (1) high average ticket ($3K-15K for installations), (2) mix of recurring maintenance contracts and one-time installs creates complex billing, (3) equipment warranty tracking is a natural add-on feature, (4) 100K+ HVAC companies in the US. Core features: pre-loaded HVAC line items (Carrier/Trane/Lennox equipment catalogs), flat-rate pricing books, equipment warranty date tracking, maintenance contract recurring billing, and HVAC-specific expense categories (refrigerant, copper line sets, condensate pumps). Price at $29/mo for solo operators, $49/mo for small companies. The trade-specific templates and pricing books are the hook — they save hours of manual estimate building.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

Small landlords managing 1-10 units need to set rents competitively but don't have access to institutional data (CoStar is $500+/mo). A tool that scrapes public rental listings, analyzes actual rented prices vs. asking prices, and delivers neighborhood-specific rent recommendations by unit type/size would be worth $15/unit/mo. Integrate with the RentReady concept from yesterday's scan (financial tracking + Schedule E) as an upsell: "Know what to charge AND track every dollar for tax time." The data moat strengthens over time as the system accumulates more rental pricing data.

## Hairdressing/Salon AI Consultation Tool — Untapped Analog Industry
- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42608478
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22496129
- **Platform**: Hacker News
- **Type**: Ask HN / Community demand signal
- **Engagement**: HN thread on "industries needing software and automation" — commenter specifically identified hairdressing as an "analogue industry" with resistance to digital adoption but clear AI opportunities
- **Revenue Data**: No existing indie product identified. Market: 1M+ licensed cosmetologists in US, 86K+ hair salon establishments. Adjacent: Fresha (salon booking) valued at $1B+, but Fresha is booking/POS only — no consultation tools. Boulevard (salon software) raised $70M+ but focuses on operations, not client consultation.
- **Boring Business Score**: 5
- **Target Industry**: Hair salons and independent stylists
- **Core Value Prop**: AI-powered visual consultation tool for hairdressers. Client uploads a selfie → AI shows realistic previews of different cuts, colors, and styles on their actual face/hair. Eliminates the #1 pain point in hair services: miscommunication between what the client imagines and what the stylist understands. "I want it shorter" means something different to everyone. Currently: clients bring Pinterest photos of celebrities with entirely different hair textures, face shapes, and skin tones.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: AI hair visualization technology exists (L'Oréal's ModiFace, Perfect Corp's YouCam) but it's embedded in consumer apps for at-home experimentation, not professional salon consultation workflows. The HN commenter nailed it: current apps feel "gimmicky" because they're consumer-facing. The professional use case is different — the stylist needs to: (1) show the client realistic outcomes given THEIR hair (texture, density, damage level, growth pattern), (2) document the agreed-upon look as a "service contract" (reducing disputes), (3) track color formulas and cutting techniques per client across visits. No tool connects the AI visualization to the professional workflow (booking, color formula recording, before/after portfolio, rebooking). The salon industry is notoriously tech-resistant but has high willingness to pay for tools that reduce client complaints and increase rebooking rates.
- **Our Angle**: Build "StylePreview" — an iPad app for salon chairs, not a consumer phone app. Core flow: (1) stylist photographs client in salon lighting, (2) AI generates 3-5 style previews based on client requests, adjusted for their actual hair properties, (3) client and stylist agree on a look (saved as the "service record"), (4) stylist records color formula and technique notes, (5) before/after photos auto-saved to client profile for next visit. Price at $79/mo per stylist seat. The iPad-in-salon deployment is deliberate — it keeps the tool professional (not "download our app"), creates a premium experience for clients, and the stylist controls the interaction. Distribution: hair product distributors (SalonCentric, CosmoProf) who have direct relationships with every salon. Partner with one distributor for co-marketing. The before/after photo portfolio becomes a marketing asset for stylists on Instagram — natural viral loop.
- **LTD Potential**: 4

Enterprise tools: Guesty ($500+/mo, targets 10+ property managers), Hostaway ($100+/mo, growing complexity). Small host tools: iGMS, Hospitable ($29-79/mo) but focused on messaging automation, not operations. The gap: comprehensive lightweight management for 1-5 property hosts at $19-39/mo.
- **Boring Business Score**: 4
- **Target Industry**: Individual short-term rental hosts (1-5 properties on Airbnb/VRBO)
- **Core Value Prop**: Multi-platform listing management (Airbnb + VRBO + Booking.com sync), dynamic pricing suggestions based on local events/seasonality, automated guest communication templates, cleaning crew scheduling and coordination, and financial reporting by property. The target user manages 1-5 vacation rentals as a side business or primary income and currently juggles between platform dashboards, a cleaning crew group text, and a spreadsheet for finances.

Core flow: (1) sync bookings from Airbnb/VRBO via API, (2) auto-generate turnover schedule with cleaner assignments, (3) push notification to assigned cleaner with property-specific checklist and access instructions, (4) cleaner completes checklist with photo verification, (5) host gets "unit ready" confirmation. That's it — no pricing tools, no messaging automation, no financial reports. Just turnover coordination done perfectly. Price at $9/property/month. A 3-property host pays $27/mo — trivially cheap for the peace of mind. The cleaning checklist with photo verification is the hook (proves the unit is guest-ready, useful for damage disputes). Expand to maintenance request tracking as a natural upsell.
- **LTD Potential**: 5
