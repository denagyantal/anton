## Executive Summary

The boring business software sector is experiencing a genuine inflection point. ServiceTitan's successful December 2024 NASDAQ IPO (ticker: TTAN), trading up 40%+ on day one, validated the $1.5 trillion field services market and triggered a wave of investor attention, M&A, and competitor launches. AI is now being embedded directly into trades workflows — not as a wrapper, but as a purpose-built operating layer trained on industry-specific data (blueprints, service calls, SOAP notes). Meanwhile, the "paper-to-digital" wave continues across childcare, home inspection, auto repair, and food safety, and AI receptionists are emerging as the single hottest product category for small contractor businesses.

## Trend 1: AI Quote & Estimating Engines for Trades

**Category:** Vertical AI
**Signal Strength:** Strong

- https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260310158768/en/Rebar-Closes-$14M-Series-A-Led-by-Prudence-to-Rapidly-Scale-its-AI-Platform-for-HVAC-Electrical-and-Plumbing-Industries

- https://roofr.com/
- https://handoff.ai/
- https://x.build/roofing-estimating-software

### Description
Trades contractors still generate quotes manually — reading blueprints by hand, building materials lists from memory, and typing proposals in Word. AI is now automating this 4-8 hour workflow into minutes. Rebar (March 2026, $14M Series A) uses computer vision trained on millions of HVAC blueprints to auto-read construction documents, build bills of materials, and generate quotes. Early customers report 60-70% faster quoting and 2-3x higher win rates. Roofr ($12M Series A) does the same for roofing: satellite imagery + AI estimates. Handoff.ai targets remodelers and handymen. The pattern is identical: vertical AI trained on domain-specific documents eliminates the most painful manual step in the trades sales process.

### Target Industries
HVAC suppliers & distributors, Roofing contractors, Electricians, Plumbers, Remodelers, General contractors

### Market Timing
**Early-to-Growing** — Rebar just closed Series A in March 2026. Most trades are not yet using AI estimating.

### Example Products
- Rebar (https://www.withrebar.ai/) — $14M Series A, HVAC/Electrical/Plumbing blueprints-to-quotes
- Roofr (https://roofr.com/) — $12M Series A, roofing measurements + proposals
- Handoff AI (https://handoff.ai/) — AI estimating for remodelers and handymen
- XBuild (https://x.build/roofing-estimating-software) — roofing proposals in minutes

### White Space
- Electrical estimating specifically (Rebar says it's next, but not built yet)
- Plumbing takeoff from blueprints (very early)
- Landscaping & irrigation project estimating
- Integration layer connecting AI estimating → job scheduling → purchasing → invoicing

### Speed to Market
**Moderate (6-12 weeks MVP)** — Core blueprint parsing requires ML model training. A lightweight version targeting simpler trade types (cleaning, painting, landscaping) with structured input forms + AI pricing could be built in 2-4 weeks.

### LTD Fit
**Moderate** — Contractors buy tools. But the AI model quality is the product, so a "lifetime deal" with ongoing model improvement is tricky to sustain. One-time payment for a simpler estimating helper (not full AI) could work well on AppSumo at $79-$149.
