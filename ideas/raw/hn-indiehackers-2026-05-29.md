- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47509571
- **Additional Links**: https://www.onhand.pro/ (founder's company)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Discussion / personal essay (reached front page May 2026)
- **Engagement**: High engagement, 100+ comments — multiple HN veterans validated the thesis
- **Revenue Data**: N/A (pre-revenue, founder learning the domain)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Pest control (and by extension: HVAC, cleaning, landscaping — same profile)
- **Core Value Prop**: Founder took a technician job to deeply understand pest control operations before building software, rejecting the venture narrative in favor of a bootstrapped domain-expert approach.

- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — recurring compliance and scheduling workflows mean high retention; one-time deal can onboard early adopters who then convert to MRR.

## 2. AI Receptionist for Veterinary Clinics (LiveTok)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47273350
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44370222 (Zooesis — related vet AI tool)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN — March 6, 2026
- **Engagement**: Featured on HN front page; vet clinics are notoriously phone-heavy and understaffed
- **Revenue Data**: Not disclosed; product live and taking sign-ups
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Veterinary clinics
- **Core Value Prop**: AI phone receptionist that answers 24/7, books appointments, handles FAQ (hours, vaccines, pricing), and routes complex calls to a vet tech. Vet clinics miss dozens of calls/day — each missed call = 1–3 lost appointments.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Most vet clinic software (AVImark, Cornerstone, ezyVet) has no AI phone layer. Solo/small clinic owners can't afford a full-time receptionist. Clinics are desperate to reduce phone load on staff.
- **Our Angle**: Narrow to independent / single-vet practices (largest underserved segment). Add SMS follow-up for appointment reminders and no-show reduction. Bundle with a simple booking page. $99–149/mo is trivial compared to one recovered appointment per week.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — obvious ROI, small clinics have budget, lifetime deal at $299–499 covers 3 months of subscription and makes for a compelling AppSumo pitch.

## 3. AI Veterinary Lab Test Analysis (Zooesis)

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44370222
- **Additional Links**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47273350 (LiveTok, same vertical)
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN — June 24, 2025
- **Engagement**: Reached HN front page; vets and vet techs commented positively
- **Revenue Data**: Not disclosed
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Veterinary practices
- **Core Value Prop**: Photo → AI analysis of CBC, biochemistry panel, urinalysis results in seconds. Generates a clear, plain-English report the vet can hand to the client. Saves 10–15 minutes per lab result interpretation and reduces errors.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Vet labs send raw numbers; clinics either memorize reference ranges or look them up manually. No affordable tool bridges the gap. Large vet software suites ignore this workflow entirely. Target: general practice vets, mobile vets, new graduates overwhelmed by lab interpretation.
- **Our Angle**: Mobile-first (photo on phone), freemium (first 5 analyses free), then $39/mo. Expand to exotic animal reference ranges as a differentiator. Could bundle with the AI receptionist (LiveTok angle) for a complete "AI vet clinic assistant" suite.
- **LTD Potential**: 3/5 — AI-heavy products are harder to LTD; but a per-practice license at $149–249 could work on AppSumo.

## 6. Invoice Collection Automation for Small Service Businesses

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47638685
- **Additional Links**: https://www.revk.uk/2026/03/late-payments.html
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Ask HN (May 2026) — 39 points, 50 comments
- **Engagement**: Active discussion; the OP explicitly described building a B2B tool for this pain
- **Revenue Data**: N/A (demand signal only)
- **Boring Business Score**: 4/5
- **Target Industry**: Small B2B service businesses: trades, agencies, freelancers, cleaning companies, property maintenance
- **Core Value Prop**: Overdue invoice follow-up is universally manual and awkward. QB/Xero auto-reminders fail. WhatsApp works (gets read, gets replies) but nobody does it consistently. A tool that automates personalized, multi-channel invoice follow-up (email → SMS → WhatsApp) with smart escalation and relationship-sensitive tone would solve a real daily pain.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: No tool squarely targets the WhatsApp follow-up flow that actually works. Existing AR tools (Melio, Chaser, YayPay) target mid-market. Small tradespeople and service businesses (plumbers, electricians, cleaning companies) don't know these tools exist and wouldn't pay enterprise pricing anyway. Need: dead-simple mobile app + WhatsApp Business API integration.
- **Our Angle**: "Invoice Sheriff" — automated overdue invoice follow-up via WhatsApp + SMS for trades/home services. Connect to QB/Xero to read invoices, trigger escalating friendly reminders, stop service access after X days. Target $29–49/mo or flat LTD for solo operators.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — clear ROI (one recovered invoice pays for lifetime), simple enough for AppSumo. Risk: WhatsApp Business API complexity.

## 10. Centori — Software-Enabled Service Hybrid ($14k MRR)

- **Source**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/services/from-failed-saas-to-14k-mrr-software-enabled-service-BsxsALYvpfriOYumgU0l
- **Additional Links**: https://centori.io/
- **Platform**: Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Founder interview (November 2025)
- **Engagement**: 53 upvotes, 8 bookmarks
- **Revenue Data**: $14k MRR, solo founder
- **Boring Business Score**: 2/5 (SEO/content marketing — not a hard boring industry)
- **Target Industry**: Small B2B companies needing SEO/content marketing
- **Core Value Prop**: SEO + content writing service, priced as productized packages. Founder discovered "no founder wants to do SEO themselves and most had bad experiences with agencies." Pivoted from pure SaaS to service + software.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The "software-enabled service" model is directly applicable to boring industries — e.g., "HVAC software + done-for-you customer review management" or "pest control software + AI-drafted customer follow-up emails." The service layer reduces churn (switching cost = losing your accumulated data + the person who knows your operations).
- **Our Angle**: When building boring-business SaaS, consider bundling a done-for-you service tier (e.g., $299/mo = software + "we set up your scheduling and send your invoices for the first month"). Reduces abandonment, accelerates time-to-value, creates upsell.
- **LTD Potential**: 2/5 (service component makes LTD hard to structure profitably)
