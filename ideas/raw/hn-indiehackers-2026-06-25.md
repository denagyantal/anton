## Key Themes

**1. The ServiceTitan pricing gap is real and getting bigger.** At $245+/tech/month, ServiceTitan prices out 90% of HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors (who average 2–5 techs). The budget alternatives (Jobber, Housecall Pro) leave specific high-value features unaddressed: service agreement automation, maintenance contract management, and granular job profitability reporting. Every new entrant in this space has a structural tailwind.

**2. AI voice receptionists for trades are at the exact right moment.** The technology is ready (Vapi, ElevenLabs), the market is proven (Indie Hackers model showing $300–800 MRR per client at 80% margins), and the target customers (HVAC/dental/plumbing) are actively losing money on missed calls. A domain-specific AI receptionist for a single vertical could build a $500K–2M ARR business in 18 months with minimal competition.

**3. PE roll-ups are creating a new software buying category.** The Apex Service Partners $10B valuation / Apollo $2B investment signals massive capital flowing into home services consolidation. Roll-up management companies need software that doesn't exist: multi-brand operations dashboards, cross-location price book management, technician performance benchmarking. This is a mid-market ($2K–5K/month) opportunity with very little competition.

**4. Compliance software for boring industries is a recurring playbook.** Humadroid's $500/month on SOC 2 compliance, extrapolated to trades licensing compliance or small fleet DOT compliance, is a compelling pattern. Regulatory requirements create recurring, non-discretionary software budgets. Trades contractors pay for compliance tools whether times are good or bad.

**5. HN/IH developers rarely build for trades — that's the opportunity.** The most consistent finding across all searches: boring business verticals are almost absent from HN "Show HN" posts and IH side project threads. This is not because the markets are small (HVAC alone is a $185B industry in the US). It's because the developers who build for these markets don't participate in HN/IH culture. That disconnect is the moat — if you're willing to learn the domain, you face almost no competition from the tech-builder community.

**6. Distribution into trades requires non-HN channels.** Facebook groups (HVAC Business Owners, Plumbers & Plumbing, Lawn Care Business Owners), trade association newsletters (ACCA, NATE, OOIDA), and word-of-mouth from industry coaches (BDR, Service Excellence Training) are the actual distribution channels. Reddit and HN are noise for this audience.
