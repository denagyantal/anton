## Summary

**Top 3 Most Compelling Opportunities:**

**1. AI Voice Agents for Home Service Trades** — Avoca AI just raised $125M+ at a $1B valuation (Kleiner Perkins-backed) to handle inbound calls for HVAC, plumbing, and roofing businesses. The validated problem: contractors lose $45K–$125K per year in missed calls. There is clear whitespace at the SMB/indie level — most AI call solutions target $1M+ annual revenue shops, leaving solo operators and smaller shops with no affordable option. A lightweight, $99/month AI receptionist for trades with under $500K revenue would face zero head-on competition from Avoca or Sameday AI.

**2. ServiceTitan Refugee Market** — ServiceTitan's per-technician pricing (~$350+/month/tech) is triggering mass departures in 2026. Contractors are actively hunting for affordable alternatives, with search volume exploding around "ServiceTitan alternatives." The winning wedge is mobile-first simplicity at $49–$149/month flat rate for small shops (1–5 technicians). Incumbents like Jobber and Housecall Pro are mid-market; nobody owns the true small-shop segment with AI features built in.

**3. Vertical AI for Specific Boring Trades** — Rather than generic AI, the category emerging in 2026 is AI purpose-built for a single trade: dental no-show prediction, HVAC parts inventory AI, trucking compliance automation. These tools land fast (trade owners buy on ROI not features), retain well (operationally embedded), and have essentially zero competition from horizontal AI tools. The underserved niches: pest control AI, pool service route optimization AI, independent plumber business management AI.
