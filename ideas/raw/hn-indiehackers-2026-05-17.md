- **Type**: Show HN

## InstaBuild — Invoicing and Payment Reminders for Construction Projects

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44246559
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: Unknown (June 2025)
- **Revenue Data**: No public revenue data
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Construction / general contracting
- **Core Value Prop**: Create professional invoices in seconds, track hours, materials, and costs per project, auto-reminders for payment. Specifically designed for contractors managing subs and material costs.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Construction has the worst cash flow of any industry — contractors routinely wait 60–90 days for payment. The existing tools (QuickBooks, Wave, FreshBooks) are built for accountants, not people who are covered in sawdust. InstaBuild is narrow enough (construction only) to be interesting but they haven't cracked distribution.
- **Our Angle**: Construction-specific invoicing + "pay me now" link via SMS + materials cost tracking. Add a lien waiver generator (legal doc contractors need for every payment) to make this dramatically stickier. Very high willingness to pay: one recovered invoice = $5k+ for the contractor.
- **LTD Potential**: 4/5 — "never chase a payment again" is a very sellable LTD pitch to a contractor audience.

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45906762
- **Additional Links**: https://securvo.com/
- **Platform**: HN
- **Type**: Show HN
- **Engagement**: 2 points, 0 comments (November 12, 2025)
- **Revenue Data**: No public revenue data
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: Service businesses (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, cleaning — any licensed/insured trade)
- **Core Value Prop**: Compliance management for service businesses — tracking contractor licenses, insurance certificates, OSHA certs, bonding documents
- **Gap/Opportunity**: Trades businesses that work with property managers or commercial clients must track compliance docs for themselves and their subs. ## Macro Trend: Blue-Collar AI Adoption Accelerating

- **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44923187
- **Additional Links**: https://www.indiehackers.com/post/2026-saas-market-report-key-insights-95423fc66b
- **Platform**: HN + Indie Hackers
- **Type**: Discussion / Market trend
- **Engagement**: HN thread — "Blue-collar jobs gaining popularity as AI threatens office work"
- **Revenue Data**: N/A (trend signal)
- **Boring Business Score**: 5/5
- **Target Industry**: All blue-collar / trades industries
- **Core Value Prop**: AI is being adopted by trades businesses for back-office work (quoting, scheduling, customer communication, compliance) even as AI can't replace the physical work itself.
- **Gap/Opportunity**: The 2026 IH SaaS Market Report confirms: vertical SaaS + AI agents-as-enhancements (not replacements) is the primary growth vector. Founders from trades backgrounds have an enormous advantage. The field service market is projected to shift from "automation to autonomy" in 2026 (multiple industry sources confirm). Nobody has built a truly agentic AI assistant for a plumber that handles: inbound inquiry → quote → scheduling → reminder → invoice → follow-up review request. This end-to-end AI workflow for a one-person trades business is a massive unsolved problem.
- **Our Angle**: "AI office manager for your trades business." One product that handles customer communication (AI SMS/WhatsApp), quoting (AI estimate from job description), scheduling (calendar management), invoicing (auto-send on job completion), and review generation (auto-send Google review request 2 days after job). $49/mo or $299 LTD.
- **LTD Potential**: 5/5 — highest potential of any finding in this scan. Every plumber, HVAC tech, and electrician is their own admin. This is a pain they feel daily. Direct distribution via Reddit (r/HVAC, r/Plumbing, r/Electricians), Facebook Groups for trades.
