## One-Line Pitch
Turn public building permit data into booked jobs for HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and roofing contractors — automated permit alerts mean your quote arrives before the competition knows the project exists.

## Problem
When a homeowner pulls an HVAC permit, a plumbing permit, or a roofing permit, every contractor in that zip code would pay good money to know about it instantly. But today, that data sits in county permit offices as PDFs and CSV exports that no contractor has time to monitor. Quipli (equipment rental) built this concept for their niche and got featured at Stripe Sessions 2026 — their AI engine "Quinn" monitors new building permits nearby and auto-generates leads for equipment rental dealers. The same pattern works across every trade: HVAC permit pulls → HVAC dealers, electrical permits → electricians, plumbing permits → plumbers, roofing permits → roofers.

**The pain for contractors today:**
- Google LSA leads cost $400-700 per HVAC booked lead
- Facebook ads require creative production and ongoing budget
- Angi/HomeAdvisor leads are shared with 3-5 competitors simultaneously
- Permit data is public but requires human effort to monitor daily → no contractor does it

## Market Evidence
- Quipli "Quinn" AI (permit → equipment rental lead gen) featured at Stripe Sessions 2026 = model validated
- HVAC contractors pay $600/closed lead from Google LSA (HN data) → permit lead at $99/mo = compelling ROI if even 1 job/month is closed
- Building permits are public records in all 50 states; APIs exist for major metros (OpenPermit.com, LADBS, NY Buildings, etc.)
- 500K+ HVAC+plumbing+electrical businesses in the US, the majority spending on lead gen
- Pattern validated in adjacent verticals: AlertMedia for storm damage leads; BuildZoom for contractor discovery

## Key Differentiators
1. **Trade-specific**: Not generic permit alerts — HVAC alerts go to HVAC contractors, plumbing to plumbers
2. **Auto-outreach**: Permit detected → pre-written outreach SMS/email generated, one-click send
3. **Hyper-local**: Contractors define their service radius; only permits in that zone
4. **Multi-trade**: One subscription covers HVAC + plumbing contractors in same shop (common combo)
5. **First-mover**: No direct competitor exists in this specific form

## Build Plan
- **MVP (2-3 weeks)**: Connect to OpenPermit or one city's permit API → email alert to contractor → contractor clicks to see job details
- **Phase 2**: Auto-generated outreach templates + CRM integration (Jobber/HCP webhook)
- **Phase 3**: Multi-city coverage + auto-outreach sequences
- **Pricing**: $99/mo per trade type per market; $149 LTD for one trade in one city (entry offer)
- **Distribution**: Same channels as FSM tools — r/HVAC, HVAC-Talk.com, Facebook "HVAC Business Owners"

## Next Steps
1. Pull one city's HVAC permits for one week and present to 10 HVAC contractors — will they pay $99/mo?
2. Build permit monitoring for one major metro as validation; target Houston or Phoenix (high construction volume, year-round)
3. Partner with a local HVAC supply house as distribution channel

## Risks
1. Permit data quality varies drastically by county — some cities have no API, only PDF downloads
2. Contractors need auto-outreach templates to make this valuable, not just data alerts
3. Timing gap: permits often filed weeks before job starts; window for early outreach is narrow
4. BuildZoom and similar services have permit data; could pivot to this feature
