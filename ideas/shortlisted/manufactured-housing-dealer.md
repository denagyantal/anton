# Manufactured Housing Dealer Management

**Score: 79/105** | **Verdict: EXPLORE FURTHER** | **Evaluation Date: 2026-04-14**

## One-Line Pitch
The only modern cloud SaaS for manufactured housing dealers — lot inventory, buyer CRM, financing calculator, and state DMV paperwork in one place.

## Problem
Manufactured housing dealers (~10,000 in the US) have been abandoned by software vendors. Per a high-signal HN thread ("What Industries Are Underserved by Software?" — 192+ upvotes), dealer management software vendors have "disappeared," leaving operators on legacy desktop software or spreadsheets. A bootstrapped product was mentioned as filling this gap with paying customers — confirming willingness to pay — but no modern SaaS exists.

Dealers manage:
- Lot inventory (new and used manufactured homes, models, floor plans, specs)
- Buyer CRM (lead tracking, financing applications, customer communication)
- Financing calculations (chattel loans, HUD-insured loans, land-home packages)
- State DMV/HUD paperwork (titles, transport permits, HUD data plate tracking)
- Installation coordination (site prep, transport, installation contractors)

None of this is served by generic CRMs or inventory systems — the manufactured housing industry has unique regulatory and operational requirements.

## Market Evidence
- ~10,000 manufactured housing dealers in the US
- Manufactured housing = ~6% of US housing units (10M+ homes); industry growing as affordable housing crisis deepens
- MHCA, TMHA, FMHA = active industry associations with engaged member communities
- HN community specifically called out: "proven paying customers with zero modern competitors" (2020 thread, still valid in 2026)
- Legacy software from vendors that exited the market = stranded customer base actively looking for alternatives
- Bootstrapped incumbents exist but with no modern UX or SaaS delivery model

## Key Differentiators
1. **Zero competition** — first modern SaaS in the category
2. **HUD-specific workflows** — chattel loan tracking, HUD data plate management, title and transport documentation baked in
3. **State DMV paperwork generator** — different titling requirements per state (manufactured homes are titled differently than site-built homes in most states)
4. **Financing options calculator** — dealers need to show buyers Fannie Mae MH Advantage, FHA Title I/II, VA loan, and conventional chattel loan comparisons in one view
5. **Lot map visual** — dealers often have 20-100 homes on their lot; visual inventory browser (floorplan + status + price) replaces spreadsheets

2. **Interview 3 dealers** — understand what DMV/HUD paperwork is most painful; this informs MVP scope
3. **Build MVP**: lot inventory browser + buyer CRM + financing options comparison table + PDF generator for key state forms; $79/mo or $499 LTD
4. **Target TMHA (Texas Manufactured Housing Association)** and similar state associations for initial outreach

## Risks
1. **Small TAM** (~$200M) — lower ceiling than most other opportunities in this pipeline
2. **Offline sales process** — manufactured housing dealers often sell via in-person lot visits; no self-serve path; direct outreach required
3. **State regulatory complexity** — HUD titles and DMV processes vary significantly by state; may need per-state form templates
4. **Long sales cycle** — conservative non-technical buyers; slower to adopt
