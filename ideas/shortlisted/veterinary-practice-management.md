## One-Line Pitch
Modern cloud PIMS for solo vet practices — SOAP notes, Rx labeling, controlled substance log, and same-day support at $99/mo flat — for the 1–3 vet practice that Vetspire and Impromed left behind.

## Problem
Solo and small veterinary practices (1–3 vets) are caught between two bad options:
- **Legacy PIMS** (Impromed, Cornerstone) acquired by Covetrus/IDEXX: support has degraded from same-day phone to multi-day resolution since acquisition; staff unfamiliar with products; prices increased
- **Corporate-focused cloud tools** (Vetspire): priced at $200+/vet/mo, designed for multi-location corporate groups

When Covetrus acquired Impromed, solo vet practices lost same-day phone support. Shepherd (a newer PIMS built by a practicing veterinarian) has validated demand with high satisfaction scores — "support responds in under two minutes" — but has limited availability.

The gap: affordable cloud-native PIMS with SOAP notes, prescription labeling, controlled substance log, inventory tracking, and reliable support — for solo practices under $150/month.

## Target Customer
- Solo and small veterinary practices (1–3 DVMs)
- General practice (dog, cat, some mixed-animal)
- Currently on Impromed/Cornerstone (support degraded) or evaluating Shepherd (waitlisted)
- Annual revenue $300K–$1.5M
- Pain: can't get support when urgent issues arise; paying enterprise prices for a 1-vet practice

## Market Evidence
- ~30,000 private practice veterinary clinics in the US
- Majority are small practices (1–3 DVMs)
- Covetrus acquisition complaints consistent and documented through 2025 Capterra reviews
- Shepherd's rapid rise on satisfaction rankings = unmet demand confirmed
- NectarVet (cloud-native, mid-priced) and Shepherd show market appetite for modern alternatives

## Key Differentiators
1. **Support responds in <2 hours** (vs Impromed's multi-day resolution) — this alone is the switching trigger
2. Species-specific SOAP note templates (dog/cat/exotic/equine) vs generic templates
3. DEA-compliant controlled substance log built-in (not an add-on)
4. Modern cloud interface vs Impromed's dated desktop client
5. Free migration assistance from Impromed/Cornerstone

## Risks
1. **DEA compliance**: controlled substance logs are federally regulated (DEA Form 222, Schedule II documentation); any errors = legal liability
2. SOAP note templates are clinically sensitive; wrong templates = low adoption and churn
3. Shepherd (well-reviewed, "built by a vet") may expand availability and fill this gap before build is complete
4. Low LTD fit limits AppSumo as a channel; must rely on vet community outreach (VIN, associations)
5. Controlled substance log requirements differ by state; federal minimum is high bar

## Next Steps

2. Validate controlled substance log requirements with a vet practice consultant before building
