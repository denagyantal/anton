## One-Line Pitch
Rentman at US small-shop price: equipment rental tracking, crew scheduling, quoting, and invoicing for 1–15 crew AV/staging/lighting production companies — before Rentman notices them.

## Problem
AV rental and event production companies (staging, lighting, broadcast, audio-visual for corporate events) operate with "spreadsheets held together with tape or software built for other industries." The specific pain: tracking which equipment is deployed to which job, crew scheduling across simultaneous events, fast quotes that include equipment + crew + logistics, and invoicing — all in one place.

Rentman solved this at $15–20M ARR, but serves the mid-to-large segment ($500K+ revenue companies, 70+ countries). **The US small-to-mid production company (1–15 crew, $100K–500K revenue) that can't afford Rentman's enterprise tier has no purpose-built tool.**

## Market Evidence

- IH investor: "biggest moats are quietly being built right now in AV, HVAC, dental, and restoration"
- AV/event production = fragmented globally across 70+ countries; horizontal SaaS players bolt on modules but can't match workflow depth
- 15,000+ small AV/staging companies in the US; most under $500K revenue = below Rentman's target

## Key Differentiators
1. **Equipment availability calendar** — real-time view of which gear is deployed, available, or in maintenance
2. **Crew assignment by event** — assign crew with skill/experience matching, track availability conflicts
3. **Fast quoting** — select equipment + crew + logistics → auto-generate client-facing PDF quote in <2 minutes
4. **Mobile crew check-in** — tech scans QR on equipment at load-in, confirms checklist
5. **Flat pricing** — $99/mo for up to 10 crew, no per-equipment fees

## Risks
1. Complex data model (equipment rental with quantity tracking + crew scheduling + job costing = 6–8 week MVP)
2. LTD fit is lower — event companies have recurring revenue and might prefer annual plan over LTD
3. Smaller US TAM than trade verticals (15K AV shops vs. 300K+ FSM shops)
4. Community access harder than trade verticals (no equivalent of r/electricians for AV pros)
