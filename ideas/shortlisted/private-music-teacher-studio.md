**Verdict**: EXPLORE FURTHER → BUILD

## One-Line Pitch
All-in-one scheduling, billing, and parent portal for 250,000+ private music teachers who currently cobble together Calendly + Venmo + text messages.

## Market
- 250,000+ private music teachers in the US
- Teachers charge $50–150/hour = strong willingness to pay
- Market currently fragmented: Jackrabbit Music, Teachworks, and generic tools (Calendly, MusicTeacherHelper) serve parts of the need but nothing dominates
- Existing tools are either too generic (Calendly) or niche with poor UX (Jackrabbit)

## Core Problem
Private music teachers piece together 3-5 disconnected tools:
- Google Calendar for scheduling
- Venmo/PayPal for payment (no invoicing, no payment tracking)
- Text messages for parent communication
- Paper or spreadsheet for practice notes and progress tracking
- No recital or semester management

Specific unmet needs: semester-based billing, makeup lesson tracking, recital management, practice logs shared with parents, method book progress tracking, multi-teacher studio support.

## Key Differentiators
1. **Parent portal** — parents want visibility; practice log + lesson notes visible to parents
2. **Semester-based billing** — music teachers bill by semester, not monthly; no other tool handles this well
3. **Makeup lesson tracking** — teachers manually track makeup credits; huge admin pain
4. **Recital management** — repertoire assignment, program order, seat assignment
5. **Multi-teacher studio support** — small music schools with 3-10 teachers = premium tier

## Target Channels
- Music Teachers National Association (MTNA) — 200K+ members, annual conference
- "Private Music Teacher" Facebook groups (50K+ combined members)
- r/piano, r/musicteachers subreddits
- Music teacher YouTube channels (many have 10K-100K subscribers)
- "Piano Teacher" podcast community

## Pricing Strategy
- Solo teacher: $15/month or $99 LTD
- Small studio (up to 5 teachers): $49/month or $199 LTD
- School/large studio (5+ teachers): $99/month (subscription only)

## Top 3 Risks
1. Jackrabbit Music and Teachworks may be better than research suggests — need hands-on testing
2. Free tier competition (Calendly, Google Calendar) — teachers are price-sensitive
3. Market may prefer $15/month subscription over LTD — validate before investing in AppSumo launch
