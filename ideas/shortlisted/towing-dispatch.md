## One-Line Pitch
Mobile-first towing dispatch for independent operators — one-tap job creation, vehicle photo documentation, and instant invoicing designed for dispatching from the truck cab.

## Problem
Independent tow truck operators (1-5 trucks) make up the majority of the 35,000+ US towing companies ($11B industry). The market leader Towbook is desktop-first — "freezes up requiring a computer reset" and "runs slowly during month-end billing." Traxero (enterprise) and Dispatch Anywhere (slow updates) are priced or designed for large fleets. TraxeroGO is free but intentionally limited to funnel into paid Traxero. The fundamental disconnect: tow truck drivers live on their phones and dispatch themselves from the truck cab, but existing tools were designed for desktop dispatchers in an office. Independent operators use phone calls and texts for dispatch, paper logs for records, personal camera roll for vehicle photos, and QuickBooks for invoicing — a scattered workflow that loses time and creates liability exposure.

## Market Evidence
- $11B US towing industry; 35K+ towing companies
- Towbook dominates affordable tier (~$49/mo for 250 calls) but has documented performance issues
- Traxero merged TOPS + VTS — targeting large fleets, leaving independents behind
- Towing FB groups (67K+ combined) are very active; Tow Times Magazine for industry reach
- Motor club integrations (AAA, Agero) are important but mostly locked behind enterprise pricing
- Vehicle condition photo documentation is critical for liability — no affordable tool handles this

## Key Differentiators
1. $35/mo flat — all features, up to 5 drivers
2. True mobile-first — designed for dispatching from the truck cab, not a desktop
3. Vehicle condition photo documentation with timestamps for liability protection
4. One-tap invoicing with instant payment processing (card + cash)
5. Impound lot management with automated storage fee tracking
6. No contracts — month-to-month

## Next Steps

3. Niche TAM (35K companies) limits ceiling
4. Impound management adds regulatory complexity that varies by jurisdiction
5. Towing is 24/7 — support expectations may be high
