## Team Fit (4 co-founders)
- **Front-end**: Branded client portal, document upload UX, checklist interface, e-signature flow
- **Back-end**: File storage, automated reminders, e-signature integration, notification engine
- **Designer**: Client-facing portal must be dead-simple (clients are non-technical), professional branding
- **Generalist**: Tax season launch timing (Nov-Dec), CPA community outreach, comparison content

## Key Differentiators
1. **Supplement, not replace** — works alongside existing tax software (Drake, Lacerte, UltraTax)
2. **Simple pricing** — $79-99 one-time vs $30-100/user/mo for SafeSend/Liscio
3. **Client-friendly UX** — clients must actually use it; most portals are too confusing
4. **Automated follow-up** — "still need W-2 from employer X" reminders reduce manual chasing
5. **Branded portal** — firm's logo/colors, not generic software branding

## MVP Feature Set
1. Branded client portal (firm logo, colors, custom domain)
2. Document upload with per-client checklist (W-2, 1099, mortgage interest, etc.)
3. Automated email/SMS reminders for missing documents
4. Document status tracking dashboard (submitted, reviewed, needs correction)
5. E-signature integration for engagement letters and 8879
6. Secure messaging between firm and client
7. Tax year templates (pre-built checklists for 1040, 1065, 1120S, etc.)

## LTD Pricing Strategy
- **Tier 1**: $79 LTD — up to 50 clients
- **Tier 2**: $99 LTD — unlimited clients, custom domain
- **Post-LTD**: $99/year (up to 50 clients) / $199/year (unlimited clients)
- Note: Annual pricing is more natural than monthly for seasonal usage

## Launch Timing
- **Critical**: Launch in October-November for maximum urgency
- Tax pros prepare for season in October-December
- January is too late — they're already in the trenches
- Post-April follow-up campaign for extension-heavy firms

## Next Steps
1. Build MVP: branded portal + checklist + upload + automated reminders + e-signature
2. Launch before tax season (target October-November 2026)
3. Position as add-on to existing tax software (not replacement)
4. Seed r/taxpros and accounting FB groups with "built for tax season sanity" positioning

## Risks
1. Seasonal demand — 70%+ of usage concentrated in Jan-April
2. Some tax software suites (Drake, Lacerte) adding portals as features
3. Client adoption friction — clients need to be convinced to use a portal vs just emailing
4. TaxDome is expanding rapidly and may dominate all-in-one space
