# Small Landlord Property Management

## One-Line Summary
All-in-one rent collection, maintenance tracking, and tenant communication for 1-10 unit landlords — $15/mo vs Buildium's $58/mo minimum.

## Problem
"Accidental landlords" (1-4 units, often inherited or investment properties) are massively underserved. They don't need enterprise property management — they need: online rent collection, maintenance request tracking, tenant communication log, and basic lease/document storage. Buildium starts at $58/mo for a minimum of 20 units. Cozy was killed by CoStar. Nothing affordable remains.

## Target Customer
- 1-10 unit landlords (the "accidental landlord" — bought a second home, inherited a property, bought a duplex)
- Currently using Venmo/Zelle for rent, texting tenants, paper lease files
- Pain: No paper trail, no automated late notices, tenants text at midnight about leaky faucets
- NOT property management companies (those use AppFolio/Buildium)

**Gap**: $15-$29/mo flat for 1-10 units with real rent collection + maintenance tracking

## Product Concept
- **Online rent collection** — ACH + card, auto-late fees, payment history
- **Maintenance request portal** — tenant submits with photo, landlord approves/assigns/tracks
- **Tenant communication log** — all SMS/email in one thread, timestamped
- **Lease storage + renewal reminders** — upload lease, auto-alert 60 days before expiry
- **Basic income/expense tracking** — for Schedule E at tax time
- **Pricing**: $15/mo (1-4 units), $29/mo (5-10 units), $59 LTD for 1-4 units on AppSumo

## Revenue Model
- $20/mo avg × 500 landlords = $10,000 MRR / $120K ARR
- LTD at $59 (1-4 units): AppSumo launch = 1,000 units = $59,000 upfront
- ACH transaction fee upsell: $1/transaction = additional revenue at scale

## Decision
