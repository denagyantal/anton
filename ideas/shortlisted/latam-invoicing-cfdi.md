## One-Line Pitch
Spanish-language invoicing and CRM built natively for CFDI (Mexico), DIAN (Colombia), and NF-e (Brazil) tax compliance — the regulatory moat that machine-translated US tools cannot cross.

## Market
- 10M+ SMBs in Mexico requiring CFDI compliance (legal mandate since 2013, enforcement tightened 2022-2026)
- A Mexican plumber, accountant, or contractor legally cannot use QuickBooks — it doesn't generate CFDI-compliant invoices
- Superframeworks rates this Gap Score 10/10 — their highest-rated niche
- $8.5B global SMB SaaS localization opportunity
- Near-zero churn once adopted: switching invoicing software means migrating all tax records

## Core Problem
LATAM SMBs are caught between two bad options:
1. **US SaaS tools** — machine-translated, don't understand local tax codes, can't generate legally compliant invoices (CFDI in Mexico requires digital signature via SAT)
2. **Local tools** — often poorly built, desktop-only, expensive, or focused on large enterprises

Specific regulatory requirements:
- **Mexico CFDI**: Digital invoice must be certified through SAT (tax authority) API in real time; requires RFC (tax ID), digital certificate, and official XML format; mandatory for all businesses since 2022
- **Colombia DIAN**: Similar electronic invoice mandate
- **Brazil NF-e**: Most complex; item-level tax codes required

The regulatory moat means that once a LATAM business builds their invoicing workflow in your tool, switching costs are enormous.

## Key Differentiators
1. **Native CFDI generation** — real-time SAT API integration; auto-generates compliant XML; no manual process
2. **Local payment gateways** — Mercado Pago, OXXO pay, SPEI bank transfers; customers pay how they actually pay in Mexico
3. **Spanish-first UX** — not translated, not adapted; built in Spanish with LATAM workflow norms
4. **RFC/tax ID management** — customer tax ID management + CFDI recipient configuration built into client contacts
5. **Accounting firm integration** — Mexican accountants (contadores) are key distribution channel; partner program

- Contador (accountant) networks — they recommend invoicing tools to clients

## Top 3 Risks
1. **SAT API complexity** — Mexico's tax authority API is notoriously unreliable and changes frequently; requires ongoing maintenance and specialized legal/accounting knowledge
2. **Team composition** — requires Spanish fluency, Mexico residency or strong local presence, and tax domain expertise; hard for US/EU team to execute alone
3. **GTM motion is fundamentally different** — accountant channel, WhatsApp communities, local events; AppSumo/Product Hunt don't apply; requires LATAM-specific playbook
