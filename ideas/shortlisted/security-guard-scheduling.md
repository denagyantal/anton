## One-Line Pitch
Purpose-built scheduling and compliance platform for small security guard agencies (10–75 guards) — digital post orders, QR patrol checkpoints, and incident reporting at $99/month, replacing the Connecteam + paper stack that smaller agencies currently cobble together.

## Problem
Security guard companies face a uniquely difficult workforce scheduling challenge: non-desk workers with split shifts, overnight rotations, last-minute callout replacements, and mandatory post coverage (a site can never go unstaffed).

Enterprise tools (TrackTik, Trackforce Valiant) start at $500–$2,000+/month and target agencies with 200+ guards. Generic scheduling tools (Connecteam, $35–$99/mo) handle scheduling but completely lack security-specific features:

1. **Post orders** — each site has a binder of instructions, emergency contacts, and procedures guards must review before their shift. Paper binders get lost; guards show up unprepared.
2. **Guard tour checkpoints** — guards scan NFC/QR codes at regular intervals to prove patrol rounds were completed. Without this, clients have no proof of service.
3. **Incident report logging** — every security incident must be documented with timestamp for client/liability reporting. Paper forms create liability exposure.
4. **Overtime compliance alerts** — many security companies operate in states with complex OT rules; dispatch scrambles to fill posts without triggering premium pay.

Small agencies (10–50 guards, 5–15 client sites) currently use Connecteam for scheduling + paper post orders + phone calls for callouts + paper incident forms — a fragmented stack that creates compliance exposure.

## Key Differentiators
1. **Digital post orders** — PDF per site, guards confirm read before clocking in (proves compliance)
2. **QR/NFC patrol checkpoints** — auto-generates patrol report for client after each shift
3. **Mobile incident reports** — photos + timestamp + instant notification to supervisor
4. **Open shift pool** — guards self-assign from app, reducing dispatcher workload
5. **Overtime alerts** — flag before scheduling would breach threshold
