## One-Line Pitch
Digital food safety checklists, temperature logs, and HACCP documentation that auto-generate an inspection-ready PDF — for independent restaurants and food trucks that can't afford Jolt ($200+/mo).

## Problem
Independent restaurants and food trucks still use paper logs or spreadsheets for health code compliance. Digital food safety tools (Jolt, FoodDocs) are priced for chains at $200+/month. FDA violations average $12,000 in fines for documentation failures. The compliance obligation is the same whether you're a 3-location chain or a solo food truck — but the tools only serve the chain.

Health inspectors walk in and see clipboards. A simple digital compliance app with daily logs, temperature records, and a one-tap "export for inspector" button would be a no-brainer purchase at $49/month or $499 one-time.

## Market Evidence
- FDA documentation violation fines: avg $12,000 per incident
- Jolt (chain-focused): $200+/month — complete pricing overkill for independent operators
- FoodDocs: enterprise pricing, heavy implementation
- Independent restaurants: 300K+ in the US; food trucks: 35K+
- Health inspectors are a grassroots distribution channel — they see the paper chaos on every visit
- Restaurant supply companies as partner channel for distribution

## MVP Scope (2–3 weeks)

1. **Daily Temperature Logs** — fridge/freezer temps with pass/fail thresholds; auto-flag violations
2. **Opening/Closing Checklists** — customizable per restaurant type; digital sign-off with timestamp
3. **Pest Control Log** — date, contractor, findings, next visit due
4. **HACCP Documentation** — critical control points, corrective actions, verification records
5. **Inspector Export** — one-tap PDF with all logs organized by date, ready for walk-in inspection
6. **Multi-User** — owner + manager + shift leads can all log from their phones

## Go-to-Market
1. AppSumo LTD launch at $499 (one location) / $799 (up to 3 locations)
2. Food handler certification course providers as distribution partners (they reach exactly the right buyers)
3. Restaurant supply companies (Sysco, US Foods local reps talk to operators daily)
4. SEO: "never fail a health inspection", "restaurant food safety app", "HACCP software small restaurant"
5. Health inspector word-of-mouth (tell inspectors to recommend it when they see clipboards)

## Key Differentiators
1. **One-tap inspector export** — organized PDF ready for any walk-in visit
2. **No enterprise setup** — live in 5 minutes, no consultant needed
3. **$49/month or $499 LTD** — vs Jolt's $200+/month chain pricing
4. **Food truck mode** — mobile-first for operators without a fixed location

## Risks
1. Health inspection software is unsexy for paid acquisition; organic/word-of-mouth GTM required
2. Restaurant churn is high (restaurants close) — retention is lower than B2B SaaS
3. Food trucks vs restaurants have meaningfully different workflows
4. Multi-location restaurant chains (the higher-value customer) already have Jolt
