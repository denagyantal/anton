## One-Line Pitch
Purpose-built booking and client management for independent fishing charter captains who currently use FareHarbor (built for tours, wrong UX) or paper ledgers.

## Market
- Multi-billion dollar US charter fishing industry
- 10,000+ independent charter captains (fresh and saltwater)
- Captains charge $300–2,000+ per trip; strong willingness to pay for tools
- FareHarbor/Checkfront validate the general tour booking market; no dedicated fishing tool dominant
- Superframeworks identified as "pure founder blind spot" — captains "do not read Hacker News"

## Core Problem
Independent charter captains have hyper-specific needs that generic tour booking tools ignore:
- Half-day vs. full-day vs. overnight trip types (different pricing structures)
- Headcount-based pricing (cost per person, group minimums)
- Tide/weather-based rescheduling (critical for safety and satisfaction)
- Per-trip catch logs to share with clients (marketing tool + memory)
- Fishing license verification for guests (legal requirement)
- Equipment maintenance reminders (life safety)

Current workarounds: FareHarbor (wrong UX), paper ledgers, spreadsheets + personal phone.

## Key Differentiators
1. **Catch log + photo sharing** — clients receive a shareable "trip recap" with catch photos; built-in viral loop (they share on social media, driving referrals)
2. **Weather API integration** — smart rescheduling suggestions based on forecast + tide charts; reduces admin when weather forces cancellation
3. **Fishing license verification** — remind guests to bring licenses; log verification at check-in (legal requirement many captains manually track)
4. **Half-day / full-day / overnight trip types** — pricing and duration built-in, unlike generic tour booking
5. **Equipment maintenance reminders** — USCG compliance; boat service logs + safety equipment expiry

## Pricing Strategy
- Solo captain: $39/month or $99 LTD (per boat)
- Small fleet (2-5 boats): $79/month or $199 LTD
- Processing fee: 1-2% on deposits collected through platform
