**Verdict**: BUILD

## One-Line Pitch
Route-aware mobile mechanic app with customer booking, clustered daily scheduling, digital inspection reports, and Stripe invoice+tip at $49/mo — built for the mechanic who works out of a van, not a shop.

## Problem
Mobile mechanics (1-person operations working from a van or trailer) use a patchwork of Google Maps for routing, Square for payment, and text messages for scheduling. All existing auto repair shop management software (Mitchell1, Tekmetric, Shop-Ware) is built around a service bay workflow: labor guides, lift-based jobs, parts room inventory. Mobile mechanics don't have a bay, don't need inventory management for a parts room, and need route-clustered scheduling to minimize drive time between residential jobs. No purpose-built mobile mechanic tool exists at any price point.

## Market Evidence
- Mobile mechanic market projected $23.1B by 2033 — fastest-growing auto service segment
- 150K+ independent mobile mechanics in the US; majority solo operators
- Tekmetric, Mitchell1, Shop-Ware: $50–250/mo, built for shop bays (overkill for mobile)
- YourMechanic, Wrench: marketplace model, not a tools product
- r/AutoMechanic: mechanics consistently cite "shop software doesn't work for me" — service bay features never used
- Build feasibility 5/5: no ELD certification, no compliance complexity — pure booking + routing + inspection + payment

## Key Differentiators
1. Route-clustered daily schedule — groups nearby jobs to minimize drive time (like Uber route batching)
2. Mobile-first digital inspection: photo capture at job site, markups, share to customer via link
3. Customer booking portal with vehicle history (year/make/model/mileage per customer vehicle)
4. Pre-authorize cards at booking; auto-charge on job completion (no chasing payment)
5. No service bay features: no parts room inventory, no lift scheduling, no shop overhead
