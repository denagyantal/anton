## One-Line Pitch

Self-managed delivery platform for independent local food stores — online order intake, route optimization, and customer SMS tracking link without Uber Eats 15-30% commission or Shopify+RouteXL fragmentation.

## Problem

Independent neighborhood grocery stores, specialty food stores (butchers, fishmongers, ethnic grocers), and local farm CSA operations offering their own delivery service have no affordable tool for:
- Online order intake integrated with their inventory
- Delivery schedule management
- Route optimization for 10-50 daily deliveries
- Customer notification ("your order is out for delivery")

The COVID-era surge created a lasting segment of small local food businesses managing delivery via spreadsheet + phone + Google Maps. Third-party platforms (Uber Eats, DoorDash) charge 15-30% commissions that eliminate all margin for food businesses with 10-30% gross margins. Building a custom store + own delivery on Shopify ($29/mo) + RouteXL ($14/mo) + custom SMS costs $100+/month in subscriptions with zero integration.

## Market Evidence

## Target Market

- Independent neighborhood grocery stores, specialty food stores (butchers, fishmongers, ethnic grocers)
- Local farm CSA operations with weekly delivery box subscriptions
- Small food businesses doing 10-50 daily deliveries
- Estimated 50,000-100,000 operators in the US

## Potential Approach

Single platform:
- Product catalog (embeddable web widget on existing site)
- Customer orders online
- Route optimization for the day's deliveries
- Driver app (GPS navigation, mark delivered, collect signature)
- Customer SMS tracking link
- $29/mo for up to 100 orders/month; $49/mo unlimited

## Key Risks

1. Existing Shopify + RouteXL patchwork is functional enough for most operators
2. Very fragmented target market (no single community to reach them)
3. Uber Eats/DoorDash competition is entrenched for demand generation (though self-delivery is about margin preservation)
4. Small per-operator revenue; need many customers for meaningful MRR
