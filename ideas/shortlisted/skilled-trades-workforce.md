## One-Line Pitch
Apprenticeship progression tracker + subcontractor credentialing for electrical contractors — purpose-built for the 439,000-worker shortage driven by the AI data center construction boom.

## Problem
The AI data center construction boom has created a structural workforce crisis: demand for skilled trades is growing 3x faster than professional roles (Randstad 2026), and the construction industry faces a 439,000-worker shortage. Electricians on data center projects in Texas are earning $240K-$280K/year. BlackRock launched a $100M initiative to train 50,000 Americans in trades (March 2026).

Electrical contractors scaling from 20 to 200 workers for data center projects have no purpose-built software to:
- Track apprenticeship progression (hours logged → journeyman → master — IBEW/JATC requirements)
- Manage subcontractor credentialing (license type, expiry, insurance certificates)
- Onboard new crew members with safety certifications tracked
- Alert when CDLs, OSHA certs, or specialized licenses expire

Existing tools: Workyard and Connecteam are horizontal (not trades-specific, no IBEW/JATC awareness). Granum/Greenius is the only vertical model — built for landscaping crew onboarding, not electrical.

## MVP Scope (4-6 weeks)
1. Crew member profiles: name, role (apprentice/journeyman/master), employer, hire date
2. Certification tracker: CDL, OSHA 10/30, state license, insurance cert — with expiry alerts
3. Apprenticeship progression log: hours worked toward journeyman milestone
4. Email/SMS alerts: 30/60/90 days before cert expiry
5. GC dashboard: view all subcontractor credential status in one table
6. PDF export: credentialing report for bid compliance

## Key Differentiators
1. **IBEW/JATC-aware** — apprenticeship hour milestones built-in
2. **Subcontractor credentialing** — GC-facing view of all subs' cert status
3. **Expiry alerts** — prevent OSHA violations before they happen
4. **Data center focus** — marketing specifically to the AI infrastructure boom

## Go-to-Market
1. IBEW local union forums and contractor associations (NECA)
2. Data center general contractor outreach (credentialing compliance angle)
3. LinkedIn electrician/HVAC contractor groups

## Risks
1. Long B2B sales cycle (HR/ops decision, not owner-operator)
2. IBEW/JATC requirements vary by local union — data complexity
3. Data center boom is a macro trend — could cool if AI capex pulls back
4. Larger HR platforms (Workyard, Connecteam) could add vertical features
