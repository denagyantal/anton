# HOA & Community Self-Management Platform

**Score**: 84/105

## One-Line Pitch
Free-tier HOA platform for self-managed communities — dues collection, financial dashboards, maintenance requests, and document library replacing Google Drive + Venmo + email chains.

## Problem
60% of US HOAs (222K+ communities) are self-managed by volunteer board members with no professional management company. These boards struggle with financial tracking, maintenance requests, document management, and owner communication. When board members turn over, they take the Google Drive access with them. Existing platforms (AppFolio $1.40-3/unit/mo, Buildium $55-174/mo, Condo Control $1.25+/unit/mo) are designed for professional management companies, not volunteer boards.

## Market Evidence
- 370,000+ HOAs in US, 60% self-managed
- 74 million Americans live in HOA communities
- HOA annual budgets $50K-500K — real money for operational tools
- Board member turnover creates recurring pain AND built-in referral cycle
- Current workaround: Google Drive for documents, Venmo for dues, email chains for communication, spreadsheets for accounting

## Must-Have Filters
- [x] Problem is real (board turnover chaos, lost financials)
- [x] Can build without deep domain expertise
- [x] No dominant player for self-managed HOAs
- [x] Revenue potential > $10K MRR within 12 months (transaction fees on 200+ communities)

## Key Differentiators
1. Free for under 50 units — paid platforms start at $1.25+/unit/mo
2. Revenue from payment processing fees (2.5%) on dues collection, not per-unit subscription
3. Board turnover-proof — platform survives member changes (unlike Google Drive access)
4. Auto-generated treasurer reports for board meetings

## Next Steps
1. Build dues collection (ACH/credit card) + financial dashboard + document library
2. Add maintenance request portal + owner directory
3. Target self-managed HOA FB groups and r/HOA

## Risks
1. Volunteer boards are notoriously hard to sell to — decision-making is slow
2. Low willingness to pay from community budgets
3. Free tools (Google Drive + Venmo) are "good enough" for many boards
4. Board turnover means constant re-education on the platform
