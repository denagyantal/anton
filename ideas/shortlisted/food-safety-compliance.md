## One-Line Pitch
AI-generated HACCP plan + daily digital food safety checklists for single-location restaurants at $19–49/mo — the affordable FoodDocs alternative before FSMA 204 enforcement lands.

## Problem
FSMA 204 (Food Safety Modernization Act traceability rule) is forcing food businesses to implement "one step forward, one step back" traceability records. Small restaurants still mostly use:
- Paper temperature logs (3-ring binders that inspectors flip through)
- Manual daily checklists on clipboards
- Verbal handoffs on food storage and rotation

FoodDocs uses AI to auto-generate HACCP plans — but starts at $169/mo, out of reach for single-location operations. Multi-location chains have enterprise tools; the gap is independent restaurants with 1–5 locations ($300K–$2M revenue).

**The compliance urgency**: A failed health inspection = immediate closure and local media coverage. License revocation is an existential business risk. "Peace of mind" is a powerful purchase driver.

## Market Evidence
- ~1M+ independent restaurants in the US; 70% are single-location
- FSMA 204 traceability rule enforcement creates government-mandated adoption timeline
- FoodDocs starts at $169/mo = clear price gap for small operators
- Jolt, Operandio, DataHex all exist but target multi-location or enterprise
- Spanish-language kitchens: large unserved demographic (many independent restaurants operate with non-English-speaking kitchen staff)

## Product Concept (MVP: 3–4 weeks)

**Core features:**
1. **AI HACCP plan generator**: Input food type + menu + location → AI generates complete HACCP plan with critical control points, corrective actions, and record-keeping requirements
2. **Daily digital checklist**: Customizable daily food safety checklist (temperature checks, storage, cross-contamination, sanitation) with timestamp + employee signature
3. **Temperature log**: Digital log with alerts when readings fall outside safe ranges (integrates with manual entry or digital thermometer)
4. **Inspector export**: One-click PDF export of all compliance records for health inspector review
5. **FSMA 204 traceability**: "One step forward, one step back" supplier/receiver tracking for food lots

**Phase 2:**
- Spanish-language interface (full EN/ES toggle)
- Photo evidence capture for compliance records
- Multi-location management
