## One-Line Pitch
Ekos costs $700+/month and is clunky — 5,000+ nano- and micro-breweries need TTB-compliant batch tracking and inventory management at $299-499 one-time.

## Problem
Craft breweries have complex inventory that standard tools can't model: raw ingredients (grain, hops, yeast, adjuncts) → wort → fermented beer → kegs/cans/bottles. Each batch must be tracked for TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau) reporting — a federal requirement. The TTB requires Brewer's Reports of Operations (monthly or quarterly) documenting production by product class, materials used, and taxes owed.

Nearly 10,000 US breweries, mostly under 10 employees, are paying $500-2,000+/month for legacy brewery management software (OrchestratedBEER, Ekos) that's clunky and expensive. The 5,000+ nano- and micro-breweries (producing under 1,000 barrels/year) are currently on spreadsheets, QuickBooks hacks, and manual TTB reports.

Breweries also lose ~$12,000/year on average to poor inventory management — over-purchasing ingredients, waste from poor tracking, lost yield from imprecise recipe scaling.

## Key Differentiators
1. **TTB Brewer's Report of Operations** — auto-generate federal compliance reports from batch records
2. **Batch tracking through production stages** — grain → wort → fermented → packaged (kegs/cans/bottles)
3. **Ingredient yield tracking** — compare actual vs expected yield per batch; flag waste and losses
4. **Taproom pour log** — draught beer consumed on-premise must be tracked separately for TTB
5. **Recipe scaling** — scale recipes to any batch size with auto-calculated ingredient adjustments
6. **$299-499 LTD vs $8,400/yr Ekos** — pitch writes itself

## MVP Scope (5-6 weeks)
1. Recipe management (ingredients, process steps, expected OG/FG)
2. Batch record creation (link recipe, actual ingredients used, dates, tanks)
3. Production stage tracker (brew day → fermentation → conditioning → packaging)
4. TTB Brewer's Report auto-fill (production by class, materials used, tax-paid removals)
5. Ingredient inventory with reorder alerts
6. Basic taproom pour tracking (firkin/keg taps consumed on-premise)
