## One-Line Pitch
All-in-one church management at $49–$99/mo vs. Planning Center's expensive per-module pricing — built for the 300,000+ small US churches that still run on spreadsheets and email blasts.

## Problem
Planning Center charges per-module and gets expensive fast for small churches. Breeze was acquired. Rock RMS is free/open-source but requires IT expertise. The average US church has ~75 members and operates on a tight budget with volunteer admin staff — they need simple + affordable, not enterprise. 13% of US churches still use no software at all.

## Market Evidence
- 300,000+ churches in the US (majority under 200 members)
- The Church Co. (indie competitor) reached $25K MRR — confirms bootstrapped path is viable
- Planning Center is the dominant modern player but per-module pricing creates clear gaps
- Pushpay, Breeze (acquired), Rock RMS (open-source) prove the market

## Key Differentiators
1. **All-in-one at flat pricing** — $49-99/mo covers member database + attendance + giving + volunteer scheduling + events + communication, vs. Planning Center's per-module pricing
2. **Built-in online giving** — take a small processing cut (1% + standard processing) vs. Pushpay's high fees
3. **LTD-friendly for budget cycle** — one-time payment for churches that get annual budget approval
4. **15-minute setup** — target churches run by volunteers with no IT staff

## Target Customer
