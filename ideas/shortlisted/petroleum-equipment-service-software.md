## One-Line Pitch

Compliance-first field service management for petroleum equipment inspectors and service companies — digital EPA inspection reports, mandatory re-inspection scheduling, and regulatory documentation that currently lives in paper binders.

## Problem

Petroleum equipment service companies manage: tank inspection schedules (EPA/state UST compliance), service work orders, technician dispatch, regulatory documentation, and environmental violation tracking. All of this is done on paper or generic spreadsheets. An HN commenter confirmed: "Bought a business in the petroleum equipment service space. Building internal tools for ourselves. Pen and paper still dominates the industry."

This is a classic insider-validates-gap scenario. The industry is:
- **Regulated by multiple layers**: EPA Underground Storage Tank (UST) regulations, state environmental agencies, local fire marshals, and OSHA all require documentation of tank inspections, leak detection tests, and equipment servicing.
- **Service schedules are mandatory**: UST regulations require periodic inspection and testing (annual, every 3 years, etc.). Missing a schedule = fines + facility shut-down. This creates built-in recurring service contracts.
- **No viable software exists**: Legacy enterprise ERP vendors ignore this niche. The only tooling is generic FSM (Jobber, ServiceFusion) that doesn't know an ATG from a spill bucket.

## Market Evidence

- Heavily regulated = mandatory recurring service contracts = built-in recurring revenue for software
- EPA compliance documentation is a legal requirement — pain that creates urgency and high WTP
- No prominent indie/bootstrapped software solution in the market (confirmed by research)
- Adjacent regulated compliance verticals (fire suppression inspection, elevator inspection, commercial kitchen exhaust) follow the same pattern
- PEI (Petroleum Equipment Institute) is the industry association — clear conference + newsletter distribution channel
- HN comment thread March 2026: Buyer confirming pen-and-paper dominates = first-party validation

## Key Differentiators

1. **EPA UST compliance templates** — pre-built digital inspection forms matching federal/state requirements; auto-generates compliance certificates for customer download
2. **Mandatory re-inspection calendar** — auto-schedules required annual/tri-annual inspections; sends 60/30/7 day warnings to customers
3. **Technician certification tracking** — monitors API and state license expiration dates for each technician
4. **Environmental violation incident log** — structured documentation if a spill or leak is detected; generates the required EPA incident report
5. **Customer compliance portal** — each gas station / fleet owner can log in and see their equipment's compliance status + download certificates

## Top 3 Risks

3. **Long sales cycle** — regulated buyers are risk-averse and slow to adopt new software; internal tooling bias (HN commenter built his own)
