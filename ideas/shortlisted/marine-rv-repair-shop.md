## One-Line Pitch
The Fullbay for marine and RV repair shops — modern work orders, DVI, and customer texting for 12,000+ specialty shops still running on whiteboards and QuickBooks.

## Problem
Marine repair shops and RV repair facilities share the same operational pain as independent auto repair shops, with zero purpose-built software. Fullbay (commercial trucking repair) was acquired for $167M after serving heavy-duty shops — the light-duty and specialty repair verticals (RV, marine, motorcycle, farm equipment) are completely unaddressed by modern SaaS. Every RV repair shop and marine service center runs on a combination of paper work orders, whiteboards, QuickBooks, and phone calls. The same pricing dead zone that existed in auto repair ($0 or $179+/mo, nothing in between) exists here, except there isn't even a $179/mo option.

- **RV repair shops**: ~12,000 in the US; growing as used RV market expands (21% growth post-COVID)
- **Marine repair shops**: ~10,000+ in the US coastal and lake regions
- **Current state**: No specialty shop management software exists for this vertical; all use generic auto repair tools that lack specialty-specific features (RV slide-out diagnostics, marine engine hours tracking, winterization checklists, warranty claim tracking)
- **Validated analog**: Fullbay ($167M acquisition) = trucking; AutoFluent = auto; nobody for marine/RV

## Market Evidence
- Fullbay case study at Stripe Sessions 2026: embedded payments drove $4,200 incremental ARR per customer = payments are the monetization key, not just software
- Tekmetric/Shopmonkey serve auto repair but explicitly do not support RV or marine job types (no equipment hour tracking, no winterization templates)
- RV/marine shops are seasonal-revenue businesses — complex deposits, partial payments, and warranty claims that generic invoicing tools can't handle
- RVIA (Recreation Vehicle Industry Association): 600,000+ RVs sold in 2025; growing backlog of repairs
- Marine repair: $11B US marine products services market; repair & maintenance = significant share

## Must-Have Filters
- [x] Problem is real (Fullbay validates adjacent trucking category at $167M acquisition; RV/marine shops have identical operational pain)
- [x] Can build without deep domain expertise (work order + DVI + SMS + invoicing workflow is same as auto repair)
- [x] Market is not dominated by a single unbeatable player (no dedicated marine/RV SaaS exists)
- [x] Revenue potential > $10K MRR within 12 months (22K+ shops × $79/mo × even 0.1% = $1,738/mo; 1% = $17,380/mo)

## Key Differentiators
1. RV-specific job templates: slide-out diagnostics, roof seal inspection, appliance repair, generator service
2. Marine-specific job types: engine hour tracking, bottom paint, winterization/spring commissioning checklists
3. Warranty claim tracking for manufacturer warranty repairs (common in RV/marine)
4. Deposit + partial payment workflows for expensive multi-week jobs
5. Seasonal scheduling and customer reminders for winterization/commissioning

## Build Plan
- **Fastest wedge**: Digital work order + DVI with SMS approval — same 3-week MVP as auto repair
- **Phase 2**: RV-specific job templates + engine hour tracking for marine
- **Phase 3**: Warranty claim portal, parts supplier integrations (RV parts from suppliers like PPL, etrailer)
- **Distribution**: RVTech Talk forum, RVIA member directory, Facebook "RV Repair Professionals"

## Next Steps
1. Talk to 10 RV repair shop owners — verify they're using paper or generic tools and what they pay now
2. Build on top of auto repair shop MVP codebase (reuse work order + DVI + invoicing core)
3. Launch with RV-specific job templates as the differentiator vs. Tekmetric/generic

## Risks
1. Smaller absolute TAM than auto repair (22K vs 280K shops) — may cap revenue
2. Seasonal demand in many regions; RV/marine shops have slow winters
3. Fullbay or AutoFluent could expand to RV specialty if they see the gap
4. Regulatory complexity for warranty claims (varies by manufacturer)
