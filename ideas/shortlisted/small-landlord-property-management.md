# Small Landlord Property Management (5-20 Units)

**Score**: 85/105 | **Tier**: 1 | **Verdict**: EXPLORE FURTHER | **Evaluation Date**: 2026-03-07

## One-Line Pitch
All-in-one property management for small landlords (5-20 units) at $19-29/mo flat vs AppFolio's 50-unit minimum and $298/mo.

## Problem
91% of landlords own 10 or fewer units. They're in no-man's land: too many units for spreadsheets, too few for enterprise PM software. AppFolio requires 50+ units ($298/mo minimum). Avail raised prices 300%+ after Realtor.com acquisition. DoorLoop charges $0.99/mo ACH fees. Tenant expectations for self-service portals, e-signatures, and mobile access are now baseline.

## Market Evidence
- 91% of US landlords own ≤10 units — massive underserved segment
- Avail: 300%+ price increase post-acquisition, users actively migrating
- FTC Dec 2025: warning letters to 13 PM software providers — regulatory disruption
- BiggerPockets 2M+ members actively discussing software alternatives
- Property management software market reaching $7.8B by 2033

## MVP Scope (6-8 weeks — complex)
1. Rent collection (ACH/credit card via Stripe)
2. Maintenance request portal for tenants
3. Lease management with e-signatures
4. Income/expense tracking with tax reports
5. Tenant communication (in-app messaging)
6. Basic tenant screening integration

## Go-to-Market
1. BiggerPockets forums and community
2. r/landlord, r/RealEstate, r/PropertyManagement
3. Content: "Best property management software for small landlords" — massive SEO
4. Price: $19-29/mo flat for up to 20 units, or $149-249 LTD with unit cap
